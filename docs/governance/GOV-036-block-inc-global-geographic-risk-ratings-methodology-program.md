## Block Inc. Global Geographic Risk Ratings Methodology & Program

## Introduction

The Global Geographic Risk Ratings Methodology & Program (the "Program") details the methodology and data sources used to calculate Geographic Risk Ratings (GRR) for money laundering, terrorist financing, economic sanctions, bribery, and corruption. The methodology includes a quantitative risk analysis based on data points and research, as well as a qualitative risk analysis conducted by Compliance Subject Matter Experts (Compliance SMEs).

This Program is established by Block, Inc. ("Block") and is applicable globally across all Block business lines, subsidiaries, and jurisdictions. References to "staff" throughout this document shall refer to all full-time employees and fixed-term and contingent workers including interns, temps, consultants, vendors, third-party providers, and contractors.

### Definitions

| Terminology | Definition |
|---|---|
| **FATF** | The Financial Action Task Force (FATF) leads global action to tackle money laundering, terrorist and proliferation financing. |
| **FinCEN** | The Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) is a bureau of the U.S. Department of the Treasury which safeguards the financial system from illicit use and money laundering. |
| **FINTRAC** | The Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada (FINTRAC) is Canada's financial intelligence unit and anti-money laundering and anti-terrorist financing supervisor. |

### Roles & Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Global Policy & Governance** | The Global Policy & Governance team's responsibility is to ensure geographic risk methodology accurately reflects company risk with respect to the compliance subject matter, approve the final risk scores, and maintain geographic risk scoring and integrate into company risk assessments where applicable, including the annual AML & Sanctions Risk Assessments, new product risk assessments, or any other assessment of inherent risk that implicates country risk from a compliance perspective. |
| **Compliance Engineering & Technology** | The Compliance Engineering & Technology team's responsibility is to implement and maintain final scoring in enhanced customer risk models and transaction monitoring systems, as applicable. |
| **Business Unit's Compliance Teams** (Including International Compliance Leads, Product Compliance and Operational decision making) | The Business Unit's Compliance teams responsibilities are to operationalize the final risk scoring into underlying procedures and processes (e.g. due diligence, product risk assessments, new business ventures). |
| **Strategic Operations** (i.e. Deal Desk) | The Strategic Operations team's responsibility is to integrate risk scoring into Merger and Activity due diligence and discussions. |
| **Procurement** | The Procurement team's responsibility is to integrate risk scoring into vendor due diligence, including ongoing due diligence. |

## Block, Inc. Requirements

### Methodology

For the quantitative risk analysis, Block leverages Knowyourcountry.com's Country Ratings, which is a composite financial crimes index with each country receiving a score between 1 (highest risk) and 100 (lowest risk). It assesses corruption, money laundering, tax haven, terrorist financing, and sanctions risks, among other data points.

Knowyourcountry.com's scores are converted to Geographic Risk Ratings of Low, Medium, and High, as illustrated in this chart.

| Score | Country Risk Tier | Country Risk Rating |
|---|---|---|
| 70.01 - 100 | 1 | Low |
| 50.01 - 70 | 2 | Medium |
| 0 - 50 | 3 | High |

In order to tailor these scores/ratings to the specific financial crimes risks facing Block and its subsidiaries, as well as to align with regulatory guidance on high risk countries for AML purposes, Block then considers the following risk factors into the final scores/ratings.

| Criteria | Additional Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing Risk Factors | Scoring/Rating Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Countries identified as high risk for AML/CTF purposes by Australia, the European Union, the United Kingdom, the United States, Japan, New Zealand and countries in which Block has offices. | High Risk override |
| 2 | Financial Action Task Force (FATF): High-Risk Jurisdictions Subject to a Call for Action; Jurisdictions Under Increased Monitoring | High Risk override |
| 3 | Countries identified as uncooperative tax havens | High Risk override |
| 4 | Countries identified as State Sponsors of Terrorism | High Risk override |
| 5 | Countries identified as high risk by Block's global leadership. | High Risk override |
| 6 | Other applicable regulatory advisories on countries of concern (e.g., U.S. State Dept., FINTRAC, etc.) | Up to -30 points |
| 7 | Recent, material negative news about a country's AML framework | Up to -10 points |
| **Additional Sanctions Risk Factors** | | |
| 8 | Countries/regions subject to comprehensive sanctions are considered prohibited under Block's Global Sanctions Policy. | Prohibited |
| 9 | Countries with governments subject to applicable, asset freezes/blocking sanctions programs | -25 points |
| 10 | Countries subject to applicable, sectoral or thematic sanctions & Countries with applicable, list-based asset freezes/blocking sanctions programs | -15 points |
| 11 | Countries bordering or conducting significant trade with countries subject to applicable, comprehensive sanctions programs | -10 points |
| 12 | Countries with a history of evading applicable sanctions programs | -10 points |

Compliance SMEs will then review the GRR scores to ensure they adequately reflect the risk as it relates to Block and its subsidiaries. Any proposed changes must be documented and approved by the Global Policy & Governance (GPG) lead or the Head of Sanctions. The new risk scores will be implemented after approval by the SMEs and the GPG lead or the Head of Sanctions.

## Compliance & Enforcement

### Governance

This methodology provides a reasonable estimate of inherent risk on a country-by-country basis for financial crimes concerns and may be incorporated into parallel risk analyses such as enhanced customer monitoring and business unit specific risk assessments.

The GRR will be finalized prior to the execution of the annual AML/Sanctions Risk Assessment and as required by the methodologies of other risk assessments, especially if they require more frequent updates.
