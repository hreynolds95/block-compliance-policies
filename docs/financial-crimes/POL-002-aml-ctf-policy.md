## Purpose

This policy establishes Block Inc.'s commitment to detecting, preventing, and reporting money laundering and terrorist financing in compliance with the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), FinCEN regulations, and applicable international AML/CTF laws.

## Scope

This policy applies to all Block Inc. business lines, employees, and contractors involved in the offering, processing, or oversight of financial products and services.

## Policy Statement

Block Inc. maintains a risk-based AML/CTF program that includes:

- **Customer Due Diligence (CDD):** Identity verification and risk assessment at onboarding and on an ongoing basis
- **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):** Additional scrutiny for high-risk customers, including PEPs and high-risk geographies
- **Transaction Monitoring:** Automated systems to detect suspicious activity patterns
- **Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR):** Timely filing with FinCEN when suspicious activity is identified
- **Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR):** Filing for cash transactions exceeding $10,000

## Roles and Responsibilities

**BSA Officer / Chief Compliance Officer:** Owns the AML/CTF program; primary regulatory contact.

**Financial Crimes Team:** Conducts investigations, files SARs and CTRs, and maintains program controls.

**Risk and Compliance:** Performs periodic risk assessments and program testing.

**Product and Engineering:** Builds and maintains KYC and transaction monitoring systems.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy or applicable AML/CTF laws may result in disciplinary action, regulatory penalties, and criminal liability. Employees must not tip off customers regarding SAR filings.

## Definitions

**BSA:** Bank Secrecy Act.

**SAR:** Suspicious Activity Report.

**PEP:** Politically Exposed Person.

**KYC:** Know Your Customer — the process of verifying customer identity and assessing risk.
