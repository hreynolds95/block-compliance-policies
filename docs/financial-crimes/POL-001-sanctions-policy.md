## Purpose

This policy establishes Block Inc.'s framework for complying with applicable economic and trade sanctions laws and regulations administered by OFAC, the EU, OFSI, and other relevant sanctions authorities.

## Scope

This policy applies to all Block Inc. employees, contractors, subsidiaries, and third-party partners globally who conduct or facilitate transactions or services on behalf of Block.

## Policy Statement

Block Inc. prohibits engaging in transactions or business relationships with:

- Individuals, entities, or countries subject to applicable sanctions programs
- Any party listed on OFAC's SDN List, EU Consolidated List, OFSI Consolidated List, or equivalent
- Jurisdictions subject to comprehensive sanctions (currently: Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Russia, Syria, Crimea/Donetsk/Luhansk regions)

All products and services must incorporate sanctions screening controls prior to onboarding and on an ongoing basis.

## Roles and Responsibilities

**Chief Compliance Officer:** Owns this policy and sanctions program overall.

**Financial Crimes Team:** Maintains sanctions screening lists, reviews escalated alerts, and files required reports.

**Product and Engineering:** Implements and maintains technical screening controls.

**Legal:** Advises on sanctions licenses, interpretations, and regulatory changes.

## Compliance and Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may carry civil or criminal penalties under applicable law. Suspected violations must be escalated to the Financial Crimes team immediately.

## Definitions

**OFAC:** Office of Foreign Assets Control, U.S. Treasury Department.

**SDN List:** Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons List.

**Sanctions Screening:** Automated or manual process of checking parties against applicable sanctions lists.
