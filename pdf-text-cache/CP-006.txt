1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Electronic Fund Transfer Act Policy (“Policyˮ) outlines Block, Inc.'s
U.S. commitment to adhering to regulatory requirements as well as industry standards and best
practices in the delivery of its products and services. This Policy is applicable in the United States
across all products that involve Electronic Fund Transfers (“EFTˮ) and Remittance Transfers as
those transactions are defined under the Electronic Fund Transfer Act (“EFTAˮ) 15 U.S.C. §§ 1693
et seq.) and its implementing regulation, Regulation E 12 C.F.R. Part 1005. This Policy establishes
comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries' electronic fund transfer
services while ensuring consistent consumer protections across all platforms.
The purpose of the EFTA and Regulation E is to establish the basic rights, liabilities, and
responsibilities of Consumers who use EFT and Remittance Transfer services and of the financial
institutions or other persons that offer these services. The primary objective of the EFTA and
Regulation E is the protection of individual Consumers engaging in EFTs and Remittance Transfers.
Scope: This Policy applies to Block, Inc. and its subsidiaries (collectively, "Block") in the United
States, governing all activities involving EFTs and Remittance Transfers subject to EFTA and
Regulation E. The only exception is Square Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered
Industrial Bank federally regulated by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its
own Compliance Program.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 Disclosures and Periodic Statements
Block will provide disclosures required by Regulation E to its Consumers in a form that is clear,
readily understandable, written in English, and can be kept by Consumers. The scope of
disclosures provided by Block to its Consumers includes, but is not limited to:1
● Initial Disclosures. Provided before a Consumer can make an EFT, including
information such as the liability of Block and the Consumer for Unauthorized EFTs,
contact information, available transfer types and limitations, applicable fees (including
those for ATMs), the Consumerʼs right to documentation and stop payment, and Blockʼs
confidentiality agreement.
● Change in Terms Disclosures. Provided at least 21 days before the effective date of
any change in a term or condition that was disclosed initially, if the change would result
in: increased fees for the Consumer, increased liability for the Consumer, changes in
types of EFTs available, or stricter limitations on the frequency or dollar amount of
transfers.
● Error Resolution Disclosures. Indicate how a Consumer can contact to file a NOE,
provided with the initial disclosure and thereafter either annually or with each periodic
statement.
● Receipts. Provided to a Consumer each time the Consumer initiates an EFT at an
Electronic Terminal, including the dollar amount of the EFT, date, type, Consumer
identification, terminal information, and the third-party where funds were transferred
to/from.

● Periodic Statements. Provided monthly to each Consumer that has had an EFT
processed on their account during the statement period, including transaction
information, account number, fees, account balance, and contact information.
Block must explain the consumer's right to stop payment of preauthorized electronic fund
transfers and the procedures for initiating such requests. The disclosure must address Block's
liability for failure to complete or stop transfers as requested, including the conditions under which
Block assumes liability and any applicable limitations.
2.2 Preauthorized Electronic Funds Transfers
Block will execute Preauthorized EFTs for its Consumers, upon request and with written
authorization, and provide a written notice of transfer within two 2 business days after the
transfer occurs (or is rejected) if the Preauthorized EFT is scheduled to transfer at least once every
sixty 60 days.
Block will stop payment of a Preauthorized EFT when it receives a stop payment request (orally or
in writing) at least three 3 business days before the date of the next scheduled transfer, from a
Consumer who has initiated the Preauthorized EFT.
Block will not require Consumers to repay any extension of credit by Preauthorized EFT.
2.3 Error Resolution
See “Remittance Transfersˮ section below for Error resolution requirements applicable to
Remittance Transfers. will provide its Consumers with alternative means to contact to provide a
NOE, such as through the app, chat messaging, by calling , or by mailing a letter to . will promptly
and reasonably address such NOEs as required by Regulation E. will not require its Consumers to
provide more information than is described in Regulation E before Block initiates an investigation.
Block will seek to resolve NOEs within ten 10 business days but, where circumstances so
require, may take up to forty-five 45 calendar days to do so, provided that in such circumstances
will:
● Provisionally credit the funds (including interest, where applicable) to the Consumerʼs
account within ten 10 business days;
● Advise the Consumer within two 2 business days of the Provisional Credit; and
● Give the Consumer full use of the funds during the investigation.
If, after forty-five 45 calendar days, Block has not determined whether an Error has occurred,
will make the Consumerʼs Provisional Credit permanent.
In the event that no Error, or a different Error occurred Block will:
● Provide notification to the customer advising that the Provisional Credit will be debited
within five 5 business days from the notification; and
● Specify the calendar date in which the debiting of the Provisional Credit will occur.

Upon completion of an investigation, Block will promptly report its determination to the Consumer,
and notify them of their right under Regulation E that they may request the information upon which
its investigation was based. If Block finds that an Error did occur, will refund its Consumer (or, if
applicable, finalize Provisional Credit) and provide any interest or refunds for related fees incurred
within one 1 business day. A Consumer might be held liable for an Unauthorized EFT in
circumstances where Block has provided accurate disclosures and the Consumer did not provide
timely notice, as described in Regulation E.
2.4 Access Devices
Block will only distribute Access Devices (i.e., Cash Cards) to Consumers who are soliciting a new
or replacement Access Device. will not distribute unsolicited Access Devices, unless required by
law or for purposes of account security.
2.5 Overdraft Services
Block does not provide overdraft services, as that term is defined under Regulation E, with respect
to any of its products or services.
2.6 Prepaid Accounts
Block will provide all disclosures described in this section before a Consumer acquires a Prepaid
Account. Prepaid Account disclosures must be:
● Provided to the Consumer in an electronic form when a Consumer acquires the Prepaid
Account through electronic means, including via a website or mobile application;
● Provided in a form that the Consumer can keep.
Block will provide a short-form disclosure for Prepaid Accounts that will include information
(applicable to ) such as the periodic fee, per purchase fee, ATM withdrawal fee, cash reload fee,
ATM balance inquiry fee, customer service fee, inactivity fee, variable fee, third-party fee, any
other types of fees (as appropriate), information about Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation
(“FDICˮ)/National Credit Union Administration (“NCUAˮ) insurance and registration, and
identification of the CFPB website for further information.
In addition, Block will provide a long form disclosure for Prepaid Accounts that includes content
such as a heading, fees, information on FDIC/NCUA insurance and registration, Block or its
subsidiary Contact information, and the CFPB website and telephone number.
Outside of, but in close proximity to, the short form disclosure, will disclose its name, the name of
the Prepaid Account program, any purchase price for the Prepaid Account, and any fee for
activating the Prepaid Account.

2.7 Public Posting of Prepaid Account Agreements
Block and its Bank Partner and prepaid card issuer will be responsible for updating the CFPB with
any material changes to the cardholder agreements for both non-Cash Card and Cash Card
Prepaid Accounts, respectively. Block and its Bank Partner will submit Prepaid Account
agreements to the CFPB on a rolling basis, no later than thirty 30 days after offering, amending,
or ceasing to offer any Prepaid Account agreement. Such submissions must contain identifying
information about the issuer and the agreement, the effective date, the name of the program
manager (if any), and the names of other relevant parties.
Block will post all Prepaid Account agreements that are submitted to the CFPB on its website in a
form that is readily usable by Consumers and complies with the requirements described by
Regulation E.
2.8 Gift Cards
Block offers digital gift cards that can be sent by a Consumer to any other Consumer. Prior to
purchasing a gift card, the Consumer will be provided disclosures from Block regarding terms and
conditions of expiration. The disclosures must be clear and conspicuous and contain commonly
accepted or readily understandable abbreviations or symbols. In addition, a disclosure regarding
the fundʼs expiration terms must appear electronically on the same screen as the digital gift card.
After the gift card has been purchased, Block will change neither the fees nor terms and conditions
of expiration that were disclosed prior to purchase.
2.9 Remittance Transfers
Block offers Remittance Transfer services allowing its Consumers to send and receive money
internationally. Block will disclose to its Consumers, before they pay for a Remittance Transfer,
required information such as the amount transferred, fees collected by Block, taxes incurred, total
amount charged to the sender, exchange rate used, applicable third-party fees, amount to be
received by the designated recipient, and a cancellation rights statement.
Block will adhere to requests to cancel Remittance Transfers if made within thirty 30 minutes of
the payment made when:
● The request to cancel enables to identify the senderʼs name and address or telephone
number and the particular transfer to be cancelled; and
● The transferred funds have not been picked up by the designated recipient or
deposited into an account of the designated recipient.
Block will investigate a senderʼs claim of an Error related to a Remittance Transfer when the NOE is
received from the sender within one hundred and eighty 180 calendar days of the disclosed
fundsʼ availability date. will determine whether an Error occurred within ninety 90 calendar days
of receiving a NOE.
7

Within three 3 business days of completing an Error investigation, Block will notify the sender of
the results of the investigation. If Block determines an Error occurred, Block will correct the Error
as designated by the sender within one 1 business day of, or as soon as reasonably practicable
after, receiving the senderʼs instructions regarding the appropriate remedy. Block, as appropriate,
either will:
● Refund the amount of funds provided by the sender in connection with a Remittance
Transfer which was not properly transmitted, or the amount appropriate to resolve the
Error; or
● Send the original amount as requested by the sender.
If the Error resulted because the recipient didnʼt receive the amount by the date initially disclosed,
then will do one of the following as applicable:
● Refund to the sender the amount of funds which was not properly transmitted, or the
amount appropriate to resolve the Error;
● Make available to the designated recipient the amount appropriate to resolve the Error,
without any additional cost to either party; or
● Refund any fees to the sender and, to the extent not prohibited by law, taxes imposed
for the Remittance Transfer, unless the sender provided incorrect or insufficient
information to Block in connection with the Remittance Transfer.
If it is determined that no Error or a different Error from what is alleged occurred, Block will provide
written notification to the sender of ʼs findings, which will note the senderʼs right to request
information that relied upon in making its determination. If the sender requests copies of
documentation that relied on during its investigation, Block will provide such documentation.
Block will provide further remedies to the sender as required by Regulation E and other relevant
policies.
2.10 Error Resolution Documentation
Block must document all stages of the error resolution process, including initial consumer
notification, investigation steps taken, and final resolution. Documentation must include copies of
all consumer correspondence, provisional credit notices, investigation findings, and any related
account adjustments. These records must demonstrate compliance with regulatory timeframes
and support Block's error resolution determinations.
3. Brand-Specific Requirements
Block will comply with ESign requirements, which may be detailed further in related Standards or
Procedures. This section outlines only the distinct implementations and unique requirements
specific to each subsidiary's lending products.

3.1 Cash App:
Cash App maintains distinct features in its electronic fund transfer services reflecting its
mobile-first, consumer-focused approach:
Disclosures and Periodic Statements: Cash App provides electronic disclosures through its mobile
application and website interfaces. Initial disclosures are integrated into the account opening
process, ensuring consumers receive and acknowledge required information before completing
their first electronic fund transfer. Periodic statements are delivered electronically and include
transaction details, fees assessed, and account information in a mobile-optimized format.
Error Resolution Process: Cash App provides multiple channels for error reporting, including in-app
messaging, chat support, phone support, and mail. Upon receiving an error notice, Cash App
implements a streamlined investigation process while maintaining required timelines. For
investigations extending beyond ten business days, Cash App provides provisional credit and
clearly communicates the provisional nature of such credits through in-app notifications.
Access Device Management: Cash App implements specific controls for its Cash Card program,
including specialized procedures for card activation, replacement, and security measures
integrated within the mobile application. Access device distribution occurs only upon consumer
request through the mobile application or website interfaces.
Prepaid Account Features: Cash App's prepaid account implementation includes specialized
disclosures integrated into the mobile interface. Account agreements are maintained on the Cash
App website in a readily accessible format and submitted to the CFPB as required. The platform
maintains specific procedures for updating and communicating changes to prepaid account terms.
Remittance Transfer Services: Cash App's remittance transfer service includes specialized
disclosures delivered through the mobile interface before transfer initiation. Exchange rates, fees,
and receipt requirements are integrated into the transfer flow, ensuring clear communication of
transfer terms and consumer rights.
3.2 Afterpay:
Afterpay's implementation addresses the unique aspects of buy-now-pay-later services:
Preauthorized Payment Requirements: Afterpay maintains specific requirements for recurring
payment authorizations in installment payment relationships. Prior to processing preauthorized
recurring payments, Afterpay obtains explicit authorization and provides clear confirmation of
autopay terms. The platform does not condition the extension of credit upon agreement to
recurring payments but may offer cost-related incentives for autopay enrollment.
Payment Variation Notices: When recurring payment amounts will vary from previous transfers,
Afterpay provides advance notice at least ten days before the scheduled transfer date. These
notices include the specific amount and scheduled date of the transfer.
9

Consumer Choice: Afterpay provides consumers with the ability to opt out of autopay at any time
through readily accessible interfaces. The platform maintains specific procedures for managing
payment preferences and implementing consumer choices regarding payment methods.
The above brand-specific requirements supplement the general compliance EFTA requirements
outlined in this Policy but are only applicable to their respective brands (i.e., Square Capital
requirements apply only to Square Capital operations, requirements apply only to operations, and
Afterpay requirements apply only to Afterpay operations).

4.5 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
