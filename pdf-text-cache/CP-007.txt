1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act Policy
("Policy") outlines Block, Inc.'s enterprise-wide approach to compliance with the Electronic
Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act ("ESign"). The purpose of this Policy is to
establish comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries' use of
electronic signatures and records, ensuring consistent operations while maintaining brand-specific
flexibility.
Scope: This Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act Policy (the "Policy")
outlines Block, Inc.'s enterprise-wide approach to compliance with the Electronic Signatures in
Global and National Commerce Act ("ESign"). This Policy establishes comprehensive guidelines
governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries' use of electronic signatures and records, ensuring
consistent operations while maintaining brand-specific flexibility. The only exception is Square
Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated by the
Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 Fundamental ESign Requirements
Block recognizes that electronic signatures and records form the foundation of modern digital
commerce. To ensure the validity and enforceability of electronic transactions, Block implements
four essential requirements across all its operations: 1 clear demonstration of intent to sign, 2
informed consent and demonstrated ability to access electronic records, 3 reliable association
between signatures and signers, and 4 secure record retention. These requirements enable Block
to provide information and notices electronically while maintaining compliance with all applicable
regulations.
ESign serves as an overlay to existing laws and regulations, ensuring that signatures, contracts, or
other records relating to a transaction maintain their validity when executed electronically. This
framework allows Block to facilitate efficient electronic commerce while preventing deception and
fraud in electronic transactions. Block achieves this through comprehensive systems and controls
that verify, record, and maintain electronic transactions in a manner that meets or exceeds
regulatory standards.
2.2 Consumer Notification and Transparency
Before initiating electronic communications, Block ensures transparent communication with
Consumers about the electronic relationship. Block provides clear and conspicuous information
about the scope of electronic communications, whether for a specific transaction or ongoing
relationship. This includes detailed explanations about the types of records that will be provided
electronically, such as disclosures, policies, account statements, and other important notices.
2

Block maintains transparency about Consumers' rights regarding paper records, ensuring they
understand their options to receive paper copies and any associated fees. Additionally, Block
clearly communicates the right to withdraw consent from electronic communications, including
straightforward procedures for withdrawal and any resulting consequences. This commitment to
transparency extends to keeping Consumers informed about technical requirements and
procedures for updating their electronic contact information.
2.3 Consumer Consent Process
Block implements a comprehensive consent process that ensures Consumers actively choose to
engage in electronic transactions. This process requires affirmative action by the Consumer and
demonstrates their ability to access electronic information in the formats we use. The consent
process is integrated into our various service platforms, ensuring Consumers understand and
agree to electronic communications before proceeding with transactions or account relationships.
During the onboarding process, consumers must affirmatively consent to receive electronic
disclosures. This consent is obtained in a manner that reasonably demonstrates their ability to
access information in the electronic form that will be used. Block achieves this through a process
where Consumers actively engage with electronic documents and confirm their ability to receive
and access them. Reading and acknowledging the electronic consent form through our platforms
serves as sufficient confirmation of this ability.
2.4 Withdrawal of Consent
Consumers maintain the right to withdraw their consent to electronic communications at any time.
Block provides multiple methods for withdrawal, clearly documented in initial disclosures and
readily accessible through Block service platforms. Upon receiving a withdrawal request, Block
processes it within a reasonable timeframe and provides confirmation of the withdrawal's effective
date in writing, either electronically or in paper form.
Block does not impose fees for processing consent withdrawals. However, Block clearly
communicates any consequences of withdrawal, such as potential impact on service availability or
account features. In cases where electronic communication is essential to service delivery,
withdrawal of consent may affect the Consumer's ability to access certain services. Block ensures
Consumers understand these implications while maintaining their right to choose their preferred
communication method.
2.5 Technical Requirements and Updates
Block maintains clear communication about the technical requirements for accessing electronic
records. Block provides detailed information about necessary hardware and software before any
electronic transaction occurs. This information helps Consumers make informed decisions about
their ability to engage in electronic transactions and maintain access to their records.
When technical requirements change, particularly in ways that could materially affect access to
electronic records, Block implements a comprehensive notification process. This includes:
3

● Providing clear explanations of new requirements
● Assessing potential impact on record accessibility
● Offering guidance on necessary updates
● Reaffirming consent where appropriate
● Reinforcing the right to withdraw consent without penalty
2.6 Invalid or Inoperable Contact Information
Block maintains procedures for handling cases where electronic contact information becomes
invalid or inoperable. This includes situations where email addresses or phone numbers
malfunction or become invalid. In such cases, Block may treat this as a withdrawal of consent for
electronic communications to that specific contact method. Block implements processes to:
● Detect delivery failures
● Attempt alternate contact methods
● Maintain service continuity where possible
● Protect consumer access to important information
2.7 Record Management and Accessibility
Blockʼs approach to electronic records emphasizes both security and accessibility. Block maintains
records in formats that preserve their integrity while ensuring authorized parties can access them
throughout their required retention period. This includes implementing systems for accurate record
creation, secure storage, and reliable reproduction when needed.
Block's record retention practices align with both internal policies and regulatory requirements.
Block maintains comprehensive systems to track retention periods and ensure records remain
available for the longer of any applicable retention requirements. This approach balances
accessibility needs with security considerations while maintaining regulatory compliance.
2.8 State Law Considerations
While ESign provides the federal framework for electronic signatures and records, Block
recognizes that state laws may impose additional or more stringent requirements. In jurisdictions
where state regulations overlap with ESign requirements, Block applies the more conservative
approach to ensure comprehensive compliance. This includes adherence to state-specific
versions of the Uniform Electronic Transactions Act UETA and any state-specific consumer
protection provisions.
When conflicts arise between applicable laws and these requirements, Block consults with Legal
Counsel to resolve such conflicts and adjust our practices accordingly. This ensures Block
maintains compliance while providing consistent service across our operating jurisdictions.

3. Brand-Specific Requirements
Block will comply with ESign requirements, which may be detailed further in related Standards or
Procedures. This section outlines only the distinct implementations and unique requirements
specific to each subsidiary's lending products.
3.1 Cash App:
Cash App's implementation of ESign requirements reflects its mobile-first, consumer-focused
approach to financial services. While adhering to Block's core ESign requirements, Cash App
maintains several distinct features in its implementation:
Consumer Onboarding and Consent: Cash App employs a streamlined, mobile-optimized
onboarding process where ESign consent is integrated directly into the application flow. This
differs from other Block platforms by requiring electronic consent for all consumer-facing
disclosures at the initial account creation stage, except in specific circumstances where ESign
compliance is not required. The platform's unique approach ensures that consumers can
demonstrate their ability to access electronic records through the mobile interface before
proceeding with account activation.
Communication Management: Unlike other Block platforms, Cash App treats the provision of an
invalid email address or phone number, or the subsequent malfunction of previously valid contact
information, as an automatic withdrawal of consent for that specific communication channel. This
consumer protection measure helps ensure reliable delivery of important account information and
maintains service integrity.
Service Continuity: When consent is withdrawn, Cash App implements a distinct approach to
ongoing service access. While the platform transitions affected communications to paper format,
Consumers retain the ability to electronically download documents and records through the Cash
App website, providing a hybrid solution that balances consumer preference with service
accessibility.
3.2 Square:
Square's merchant-facing systems incorporate unique technical requirements that extend beyond
Block's standard ESign framework. This includes:
● Specialized hardware and software configurations for point-of-sale systems
● Enhanced record retention procedures for merchant transactions
● Custom implementation guides for different merchant categories
● Dedicated technical support for electronic signature integration
3.3 Afterpay:
Afterpay's implementation of ESign requirements is tailored to its buy-now-pay-later service
model, with several key distinctions from Block's standard approach:
5

Installment Agreement Process: Afterpay maintains specific requirements for electronic signatures
in installment payment agreements that go beyond Block's standard framework:
● Enhanced disclosure requirements for payment terms
● Specialized consent processes for recurring payment authorizations
● Additional verification steps for installment plan acceptance
Merchant Integration Requirements: Afterpay's merchant integration model includes unique
elements not found in other Block platforms:
● Custom implementation requirements for checkout flow integration
● Specific technical standards for capturing consumer consent during purchase
● Enhanced record-keeping requirements for installment payment agreements
● Specialized procedures for managing electronic communications across the installment
period
State Law Compliance: Given the nature of buy-now-pay-later transactions, Afterpay maintains
additional state-specific requirements beyond Block's standard framework. When state regulations
impose stricter requirements than federal ESign provisions, Afterpay implements the more
conservative approach, including:
● State-specific disclosure requirements
● Additional consent verification steps in certain jurisdictions
● Enhanced record retention requirements for installment agreements
● Modified withdrawal procedures based on state regulations
3.4 Cross-Platform Considerations
While each platform maintains its distinct features, certain situations require coordination across
Block's brands, particularly when Consumers utilize multiple services. In these cases:
● Consent status is maintained separately for each platform
● Withdrawal of consent from one service does not automatically affect others
● Technical requirements are platform-specific
● Record retention follows the most conservative requirement applicable
● Consumer communications clearly distinguish between platform-specific and
enterprise-wide notifications
The above brand-specific requirements supplement the general compliance ESign requirements
outlined in this Policy and are only applicable to their respective brands,

4.5 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
