1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The Financial Crimes Transaction Monitoring Program о‚ЃTMPо‚‚ is a key
component of Square AU Pty Ltd's ("Square AU") Anti-Money Laundering & Counter-Terrorism
Financing Program, designed to meet the obligations set out by the Australian Transaction Report
and Analysts Centre о‚ЃAUSTRACо‚‚.
The Financial Crime Transaction Monitoring Program for Square AU is overseen by the Square AU
CCO, who provides approval and ensures regulatory compliance. The AML/CTF Compliance
Officer is responsible for managing and updating the program when required, ensuring it adheres
to local regulatory requirements.
In accordance with Square AU Anti-Money Laundering & Counter-Terrorism Financing Program,
the TMP establishes methods to monitor, detect, and prevent possible money laundering, financial
crimes, terrorist financing and proliferation financing for Square AU's customer transactions in
Australia. The program is designed to identify, mitigate and manage money laundering, terrorism
financing and proliferation financing risks in compliance with AUSTRAC's requirements and the
AML/CTF Act. Its primary objective is to identify and report suspicious transactions, including but
not limited to those that deviate from a customer's normal behaviour, involve high-risk jurisdiction,
or trigger specific red-flag indicators.
Scope: This Program is established by Square AU Pty Ltd о‚ЃSquare AUо‚‚ and applicable to Square
AU products and services, such as Square Point of Sale ("POS") and other financial products that
process transactions which are deemed to fall within the scope of this Program. It is implemented,
managed and maintained by the Square Australian о‚ЃAUо‚‚ Compliance team and sets out the
approach to design and execution Square AU's Transaction Monitoring program.

2. Program Requirements
The Transaction Monitoring program is designed and maintained based on the annual AML/CTF &
Sanction Risk Assessment conducted by Compliance Governance and Reporting AML/CTF &
Sanctions Risk Assessment Team о‚ЃCGRо‚€RAо‚‚. This program undergoes an annual review to assess
its effectiveness, address any significant findings, and implement necessary improvements.
Additional reviews may be conducted following events such as identified issues or significant
regulatory changes. In addition, the program is subject to regular testing and independent auditing
to evaluate its performance, accuracy, and compliance with AUSTRAC requirements.
This program also outlines the compliance controls that are used for the evaluation and
management of Square's program as it relates to money laundering, terrorist financing,
proliferation financing and financial crime related risk, as well as how to identify suspicious
customer transactions.
These controls include, but are not limited to:
в—Џ Coverage Assessments;
в—Џ Model Governance and Monitoring; and
в—Џ Transaction Monitoring Alert Review Procedures and Processes.
2.1 Coverage Assessments
Square conducts annual Anti-Money Laundering о‚ЃAMLо‚‚ and Transaction Monitoring о‚ЃTMо‚‚ Risk
Coverage Assessments. These assessments, known as Typology Assessments, help identify any
gaps and inform the design of the program.
At a high level, these assessments involve identifying key inputs, compiling risk indicators and
regulatory requirements, aligned to covered markets and products. The assessments include
reviewing documentation, conducting Subject Matter Expert о‚ЃSMEо‚‚ interviews, and assessing
3

current manual and automated monitoring controls. A coverage analysis is performed to identify
gaps ensuring comprehensive risk coverage and regulatory compliance.
The Square AU Compliance team is involved in the coverage assessment process across all
phases, with involvement from the Square AU CCO, AML/CTF Compliance Officer, Compliance AU
Program Manager and Square APAC Transaction Monitoring Lead. These phases include the
creation of documentation, gathering and finalising risk rating, drafting, and approving
management action plans.
2.2 Model Governance and Queue Parameters
Square utilises various proprietary algorithms to create cases for the Compliance analysts to
review. The creation, review and governance of these models is covered by the Block | Model
Governance Policy and Block | Model Change Management Procedure. These include automated
case queues and periodic or manual reports for review.
Models cover multiple typologies to detect anomalous activity as outlined in the Coverage
Assessment, including, but not limited to:
в—Џ Structuring;
в—Џ Profiling;
в—Џ Velocity;
в—Џ Lack of Economic Purpose;
в—Џ Patterning;
в—Џ High Risk Geography; and
в—Џ Funnelling.
Square AU's Transaction Monitoring queues are established and maintained by the Compliance
Engineering & Technology team. This team will evaluate effectiveness of the alerts generated by
queues (i.e. productivity) and make recommendations for tuning the models, adding further
models or changing existing models. Detailed rules of Square AU's Transaction Monitoring queues
are maintained in Square | о‚ѓAUо‚„ TM Rulebook. Any new models or recommended changes to the
existing models are reviewed and approved in accordance with the Block | Model Change
Management Procedure.
To ensure oversight of the Transaction Monitoring queues, Square has implemented change
management and model validation policies, managed and executed by the Compliance Engineering
& Technology team, in accordance with the Block | Model Change Management Procedure.. Any
changes applicable to Square AU must be approved by both the AU Compliance Program Approver
and the AU Compliance Operations Approver, ensuring continued local oversight.
2.3 Transaction Monitoring Alert Review Procedures and Processes
The Analyst reviews alerts based on written procedures that are routinely reviewed and updated.
In general, the investigation of a case follows the steps outlined below:
1. The Analyst claims a new alert case in Regulator and notes the typologies, alert type, and
product; and
4

2. The Analyst reviews customer information in conjunction with account activity, web
presence, and potentially adverse media in order to make a determination of an appropriate
case action.
Following that, the analyst can take one of the following actions:
в—Џ Clear: Account activity represents typical product use with no adverse or potentially
unusual activity identified;
в—Џ Clear and escalate: Account activity represents typical product use with no adverse or
unusual activity identified, however potential terms of service violations or sanctions
concerns exist and require an escalation in a timely manner to relevant queues;
в—Џ Escalate to ECDD team for an ECDD review when:
в—‹ The merchant has no apparent economic or legal purpose; or
в—‹ The merchant has made unusually complex or large transactions; or
в—‹ Would involve an unusual pattern of transactions;
в—Џ Request for more information: In instances where The Analyst is not able to confidently
take a вЂњclearЛ® or вЂњdeactivationЛ® action type, the Analyst may request more information from
the merchant. Once this information is received from the merchant, the Analyst will
re-evaluate the case with the additional context in order to action the case.
в—Џ Deactivate: If account activity violates our terms without indicating any involvement in
money laundering, terrorist financing or proliferation financing, the merchantКјs account will
be deactivated with no further action required.
в—Џ Deactivate and Escalate: Account activity appears unusual and does not represent typical
product use. The merchant account will be deactivated and escalated to the Compliance
Investigation queue in a timely manner for further investigation and potential reporting to
AUSTRAC.
2.4 Systems
The Square AU Compliance Team uses a Case Management solution called Regulator. Third party
vendor tools are used to supplement reviews and include, but are not limited to, Legit Script. Some
of these tools are integrated within Regulator.
2.5 Training
Square AU ensures that the Analysts working on Transaction Monitoring cases are fully trained in
the procedures and appropriate steps to investigate and provide rationale for reviewing alerts
through a combination of new hire, on the job and ad hoc continuous training, according to Square
| Anti-Money Laundering & Counter-Terrorism Risk Awareness Training Program.
2.6 Quality Assurance
The Square Compliance Quality Team is a separate team from the Square AU Compliance Team
that exercises quality assurance by reviewing decisions made by the analysts during Transaction
Monitoring reviews. The Square Compliance Quality Team reports the results of the reviews to
individual team members, their leads and senior management.
