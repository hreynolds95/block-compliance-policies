1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Servicemembers Civil Relief Act Policy (the "Policy") outlines
Block's enterprise-wide approach to compliance with the Servicemember Civil Relief Act (“SCRAˮ).
The purpose of this Policy is to establish comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc., and its
U.S. subsidiaries' compliance with the SCRA, ensuring consistent operations while maintaining
brand-specific flexibility, where applicable. SCRA governs how financial institutions treat loans
when a borrower is called to Active Duty Military Service, and the benefits extended to the
Dependents of the impacted Servicemember. Block's Business Product Compliance Lead, Lending
& Credit, and/or their designee, is responsible for the management of this Policy.
Scope: This Policy applies to all consumer Credit Products offered by Block, Inc., its subsidiaries,
and affiliates (collectively, "Block"), in the United States, including, but not limited to installment
loans, buy-now, pay-later products, and other forms of consumer lending. The only exception is
Square Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated
by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 SCRA Policy Requirements
Block is committed to providing comprehensive SCRA benefits and protections to eligible
Servicemembers and their Dependants. The SCRA's benefits and protections typically begin on the
date that a Servicemember is called to Active Duty Military Service. For reservists and members of
the National Guard who are ordered to report to Active Duty, these protections begin on the date
they receive their orders calling them to Active Duty Military Service.
The fundamental purpose of these protections is to enable Servicemembers to focus on their
military duties without the burden of financial concerns. Block recognizes this purpose and
implements comprehensive protections that often exceed the minimum requirements of the Act.
2.2 Interest Rate and Fee Benefits
When a Servicemember enters Active Duty, Block implements significant interest rate and fee
protections. For obligations incurred prior to entering Military Service, Block reduces the interest
rate to 6% per annum, inclusive of all fees and charges. Any interest in excess of 6% is
permanently forgiven. The interest rate reduction applies from the date the Servicemember enters
Active Duty.
To qualify for the interest rate reduction, a Servicemember must demonstrate either current Active
Duty status or submit their request within 180 days of termination of Active Duty. While
Servicemembers may provide military orders or a Department of Defense short form to verify their
status, Block takes a proactive approach to verification. Upon receiving notice of potential military
service, Block independently verifies eligibility through the Department of Defense Manpower Data
Center (“DMDCˮ) Website. If eligibility is confirmed through DMDC, Block automatically applies the
benefits without requiring additional documentation from the Servicemember.
During the benefit period, Block suspends all fees of any kind on the account. Furthermore, Block
will not sell the debt to any third party or initiate legal action against the Servicemember during
Active Duty or for 90 days thereafter. These protections ensure comprehensive financial relief
during the Servicemember's Period of Military Service.
2.3 Identification and Verification Process
Block maintains a thorough yet flexible process for identifying and verifying eligible
Servicemembers. While the initial responsibility lies with the Servicemember or their family to
notify Block of their military status, Block's verification process is designed to minimize the burden
on serving military personnel.
Upon receiving notification of military service, Block initiates a verification process that includes
checking the DMDC database and reviewing any provided documentation. This documentation
may include military orders, a certified letter from a commanding officer, or other official military
documentation. Block maintains copies of all supporting documentation in the customer's file for
3

the duration of the period of Military Service and in accordance with record retention
requirements.
The verification process must be completed within 30 calendar days of the initial notification.
During this period, Block takes reasonable follow-up steps if needed documentation is not
received, including additional DMDC searches and customer outreach via phone, email, or letter. If
verification cannot be completed within the 30-day timeframe, Block accepts the Servicemember's
claim as valid pending further documentation.
For Military Reservists and those ordered to report for induction, Block recognizes that Military
Service begins on the date of receipt of orders, rather than the reporting date. This early
recognition ensures that protections are in place before active service begins.
2.4 Default Judgment Protections
Block maintains stringent procedures regarding Default Judgments involving potential
Servicemembers. Before pursuing any Default Judgment, Block conducts a mandatory search of
the DMDC Website to determine the defendant's military status. This search must be documented
and included with any court filing.
When filing for a Default Judgment, Block must submit an affidavit that either:
● States whether the defendant is in Military Service and provides supporting facts, or
● Indicates that Block is unable to determine the defendant's military status
If a defendant is found to be in Military Service, Block complies with all court-ordered protections
and stays. During any stay period, Block suspends all penalties and enforcement actions. These
protections apply to debts incurred both before and during Active Duty, providing comprehensive
protection for Servicemembers involved in legal proceedings.
2.5 Ongoing Account Management
Block implements comprehensive account management procedures for SCRA-protected accounts.
Each protected account receives special handling status to ensure all benefits and protections are
properly applied and maintained. This includes regular reviews of Active Duty status, careful
monitoring of interest rates and fees, and special notation to prevent prohibited collection
activities.
For accounts with SCRA protections, Block maintains detailed records of:
● The date protection began
● The basis for SCRA eligibility
● All rate and fee adjustments
● Communications regarding SCRA benefits
● Scheduled protection end dates
● Post-service protection periods
4

Block conducts periodic reviews of protected accounts to verify continued eligibility and ensure
accurate benefit application. When Active Duty status can no longer be verified, Block provides
advance notice before removing protections, ensuring a smooth transition from protected status.
2.6 Protection of Rights and Prohibitions
Block strictly prohibits practices that could infringe upon or discourage the exercise of SCRA
rights. Servicemembers' exercise of SCRA rights will never adversely affect their ability to obtain
future financial services from Block. Specifically, Block prohibits:
● The acceleration of principal amounts due in response to SCRA benefit requests. Block
maintains the original payment schedule, adjusted only to reflect the reduced interest rate
and fee waivers.
● Wage garnishment or asset seizure actions against protected Servicemembers. During the
protection period, Block suspends all extraordinary collection activities.
● Mandatory arbitration requirements or complex legal notice procedures for
SCRA-protected accounts. Block ensures that Servicemembers retain full legal protections
under the Act.
● Any requirement for Servicemembers to waive their SCRA rights. While Servicemembers
may voluntarily waive their rights through a signed written instrument executed during or
after their period of MIlitary Service, Block never requests or requires such waivers.
Credit-related adverse actions, including:
● Determining that a Servicemember cannot meet their obligations
● Denying or revoking credit based on SCRA status
● Changing existing credit terms due to military service
● Issuing adverse credit reports related to SCRA protections
● Noting military status in credit evaluation processes
2.7 Extended Protections
Block extends SCRA protections beyond the Servicemember to qualified family members and other
eligible parties. Dependents, including spouses and children as defined in this Policy, may request
and receive SCRA benefits on behalf of the Servicemember. Additionally, persons secondarily
liable on a Servicemember's obligation may receive similar protections through court application.
These comprehensive protections and prohibitions ensure that Block not only meets but exceeds
the requirements of the SCRA, providing robust financial protection for Servicemembers and their
families during periods of military service.
