1. Introduction
Square Kabushiki Kaisha (“Square K.K.ˮ or “Companyˮ) is a Payment Service Provider (“PSPˮ) in Japan
that is not at this point directly regulated or supervised by any authorities in Japan. However, as a
company that partners with international credit card brands, card issuers, QR-code money transfers, and
acquirers, Square K.K. is responsible for satisfying various regulations and guidelines that must be
adhered to. The parent company of Square K.K. is Block, Inc. (“Blockˮ), a merchant services aggregator
and mobile payments company based in Oakland, California, USA. Block is registered as a Money Services
Business with the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (“FinCENˮ).
Compliance is critical for Square K.K. as it ensures the company can sustain its vital partnerships and
function effectively within the payment services industry. By adhering to relevant regulations and
guidelines, Square K.K. mitigates the risk of liabilities occurring when operating in Japan. Furthermore, a
robust compliance framework fosters trust and credibility with customers and partners, enhancing the
company's reputation. Compliance also ensures that Square K.K. aligns with the strategic objectives of its
parent company, Block, promoting consistent and reliable operations across various markets.
This Compliance Management System (“CMSˮ) Policy (“Policyˮ) specifically addresses financial
regulatory compliance, focusing on adhering to the standards and requirements set by local and foreign
financial authorities and industry bodies. The CMS Policy of Square K.K. demonstrates the commitment of
the JP CCO and compliance team to implementing and maintaining effective compliance management to
meet the expected compliance standards. This commitment helps maintain strong relationships with
partners and customers and supports the Companyʼs goal of providing reliable and innovative payment
services.

2. Purpose
This Policy establishes the framework within which Square K.K. effectively implements a set of compliance
activities as part of its CMS. The Board recognizes that maintaining an effective CMS is crucial for
mitigating risks arising from legal and regulatory violations, as well as preventing potential harm to
customers and partners. It is also essential for sustaining a high level of public confidence in Square K.K.'s
safe and sound operating practices. Square K.K. achieves this by integrating a robust compliance
structure into daily operations, ensuring that the Company promptly identifies issues and initiates
appropriate and timely remediation actions.

4.1 Responsibility of CCO and JP Compliance Team
Square K.K. requires its CMS to be tailored to the organization's structure, business strategy, operational
complexity, product and service diversity, market circumstances, regulatory environment, and risk profile.
Since these essential criteria are dynamic, the Board and the JP CCO must review and revise the CMS but
also, where appropriate, broader aspects of the compliance framework, as necessary to respond to
changes.
Currently, the responsibilities of the JP CCO and the JP Compliance Team, as defined within Square K.K.'s
CMS, are as follows:
● Define roles and responsibilities for regulatory compliance activities covered by Square
K.K.ʼs CMS;
● Create and maintain the necessary policies, programs, and procedures to ensure
compliance with applicable laws and regulations;
● Monitor regulatory trends applicable to Square K.K. and revise policies, programs, and
procedures as needed to maintain internal compliance systems;
● Monitor compliance status within Square K.K.'s operations to ensure consistent and
ongoing compliance;
● Provide necessary compliance insights for business reviews and other considerations;
● Support initiatives to evaluate inherent risks, controls, and residual risks, and consider
necessary responses to identified risks;
● Develop monitoring programs and audit requirements to identify operational and
programmatic deficiencies, and support the implementation of these monitoring and audit
activities;

4.2 The Compliance Management System
4.2.1 Board Oversight and Reporting
The JP CCO , as the ultimate responsible person for the compliance operation of Square K.K. shall report
the following items to the Board and the Senior Compliance Managements of Block on a quarterly or
annual basis with support from other relevant global functions:
● The status of compliance maintenance at Square K.K. for the relevant period/year;
● Revisions and approvals of compliance policies and key programs;
● Results of risk assessments;
● Results of monitoring and audits conducted on business, and the status of corrective actions
for identified deficiencies;
● Implementation and attendance status of training; and
● Other matters deemed necessary for maintaining compliance at Square K.K. and Block.
Additionally, if any compliance-related incident which may have a material negative impact, such as a
misconduct and violation of laws and regulations, is discovered, the JP CCO must report it promptly to the
Board and the Senior Compliance Management of Block without waiting for the next board meeting.

4.2.1 取締役会の監督と報告

● 研修の実施と受講状況
● その他、当社及びBlockのコンプライアンスの維持に必要と判断されること;

Square K.K. requires that compliance-related policies, standards, and procedures be documented in
written form, in sufficient detail to implement compliance requirements, and are formally approved,
adopted, or recognized. Policies, standards, and procedures are updated periodically by their respective
owners, and are reviewed and approved as outlined in the Block Compliance Policy on Policies (“PoPˮ), as
required. All policies and standards are posted in a shared location, as outlined in the PoP.
4.2.2 ポリシー、基準、および手続き
当社は、十分に詳細なでコンプライアンス要件に基づく実務を実施できる水準まで、コンプライアンスに関連したポリシー、プ

基準は、PoPで定義された共通の場所に掲示されます。
4.2.3 Response to Regulatory Changes
The JP Legal and JP Policy identify, monitor, and track relevant material regulatory changes that may
impact Square K.K. products, services, and operations. The JP Legal and JP Policy will conduct an
applicability analysis for certain regulatory changes and, where necessary, notify key stakeholders, such
as JP CCO, JP Compliance Team, Product Direct Responsibility Individuals (“DRIsˮ).
The JP Compliance Team, with the support of cross-functional groups within Block, will request affected
business teams to identify the impacts of regulatory amendments and formulate plans to address these
changes. The affected business teams will be responsible for implementing these plans.
Block Legal, JP CCO and JP Compliance Teams are responsible for reviewing new or materially changed
business products (namely marketing materials, customer communications, products, or features) to
determine whether new or updated controls are required, Square K.K. will be responsible for reporting the
results of their review and validation to Block. The results of reviews and decisions will be documented
and maintained.

4.2.4 Compliance Operations
JP CCO and JP Compliance Team are responsible for ensuring adherence to relevant regulations, internal
policies and Terms of Services of Square K.K. services across customer onboarding and transactions. This
includes activities onboarding processes including Know Your Customer (“KYCˮ) and Know Your Business
(“KYBˮ) of merchant customers, ongoing due diligence, transaction monitoring, and other
compliance-related functions.
The JP CCO and JP Compliance Team collaborates closely with key stakeholders, including the Senior
Compliance Managements and Compliance Teams of Block. All compliance operations, decisions, and
reviews are thoroughly documented and maintained to ensure accountability. These operations are
monitored and reviewed through monitoring, internal audits, and third-party examinations, and
improvements are made if deficiencies are found, ensuring compliance for Square K.K.
4.2.4 コンプライアンスオペレーションの実施

4.2.5 Support for Risk Assessment
Block implements risk assessments that is a process for evaluating the compliance risk arising from: (i)
Square K.K.ʼs operating environments; (ii) the regulatory landscape; (iii) emerging industry and regulatory
trends; (iv) the Companyʼs size, complexity, and product offerings; and (v) the effectiveness of internal
controls. These risk assessments assist Square K.K. in identifying additional control needs, prioritizing
compliance resources, and adjusting the CMS to meet new and emerging risks.
On an annual basis, the JP CCO is responsible for supporting Blockʼscross-functional groups and
stakeholders across product, legal, and compliance in conducting the risk assessments of the Company,
and for reporting the results to the Board. This review process and its outcomes must be documented and
preserved. The CCO is required to evaluate necessary actions when risk assessments conclude
inadequate risk controls or insufficient resources to address new risks.
4.2.5 リスク評価の実施支援
親会社であるBlockは、(i) Square K.K.の運営環境、 (ii) 規制の状況、 (iii) 新たな業界および規制の傾向、 (iv) 会社の規
模、複雑さ、および製品提供、 (v) 内部統制の有効性から生じるコンプライアンスリスクを評価するリスク評価を実施します。
リスク評価を行うことにより、当社は追加のコントロールが必要であるかを特定し、コンプライアンスリソースの優先的な配分
先を決定し、新たなリスクに対応するためにCMSを調整するものとします。
JP CCOは、毎年、製品、法務、およびコンプライアンスに関係するBlockの機能横断的なグループやその他関係者が行う
当社のリスク評価の実施を支援し、結果を取締役会に報告する責任を負います。これらの評価、検討および決定について
は、文書化し、保存されます。JP CCOはリスクアセスメントの結果、リスクのコントロールが不足していると判断されたとき、

4.2.7 Monitoring and Testing
Blockʼs Compliance Team develops a monitoring and testing plan annually based on the size, scale, and
complexity of Square K.Kʼs operations and business offerings. The monitoring plan reflects the risk profile
of the Square K.K., including relevant third-party risk, as well as compliance risk impacts from new
products, regulatory changes, previous testing findings, management action plans and due dates, audit
coverage areas, and any business partner requests. Corrective action to any noted deficiencies will be
implemented efficiently and documented accordingly.
The JP CCO is responsible for supporting Block in ensuring effective monitoring and testing
implementation, and for reporting the results to the Board. The CCO is required to implement necessary
actions and monitor the progress when any deficiency and corrective actions are identified withSquare
K.K. business and compliance operations.
For more information on this process, please refer to Block'sGlobal Compliance Monitoring and Testing
Program.

4.2.8 Audit Support
Block conducts periodic risk-based independent audits of requirements of each business entity and
business, including Square K.K. In addition, Square K.K. engages in internal audit and third-party audit to
facilitate the independent review. The audit scopes may also, as and when appropriate, include
independent validation of remediation of Issues identified through the audit process.
The JP CCO is responsible for supporting the audits on Square K.K. 's compliance activities and reporting
the results of these periodic audits, including updates on Issues validation, to the Board on a periodic
basis.
4.2.8 監査対応

K.K.は、内部監査および第三者監査を通じて独立したレビュー下にあります。監査では適切と判断される場合、その監査を
通じて特定された課題の是正措置に関する検証も含まれます。
JP CCOは、Square K.K.のコンプライアンス活動に対して適切な監査が実施できるよう支援し、これらの定期的な監査の結
果、及び是正措置に関するアップデートを取締役会に定期的に報告する責任を負います。

The Board and/or Blockʼs Senior Compliance Management related to the operation of the Square K.K.
compliance activities have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this Policy, provided that
the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and regulations. Policy exception
requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and provide a description of the
controls that Square K.K. will implement to mitigate risks associated with the exception. Any approved
exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with recordkeeping requirements.
6.例外の承認
