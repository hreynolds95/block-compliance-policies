1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The Employee and Personnel Due Diligence Program (the “Programˮ)
details Square AUʼs risk based approach to screen employees and personnel to minimise the risks
that they may commit, or assist others in committing money laundering, financing of terrorism
(“ML/TFˮ) or other financial crime offences.
The purpose of this Program is to establish and maintain an effective framework for assessing and
managing the risks associated with employing or engaging individuals who may be involved in
activities that could facilitate money laundering, terrorist financing, or proliferation financing. This
program is designed to ensure that all personnel , particularly those in roles that influence
AML/CTF outcomes or have access to sensitive financial information or decision-making authority
over high-risk activities, undergo thorough background checks and risk-based ongoing
monitoring. By identifying and mitigating potential risks early, the program helps protect Square AU
from reputational, legal, and financial harm, while ensuring compliance with the AML/CTF Act and
supporting the ongoing effectiveness of Square AUʼs AML/CTF controls.
Scope: The Program applies to all employees (including full time, part time and casual workers)
and directors (collectively known as “employeesˮ) of Square AU, and personnel (including
individuals employed or otherwise engaged by Square AU who perform functions relevant to its
AML/CTF obligations).
Where individuals are engaged through employment agencies or other third-party arrangements,
Square AU satisfies its personnel due diligence obligations through contractual screening
requirements and oversight mechanisms agreed with the relevant agency or provider.
Due diligence procedures, including background checks appropriate to the AML/CTF risk of the
role, are conducted by the employment agency pursuant to the applicable services agreement.
Square AU reserves the right to review, test and audit the agencyʼs screening procedures and

requires hiring managers to attest that screening proportionate to the risk of the role has been
completed prior to commencement.
The Program applies when Square AU transfers contractors into permanent roles as direct hires of
Square AU.
Program Requirements Square AU adopts a risk-based approach to Employee and Personnel Due
Diligence. Personnel performing functions relevant to Square AUʼs AML/CTF obligations are
subject to formal due diligence requirements proportionate to their level of financial crime risk,
regulatory exposure, and decision-making authority.
In addition, to address risks arising from broader organisational structures and cross-functional
responsibilities, Square AU applies baseline due diligence measures across all employees to
support consistent risk management, governance, and oversight.

2. Role-Level Risk Assessment Framework
Square AU applies a role-level risk assessment methodology to determine the appropriate level of
Personnel Due Diligence (“PDDˮ) required for Personnel.
This assessment recognises that roles present varying levels of exposure to money laundering and
terrorism financing (“ML/TFˮ) risk based on their access, authority, decision-making capacity and
influence over AML/CTF controls.
Square AU conducts personnel due diligence both prior to employment or engagement and on an
ongoing basis during employment.
This approach is designed to ensure that personnel due diligence is risk-based, proportionate to
the AML/CTF risk exposure of the role, and aligned with Square AUʼs broader AML/CTF
compliance obligations.
The purpose of the role-level assessment is to ensure that personnel due diligence measures are
proportionate to:
● The level of decision-making authority exercised by the role;
● The roleʼs ability to influence or design AML/CTF controls;
● Access to regulatory reporting functions;
● Access to sensitive customer or business information;
● The potential impact on Square AUʼs AML/CTF compliance obligations if the role fails.
The role-level assessment applies to all Personnel performing AML/CTF functions across Square
AU, including oversight, investigations, onboarding, detection, escalation and customer-facing
functions where relevant.
The outcomes of this assessment inform the allocation of roles into the appropriate EDD Screening
Package and are documented in Appendix A.
2.1. Roles and Methodology
2.1.1. Identification of Personnel Performing AML/CTF Functions
Square AU identifies all personnel who perform, or are responsible for overseeing, influencing or
supporting functions relevant to Square AUʼs obligations under the AML/CTF Act.
Personnel Due Diligence Program | Square | Square AU Pty Ltd 4

This includes people who:
● Hold AML/CTF governance roles, such as governing body, senior manager(s) and
AML/CTF compliance officer;
● Have responsibilities under the AML/CTF program;
● Work in roles that could be exposed to ML/TF risks;
● Perform any other functions or roles that support or help perform your AML/CTF
obligations.
Only roles identified as performing AML/CTF functions are subject to role-level AML/CTF risk
assessment under this Program.
2.1.2. Methodology
Square AU applies a structured role-level risk assessment to determine the AML/CTF exposure
associated with each identified AML/CTF role.
The assessment evaluates the nature and extent of the roleʼs authority, control influence and
potential regulatory impact. In particular, Square AU considers whether the role:
● Designs, approves or materially influences AML/CTF policies, procedures or frameworks;
● Holds authority to approve or escalate high-risk customers;
● Has access to and/or authorises regulatory reporting submissions;
● Has the ability to override or bypass internal controls;
● Configures or influences transaction monitoring rules or detection parameters;
● Amends customer risk ratings or audit trails;
● Implements process exemptions or manual workarounds;
● Manages outsourcing or third-party arrangements impacting AML/CTF controls;
● Has access to highly sensitive business or customer information.
Each role is assessed by these factors and assigned an Overall Risk Rating that can be found in
Appendix A.
Personnel Due Diligence Program | Square | Square AU Pty Ltd 5

The Overall Risk Assessment reflects the highest level of authority, systemic influence or regulatory
exposure exercised by the role.
This methodology ensures that the level of personnel due diligence applied is proportionate to the
AML/CTF risk presented by the role.
2.1.2.1. Determination of High Risk Roles
Roles classified as High risk are those that:
● Hold concentrated AML/CTF decision-making authority;
● Exercise systemic control influence over AML/CTF frameworks;
● Have direct responsibility for regulatory compliance oversight; or
● Have the capacity to create material regulatory or systemic exposure if control failure
occurs.

● CCO Senior Manager)
● AMLCO
All other identified AML/CTF roles are currently classified as Medium or Low risk and are subject to
standard screening measures.
The role-level risk assessment is reviewed periodically and updated where responsibilities, authority or
risk exposure materially change.

2.2.2. Personnel Suitability and Integrity Assessments
Square AU applies a risk-based approach to assessing the suitability of personnel performing
AML/CTF-related functions.
Personnel are required to demonstrate appropriate levels of integrity, and possess the skills,
knowledge and expertise necessary to perform their AML/CTF responsibilities, commensurate with
the nature, complexity and risk exposure of their role.
Integrity is assessed through pre-employment screening processes conducted via Square AUʼs
approved screening provider, including identity verification, sanctions and adverse media
screening, and other background checks proportionate to the risk of the role.
In addition to integrity screening, Square AU assesses whether Personnel possess the skills,
knowledge and expertise required to perform their AML/CTF responsibilities. This assessment may
include consideration of:
● Prior AML/CTF experience;
● Relevant qualifications;
● Understanding of ML/TF risks and AML/CTF obligations;
● Role-specific interviews or knowledge assessments, where appropriate.
The level of assessment is proportionate to the authority, complexity and AML/CTF risk exposure
associated with the role.
Assessments are conducted prior to commencement of AML/CTF-related functions and form part
of Square AUʼs ongoing personnel due diligence framework.
Where integrity concerns or capability gaps are identified, Square AU may implement appropriate
risk mitigation measures, including enhanced supervision, targeted training, reassignment of
responsibilities or, where necessary, removal from AML/CTF-related duties.
More stringent fit and proper requirements apply to designated roles, including the AML/CTF
Compliance Officer, in accordance with applicable regulatory expectations.
2.2.3. Transfer/Promotions Checks
Square AU employees who are transferred or promoted to a role associated with higher levels of
ML/TF risk, will also undergo additional due diligence.

skills, knowledge and expertise proportionate to the risk profile of the new role.
2.3. Other Considerations
2.3.1. Additional Due Diligence
In addition to the due diligence process described above, the below considerations should be
applied if the hiring manager, talent acquisition and/or Trust and Safety deem the checks
performed were insufficient to the commensurate risk of the personnel :
● Seek advice from the Square AU CCO/Senior Manager to determine whether to proceed with
the appointment or assess the implications of the personnel's role. Depending on the
outcome, further checks may be required, such as:
○ Bankruptcy checks where risks are identified in relation to financial management;
○ Further checks where risks are identified in relation to personnel who have a working
history in high risk countries.
2.3.2. Other Obligation
All employment checks performed through First Advantage include appropriate consents which
must be agreed upon by the candidate prior to proceeding. Any information collected about
candidates is used, stored, shared and maintained in accordance with the Block Information
Security Policy.
Outcomes of checks performed through First Advantage are kept for a minimum of 7 years, in
adherence to local laws and regulations.
Square AU maintains records reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with personnel
due diligence obligations, including risk classification, screening outcomes, reassessments and
decisions taken in response to adverse findings.
2.3.3. Mandatory AML/CTF Training
All Square AUʼs employees receive general AML/CTF risk awareness training to support
understanding of Square AUʼs AML/CTF obligations and the importance of identifying and
escalating suspicious activity.

Personnel performing AML/CTF related functions must complete additional role-specific AML/CTF
training appropriate to their responsibilities and ML/TF risk exposure, in accordance with Square
AUʼs AML/CTF Risk Awareness Training Program.
2.3.4. Employee Code of Conduct and Obligations
All Square employees and personnel are expected to read and accept the and uphold the Code of
Business Conduct and Ethics (“Codeˮ) in their day-to-day activities. All employees and personnel
are additionally expected to comply with all the systems, controls, policies and procedures
established within their respective functions, in accordance with this Program and the Anti-Money
Laundering & Counter-Terrorism Financing Program.
2.3.5. Consequences of Non-Compliance
For all employees and personnel who fail, with no reasonable reason, to comply with the above,
Square AU may take the below action(s) (in accordance with applicable local law), depending on
the scale and seriousness of the violation:
● Deliver individual feedback to address errors, mistakes, or areas of improvement
● Conduct refresher or targeted training
● Issue formal verbal and/or written warnings
● Schedule performance improvement plans
● Terminate employment
Where personnel due diligence or internal monitoring identifies integrity concerns, capability gaps
or performance deficiencies relating to AML/CTF responsibilities, Square AU will take
proportionate action based on severity. Actions may include enhanced supervision, targeted
training, reassignment of AML/CTF duties or disciplinary action in accordance with internal
policies. All decisions and actions taken are documented.
2.3.6. Restricted Access to Systems
Access to Square AUʼs internal systems and customer information is highly restricted, where
access is usually reviewed and approved by team leads. Access to customersʼ secret Personal
Identifiable Information (“PIIˮ) goes through periodic audit and expiration. In addition, all Square AU
personnel are governed by the Block Information Security Program which sets out the
classification of sensitive data elements and the storage, access and other processing
requirements for each classification. These controls, in turn, mitigate the risks of personnel
abusing their access to assist in ML/TF activities.
2.3.7. Additional Controls
The Trust & Safety team, in consultation with DRIs, reviews all personnel screening packages in
First Advantage, on a periodic basis.

The Trust & Safety team additionally implemented an internal mobility auditing control. This
includes a control for auditing the process which involves the investigation of a monthly report to
determine if any transfers / internal promotions have occurred without conducting the appropriate
level of due diligence.
2.4. Ongoing Personnel Due Diligence
Square AU applies ongoing suitability monitoring on a risk-based basis to personnel performing
AML/CTF-related functions. Ongoing monitoring is designed to ensure that personnel continue to
meet integrity, competence and conduct expectations appropriate to their role.
Ongoing monitoring may include, where proportionate to the AML/CTF risk classification of the
role:
● Periodic re-screening aligned to the personnel's assessed AML/CTF risk level;
● Review of adverse information where identified;
● Reassessment following material changes in role authority or AML/CTF responsibilities;
● Reassessment following significant AML/CTF control failures within the personnel's area
of responsibility;
● Reassessment following regulatory developments materially impacting the personnel
function;
● Reassessment following disciplinary findings relating to integrity or compliance matters.
Ongoing suitability monitoring also includes consideration of whether personnel continue to
possess the skills, knowledge and expertise required to perform their AML/CTF responsibilities.
This may include review of training completion, quality assurance outcomes and demonstrated
application of AML/CTF policies.
The nature, scope and frequency of ongoing monitoring measures are determined proportionately,
taking into account the AML/CTF risk associated with the role, authority and responsibilities.
Periodic re-screening applies to High AML/CTF risk roles at least every two 2 years.
Medium and Low AML/CTF risk roles are not subject to routine periodic re-screening and are
instead reassessed on a trigger-based basis, including where there is a role change, promotion,
material change in risk exposure, adverse information identified, or other integrity concerns.
Square AU retains discretion to conduct earlier reassessment where risk indicators, control
deficiencies, role changes or other relevant factors arise.
Outcomes of ongoing suitability monitoring are documented and retained in accordance with
recordkeeping requirements and are subject to governance oversight under this Program.
2.5. Trigger-Based Reassessment
In addition to onboarding and promotion checks, reassessment of suitability may occur where
events arise that could materially affect a personnel's fitness to perform AML/CTF responsibilities.
Personnel Due Diligence Program | Square | Square AU Pty Ltd 11

Trigger events include, but are not limited to:
● Promotion into higher-risk compliance or oversight roles;
● Significant changes in Square AUʼs ML/TF risk profile affecting the role;
● The personnel becomes subject to adverse media, criminal proceedings or regulatory
investigation;
● A material AML/CTF control failure is identified within the personnel's area of responsibility;
● The personnel is subject to disciplinary action relating to integrity or compliance matters.
All trigger-based reassessments must be documented and retained in accordance with
record-keeping requirements.
