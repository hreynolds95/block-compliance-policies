1. Introduction
This Third-Party Risk Management Standard (“Standard”) outlines Afterpay US, Inc.’s (“Afterpay”)
approach to ensuring it maintains a regulatory compliant Third-Party Risk Management program.
Failure to manage Third-Party related risks can expose Afterpay to regulatory action, financial loss,
litigation, reputational damage and/or other negativeconsequences.Itisimportanttonotethatthis
Standard is meant to be viewed as a supplement to the Block, Inc. Third-Party Risk Management
Policyanditscorrespondingprocedure.
2.Purpose
Afterpay conducts business in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. The purpose of
this Standard is to outline Afterpay’s commitment to ensuring that there is a strong, proactive
risk-based framework for managing its Third-Party relationships. The Afterpay TPRO is responsible
formanagementofthisStandard.
3. Scope
This Standard is applicable to Afterpay’s Employees and sets out the approach to Third-Party risk
management oversight of Critical Vendors. This Standard has also been created to ensure that
Afterpay and relevant personnel are aware of and comply with expectations regarding Third-Party
riskmanagement,asdetailedbelow.
4.Standard Requirements
4.1 VER Process
The DRI will need to submit a go/VER ticket via ThinkSmart, which begins the Third-Party Risk
Management process. This initial process includes performing due diligence (as applicable) by
Information Security, Privacy, Business Continuity, Compliance and developing appropriate
Contracts. ThinkSmart automatically triggers required due diligence, notifies the appropriate risk
subject matter expert (“SME”) of required assessment, and notifies Legal Counsel directly when
Contractreviewisapplicable.

4.2 Third-Party Risk Management Framework
The TPRM Team is responsible for implementing the Program. The team coordinates with a broad
range of internal and external stakeholders to setclearexpectationsandexecuteThird-Partyrelated
initiatives. The team strives to promote sensible stewardship of Afterpay resources and engages
variousdepartmentsandindividualsatAfterpayintheThird-Partyrelationshiplifecycle.
ThefivephasesofthePrograminclude:
1. PlanningandAssessment;
2. InitialDueDiligence;
3. Contracting;
4. OngoingMonitoring;and
5. ContractTermination
1.PlanningandAssessment
Planning begins when the Afterpay TPRO or DRI defines business requirements or scope of work
that involves a Third-Party/Vendor. Afterpay uses a value/riskandcontrols-basedapproachtoselect
and manage Third Parties/Vendors. Duringtheplanningandassessmentphase,AfterpayTPRO’sor
DRI’s start the process by initiating a VER, a brief survey which describes the overall scope of the
engagement and helps determine the risk to which Afterpay is exposed by a particular product or
servicethatisprocured.
2.InitialDueDiligence
Initial Due Diligence is driven by the results of the “Assessment” phase,whichmeasurestheriskby
assessing a Third-Party/Vendor’s abilitytosatisfyAfterpay’srequirementsfortheproposedproducts
and services, while maintaining appropriate controls to ensure compliance with our policies and
regulatory requirements. Due diligence could include, but is not limited to, reviewing the service
provider’spolicies,procedures,internalcontrols,andtrainingmaterialsforadequacy.
The responses to the VER will usually determine the required level of due diligence that the risk
SME’sdeterminetoperform.

3.Contracting
A clearly drafted and equitable Contract protects Afterpay and provides a framework for
expectations and issue resolution. Vendor Contracts are reviewed in accordance with legal
guidelines that have been issued under the guidance of Legal. The level of detail and relative
importance of ContractprovisionsvarieswithscopeandaThird-Party’sassessedrisk.AfterpayDRIs
are responsible for understanding the content of their respective Contracts. All Contracts are
subjectedtoAfterpay’sElectronicSignaturesinGlobalandNationalCommerceActPolicy.
4.OngoingMonitoring
Third-Party/Vendors will be subject to ongoing monitoring and review to ensure compliance with
applicable Anti-Money Laundering laws, in accordance with the TPRM Policy. Tier 1
Third-Party/Vendors will be formally reviewed annually. Tier 2 Third-Party/Vendors will be reviewed
every 2 years and Tier 3 every three years. The effective date as set out in the Block-Third-Party
Contract may be used as the starting point to calculate review periods or the date of the most
recent top tier risk assessment and due diligence, unlesssomeotherstartingpointisagreedupon,
in which case that will be the effective date for future reviews. Tier 4 Third-Party/Vendors do not
requirereview.
5.ContractTermination
Contract termination may occur if there are findings discovered as a result of ongoing monitoring
that indicate a breach of contractual obligations. Examples may include, but are not limited to,
consistently failing to meet SLAs, violations of consumer protection laws, or change in
business-related priorities. When ending a Contract or terminating a Third-Party relationship, the
Afterpay TPRO or DRI should follow the Contract’s stated language, otherwisetheyshouldconsult
the Legal and Procurement team. When a relationship ends, it is the responsibility of the Afterpay
TPRO or DRI to ensure that all Afterpay assets arereturnedandthatdataiseitherreturnedtousor
securely destroyed. It is the responsibility of the TPRO or DRI to follow the requirements for the
destruction or return of data as outlined in the Third-Party Contract. And, as appropriate, the
Afterpay TPRO or DRI will make sure access, physical and electronic, is terminated, as well notify
Procurement Operations and Accounts Payable to ensure a Third-Party is made inactive in
Afterpay’s financial system upon final payment. The TPRO or DRI will work with these teams
directly.
