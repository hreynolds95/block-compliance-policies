1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Compliance Management System о‚ЃCMSо‚‚ Policy (the Policy)
outlines Square AU Pty LtdКјs (Square AU) approach to identifying, assessing, managing,
monitoring, and reporting regulatory and ethical compliance obligations and risks across its
Australian operations. The CMS promotes integrity, accountability, transparency, and continuous
improvement and is aligned to global compliance practices.
Scope: This Policy applies to all Square AU operations, officers, employees, and contractors
engaged in regulated and supporting business activities.
1

Square AU operates within a broad and dynamic Australian regulatory environment that includes
financial services, financial crime, consumer protection, and payments obligations. While the CMS
considers this entire regulatory landscape, it applies a risk-based approach to prioritise programs
and controls proportional to the nature, scale, and complexity of Square AUКјs operations.
This approach ensures resources are directed to the areas of greatest regulatory, operational, and
reputational risk, while maintaining visibility of broader obligations and emerging developments.
Key areas of focus under this Policy include:
в—Џ AFSL and Conduct Obligations under the Corporations Act 2001 о‚ЃCthо‚‚, ASIC Regulatory
Guides and Anti Bribery and Corruption;
в—Џ Financial Crime Compliance under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism
Financing Act 2006 о‚ЃCthо‚‚ and AUSTRAC Rules and Autonomous Sanctions Act 2011о‚ЃCthо‚‚;
в—Џ Consumer Protection under ASIC, ACCC, and relevant industry codes;
в—Џ Complaints Handling, including requirements under ASIC Regulatory Guide 271 and the
AFCA Rules; and
в—Џ Payments Industry Compliance, encompassing scheme participation, network standards,
and relevant prudential or operational guidelines.
Square AU operates as an independent Australian licensee within the Block group, aligning its local
CMS with BlockКјs enterprise-wide compliance standards and programs to ensure consistency,
accountability, and effective global governance.
Global Alignment (summary): Square AUКјs CMS is supported by Block Inc.Кјs global compliance
ecosystem (e.g., Global Monitoring & Testing Program, Issue Management Program, PoP, TPRM
Standard). This ensures enterprise consistency and shared tooling while Square AU retains full
accountability to Australian regulators and its Board.
Definitions:
AFSL о‚ЃAustralian Financial Services Licence): A licence granted under the Corporations Act 2001
о‚ЃCthо‚‚ authorising entities to provide specified financial services in Australia. The AFSL framework
establishes the licenseeКјs obligations to maintain organisational competence, manage conflicts,
ensure compliance, and act efficiently, honestly, and fairly.
Responsible Manager: An individual nominated under the AFSL to demonstrate the organisationКјs
competence and integrity in meeting its licensing obligations (refer ASIC Regulatory Guide 105).
Responsible Managers play a key role within the compliance management system by ensuring
controls, governance, and oversight arrangements align with licence conditions and regulatory
expectations.

Compliance Management System о‚ЃCMSо‚‚: A structured and integrated framework encompassing
governance, risk management, compliance policies, procedures, controls, monitoring, training,
reporting, and assurance mechanisms designed to ensure and evidence adherence to legal,
regulatory, and ethical obligations.
The CMS supports proactive identification and management of compliance risks, continuous
improvement, and a culture of integrity across the organisation.
Issue: A deficiency, weakness, or non-conformity in a process, control, or compliance obligation
that may impair compliance effectiveness. Issues are logged, risk-rated, and tracked to
remediation within the CMS to ensure accountability and timely resolution.
Significant Breach: A breach that meets the materiality or impact criteria under s912D of the
Corporations Act 2001 о‚ЃCthо‚‚ or other applicable laws. Such breaches must be reported to ASIC
within prescribed timeframes (generally within 30 days). The CMS should facilitate timely
escalation, assessment, and documentation of significant breaches.
Three Lines of Defence: A governance model underpinning the CMSо‚’
в—Џ 1st Line: Business and operational functions own and manage risks and controls.
в—Џ 2nd Line: Compliance and Risk functions provide oversight, guidance, and challenge to
ensure effective implementation of the CMS.
в—Џ 3rd Line: Internal Audit provides independent assurance over the design and effectiveness
of compliance and risk management frameworks.

2.3 Policies, Standards & Procedures
Square AU maintains a suite of compliance policies, standards, and procedures that define the
controls and expectations for managing regulatory and conduct risk across the business.
Each document supports the operation of the CMS by ensuring obligations are clearly articulated,
consistently implemented, and regularly reviewed for relevance and effectiveness.
All compliance documents are developed, approved, and maintained in accordance with the
Block Compliance Policy on Policies о‚ЃPoPо‚‚ and the Compliance Document Approval Matrix,
which establish governance requirements for creation, ownership, review, and retirement of
compliance documents.
Under these frameworks:
в—Џ Each policy or standard has an identified Document Owner responsible for its accuracy,
currency, and alignment with Square AUКјs risk appetite and legal obligations;
в—Џ Documents undergo formal review at least biennially, and ad hoc updates are triggered
by material regulatory changes, emerging risks, or internal audit findings;
в—Џ All approved versions are stored in a controlled central repository with version control,
audit trails, and access restrictions to ensure authenticity and integrity;
в—Џ Changes are communicated to relevant staff through targeted updates, training, and
operational change processes; and
в—Џ Retired or superseded policies are archived in line with record retention requirements to
preserve regulatory evidence.
This structure ensures that compliance documentation remains current, traceable, and effective,
while maintaining alignment with both Block Inc.Кјs global governance standards and Square AUКјs
local regulatory obligations.

2.4 Training & Awareness
Square AU recognises that a strong compliance culture relies on the capability and awareness of
its people. To achieve this, the Compliance Management System о‚ЃCMSо‚‚ embeds a structured and
risk-based training and competency program designed to ensure that all employees understand
their regulatory obligations and perform their roles in accordance with legal, ethical, and internal
standards.
All training activities are governed by the Block Global Compliance Training Program and the
Block Academy platform, which deliver consistent content across the enterprise while allowing for
localisation to Australian laws, regulatory requirements, and business risks.
Program Structure:
в—Џ Mandatory Induction Training: All new employees complete a comprehensive onboarding
module within their first 30 days. It covers AFSL and conduct requirements, AML/CTF
awareness, sanctions, complaint handling, Code of Conduct, conflicts of interest, and
escalation procedures.
в—Џ Annual Refresher Training: Delivered to all staff to reinforce core obligations and
communicate updates to laws, regulatory expectations, or policy changes.
в—Џ Role-Specific Training: Targeted modules are developed for high-risk and specialist roles,
including Responsible Managers, compliance professionals, customer-facing teams, and
operations staff involved in AML/CTF, payments, and complaint handling.
в—Џ Responsible Manager Competency Maintenance: Each Responsible Manager completes
annual fit-and-proper reviews and targeted professional development to meet ASICКјs
competency requirements under RG 105.
в—Џ Leadership and Ethics Training: Senior leaders receive tailored modules on tone from the
top, governance accountability, and decision-making ethics.
2.5 Risk Assessment
Square AU conducts periodic and event-driven compliance risk assessments across all business
operations to identify, evaluate, and prioritise regulatory and conduct risks.
These assessments form a key component of the CMS and are designed to ensure that
compliance risks are managed within the parameters of Block Inc.Кјs Enterprise Risk Policy.
Risk assessments consider both inherent and residual risk and evaluate the design and operating
effectiveness of existing controls. The process is collaborative and draws on data and insights
from monitoring activities, incident and breach reviews, regulatory change assessments, and
product or business unit feedback.
7

Risk assessments are initiated when:
в—Џ New or amended laws, regulations, or regulatory guidance are released;
в—Џ New products, features, or distribution channels are introduced;
в—Џ Material complaints, incidents, or control failures occur;
в—Џ Internal or external audits identify emerging or systemic risks.
Outcomes from each assessment are formally documented and summarised in structured
templates and working papers, including risk rating, control adequacy, and remediation plans.
While Square AU does not maintain a standalone enterprise-wide risk register, all material
compliance risks and control issues are captured and evidenced through these artefacts such as
risk assessments, breach evaluations, remediation plans, and Board reports ensuring a
continuous and traceable record of compliance risk management.
This approach supports the principles of transparency and proportionality under BlockКјs Enterprise
Risk Policy, demonstrating that compliance risk is actively identified, assessed, and mitigated
through a consistent and documented methodology.
2.6 Monitoring
Monitoring is the process through which Square AU evaluates whether its compliance obligations
and controls are operating as intended. Monitoring activities are conducted under a risk-based
Compliance Monitoring & Testing Program, aligned with BlockКјs Global Monitoring & Testing
Standard.
The Program incorporates:
в—Џ Thematic reviews of high-risk areas (e.g., AFSL conduct, AML/CTF reporting, privacy and
data handling);
в—Џ Continuous monitoring through automated data checks, complaints trend analysis, and
regulatory change tracking; and
в—Џ Targeted testing following material incidents or emerging risk indicators.
Results from monitoring and testing are analysed and reported through the quarterly reporting and
issues management systems, which highlights key trends, control performance, and areas
requiring improvement. Findings feed directly into the compliance plan, staff training, and
management review cycle, closing the loop between assessment, monitoring, and continuous
improvement.

2.7 Third-Party Risk Management о‚ЃTPRMо‚‚
Square AU engages external service providers to support core operations such as technology,
payments, and identity verification.
To ensure these arrangements do not introduce undue compliance, operational, or reputational
risk, Square AU applies a structured Third-Party Risk Management о‚ЃTPRMо‚‚ framework, aligned
with Block Inc.Кјs Global TPRM Standard and relevant Australian regulatory expectations.
All critical and high-risk vendors are subject to due diligence, contractual controls, and ongoing
monitoring. This includes assessment of regulatory standing, financial soundness, and control
effectiveness, with contracts incorporating obligations for data protection, incident escalation,
right-to-audit, and compliance with Australian law.
Vendor risk is tiered by materiality, determining the frequency and depth of reviews. Compliance
and Procurement jointly oversee TPRM activities вЂ” Procurement manages onboarding and
performance reviews, while Compliance assesses regulatory risk and monitors adherence to
policies.
High-risk or critical arrangements are reported to the Board or its delegated committees for
oversight. Findings from reviews, audits, or incidents inform control improvements and strengthen
alignment with BlockКјs enterprise-wide vendor governance standards.
2.8 Complaints Handling
Complaints management forms a core component of Square AUКјs Compliance Management
System, providing an important source of insight into conduct risk, customer outcomes, and
control effectiveness.
Square AU maintains a structured framework for handling complaints in accordance with ASIC
Regulatory Guide 271 о‚ЃInternal Dispute Resolution), the AFCA Rules, and the Square AU Complaints
Policy. This ensures that customer concerns are recorded, investigated, and resolved in a fair,
timely, and transparent manner, and that outcomes are used to strengthen the broader compliance
environment.
The Compliance function monitors complaint data and collaborates with business units to identify
emerging issues or systemic trends. Findings and key metrics are included in the Compliance
Dashboard and reported quarterly to senior management and the Board to inform risk
assessments, regulatory reporting, and continuous improvement of the CMS.
Monitoring and evaluation are integral components of Square AUКјs Compliance Management
System о‚ЃCMSо‚‚, providing assurance that compliance obligations are being met and that controls
remain effective and proportionate to risk.
2.9 Incident and Breach Management
9

Square AU is committed to identifying, assessing, escalating, and remediating incidents and
breaches in a timely and transparent manner. An incident refers to any event, error, failure, or
breakdown in processes, systems, controls, or conduct that has the potential to cause harm to
customers, regulatory non-compliance, financial loss, operational disruption, or reputational
impact. A breach refers to any failure to comply with a legal obligation, licence condition, internal
policy, standard, or regulatory requirement.
Square AU maintains a documented Incident and Breach Management Procedure, which outlines
the processes for incident detection, assessment, remediation, escalation, and reporting. All
employees must promptly report any suspected incident or breach to the Compliance team or via
designated reporting channels.
Where a breach meets the вЂњreportable situationЛ® criteria under s912D of the Corporations Act and
ASIC RG 78, Square AU will report the matter to ASIC within the prescribed timeframes and
maintain appropriate internal records. Where a matter relates to customer detriment or dispute
resolution, it must be managed in alignment with ASIC RG 271о‚’ Internal Dispute Resolution and
AFCA requirements.
Root cause analysis, customer remediation (where appropriate), and control enhancement
activities must be completed to prevent recurrence. Incident and breach trends are reported to
senior management and the Board to support effective oversight and continuous improvement.
2.10 Conflicts of Interest
Square AU manages conflicts of interest to ensure that personal, financial, or other interests do not
improperly influence business decisions or customer outcomes. A conflict of interest may be
actual, potential, or perceived, and may arise at an individual, operational, or organisational level.
Employees must identify and disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to their manager
or the Compliance team as soon as they arise. Conflicts that cannot be adequately managed or
mitigated must be avoided.
Square AU maintains a Conflicts of Interest Policy and register, and ensures Responsible Managers
and other key personnel satisfy the competence and good character standards expected under the
Corporations Act and ASIC regulatory guidance. Training is provided to employees to support
awareness and correct handling of conflicts.
Decisions must always be made in the best interests of Square AUКјs customers and in accordance
with our legal, ethical, and licence obligations.

2.12 Regulatory Change Management
Square AU manages legal, regulatory, and industry change through its formal Regulatory Change
Management Program, which provides a structured process for identifying, assessing, and
implementing relevant requirements across the business.
This program ensures that new or amended obligations are evaluated for applicability, assigned
accountable owners, and embedded within existing controls and policies. Compliance oversees
the process to confirm that all changes are implemented in a timely and risk-based manner, with
progress and outcomes reported to senior management and the Board through the Compliance
Dashboard.
The program aligns with BlockКјs enterprise standards while ensuring that Square AU maintains
direct accountability for meeting Australian regulatory obligations and responding to local
developments.
