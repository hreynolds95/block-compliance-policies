1. Introduction
ThisAfterpayUSConsumerComplaintManagementStandard(“Standard”)isestablishedbyAfterpay
US,Inc.(“Afterpay”or“AfterpayUS”)andisalignedtotheBlockGlobalComplaintPolicy.
The Block Global Complaint Policy is the complaint policy which governs allAfterpaybusinesslines,
subsidiaries, and jurisdictions globally. References to “employee” throughout this document shall
refer to all full-time employees and fixed-term and contingent workers including interns, temps,
consultants, vendors, third-party providers, and contractors. References to Afterpay Complaints
Leadership refers to all levels of leadership within Afterpay with direct or indirect responsibility for
Employeeswhoresolvecomplaints.
This Standard has been adopted by the Afterpay US Board to govern the complaint management
program for Afterpay US, Inc., and any subsidiaries thereof, including Afterpay US Services LLC
(together referred toas“Afterpay”orthe“Company”),aswellasBankPartnerproductsofferedunder
theAfterpaybrand(“BankPartnerProducts”).
Global standards for complaint management are governed by the Block Global Complaint Policy
and the Block Global Complaint Standard. These outline Afterpay’s commitment regarding
complaint definition, complaint classifications, complaint data elements, reporting, root cause
analysisandtraining.
This Afterpay US Complaint Standard outlinesregion-specificrequirementsandexpectationsforthe
US.
It is Afterpay’s policy to respond to consumer complaints and disputes from its customers in an
efficient and timely manner and to take all such complaints seriously regardless of the channel or
method through which they are received. Responsibility of monitoring and responding to
complaints under this Standard has been delegated to the Afterpay Global Complaints Leader and
the Afterpay Global Head of Customer Service with oversight by Senior Management. For the
purpose of this Standard, Senior Management consists of senior leaders of Counsel, Compliance,
Product,Marketing,Sales,RiskandCustomerServiceofBlock,Inc.

4.1 US Consumer Complaint Management
4.1.1 General Principles
Afterpay receivesconsumercomplaintsandfeedbackfromvarioussourcesandinvariousformswith
respect to its services. AfterpayUSpromptlyandeffectivelyaddressesconsumercomplaintsfromits
customers to ensure that consumers are treated fairly, respectfully, and consistently. Consumer
complaints may also assist Afterpay US in detecting violations of applicable law including, but not
limitedto,Unfair,Deceptive,orAbusiveActsandPractices(“UDAAP”),FairLendingobligationsunder
theEqualCreditOpportunityAct(“ECOA”),andtheElectronicFundsTransferAct(“EFTA”)intheUS.
ComplaintsmayassistAfterpayUSindetectingviolationsofapplicablelawincluding,butnotlimited
to, Federal and State privacy laws (as applicable), consumer protection laws (as applicable).
Consumer complaints may additionally assist Afterpay US in detecting potential violations of
statutes, rules, guidance, and principles set forth by relevant agencies,suchastheUSstatefinancial
regulators, the Federal Trade Commission (“FTC”), and the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
(“CFPB”).
4.1.2 Complaint Identification, Response, and Resolution
AfterpayUSmayreceiveconsumercomplaintsfromvarioussourcesincluding:
● Consumers who contact Afterpay US directly via email, social media, written
correspondenceoranyotherintakechannelmanagedbyAfterpayUS;
● ConsumerswhocontactAfterpayUSviaaregulatororotherthird-party;and
● ConsumerswhocontactAfterpayUSthroughanAfterpay-partneredmerchant.
A complaint is defined as an expression of dissatisfaction, regardless of validity, made by an
individual,businessentityorsomeoneauthorizedtoactontheirbehalf,withanAfterpayUSproduct,
process, service, decision, vendor or interaction in which a response or resolution is explicitly or
implicitly requested. Complaints may also include allegations thatAfterpayUSengagedinwrongful
orsuspiciousconduct.

4.2.2 Complaint Escalation
Timely and effective complaint escalation is important to ensure appropriate detection and
resolution ofsignificantissuesincludingallegationsofviolationoflaw,discrimination,orotherunfair,
deceptive, or abusive consumer treatment. Minimum escalation triggers have been established.
Where a customer service team member is unable to resolve a consumer complaint, the matter is
escalated to their Team Leader who may then escalate to Complaint leadership (Operations
Manager,RegionalLeader,GlobalComplaintsLeader).
All complaint responses for complaints filed with the CFPB, Attorney General or State Regulatory
AgenciesmustbereviewedbyCompliancebeforesendingtotheregulatorand/orcustomer.

4.2.3 Root Cause Analysis
Consumer complaints resulting from systemic issues may impact additional consumers who have
not made complaints to Afterpay US. Similarly, complaints may reoccur if the underlying cause of
those complaints is not addressed and remedied. Afterpay US is committed to conducting a root
cause analysis with respect to consumercomplaintsonaquarterlybasisandjustifiedbytheseverity
and/or volume of any such complaints. The root cause analysis will be used todeterminewhethera
justifiable change to Afterpay US’s product, technology, policies or procedures could be reasonably
expected to mitigate the occurrence of the circumstances leading to such complaints. Where an
underlying issue is identified that causes material harm to additional consumers, Afterpay is
committed to taking appropriate remedial actionregardlessofwhetherthoseadditionalconsumers
havemadeacomplainttoAfterpayUS.
The Global Complaints Leader, Global Head of Customer Service, Legal and Compliance teams, and
senior management shall be empowered to request or conduct root cause analysis where deemed
appropriate in the reasonable discretion of the head of each group or any member of senior
management.
