1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And
Marketing Act (“CANSPAMˮ) Policy demonstrates Block, Inc.'s commitment to comply with the
CANSPAM Act across all its subsidiaries and operations. This policy establishes comprehensive
guidelines governing Block's commercial email practices while maintaining brand-specific
flexibility where needed.
The CANSPAM Act serves as a crucial regulatory framework enacted to reduce spam and
unsolicited pornography by prohibiting senders of unsolicited commercial email messages from
disguising the source and content of their messages, while ensuring consumers maintain control
over their email communications. CANSPAM requires Block to follow specific procedures when
initiating email messages to customers or prospective customers, including obtaining affirmative
consent, ensuring message content is not false or misleading, and providing recipients with the
ability to opt-out of future correspondence.
Scope: This Policy applies to Block, Inc. and its applicable U.S. subsidiaries and affiliates
(collectively, "Block") that send commercial email messages. The only exception is Square
Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated by the
Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.
It governs all commercial emails sent by or on behalf of Block, including messages with both
commercial and transactional content, and third-party email services. This Policy applies to
Square, Cash App, and Afterpay's commercial email communications but does not apply to purely
transactional messages or internal communications.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 General Requirements
Block ensures all commercial email communications comply with CANSPAM requirements. This
includes emails sent directly by Block or through third parties on Block's behalf. Block maintains
appropriate controls to ensure compliance across all brands and communication types.
2.2 Message Classification and Content Requirements
Commercial Email Classification
Block classifies email messages based on their primary purpose to determine applicable
CANSPAM requirements. Messages are evaluated to determine whether they are:
● Commercial messages promoting products or services
● Transactional or relationship messages facilitating existing services
● Mixed-purpose messages containing both commercial and transactional content
Content Standards
All commercial email messages must include:
● Accurate header information that identifies the sending source
● Subject lines that accurately reflect message content
● Clear identification as advertisements when required
● Valid physical postal address
● Visible and operable unsubscribe mechanism
● Required regulatory disclosures based on message content
2.3 Recipient Rights and Controls
Opt-Out Requirements
3

Block maintains comprehensive opt-out processes that:
● Provide clear and conspicuous opt-out mechanisms
● Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days
● Maintain opt-out functionality for 30 days after message delivery
● Prohibit charging fees or requiring additional information for opt-outs
● Prevent sale or transfer of email addresses after opt-out
Consent Management
Block maintains appropriate controls for:
● Obtaining and documenting affirmative consent
● Managing consent across brands and product lines
● Honoring recipient preferences
● Maintaining records of consent and opt-out requests
2.4 Third-Party Management
Block maintains oversight of third parties involved in commercial email communications through:
● Clear contractual requirements for CANSPAM compliance
● Established approval processes for email content and configuration
● Monitoring of third-party compliance with requirements
● Integration of opt-out processes across platforms
2.5 Transactional or Relationship Email Exclusions
Block ensures transactional or relationship messages:
● Prioritize transactional content at the beginning of messages
● Maintain clear distinction from commercial content
● Include required account servicing information
● Meet timing requirements for critical notifications
3. Brand-Specific Requirements
Block implements CANSPAM requirements across its subsidiaries while recognizing the unique
characteristics of each business line's email communications. Detailed procedures for
implementation are maintained in subsidiary-specific standards and procedures.
4

3.1 Cash App
Cash App's implementation of CANSPAM requirements addresses the specific needs of consumer
financial services:
Message Classification
● Financial transaction confirmations and security alerts are treated as transactional
● Investment-related communications must meet additional regulatory requirements
● Promotional content must be clearly distinguished from account servicing communications
Consumer Protection Requirements
● Essential account and security notifications must be maintained regardless of marketing
preferences
● Investment and cryptocurrency promotional content must include required disclosures
● Cross-border payment communications must meet additional regulatory requirements
3.2 Afterpay
Afterpay's implementation focuses on the unique aspects of BNPL services:
Payment Communications
● Payment schedule and confirmation messages are treated as transactional
● Marketing communications must be clearly separated from payment notifications
● Merchant partnership promotions require clear identification of all parties
Consumer Requirements
● Payment-related communications must be maintained regardless of marketing preferences
● Credit-related disclosures must meet additional regulatory requirements
● Shop Directory promotional content must clearly identify merchant relationships
The above brand-specific requirements supplement the general compliance TILA requirements
outlined in this Policy but are only applicable to their respective brands (i.e., Square Banking
requirements apply only to Square Banking operations, Cash App requirements apply only to Cash
App operations, and Afterpay requirements apply only to Afterpay operations).

All Documents are retained according to the longer of:

4.5 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
