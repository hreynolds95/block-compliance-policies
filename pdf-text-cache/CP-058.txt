Customer Communication Standard
Squareup International Limited
Last Approved: Dec 17, 2025
Effective Date: Dec 17, 2025
1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Customer Communication Standard (“Standardˮ) outlines
Squareup International Limitedʼs (“SILˮ) approach for designing, testing, and monitoring customer
communications. The Head of Compliance is responsible for management of this Standard.
The purpose of this Standard is to ensure that SIL communicates with customers in a manner that
is clear, fair, and not misleading, supporting good outcomes and consumer understanding while
meeting applicable laws and regulatory expectations.
Scope: This Standard applies to all oral and written customer communications across SILʼs
channels, including but not limited to:
● Website and app content
● Onboarding and welcome communications
● Account updates
● Marketing and promotional materials
● Product disclosures (e.g., terms, pricing, and fees)
● Sales and Customer support interactions
● Social media/Telephone communications
● Complaint responses
This Standard does not apply to:
● Internal employee communications or training materials
● Communications related to supplier or partner agreements
● Automated transaction confirmations (e.g., receipts) where no decision-making is involved.
It covers both new and existing communications, ensuring legacy content is also brought up to
Standard.

2. Standard Requirements
The Standard mandates that all SIL communications must:
● Be clear, fair, and not misleading;
● Highlight key information customers need to make decisions;
● Present information in an accessible and understandable manner; and
● Be tested, monitored, and refined based on actual customer outcomes.
2

2.1. Communication Design
All customer communications must be designed and delivered in alignment with the following
principles:
1. Language and Clarity
● Use plain, everyday language appropriate for the target audience; avoid technical
jargon where possible.
● Ensure key terms, risks and pricing are prominent, understandable, and easy to
locate.
● Present content in a clear, concise, and factually accurate manner, consistent across
all channels.
2. Accessibility and Inclusion
● Design communications to be accessible to all customer groups, including those with
characteristics of vulnerability.
● Use a readable, user-friendly layout with bullet points, clear headings, summaries,
diagrams, and infographics, where helpful.
● Offer information through multiple accessible formats and channels (e.g., email,
phone, chat, video tutorials, alternative formats).
3. Channel Effectiveness and Support
● Assess and select communication channels based on their ability to deliver clear,
timely, and accessible information to different customer segments.
● Monitor customer engagement across channels to identify gaps and determine
whether additional support or alternative formats are required.
4. Journey Mapping and Friction Reduction
● Map the customer journey across all channels to identify pain points where users
experience frustration, confusion, delays, or obstacles that impede customer
understanding, decision-making, or task completion. Use these insights to eliminate
unnecessary friction and create a smoother, more intuitive experience.
● Tailor communications to reflect customer needs, preferences, and their stage in the
journey.
● Use a layered approach where appropriate, offering key information upfront with
options to access more detailed explanations.
● Ensure proactive delivery of important information at the right time at key decision
points (e.g., post-sale) to help the customers assess their options, understand
associated risks, and make choices that align with their needs.
5. Regulatory Disclosure and Transparency
● Clearly distinguish between regulated and unregulated products, and explicitly
communicate the regulatory status and associated protections of each.
● Include the following regulatory disclosure, in a durable medium, in all regulated
customer-facing documentation (e.g., letters, emails, marketing content, websites)
“Squareup International Limited, trading as Square is regulated by the Central Bank

2.2. Telephone Communication and Personal Visits
Square may, from time to time, need to contact existing, potential or former customers via telephone or
by visiting their business premises. Permitted contact with a customer by telephone or by visit, must
be made only between 9.00 a.m. and 9.00 p.m. Monday to Saturday, excluding public holidays, unless
otherwise agreed in writing with the consumer.
When making a personal visit or initiating communication by means of telephone call, advocates must,
and in the following order provide:
● their name and the company name
● the purpose of the telephone call or visit
● inform the consumer that telephone contact is being recorded, if this is the case
● establish if the consumer wishes the personal visit or telephone call to proceed and, if not,
end the contact immediately
Example - Hi, my name is John, calling from Square about X, before proceeding i need to inform you,
this call is recorded for training and verification purposes, is that OK?
Telephone Calls with Existing Customers
SIL may make telephone calls with existing customers only when one or more of the following specific
conditions is met:
● The consumer holds a product which requires SIL to maintain contact with the consumer in
relation to that product
● The contact must directly relate to that existing product
● SIL has provided that consumer with a financial service similar to the one that is the purpose
of the contact within the previous 12 months
● The similarity of services must be clearly documented and justifiable
● Records must be maintained showing the previous service relationship and timing.
● The necessity for ongoing contact must be clearly established and documented
● The consumer has given written consent to being contacted by telephone
4

Telephone Calls with potential or former Customers
SIL may initiate telephone calls with potential customers or former customers only if one of the
following specific conditions is met:
● The contacted person has provided written consent for SIL to contact them for a specific
purpose within the previous 12 months
● The contact must directly relate to that specified purpose
● Consent must be documented with clear purpose specification and date
● The contacted person has made their contact details public in Ireland through Business listing
section of a current telephone directory or classified telephone directory Trade or
professional directories or a website, software app, or other digital technology operated by a
business (with the person's consent)
● The contacted person is a director of a company or partner in a firm with an entry in the
directories or digital platforms referenced above. Contact must be made via the business
contact details of the company or firm and must be in connection with their role as director or
partner
All telephone communications must ensure that the extent of communications is proportionate,
reasonable, and not excessive. SIL must take into account the circumstances of the consumer,
including, contact frequency, timing and duration of calls.
Personal Visits
SIL may make a personal visit to a consumer only if that consumer has provided written consent
covering all of the following elements:
● Clear specification of the purposes for which the personal visit is to be made
● For sales and marketing visits, explicit identification of the product to be discussed during the
personal visit
● documentation of the specific business objectives and intended outcomes
● Agreed time, date, and location for the personal visit
● If applicable, clear disclosure of any fee that SIL proposes to charge for the personal visit

All written consent must be obtained prior to the visit, properly documented, and retained in
accordance with record retention requirements.
2.3. Review and Approval
The first line of defence must ensure that new customer communications, or updates to existing
communications, undergo a structured review and approval process before deployment. The review
must cover three key dimensions to ensure content is accurate, clear, and compliant:
● Customer Understanding Review: Ensure that the content meets the design principles
outlined in Section 2.1, with a focus on clarity, consistent branding, accessibility, and ease of
understanding for the target audience.
● Subject Matter Expert (“SMEˮ) Review: Relevant SMEs - Product, Marketing, Risk, Customer
Success, etc must validate the content for accuracy, ensuring it aligns with the operational
process and customer expectations.
● Legal and Compliance Review: Ensure alignment with applicable laws, regulations, and
Consumer Duty requirements.
Each review must be documented, with sign-offs obtained from the appropriate stakeholders prior to
deployment.

2.5. Testing
Testing aims to validate that customer communications meet the design principles outlined in Section
2.1. The depth and method of testing should be proportionate to the criticality of the communication, as
defined in Section 2.3.
● High-Criticality Communications must undergo comprehensive testing to ensure they are
understood and do not lead to customer harm.
● Medium-Criticality Communications require focused testing, such as sample-based feedback
or user surveys to assess clarity and tone.
● Low-Criticality Communications may benefit from light-touch review.
Additionally, all key customer communications such as those relating to new product launches, material
changes, or required customer actions must be retested regularly ensuring the communication remains
relevant and effective over time. The following methods may be used:

the communication, ensuring ongoing clarity, accuracy, and effectiveness while supporting good
customer outcomes and meeting regulatory expectations.

2.7. Escalation
Issues identified through monitoring, testing and the Issue Management Program that indicate potential
customer harm, regulatory breaches, or communication design failures must be addressed as follows:
● The issue should be flagged to Compliance and the Regulatory Governance Committee
(“RGCˮ) with relevant context, evidence, and potential impact for review and oversight.
● Communication content must be updated promptly. Revisions may include improving
accuracy, clarifying language, enhancing format or delivery, or increasing accessibility.
● Revised communications must be reviewed by Compliance and relevant SMEs before reissue
to ensure the issue is effectively resolved and customer understanding Standards are met.
● Insights gained should be used to inform process improvements, staff training, and broader
policy updates to prevent recurrence.
● All escalations and subsequent actions must be documented and retained for audit and
continuous improvement purposes.
2.8. Governance and Oversight
Clear escalation protocols are established to ensure significant communication issues are promptly
addressed. When substantial risks or failures are detected, they are escalated to Compliance, the RGC,
and the SIL Board for appropriate oversight and corrective action. Additionally, Compliance provides
regular updates to the RGC and SIL Board on communication reviews, customer feedback, identified
risks, testing and monitoring outcomes, audit findings, training activities, and complaints data.

3.4. Exceptions
The Standard Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with
the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the Policy
Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership and
applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
