1. Introduction
ThisGlobalSanctionsCompliancePolicy(“Policy”)outlinesBlockInc.’s(“Block”)approachtoensuring
compliance with applicable Economic Sanctionslawsandregulations.TheGlobalHeadofSanctions
isresponsibleformanagementofthisPolicy.
2.Purpose
The Policy sets forth Block’s commitment to comply with applicable sanctionslawsandregulations
in all jurisdictions in which it does business, including but not limited to those implemented and
enforced by (i) theUnitedNations;(ii)theUnitedStates;(iii)Canada;(iv)theEuropeanUnionandany
relevant member state thereof; (v) the United Kingdom; (vi) Australia; (vii) New Zealand; and (viii)
Japan. Compliance with this Policy and relevant laws and regulations is to be achievedthroughthe
implementation of risk-based compliance programs, including but not limited to the Global
Sanctions Compliance Program (the “Program”). This Policy is intended to be supplemented by
additionaldocumentationasnecessary.
3. Scope
This Policy is established by Block and is applicable globally across all Block business lines,
subsidiaries,andjurisdictions.

4.3 Internal Policies, Procedures, and Controls
The Policy requires Block to implement and maintain policies, procedures, controls, processes, and
systems reasonably designed to ensure compliance with all applicable sanctions laws and
regulations. Controls may consist of, but are not limited to, customer screening and due diligence,
transaction screening, and geoblocking. Block maintains a risk-based approach to implementing
controls that is commensurate with sanctions risks posed by Block’s business model, geographic
operations,andproductofferings.
4.4 Risk Assessment
The Policy requires Block to conduct sanctions risk assessments in order to identify potential
sanctionsrisksandtoinformthedesignandprioritiesoftheProgram.
4.5 Independent Testing
The Policy requires Block to be subject to independent, periodic risk-based audits on the overall
adequacyandeffectivenessofthePrograminaccordancewithregulatoryrequirements.
4.6 Regulatory Reporting
The Policy requires Block to comply with sanctions reporting obligations required by relevant
regulatory authorities, including but not limited to annual reports ofblockedorfrozenpropertyand
ad-hocreportingofcertainblocked,frozen,orrejectedtransactions.
4.7 Duty to Report/Non-Retaliation
Any potential violations of this Policy or any sanctions-related law or regulation must be reported
immediately to the Global Head of Sanctions or by making a report—including anonymously—at
ethics.block.xyz. Block will not tolerate retaliation against any individual who submits a good faith
allegationofasuspectedviolationoflaworregulationorthisPolicy.

6.Conflicts of Law
No part of this Policy or its supporting documentation should be interpreted as contravening or
superseding any other legal or regulatory requirements imposed upon Block. Block and its Staff
Members may be subject to sanctions laws in the jurisdictions in which they are located or do

are citizens or residents, as well as where they are physically located. Any conflicts between this
Policy and Block’s other legal or regulatory obligations, or between the laws of applicable
jurisdictions, should be submitted immediately to the Global Head of Sanctionsand/ortherelevant
CountryComplianceLeadforfurtherevaluationand/orsubsequentsubmissiontotheCCOorLegal.
