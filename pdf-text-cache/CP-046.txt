1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Policy communicates Square Capital, LLC's commitment to
compliance with both consumer and commercial fairness requirements through:
● The Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (“UDAAPˮ) Standards contained in
sections 1031 and 1036 of the Dodd-Frank Act
● The Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices (“UDAPˮ) provisions in Section 5 of the Federal
Trade Commission Act
● State laws prohibiting similar acts or practices in both consumer and commercial contexts
This Policy sets forth comprehensive guidelines governing Square Capital, LLC, ensuring
consistent operations while maintaining appropriate flexibility to address unique business needs.
Square Capital's prevention of UDAAP and UDAP violations requires transparency, truthfulness,
suitability, and fairness across all transactions and dealings with both consumers and merchants.
The distinction between UDAAP and UDAP requirements reflects the different contexts in which
Square Capital, LLC operates:
● Consumer financial products and services (subject to UDAAP under Dodd-Frank)
● Commercial and merchant services (subject to UDAP under the FTC Act)
● Products with potential dual consumer and commercial applications
This comprehensive approach ensures appropriate protections while recognizing the different
needs and sophistication levels of consumers and commercial customers.
Scope: This Policy applies to Square Capital, LLC (collectively, "Square Capital").
This policy does not apply to Square Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Block, Inc.ʼs Utah state-chartered
Industrial Bank federally regulated by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation.

Policy Statement
Square Capital is committed to offering both consumer and commercial products in a fair and
responsible manner. For consumer financial products, Square Capital complies with UDAAP
requirements under the Dodd-Frank Act. For commercial products, Square Capital complies with
UDAP requirements under the FTC Act. Square Capital ensures that terms and conditions are
communicated clearly and transparently to both consumers and commercial customers.
Wherever state or local regulations overlap with, and are stricter than the requirements set out in
this Policy, the more conservative or restrictive approach will be applied in those jurisdictions
provided such approach is lawful. If any applicable laws conflict with this Policy, Square Capital will
consult with appropriate Legal Counsel to resolve the conflict and adjust Square Capitalʼs policies
and procedures to ensure compliance.
2. Requirements
2.1 General Standards
2.1.1 UDAAP Standards
Square Capital's commitment to preventing unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices is
demonstrated through comprehensive standards that apply across all entities and operations.
These standards establish the foundation for fair treatment of consumers and merchants
throughout their relationship with Square Capital.
Unfair Acts or Practices: An act or practice is unfair where it causes or is likely to cause
substantial injury to Consumers which cannot be reasonably avoided and the injury is not
outweighed by countervailing benefits to Consumers or to competition. An act or practice that
causes a small amount of harm to a large number of people may be deemed to cause substantial
injury. However, an act or practice is not considered to be unfair if Consumers may reasonably
avoid injury. Consumers cannot reasonably avoid injury if the act or practice interferes with their
ability to make decisions or take action to avoid injury.
Public policy, as established by statute, regulation, or judicial decisions, may be considered with
other evidence to determine whether an act or practice is unfair and the particular facts in a case
are crucial to the determination of unfairness.
3

Square Capital complies with the guidance that discrimination may be considered an unfair act or
practice. As such, Square Capital will treat all applicants and Consumers equally, and will not
discriminate during the stages of the product lifecycle, including marketing, account opening,
servicing, or collections.
Deceptive Acts or Practices:
An act or practice is deceptive where there is representation omission, or practice that misleads or
is likely to mislead Consumers; the Consumer's interpretation of the representation, omission, act
or practice is reasonable under the circumstances; and the misleading representation, omission,
act or practice is material.
A deceptive representation may be an express or implied claim or promise and it may be written or
oral. The omission of material information may be deceptive if disclosure of the omitted
information is necessary to prevent a Consumer being misled. When representations or marketing
practices are targeted to a specific audience, such as the elderly, young people, or financially
unsophisticated, the standard is based upon the effects of the act or practice on a reasonable
member of that group.
Abusive Acts or Practices:
An act or practice is abusive if it materially interferes with the ability of a Consumer to understand
a term or condition of a product or service or takes unreasonable advantage of: (i) the lack of
understanding on the part of the Consumer of the material risks, costs, or conditions in the product
or service; (ii) the inability of the Consumer to protect their interests in selecting or using the
financial product or service; or (iii) the reasonable reliance by the Consumer on a covered person
to act in the interests of the Consumer.
2.1.2 Consumer and Commercial Harm
Any unfair, deceptive, or abusive act or practice can result in harm to consumers or merchants.
Square Capital seeks to prevent harm through clear, transparent, and accurate delivery of products
and services in accordance with applicable federal and state laws and regulations.
For consumers, harm may include:
● Actual or potential financial loss
● Non-financial impacts
● Violations of consumer protection laws
● Impacts from compliance management weaknesses
For commercial customers, harm may include:
● Business losses or operational impacts
● Disruption to merchant services
● Financial impacts on business operations

● Reputational harm
2.2 Product Design and Delivery
2.2.1 Advertising, Marketing, and Disclosure Guidelines
Square Capital maintains comprehensive standards for marketing, advertising, and sales materials
across both consumer and commercial products. All communications must present information
fairly, clearly, and accurately, with appropriate disclosures of terms, benefits, costs, risks, and
material limitations of products or services being offered. This includes any related or optional
products and services that may be available to customers.
In developing marketing materials, Square Capital prohibits any intentional misrepresentation of
terms, benefits, costs, risks, or material limitations, whether through direct statements or by
omission. Headlines and promotional language must be truthful and supported by clear,
conspicuous disclosures rather than relying on fine print or inconspicuous disclaimers to clarify
potentially misleading statements. All representations made in communications must have a
reasonable factual basis and be appropriately documented.
Square Capital ensures that key terms and conditions are prominently displayed and easily
understood, enabling customers to make informed decisions about products and services. This
includes clear and timely disclosure of fees, penalties, or other charges, along with explanations of
when and why such charges may be imposed. When contract terms permit changes to the
agreement, these provisions are clearly communicated. In cases where approved account terms
differ from advertised terms or previously disclosed information, these differences are prominently
highlighted.
When using terms such as "pre-approved" or "guaranteed," Square Capital clearly discloses any
limitations, conditions, or restrictions that may apply. Marketing materials avoid claims,
representations, or statements that could mislead the target audience about cost, value,
availability, cost savings, benefits, or terms of the product or service. Additionally, Square Capital
does not advertise services in connection with accounts unless there is both the intent and ability
to provide and monitor such services for account holders.
For commercial products and services, Square Capital ensures:
● Fair representation of merchant terms and conditions
● Clear disclosure of business obligations
● Transparent presentation of processing requirements
● Accurate representation of merchant benefits
● Fair presentation of qualification criteria
2.2.2 Marketing Review Process
Square Capital implements a comprehensive marketing review process to ensure compliance with
UDAAP and UDAP requirements while supporting business objectives. All marketing materials and
5

customer communications undergo review by Compliance and Legal teams prior to distribution to
ensure fair representation of product features, terms, and conditions. Documentation of marketing
review and approval processes is maintained for at least 25 months from public launch. Marketing
teams engage with Product Compliance and Legal counsel during strategy development to prevent
potential UDAAP, UDAP, or fair lending concerns.
2.2.3 Collections and Communications Standards
Square Capital's collection and communication practices comply with applicable federal and state
requirements, including UDAAP/UDAP standards. Detailed requirements for collections and
communications activities, including specific standards for consumer and commercial collections,
are outlined in Block, Inc.'s Fair Debt Collection Practices Act FDCPA Policy.
For matters not specifically addressed in the FDCPA Policy, Square Capital ensures all collections
and communications practices align with this Policy's principles of fairness and transparency,
whether dealing with consumers or merchants.
2.3 Operations and Controls
2.3.1 Compensation and Performance Evaluations
When Square Capital or any third-party vendors provide support center servicing for consumers or
merchants, which includes any servicing communications delivered via existing communication
methods, Square Capital ensures that compensation and performance evaluations of internal
employees and performance oversight of vendor advocates do not contribute to UDAAP or UDAP
risk. This applies across both consumer financial services and merchant support operations,
recognizing the different requirements for each customer type.
Square Capital maintains relevant metrics and controls, which may include quality assurance and
case monitoring, to ensure support operations promote fair treatment regardless of whether they
serve consumers or commercial customers. Performance evaluations and incentive structures are
designed to prevent practices that could harm either consumers or merchants while supporting
compliant operations across all business lines.
2.3.2 Servicing Guidelines
Square Capital maintains comprehensive servicing standards that address both consumer financial
services and merchant/commercial relationships. For third parties or service providers engaged in
any form of servicing, whether consumer loan servicing or merchant support, relevant policies,
procedures, programs, and communications are reviewed by Compliance prior to and during their
use to ensure compliance with both UDAAP and UDAP requirements as applicable.
For consumer financial services, Square Capital ensures servicing practices meet consumer
protection requirements and prevent unfair, deceptive, or abusive practices. For merchant and
commercial relationships, servicing standards focus on fair commercial practices and prevention
of unfair or deceptive acts. Square Capital prohibits practices that could harm either consumers or
6

merchants, or damage Square Capital's reputation, whether conducted by Square Capital
personnel or third-party service providers.
2.3.3 Third-Party Risk Management Requirements
Square Capital maintains oversight of Third-Party Service Providers who perform activities on
Square Capital's behalf, whether serving consumers or merchants. All Third-Party Service
Providers must comply with applicable UDAAP and UDAP requirements as outlined in their service
agreements and the Block, Inc. Third-Party Risk Management Policy.
Third-Party Service Providers that interact with consumers or merchants must adhere to the same
standards of fairness and transparency that apply to Square Capital's direct operations. This
includes customer service providers, collection agencies, marketing partners, and other vendors
who may impact the customer experience. Square Capital ensures this compliance through service
agreements that clearly establish UDAAP and UDAP requirements, ongoing monitoring of
Third-Party Service Provider activities, and regular assessment of customer complaints regarding
Third-Party Service Providers.
Square Capital maintains ultimate responsibility for compliance with UDAAP and UDAP
requirements, regardless of whether activities are performed internally or by Third-Party Service
Providers. For detailed requirements regarding Third-Party Service Provider management, refer to
the Block, Inc. Third-Party Risk Management Policy.
2.4 Oversight Framework
2.4.1 Complaints Management
Both consumer and merchant complaints play a key role in detecting potential UDAAP/UDAP risk.
Block, Inc.'s Global Complaints Policy and underlying Block, Inc. Global Complaints Standard
include minimum requirements for Square Capital in relation to the identification, classification,
treatment, and reporting of complaints from all customers.
Block, Inc. maintains distinct procedures for:
● Consumer complaints related to financial products and services
● Merchant complaints regarding commercial products and services
● Complaints involving dual-purpose products
Square Capital complies with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements governing the
handling of all complaints arising from Square Capital's products and services, including specific
requirements for different customer types and product categories.

Square Capital maintains a comprehensive monitoring and testing program to ensure compliance
