1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Complaints Policy (“Policyˮ) outlines Square AU Pty Ltdʼs (“Square
AUˮ) approach to managing complaints in accordance with applicable Australian legislation, Australian
Financial Complaints Authority (“AFCAˮ) Rules and Australian Securities and Investments Commission
(“ASICˮ) Regulatory Guides. The Chief Compliance Officer CCO is responsible for overseeing this
Policy and ensuring compliance with all relevant legal and regulatory obligations.
This Policy supplements Block Incʼs global Complaints Management framework by setting out specific
requirements for managing complaints within Square AU. Where any inconsistencies arise, the
requirements of local policies and standards take precedence.
Square AU is committed to fair, transparent, and timely complaint handling. The regulated Internal
Dispute Resolution (“IDRˮ) process provides customers with a fair opportunity to have their concerns
addressed internally before escalating a complaint to AFCA.
Scope: This Policy is established by Square, sets out its approach to managing complaints and is
applicable to all its Employees. Square AU deals with Complaints in accordance with the procedures
set out in this Policy.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1 Regulatory Framework
Square AU, as an Australian Financial Services Licence AFSL holder and a member of the Australian
Financial Complaints Authority AFCA, is required to comply with a robust regulatory framework
governing complaint handling. This framework ensures that all complaints are managed fairly,
promptly, and consistently in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and industry best practice.
2

Key components of the regulatory framework include:
● Corporations Act 2001 Cth, particularly the obligations under section 912A relating to
efficient, honest, and fair management of financial services.
● ASIC Regulatory Guide 271 – Internal Dispute Resolution RG 271, providing guidance on the
requirements and expectations for complaint handling by AFSL holders.
● AFCA Rules and Terms of Reference, which govern external dispute resolution and outline the
rights of complainants to escalate unresolved matters.
Square AU is also subject to mandatory reporting obligations, including the submission of complaints
data to ASIC on a six-monthly basis, in line with regulatory requirements. Compliance with this
framework ensures that Square AU maintains transparency, accountability, and a customer-focused
approach to complaint management.
2.2 Guiding Principles
The guiding principles of this Complaints Policy ensure that all complaints are managed in a fair,
transparent, and accountable manner. Square AU is committed to fairness and impartiality, ensuring
all complaints are handled objectively and without bias. The complaints process is accessible and
visible, allowing customers to easily lodge complaints through multiple channels and receive
appropriate support. Confidentiality is maintained at all times to protect customer information and
privacy. Complaints are addressed with timeliness, acknowledging and resolving matters as quickly as
possible in line with regulatory requirements. The process also emphasises systemic issue
identification, using complaint insights to detect broader issues that may affect multiple customers.
Finally, Square AU is dedicated to continuous improvement, regularly reviewing complaints data and
feedback to enhance its products, services, and customer experience.
2.3 What is a Complaint?
Square AU considers the Australian legislative definitions and guidelines when considering whether or
not a matter constitutes a complaint.
AS/NZS 100022014 sets out the following definition of ‘complaintʼ: An expression] of dissatisfaction
made to or about an organization, related to its products, services, staff or the handling of a
complaint, where a response or resolution is explicitly or implicitly expected or legally required.
Complaints may be received in a variety of forms e.g. in a letter, by email or by telephone and through
posts on a social media channel / account owned or controlled by Square AU where the author is
identifiable and contactable and where the post meets the definition of complaint.
A complaint may also include complaints about a matter that is the subject of an existing remediation
program or about the remediation program itself (e.g. delays, lack of communication).
The following is an excerpt from RG 271, regarding matters not considered to be complaints:
For avoidance of doubt, we do not consider the following to be ‘complaintsʼ:
3

(a) employment-related complaints raised by financial firm staff;
(b) comments made about a firm where a response is not expected, such as:
(i) feedback provided in surveys; or
(ii) reports intended solely to bring a matter to a financial firmʼs attention—for example, that
an automatic teller machine ATM is damaged;
(c) hardship notices or requests to postpone enforcement proceedings, unless the customer raises
issues that meet the definition of complaint; and
(d) reports of unauthorised transactions under the ePayments Code and disputed transactions under a
chargeback process.
However, we consider that a complaint has been made if the consumer raises separate issues related
to the transaction that meet the definition of a complaint, or expresses dissatisfaction with the
outcome or handling of the unauthorised or disputed transaction.
2.4 Complaints Handling Process
Square AU ensures all complaints are managed in a timely, fair, and consistent manner in accordance
with regulatory requirements. The below diagram is an example of how this process is handled at
Square AU, from receipt of complaint through to resolution and closure:

The complaint handling process more specifically includes the following key stages:
Acknowledgement and Recording – If a customer lodges a complaint through any channel, Square AU
will confirm receipt of their complaint within 24 hours (or one business day), or as soon as practicable
in the event of a delay. Where possible, acknowledgement will be provided using the same method the
complaint was received, or by another method as agreed with the customer. The complaint is
documented within the appropriate platform and a record of these complaints is maintained.
Assessment and Investigation – Each complaint is promptly assigned for review and investigation.
Relevant facts are gathered, documented, and assessed to ensure an objective and evidence-based
outcome.
Response – Where a complaint is not resolved within 5 days of receipt of the complaint, a written
Internal Dispute Resolution IDR response is required. The IDR must be provided within 30 calendar
days for financial services complaints, or within 21 calendar days where the complaint involves
financial hardship. Where the deadline for an IDR falls on the weekend, all endeavours must still be
made to respond within the mandated timeframe.
Resolution – The response must clearly outline the outcome of the complaint and any remedial actions
taken, such as a correction, refund, apology, or explanation. All endeavours must be made to resolve
the complaint internally and as soon as possible after receipt of complaint, however IDR responses
must also advise the complainant of their right to escalate the complaint to AFCA.
Employees must follow internal procedures for escalating complaints that cannot be resolved at the
frontline or that raise potential regulatory, reputational, or systemic risks. More details on internal
escalation are contained with section 2.10 of this Policy.
2.5 Internal Dispute Resolution Response Requirements
Where a complaint cannot be resolved within 5 days, an IDR response is required to be provided to the
complainant. IDR responses are also required for all complaints relating to financial hardship. Each final
IDR response issued by Square AU must meet the requirements set out in ASIC Regulatory Guide 271 –
Internal Dispute Resolution and the ASIC Corporations, Credit and Superannuation Internal Dispute
Resolution) Instrument 2020/98.
An IDR response must:
● Provide a clear explanation of the outcome of the complaint and the reasons for the decision,
including reference to any relevant evidence or Terms and Conditions;
● Inform the complainant of their right to escalate the complaint to the Australian Financial
Complaints Authority AFCA if they are dissatisfied with the outcome; and
● Include the contact details for AFCA to facilitate escalation.
Where the complaint is not resolved within the applicable timeframe of 30 days or 21 days for
hardship-related complaints, Square AU must issue a written delay notification explaining the reason
for the delay, the complainantʼs right to take the matter to AFCA, and AFCAʼs contact details.
5

2.6 External Dispute Resolution
Square AU is a member of the Australian Financial Complaints Authority AFCA, which serves as the
independent external dispute resolution EDR body for financial services complaints. Customers have
the right to escalate a complaint to AFCA if they are not satisfied with the outcome of Square AUʼs IDR
process, or if the complaint has not been resolved within the prescribed IDR timeframes.
AFCA provides an accessible and independent forum for the resolution of disputes and can be
contacted using the following details:
Website: www.afca.org.au
Phone: 1800 931 678
Email: info@afca.org.au
Mail: GPO Box 3, Melbourne VIC 3001
Square AU will cooperate fully with AFCA and comply with its determinations, reinforcing our
commitment to fair, transparent, and accountable complaint resolution.
2.7 Vulnerable Customers
Square AU is committed to treating all customers fairly, including those who may be considered
vulnerable due to personal, financial, or situational circumstances, in line with our Vulnerable
Customers Standard. Vulnerable customers may include, but are not limited to, individuals
experiencing financial hardship, mental or physical health challenges, limited English proficiency, or
other factors that affect their ability to access, understand, or use our products and services.
Employees endeavour to identify potential vulnerability during interactions and take appropriate
measures to ensure customers receive clear, accessible, and respectful support. This includes
providing additional explanations, offering alternative communication channels, and making
reasonable adjustments to processes to ensure equitable outcomes. Square AU is dedicated to
ensuring appropriate support for customers experiencing vulnerability.
2.8 Complaints Relating to Hardship
Square AU recognises that customers may face temporary or ongoing financial hardship that affects
their ability to meet contractual obligations. Customers experiencing hardship are encouraged to
contact Square AU promptly, and their requests will be assessed in a fair, timely, and empathetic
manner, in line with the Square AU Hardship Support Standard.
The hardship process is designed to provide reasonable assistance, which may include flexible
payment arrangements, deferrals or other tailored solutions. Hardship requests are handled
confidentially and in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and internal procedures. Customers
are informed of the outcomes and any options available to them and employees are trained to ensure
that hardship assessments are conducted consistently and without discrimination.
6

Where a complaint may arise relating to a customerʼs hardship situation, or our internal process in
handling a hardship situation, the complaint is handled in accordance with RG 271 with additional care
and shortened timeframes of 21 days for final IDR responses. All complaints relating to hardship are
promptly escalated to the Complaints Management team for resolution.
2.9 Accessibility and Support
Square AU is committed to ensuring that its complaints and dispute resolution processes are free,
easily accessible, and inclusive for all customers. To support this, the company provides access to
translation services, interpreters and additional assistance for customers who may require it, including
those identified as vulnerable.
The process also incorporates references to available hardship or vulnerability assistance, ensuring
that customers experiencing financial, personal, or situational challenges can engage with Square AU
in a fair and supportive manner. This approach ensures that all customers can lodge complaints, seek
assistance, and have their concerns addressed effectively, regardless of their circumstances.
2.10 Systemic Issue Management
Square AU is committed to identifying, escalating, and addressing systemic issues that may arise from
customer complaints. A systemic issue is any matter that indicates a recurring problem, process
failure, or potential risk of harm affecting multiple customers.
The complaints team is responsible for monitoring and analysing complaints data to detect trends or
patterns that may indicate potential systemic issues. Where such an issue is identified, the complaints
team must:
● Notify the Square AU Compliance team and relevant business or functional area for
assessment and remediation; and
● Escalate the matter through the appropriate Square AU Leadership and Management
Committees for oversight and reporting.
Remedial actions may include providing feedback to other internal teams, implementing process or
system improvements, updating staff training, or adjusting products and services to prevent
recurrence.
If a systemic issue may involve a significant breach of regulatory obligations or indicate systemic
misconduct, the matter must be escalated to the Compliance team for assessment under the Square
AU Breach Reporting Policy, and reported to ASIC as required.
This approach ensures that systemic issues are addressed in a timely, transparent, and coordinated
manner—protecting customers, strengthening controls, and maintaining regulatory compliance.
7

2.11 Internal Escalation and Feedback
In accordance with RG 271, all expressions of dissatisfaction that meet the definition of a complaint
must be treated and managed as a complaint from the time they are received, regardless of which
team or channel receives them. Square AU staff must make all reasonable endeavours to escalate
complaints internally to the appropriate team to ensure they are managed by staff with the relevant
expertise and authority. Complaints that raise complex, systemic, or higher-risk issues must be
referred to the Complaints Management Team for oversight and coordination.
The Complaints Management Team may also seek input or assistance from relevant internal
stakeholders, such as Legal, Compliance, Risk, Product, or Customer Operations, to support a
thorough and fair resolution process.
Where a complaint relates to a specific product, service, process, or internal procedure, feedback will
be provided to the relevant business area to promote continuous improvement and prevent recurrence.
All escalations and feedback loops must be documented in accordance with Square AUʼs complaints
handling and record-keeping requirements to ensure transparency, accountability, and compliance
with RG 271.

3.2. Regulatory Program Monitoring, Testing and Reporting
Square AU maintains a structured and risk-based approach to monitoring and reporting on its
regulatory programs and in accordance with the compliance Policy. Oversight is coordinated by the
Compliance and Risk functions, with input from stakeholder teams.
Square AU focuses on three key activities:
● Monitoring: Monitoring activities are conducted on a risk-based schedule to confirm that key
compliance controls are operating effectively. These activities may include but are not limited
to dashboards, data reviews, reporting reviews, exception reporting and analysis of
complaints or incidents. Higher-risk areas are subject to more frequent and detailed
monitoring to ensure timely detection and remediation of issues.
● Testing: Independent and Internal testing and thematic reviews are undertaken through
Compliance, Risk, or Internal Audit to verify that controls are working as intended. Results are
documented, issues are tracked and remediation actions are followed through until closed.
● Reporting: Square AU provides risk based reporting on the performance and effectiveness of
its compliance programs to ensure transparency, accountability, and informed
decision-making. Reports highlight program health, key risks, control effectiveness and
remediation progress, giving senior leaders and committees clear visibility of compliance
outcomes and areas requiring attention. This ensures consistent oversight and supports a
strong culture of governance across the business.
