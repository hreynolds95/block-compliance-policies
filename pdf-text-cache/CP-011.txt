1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Fair Debt Collection Practices Act Policy (the "Policy") outlines
Block, Inc.'s enterprise-wide approach to collections. The purpose of this Policy is to establish
comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc., and its U.S. subsidiaries' utilization of the Fair
Debt Collection Practices Act “FDCPAˮ) framework, ensuring consistent operations while
maintaining brand-specific flexibility, where applicable. Block's Business Product Compliance
Lead, Lending & Credit, and/or their designee, is responsible for the management of this Policy.
This Policy establishes comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc., and its U.S. subsidiaries'
approach to debt collection practices, ensuring consistent operations while maintaining
brand-specific flexibility.
Scope: This Policy applies to all Credit Products offered by Block, Inc., its subsidiaries, and
affiliates (collectively, "Block"), in the United States, including, but not limited to installment loans,
buy-now, pay-later products, and other forms of consumer lending. The only exception is Square
Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated by the
Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.
While Block and its subsidiaries do not meet the definition of a Debt Collector under the FDCPA,
Block has elected to implement FDCPA guidelines as a best practice when collecting debts or
engaging third-party debt collection agencies that may collect debts on behalf of Block.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 General Policy Requirements
Block is committed to ensuring that collection activity across all Credit Products is conducted in a
manner consistent with the FDCPA. The primary purposes of this Policy are to eliminate abusive,
deceptive, and unfair debt collection practices, promote fair debt collection, provide consumers
with an avenue for disputing and obtaining validation of debt information, and promote consistent
state action to protect consumers against debt collection abuses.
2

Where state or local regulations overlap and are stricter than the requirements set out in this
Policy, the more conservative approach will be applied. If any applicable requirements conflict with
this Policy, Block will consult with appropriate legal counsel to resolve the conflict and adjust
policies and procedures to ensure compliance.
2.2 Communication Standards
Block maintains strict standards regarding the timing and frequency of consumer communications.
Communications with consumers in connection with debt collection may not occur at unusual
times, specifically before 800 a.m. or after 900 p.m. in the consumer's time zone, unless Block
has knowledge of circumstances indicating that such times would not be inconvenient to the
consumer.
In alignment with regulatory requirements, Block has implemented specific frequency limitations
for telephone communications. Telephone calls to a consumer about a particular debt are limited to
no more than seven attempts within a seven-day period. Additionally, once a telephone
conversation occurs regarding a debt, Block observes a seven-day waiting period before placing
another call, unless the consumer explicitly requests additional contact.
When initiating communication about a debt, Block ensures proper disclosure of its identity and
purpose. The initial written communication, and if applicable, the initial oral communication,
includes a clear statement that Block is attempting to collect a debt and that any information
obtained will be used for that purpose. Subsequent communications maintain transparency by
consistently identifying the communication as coming from a debt collector.
For voicemail communications, Block utilizes the concept of "limited-content messages." These
messages include only essential information: a business name that does not indicate debt
collection activity, contact information for consumer response, and a request for reply. This
approach protects consumer privacy while maintaining effective communication channels.
2.3 Consumer Rights and Protections
Block respects and upholds consumers' right to control communication regarding their debt. Upon
receiving written notification from a consumer refusing to pay a debt or requesting cessation of
communication, Block discontinues further contact, with limited exceptions. These exceptions
include advising the consumer that collection efforts are being terminated, notifying the consumer
of specific remedies that may be invoked, or informing the consumer of intended remedy
implementation.
In cases where a consumer has secured legal representation regarding a debt, Block directs all
communications to the attorney of record, provided the attorney's contact information is available
or can be readily obtained. This protocol remains in effect unless the attorney fails to respond
within a reasonable timeframe or explicitly permits direct consumer communication.
Block maintains strict protocols regarding workplace contact. Communication with consumers at
their place of employment is prohibited when Block knows or has reason to know that the
3

employer prohibits such communication. This protection ensures that debt collection activities do
not interfere with consumers' employment relationships.
2.4 Third-Party Communications and Privacy
Protection of consumer privacy is important in Block's collection practices. Communication with
third parties regarding a consumer's debt is strictly limited. Without prior consumer consent given
directly to Block, communication about a debt is restricted to the consumer, their attorney,
consumer reporting agencies (if permitted by law), the creditor, and their respective legal
representatives.
2.5 Ethical Collection Practices
Block prohibits any conduct that could reasonably be considered harassing, oppressive, or abusive
in connection with debt collection. This includes refraining from threats of violence, use of
obscene language, publication of consumer lists, or coercive advertising of debts. Additionally,
Block ensures all communications are truthful and transparent, avoiding any false, deceptive, or
misleading representations regarding debt collection.
In the handling of consumer debts, Block employs fair and ethical practices. This includes accurate
representation of debt amounts, clear communication of legal status, and appropriate handling of
disputed debts. Block does not collect amounts unless expressly authorized by the agreement
creating the debt or permitted by law, and ensures all collection actions are legally enforceable and
intended.

3.5 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
