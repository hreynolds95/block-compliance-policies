Customer Conduct & Staff Protection Policy
Block, Inc.
Last Approved: Mar 25, 2026
Effective Date: Mar 25, 2026
1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Customer Conduct & Staff Protection Policy (the "Policy") outlines
Block, Inc.'s approach to persons whose behavior has been assessed as being abusive. The
Consumer Protection Compliance Lead is responsible for management of this Policy.
The purpose of this Policy is to establish comprehensive guidelines governing Block's approach to
managing unacceptable customer behavior and ensuring consistent operations while maintaining
brand-specific flexibility.
Scope: This Policy is established by Block, Inc., its subsidiaries, and affiliates (collectively, "Block")
and is applicable to all entities within Block.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1. Policy Principles
Block upholds the principle that everyone has the right to express their dissatisfaction, provide
comments and offer suggestions to contribute to the enhancement of its services. Block also
acknowledges that during challenging or distressing situations, people may display behavior
inconsistent with their usual conduct when engaging with Block. It is important to note that Block
does not categorize assertive or determined approaches as unacceptable or abusive behavior.
2

2.2. Unacceptable and Abusive Behavior
Block Staff Members are entitled to a workplace free of abusive, offensive or threatening behavior
even when a customer is under stress. Some examples of unacceptable behavior might include:
● Abusive or offensive language directed at Staff Members
● Remarks of a sexual nature directed at Staff Members
● Racist language directed at Staff Members
● Homophobic or other discriminatory remarks directed at Staff Members
● Shouting at Staff Members
● Offensive gestures
● Verbal or physical threats against Staff Members, prominent individuals, Block property or
office buildings
● Bullying or intimidating behavior
● Stalking or any other form of harassment
2.3. Unacceptable Persistent Contact
Block recognizes that some of its customers cannot or will not accept if Block is unable to assist
them further or provide a level of service other than that already provided. Customers may persist
in disagreeing with the action or decision taken in relation to their concerns or they may contact
Block persistently about the same issue(s) via the same, or a number of contact channels.
Examples of unacceptable persistent contact might include:
● Continuing to contact Block Staff Members through illegitimate communication channels,
such as LinkedIn
● Persistent contact via email, written mail or other communications to executive leadership
● Persistent refusal to accept a Final Response that has been made in relation to a complaint
● Persistent refusal to accept explanations relating to what Block can or cannot do
● Persistent refusal to follow the proper procedures explained to them in order to pursue their
issue/concern
● Making an excessive number of contacts about issues previously addressed
● Continuing to contact Block on the same issue(s) without presenting any new information
● Representing themselves as different people or under different names in order to raise the
same issues
● Contacting Block Staff Members outside of the ordinary course of business, such as at a
physical office location, or their home location
It is not necessarily the manner in which such customers communicate with Block, but their
persistence in doing so that goes beyond being reasonable. In certain instances, continuous
contact may be considered to be harassment.
In scenarios where there is a threat that exceeds any reasonable contact, Staff Members must be
escalated to Trust & Safety immediately at go/threatescalation.
3

2.4. Unacceptable Public Expression of Views
Block recognizes the importance of striking a balance between providing an opportunity for the
lawful expression of views and opinions and unacceptable behavior and actions that affect its Staff
Members. Some examples of unacceptable behavior in these instances might include:
● Congregating in and/or obstructing thoroughfares and access points of Block office
buildings
● Entering or attempting to enter restricted and/or non public areas of Block office buildings
● Recording or photographing Staff Members without their permission
● Any intentional damage to Block property
● Failing to follow instructions of employees or security Staff Members when instructed to
leave the premises
● Making threats of harm or violence against Block, and/or its Staff Members
● Stalking or harassing Block Staff Members
● Making inappropriate contact attempts against Block Staff Members, including the use of
foul language, or submitting inappropriate material through support channels
All of these behaviours must be escalated to Trust & Safety immediately. The priority for reporting
is go/threatescalation if inappropriate contact is made, reporting this to Trust & Safety officers on
site immediately if the abusive customer is onsite, and/or reach out to #trust-and-safety on Slack.
2.5. Protocol for Managing Unacceptable and Abusive Behaviors
Face-to-Face Contact
Staff Members are empowered to ask the abusive individual to leave the premises if they feel the
person is being aggressive, abusive or offensive. The Staff Member dealing with the person has
the right to make this decision after that person has persisted in their disruptive behavior after
being advised that they are behaving unacceptably.
In scenarios where Staff Members feel an immediate risk to their personal safety or Block property,
they should contact the local Trust & Safety Security Operations officer, if they are in a location
where one is present, and the Global Security Operations Center GSOC on Slack
(#trust-and-safety) to ask for a private message. If there is no officer on site/duty, or Staff
Members have been approached outside of Block, immediately contact the local emergency
services number and then contact the Global Security Operations Center on Slack.
Local Emergency Services Numbers:
● USA - 911 for Harm and 988 for Self-Harm
● Canada - 911 for Harm and 988 for Self-Harm
● UK - 999 for Harm or 116 123 for Self-Harm Samaritans)
● Ireland - 999 for Harm and 112 for Self-Harm
● Australia - 000 for Harm and 13 11 14 for Self-Harm
4

2.6. Telephone Calls
The use of abusive, offensive and harmful language towards Block Staff Members will not be
tolerated. Staff Members will end any telephone call if they feel the caller is being aggressive,
intimidating, abusive, threatening or offensive. The person taking the call is empowered to make
this decision.
The caller will be advised that their behavior and/or language is unacceptable. If the person
persists and refuses to conclude the conversation, the call will be ended politely. In rare cases, if a
warning cannot be issued due to immediate concerns, the Staff Member has the authority to end
the call promptly.
In some scenarios, individuals may make threats against Block that are severe in nature which
might include:
● Death threats
● Threat of gun violence
● Bomb threat
● Threat of self harm
● Cyberattack threat
Staff Members are encouraged to keep the caller on the line for as long as possible during which
they should take detailed notes and ask as many questions as possible to gather as much
information as possible. Once the call has ended, immediately contact a Trust & Safety Security
Operations officer, file a ticket at go/threatescalation.
2.7. Correspondence
Block Staff Members will not process any abusive correspondence (chat, email, letter or fax). Upon
receiving such communications, Block will advise the individual that their communication is
considered inappropriate and offensive. The person will be respectfully asked to stop
corresponding in this way and informed that if they do not stop, Block will not respond to any
further communication from that individual, except in cases where regulatory requirements
mandate a response.
2.8. Customer Access
Where there is a concern about the behavior of a customer that may fall within the scope of this
Policy, Block Staff Members will initially request in writing that the customer modify their behavior.
If the customer's behavior persists and remains a cause for concern after the initial warning, then
Block may proceed to denylist the customer's account(s).
Where a customer's behavior is so extreme that it poses an immediate threat to the health, safety
or wellbeing of Staff Members, the customer may not receive prior warning that access may be
denylisted. Block will however provide written confirmation of its decision to the customer, where
possible. Sometimes a customer's behavior can cause significant alarm, distress or concern that a
criminal act may have taken, or is about to take place. When such situations arise, Block will report
the matter to Trust & Safety who will assess the matter and contact the police or other relevant
5

authorities. Trust & Safety will base the decision on an assessment of risk. The underlying principle
will be the need to protect Block's Staff Members and others from harm or the threat of harm.
2.9. Restricting a Customer's Account
If Block does decide to place restrictions on the customer's account(s), it may decide to adopt one
or more of the following actions (or any other action deemed appropriate):
● Request the customer to only send communication to a dedicated email address or
telephone number
● Place time limits on telephone calls and/or personal contacts
● Record all telephone calls and/or personal contacts
● Arrange for a named Staff Member to deal with all calls or correspondence from the
customer
● Limit communication to written only
● Inform the customer that their correspondence will only be read (to ensure no new issues
have been raised), acknowledged and filed for record-keeping purposes. However, unless
a new issue is raised, Block will not issue duplicate responses
● Apply a "warning flag" on the service user's electronic record to classify them as an
abusive customer
● Consider working with Trust & Safety to develop an Alias program, for each team, so that
anyone on the team can respond to these communications without their actual name being
used
3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1. Roles and Responsibilities
Block, Inc. Roles:
● Business Units:
○ Implement requirements of this Policy and escalate any exceptions, violations or
other identified risks to Trust and Safety.
● Compliance:
○ Provide oversight and guidance to the Business Units in implementing the
requirements of this Policy.
● Trust and Safety:
○ Provide escalation channels to ensure correct channel intake. Once the threats are
escalated, Trust and Safety are responsible for assessing the severity and selecting
the best possible mitigation strategy to put in place. This could include guidance to
de-platform bad actors/threat makers via specialized teams. Trust and Safety are
responsible for reviewing any changes to threat escalation policies procedures for
consistency with the threat intake/assessment industry standards.

3.4. Exceptions
The Document Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to
this Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws
and regulations. Policy exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a
request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks
associated with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and
retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be
submitted to the Policy Governance Team at go/policyhelp. The Document Owner and/or designee
will inform Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.

Appendix A Post-Incident Support for Staff
Block is committed to supporting Staff Members who experience unacceptable customer behavior.
The following support measures are available:
Immediate Actions:
● Debrief with your Team Lead or Manager
● Log time spent managing the incident as Non-Core Work
● Access Employee Assistance Program EAP and Mental Health Services
Additional Support:
● Participate in optional feedback sessions for process improvements
● Receive counseling and support services through Block's EAP (available 24/7
● Access to mental health resources through Block's benefits portal
Reporting & Documentation: All incidents should be documented in appropriate systems to
ensure:
● Proper tracking of patterns or repeat offenders
● Staff safety monitoring
● Compliance with regulatory requirements

Appendix C Standardized Warning Ladder
Before terminating a conversation, staff should apply a tiered warning approach unless an
immediate threat is present:
First Warning
"I want to help resolve this, but I need the conversation to remain respectful."
Final Warning
"If the behavior continues, I will need to end this interaction."
Termination Message
"Due to continued inappropriate behavior, I am ending this conversation now."
10

Important Note
No warnings are needed for egregious behavior (e.g., physical threats, hate speech, doxxing).
These communications should be ended and the case escalated immediately to Trust & Safety.
