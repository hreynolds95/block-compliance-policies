1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Square AU Pty Ltd (“Square AUˮ) is committed to the fair treatment of
customers and the delivery of positive customer outcomes. This Consumer Protection Policy
(“Policyˮ) outlines the principles, responsibilities, and procedures that govern how Square AU
manages consumer protection risk in the context of its payment services business in Australia.
The Policy forms part of Square AUʼs broader risk and compliance framework and is designed to
ensure compliance with Australian Consumer Law, the Corporations Act 2001, the ASIC Act 2001,
and ASIC Regulatory Guides. Square AU recognises that the regulatory landscape imposes both
legal obligations and best practice standards to ensure that financial products and services are
safe, fair, and accessible.
This Policy establishes a clear framework for identifying, managing, and mitigating consumer
protection risks across all regulated and non-regulated activities under Square AUʼs Australian
products and services, outlining the responsibilities of the business, staff, and compliance teams to
ensure all customers are treated fairly and in accordance with legislation.
Scope: This Policy applies to all Square AU staff members, including employees, contractors,
temporary workers, and third-party service providers engaged in delivering services to Australian
customers. It covers all regulated activities and interactions where there is potential for consumer
detriment, including marketing, sales, customer service, complaints handling, and product
development.
The Policy does not apply to Square products that are distributed exclusively through third-party
arrangements where Square does not control the productʼs features, pricing, or delivery.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1 General Principles
Under Australiaʼs consumer protection laws and regulatory guidance, financial services providers
must act fairly, honestly and efficiently when dealing with customers. These principles underpin all
aspects of product design, disclosure, sales, and ongoing customer support to ensure customers
are protected from harm and treated with integrity.
2.1.1 A Customer Centric Culture
Square AUʼs culture is rooted in its mission to create economic empowerment and a financial
system that is accessible, ethical, and customer-focused. Fair treatment of customers is a
fundamental aspect of how Square AU conducts business. This culture is embedded in day-to-day
decision making, staff training, and the continuous review of products and services to ensure they
meet the needs and expectations of customers, fostering trust and long-term relationships.
2.1.2 Delivering Fair Outcomes
Square AU prioritises delivering fair, accessible, and inclusive services to all customers. We strive
to understand customer needs and design products that meet those needs effectively, while
communicating clearly, accurately, and honestly. Our approach emphasises respectful and
efficient handling of customer queries and issues, ensuring that services are reliable, high quality,
3

and aligned with customer expectations. We are also committed to resolving complaints promptly
and fairly, supporting trust and confidence in Square AUʼs products and services.
These commitments extend to all Square AU users, including small businesses, sole traders, and
marginalised communities.
2.1.3 Regulatory Framework and Governance
Square AU operates within a comprehensive Australian regulatory framework, including the
Australian Securities and Investments Commission Act 2001, Corporations Act 2001 (and
associated Design and Distribution Obligations), Australian Consumer Law, Payment Systems
Regulation) Act 1998, Privacy Act 1988, and Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism
Financing Act 2006. To ensure robust governance, the Square AU Board maintains ultimate
responsibility for approving the consumer protection strategy and risk appetite, allocating
adequate resources, and overseeing policy implementation. The Board is regularly informed of
consumer protection activities, significant complaints, and systemic issues. Governance
committees, including a dedicated Risk and Compliance Committee, Product Governance
Committee, and Consumer Outcomes Committee, meet on a regular basis and are accountable for
reviewing regulatory compliance, consumer outcomes, and internal policy adherence.
Square AU applies a “three lines of defenceˮ model to consumer protection risk management. The
first line consists of business units and front-line staff tasked with executing controls and
delivering products and services in line with policy requirements. The second line comprises Risk
and Compliance functions, which independently monitor, provide oversight, and assist in policy
development and risk assessment. The third line is Internal Audit, providing independent assurance
of system effectiveness and adherence.
2.2 Consumer Guarantees
Under the Australian Consumer Law ACL, consumers are automatically entitled to certain
guarantees when they buy goods and services. They are designed to ensure fair treatment for
consumers and provide remedies when goods or services fail to meet expectations.
For goods (such as Square terminals and 3rd Party hardware sold by Square), consumer
guarantees include that:
● Products are of acceptable quality (safe, durable, free from defects, acceptable in
appearance);
● Products are fit for their intended purpose;
● Products match their description or any demonstration model;
● The business has the right to sell the product (clear title);
● Spare parts and repair facilities are reasonably available.
4

For services (such as the provision of payment processing), guarantees include that:
● Services are provided with due care and skill;
● Services are fit for the purpose the customer made known;
● Services are provided within a reasonable time, if no time was agreed.
2.2.1 Consumer Guarantees and Warranties
All warranty and consumer guarantee related claims are assessed on a case-by-case basis, and
Square AU is committed to acting in accordance with legislation regarding the handling of these
matters. Square AU ensures that customers are provided with clear, accurate and timely
information about their rights and available remedies. Where uncertainty arises about the
application of consumer guarantees, employees are encouraged to seek internal guidance to
ensure consistent, fair and legally compliant outcomes.
2.3 Vulnerable and Hardship-Affected Customers
Square AU is committed to identifying and supporting customers who may be vulnerable due to
health issues, disability, age, financial stress, family violence, literacy or language barriers, cultural
background, or life events like bereavement or unemployment.
The Square AU Vulnerable Customer Standard aims to provide guidance to the relevant staff to
recognise indicators of vulnerability and hardship, using standardised criteria. Customers may
self-disclose vulnerability at any time and are encouraged to do so by accessible and supportive
communication channels. Measures to assist identified vulnerable or hardship affected customers
may include dedicated support teams, flexible communication formats, appropriate product
modifications, fee waivers where justifiable, and swift internal or specialist referrals. Square AU
maintains specific provisions, protocols, and training relating to family violence, ensuring affected
customers have immediate access to confidential support, specialist case management, and
financial safety interventions.
In accordance with ASICʼs expectations, we aim to provide training training to the relevant staff in
order to:
● Recognise signs of vulnerability or hardship;
● Respond with empathy and flexibility;
● Offer tailored support or product adjustments;
● Refer customers to specialist internal teams or external support services.
Where a customer is experiencing financial hardship, Square AU will consider reasonable support
options such as payment deferrals or fee waivers. Complaints involving hardship are prioritised
and handled within the reduced timeframes set out in RG 271.

2.4 Accessibility and Inclusivity
Square AU strives to ensure its products, services, and customer support are accessible and
inclusive, consistent with applicable Australian laws and reasonable business practices. We
recognise that some customers may experience barriers to access due to factors such as culture,
language, disability, age, digital literacy, or financial circumstances.
Where appropriate and permitted by law, Square AU may take reasonable steps to promote
equitable access to its services and to address barriers identified through customer interactions,
feedback, or regulatory developments.
This commitment reflects Square AUʼs aspiration to foster accessibility and inclusion within the
bounds of applicable laws and regulatory requirements.
2.5 Product Development
All Square AU product development and amendment is subject to a product governance process
as outlined in the Product Launch Governance Procedure. Distribution channels and marketing
approaches are assessed to ensure that products are marketed and made available in a manner
that is fair, accurate, and compliant with consumer protection requirements under the Australian
Consumer Law. Product design and review processes also ensure that products deliver fair value,
align with customer expectations, and comply with all applicable legal and regulatory obligations.
2.6 Small Business Protections
In accordance with the ACL and broader regulatory expectations, Square AU provides protections
for small business customers. Square AU is committed to transparent dealings with small business
customers and supports fair, prompt, and accessible services that contribute to their sustainability
and financial wellbeing.
Square AU ensures that its standard form contracts with small businesses comply with legislation
prohibiting unfair contract terms. Contract terms are reviewed to ensure they are transparent,
balanced, and do not impose a significant imbalance in the rights and obligations of the parties.
We also clearly disclose all applicable fees, including payment processing costs, to support
informed decision making.
2.7 Misleading or Deceptive Conduct
Square AU must not engage in conduct that is misleading or deceptive, or that is likely to mislead
or deceive, in accordance with Australian legislation. This obligation applies to all representations
made to customers, including advertising, marketing materials, product information, website
content, social media, and direct communications.
Conduct may be misleading or deceptive if it creates a false impression about a product, service,
price, feature, or customer rights, even if that was not the intention of the person making the
6

statement. Silence or omission of key information may also amount to misleading or deceptive
conduct where it leads a consumer to form an incorrect understanding.
Square AU must ensure that all representations, statements, comparisons and claims about its
products and services are accurate, substantiated, and presented in a clear and balanced manner.
Marketing and promotional activities should be reviewed for compliance before release to ensure
that customers are not misled, confused, or disadvantaged. Employees must take particular care
when discussing product features, pricing, terms and conditions, and customer rights, and must
promptly correct any misleading impression if one is identified. Any instance or allegation of
misleading or deceptive conduct must be escalated to the Compliance team immediately for
assessment and remediation.
2.8 Unfair Contract Terms
Square AU must ensure that all customer contracts comply with the Australian Consumer Law
Schedule 2 of the Competition and Consumer Act 2010 Cth, which prohibits unfair terms in
standard form consumer and small business contracts. A term is considered unfair if it creates a
significant imbalance in the partiesʼ rights and obligations, is not reasonably necessary to protect
Square AUʼs legitimate business interests, and would cause detriment to the customer if relied
upon.
All contracts must be drafted in clear, plain language and presented transparently to ensure
customers understand their rights and obligations. Square AU must not include terms that mislead,
limit statutory rights, or impose unreasonable burdens on customers. Legal, Product, and
Compliance teams are responsible for reviewing contracts to identify and remediate any potentially
unfair terms before use or renewal.
2.9 Unconscionable Conduct
Square AU must not engage in conduct that is harsh, oppressive, or exploits a customerʼs
vulnerability, disadvantage, or lack of understanding, in accordance with Australian Consumer
Law. Unconscionable conduct includes taking unfair advantage of a customerʼs financial hardship,
limited literacy, language barriers, or personal circumstances to secure agreement or payment.
All employees are expected to act honestly, fairly, and in good faith in every customer interaction.
Business practices, sales activities, and customer support processes must promote fair outcomes
and avoid any action that could be perceived as manipulative or exploitative. Any potential
instances of unconscionable conduct must be escalated immediately to the Compliance team for
investigation and appropriate remediation.
2.10 Complaint Handling and Dispute Resolution
Square AU is committed to fair, timely and transparent resolution of customer complaints, in line
with Regulatory Guides and Square AU Complaints Policy and Block Inc Global Complaints Policy.
7

Square AU supports the role of AFCA in resolving disputes fairly and independently. Where
complaints are referred to AFCA, Square AU fully cooperates with AFCAʼs processes and
implements any determinations.
Complaint data is tracked and reviewed regularly to identify trends, systemic issues and
opportunities for service improvement. Complaints data is also compiled in regular reports to the
Square AU Board and a regular report is issued to the business from the global complaints team
which encompasses Australian complaints. This commitment to strong complaints governance
ensures Square AU delivers fair outcomes and maintains customer trust.
2.11 Breach Reporting
All breaches that could result in detriment to customers, including breaches of the Australian
Consumer Law, regulatory obligations, or internal consumer protection policies, must be reported
and managed in accordance with the Square AU Breach Reporting Policy. A breach may include:
● Misleading communication to a customer;
● Denial of a consumer guarantee under ACL;
● Failure to address a complaint within the required timeframe;
● Unfair treatment of a vulnerable customer.
Square AU fosters a culture in which employees are encouraged to speak up, ensuring that breach
reports are handled confidentially and without retaliation. Block Inc also fosters a culture of
openness with its global Speak Up Policy, which allows employees to raise concerns about
potential misconduct, violations of company policies, or unlawful behavior, without fear of
retaliation.
2.12 Third-Party Service Providers
All third-party service providers with potential customer impact are subject to rigorous due
diligence, with contractual requirements including performance standards and consumer
protection obligations. This is managed in accordance with the Square AU Third Party Risk
Management TPRM Policy and the Block Inc global Third Party Risk Management TPRM Policy.
Subject to risk, periodic reviews are conducted to ensure that providers continue to meet agreed
standards, and any material issues or breaches must be promptly reported and remediated.
Controls are designed to ensure that third-party relationships do not compromise Square AUʼs
compliance with legal, regulatory or consumer protection obligations.
2.13 Conflicts of interest
Square AU is committed to identifying, managing and mitigating conflicts of interest to ensure that
all business decisions and customer interactions are conducted with integrity and fairness. A
conflict of interest arises when a personʼs personal interests, relationships, or external obligations
8

could improperly influence, or be perceived to influence, the performance of their professional
duties or Square AUʼs obligations to its customers or regulators.
Employees must immediately disclose any situation that could give rise to a conflict of interest,
whether financial or non-financial, direct or indirect. This includes, but is not limited to, personal
investments, external business interests, relationships with suppliers or customers, or situations
where personal gain could conflict with Square AUʼs obligations to its customers. All conflicts must
be documented and managed in accordance with the Square AU Conflicts of Interest Policy and
Block Inc global Conflicts of Interest Policy.
Square AU will not tolerate any conduct that prioritises personal or commercial benefit over the fair
treatment of customers or the integrity of the market. Regular training, monitoring and compliance
reviews are undertaken to ensure ongoing awareness and effective management of conflicts
throughout the business.
2.14 Monitoring, Testing and Reporting
Square AU maintains a structured and risk-based approach to monitoring, testing and reporting on
its regulatory programs. Oversight is coordinated by the Compliance and Risk functions, with input
from stakeholder teams.
We focus on three key activities:
Monitoring: Monitoring activities are conducted on a risk-based schedule to confirm that key
compliance controls are operating effectively. These activities may include but are not limited to
dashboards, data reviews, reporting reviews, exception reporting and analysis of complaints or
incidents. Higher-risk areas are subject to more frequent and detailed monitoring to ensure timely
detection and remediation of issues.
Testing: Independent and Internal testing and thematic reviews are undertaken through
Compliance, Risk, or Internal Audit to verify that controls are working as intended. Results are
documented, issues are tracked and remediation actions are followed through until closed.
Reporting: Square AU provides risk based reporting on the performance and effectiveness of its
compliance programs to ensure transparency, accountability, and informed decision-making.
Reports highlight program health, key risks, control effectiveness and remediation progress, giving
senior leaders and committees clear visibility of compliance outcomes and areas requiring
attention. This ensures consistent oversight and supports a strong culture of governance across
the business.
2.15 Training and Competency
All relevant staff participate in ongoing, mandatory consumer protection training. Training is
delivered initially at onboarding, and refreshed annually, with focus on vulnerable customer
support, complaint handling, and policy or regulatory updates. Our customer-facing teams are
trained to deliver service that is ethical, transparent, and helpful. Customer facing operations are
9

monitored for quality and timeliness, with regular analysis of feedback and complaints used to
drive improvements.

3.4 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
