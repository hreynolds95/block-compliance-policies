1. Introduction
This Compliance Training Program Standard (“Standard”) outlines Block,Inc’s.(“Block”) approachto
ensuring that appropriate Staff Members are trained on applicable aspects of laws and regulations
related to their job functions. Block requires all Staff Members to complete and understand
mandatory Compliance Training within established timeframes. The Compliance Training Lead is
responsibleformanagementofthisStandards.
Enterprise-wide mandatory compliance training administered in person and through Block’s
Learning Management System (“LMS”) is managed under this Standard. Block updates its
Compliance Training Calendar on an annual basis1, which details the schedule and audiences for
globalandregionalmandatorycompliancetrainingforthecalendaryear.
This Standard is established by Block and is applicable globally across all Block business lines,
subsidiaries, and jurisdictions. References to “Staff Members'' throughout this document shall refer
toallemployeesandexternalworkers(e.g.contingentlabor,contractors)hiredbyBlockwhosupport
operationsand/orsubsidiariescoveredbythisStandard
2.Purpose
This Compliance Training Program Standards (the “Standards”) details Block’s Compliance Training
framework outlining requirements for developing Compliance Training, including certifications and
continuingeducation,boardtraining,intakeprocess,communicationplans,andexceptionwaivers.
3. Scope
The scope of this Standard covers all directors, officers, Board members, and Staff Members
(including temporary or contract staff) of Block globally. Specifically, this Program addresses the
followingtraining:
● FoundationalcorporateComplianceTraining;

developmentmayqualifyandwillbeconsidereduponrequestandwithLeadapproval.

4.5.3TrainingNon-Completion
Block requires all Staff Members to complete and understand mandatory Compliance Training
within established timeframes. Staff Members who do not complete training by established due
dates may be subject to further escalations and or disciplinary action. Upon the conclusion of a
TrainingCampaigncycle,theCTTwillprovideafinallistofStaffMemberswithincompletetrainingto
theHRBPsatthecompletionoftheTrainingCampaign.
For U.S. based employees, appropriateconsequencesareoutlinedintheEscalationFramework,and,
in the U.S., may include disciplinary actions such as limitation of internal mobility, ineligibility for
promotion, loss of system access, and potential termination. Outside the U.S, consequences for
training non-completion are managed on a case-by-case basis, in accordance with local
employmentlawsandregulations.
4.6 Enrollment Waivers
At times, it may be necessary, based on audience updates or changes to training requirements, to
make changes to an approved Enrollment Audience after training hasbeenassignedtoemployees
via LMS. The CTT manages a formal exception process for how these changes are managed and
processed,followingoneofthetwopathwaysdescribedbelow.

parameters for an individual or group of individuals. Waivers are reviewed on a

6.Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Standards Owner and/or their designee will monitor Standards related
activities to evaluate adherence to the Standards and determine whether outputs adhere to
establishedobjectives.
Specifically, training results are reported and submitted to the Block Audit and Risk Committee
(“ARC”) on a quarterly basis. Training reports include completion rates for each training and can be
broken down by region, cost center, and team code. Training results are reported and submitted to
theBlockAuditandRiskCommittee(“ARC”)onaquarterlybasis.
