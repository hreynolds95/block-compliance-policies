1. Introduction
This Complaint Policy (the “Policy”) outlines Afterpay Canada Ltd.’s (“Afterpay CA”, “the Company”)
approach to managing complaints. The purpose of this Policy is to further Block’s Global Complaint
Policy (“Block’s policy”) and Block’s Global Complaint Program by providing for applicable Canadian
complaint requirements to ensure Afterpay CA’s compliance.The Head of Compliance, Afterpay CA is
responsible for management of this Policy.
2. Purpose
In addition to aligning with Block’s Policy, this Policy ensures that Afterpay CA’s complaint handling
processes comply with all local laws and regulations in Canada, where the Afterpay product is
offered. This Policy should be read together with the requirements set out in Afterpay’s Complaints
Handling Standard Operating Procedure (“SOP”).
The objective of the Policy is to ensure that Afterpay CA establishes and adheres to a consistent
process for handling complaints. This involves effectively investigating, addressing, tracking,
managing, and responding to Customer complaints, as well as overseeing and monitoring
complaint trends to identify training needs. Additionally, the Policy sets country-specific
expectations that exceed those of the Block Policy and require periodic reporting to Afterpay CA’s
bank partner(s). This Policy applies to all complaints related to products offered under the Afterpay
brand in Canada but does not cover routine customer inquiries about limits, features, or their
Afterpay account, nor does it apply to other products offered by Block Inc.
3. Scope
This Policy is established by Afterpay CA and is applicable across all Afterpay CA business lines..
Afterpay CA follows all definitions, timeframes, and expectations as outlined in Block’s Policy and
Program. If Canada or a banking partner has stricter requirements than the Block Policy and

4. Policy Requirements
5.1 Managing Complaints (Regional Procedures)
Afterpay’s Complaints Handling Procedure will ensure:
● Complaints raised in the first instance will be handled by a representative from a
customer service team; and
● A customer service representative may escalate complaints to an escalations team where
relevant.
5.2 Managing Complaints (Guiding Principles)
When dealing with complaints, Afterpay CA must:
● Identify itself, record complaint details clearly, and determine what the Customer wants;
● Confirm the details received from the Customer;
● Empathize with the customer and deal with all Customers in a sensitive and courteous
manner, taking extra care where Afterpay CA identifies instances of Customer
vulnerability; and
● Attempt to resolve the complaint as soon as possible.
5.3 Managing Complaints (Written Responses)
Afterpay CA aims to resolve a customer complaint within 15 business days and may be required to
provide certain written responses to complaints according to local regulations.
5.4 Timeframes
Afterpay acknowledges all complaints within 5 business days and provides an initial response within
the same timeframe. Additionally, Afterpay makes every effort to resolve customer complaints within
15 business days.

5.8 Legal Instruments
Afterpay’s complaint handling processes in Canada have been developed to ensure Afterpay CA
complies with:
● Federal and Provincial Privacy Laws:
○ Afterpay CA adheres to applicable federal and provincial privacy laws governing
the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information, ensuring the protection
of customer data.
● Provincial Consumer Protection Laws:
○ Afterpay CA complies with provincial consumer protection laws that govern
consumer transactions, ensuring fair practices and protection of consumer rights.
● Other Guidance as Required:
○ Afterpay CA follows any additional guidance or regulations pertinent to its
operations in Canada to uphold ethical standards and legal obligations.
● Quebec Consumer Protection Act (CPA) and Charter of the French Language
(Charter):
○ In Quebec, Afterpay CA abides by the provisions of the Quebec Consumer
Protection Act (CPA) and adheres to the requirements outlined in the Charter of
the French Language to support French-language rights.
● Financial Consumer Agency of Canada (“FCAC”):
○ Afterpay follows FCAC guidelines regarding complaint handling in banking and
adheres to best practices for effectiveness of external complaints bodies, ensuring
transparency and accountability in resolving consumer disputes.

All relevant Staff Members will receive training at least annually covering applicable requirements

7. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Policy Owner and/or their designee will monitor Policy related activities to
evaluate adherence to the Policy and determine whether outputs adhere to established objectives.
As outlined in the Compliance Management System (“CMS”) Policy, Compliance will provide regular
updates to the Block, Inc. Board (“Board”) and executive management. This reporting may include
specific information related to policies, programs, procedures, audit/exam findings, issues
management, training, and complaints.
8. Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy. Policy exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team at go/policyhelp. The Policy Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.

9. Record Retention
It is Afterpay CA’s policy to ensure that all complaints and data pertaining to complaints are retained
for a sufficient period of time to satisfy compliance requirements under all applicable state and
federal laws and regulations. Afterpay CA will maintain records of this Policy in accordance with
BlockAfterpay’s document retention guidelines. Additionally, it is Afterpay CA’s policy that, unless
otherwise directed by Afterpay CA’s legal and compliance teams or Block’s Global Complaint
Program, all complaint logs and associated data must be retained for a minimum of seven (7) years.
Complaints are retained in Afterpay CA’s customer service software and are categorized accordingly.
