1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The landscape of the regulatory environment governing financial
institutions in Australia is continually evolving. The ability to effectively navigate and promptly
adapt to these regulatory changes is a critical requirement for Square AU Pty Ltd (“Square AUˮ) to
mitigate associated legal, operational, and regulatory risks.
The Regulatory Change Management Standard (the “Standardˮ) is designed to create a robust
system for proactive monitoring, interpretation, and effective responses to changes in regulations.
This Standard encompasses critical assessments, planning, implementation and monitoring
processes to ensure Square AU is aligned with all relevant regulatory requirements. It also covers
key elements such as identifying business stakeholders, assessing the impact of a regulatory
change, developing plans to address changes, and actively monitoring these plans to ensure
ongoing effectiveness.
Scope: This Standard is established by Square AU and applicable to Covered Products offered by
Square AU. This Standard is managed and maintained by the Square AU Compliance team and sets
out the approach to design and execution Square AUʼs Regulatory Change Management Standard.
The Standard also applies to material third-party vendors or fintech partners where regulatory
change affects their activity on behalf of Square AU.

2.2. The Regulatory Change Management Process
Regulatory changes that may impact Square AU, including but not limited to those relating to
AML/CTF or counter-proliferation financing, AFSL and licensing obligations, financial services or
payments regulation or consumer protection, are actively monitored through trusted external
sources such as the the federal register of legislation, ASIC newsletter and AUSTRAC InBrief
Newsletter. The Square AU Team, including but not limited to Compliance, Legal and Risk
areresponsible for monitoring the changes and documenting the changes in AU Regulatory Change
Tracking and Management.
A comprehensive register is maintained for:
● All considered regulatory changes (including those deemed not applicable)
● Dates of change, source, impact assessment, status, action owner, SLA for completion, and
closure

Additionally, the Square AU Regulatory Program Manager, in conjunction with internal Legal Counsel,
conducts a preliminary assessment to ascertain whether the regulatory change is relevant. Where
applicable, an impact assessment will be undertaken, followed by a decision-making process to
determine if modifications to existing programs and procedures are necessary. Where required,
external Legal advice may be sought to ensure that the organisationʼs interpretation of regulatory
requirements is accurate and that all proposed changes are both adequate and compliant with
applicable laws and regulations.
2.2.1 Actioning AUSTRAC guidance
In particular, under AML/CTF regulations, Square AUʼs AML/CTF compliance officer must conduct a
review of its risk assessment if AUSTRAC communicates information to Square AU or to its sector
more broadly - including through published AUSTRAC guidance, that identifies or assess risk
associated with the provision of Square AUʼs designated services.
The risk assessment must be reviewed as soon as practicable after AUSTRAC communicates the
relevant information. Any updates resulting from the review must be made as soon as practicable
after the review is completed. Where updates are required, they must be approved by Square AU
CCO.
Square AUʼs AML/CTF compliance officer must notify the board in writing as soon as practicable
after making any update to the risk assessment.
Following the review of the risk assessment, Square AUʼs AML/CTF compliance officer must also
review and, if necessary, update its AML/CTF policies as soon as practicable. Any updates must be
documented in Square AUʼs AML/CTF Program, including the date the updates were made, within 14
days of the update being implemented.
The Square AU CCO is ultimately accountable for the management and oversight of the Regulatory
Change Management Standard.
2.3 Communication and Training
When any regulatory change is confirmed to be applicable to Square AU and required updates to any
relevant program and/or procedure have been implemented, the Square AU CCO and delegates are
responsible for communicating the change to the organisation and its stakeholders. Training is
provided on a risk based approach and subject to the nature of the regulatory changes.

3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1. Roles and Responsibilities:
● Square Australia (“AUˮ) Chief Compliance Officer (“CCOˮ):
○ Oversees the operational functions of the Regulatory Change Management
Program (the “Programˮ) in compliance with the local regulatory requirements and
is responsible for monitoring and reporting on compliance with this Program.
○ The Square AU CCO may delegate to others the authority to adopt, develop and
implement procedures and reporting that support this Program.
○ The Square AU CCO is also responsible for approving and signing off on each
regulatory change within the register.
○ Reports on regulatory change to global and local governance committees as
appropriate and based on risk.
● Square AU Regulatory Counsel:
○ Provide specialist legal insight and support in the assessment and interpretation of
new or amended regulatory requirements.
○ Works closely with the Regulatory Program Manager to conduct preliminary impact
assessments of regulatory changes, determine their applicability to Square AUʼs
operations, and ensure that the organisationʼs response is aligned with applicable
laws.
● Square AU AML/CTF Officer :
○ Review and update the ML/TF risk assessment where AUSTRAC communication
identifies or assess relevant risks.
○ Notified the Board in writing of any updates to the risk assessment.
○ Review and update AML/CTF policies as necessary following the risk assessment
review.
● Square AU Regulatory Program Manager:
○ Oversees the Regulatory Change Management Program to ensure compliance with
local regulatory requirements, including monitoring and documenting regulatory
changes, conducting impact assessments, managing program or procedural
updates, maintaining the Regulatory Change Register, and providing stakeholder
training.
○ Manage and implement changes to the program and/or procedure, and where
needed, delegate to relevant team members/stakeholders the responsibility of
implementation when enhanced control is required.
○ Provide guidance to relevant stakeholders where required regarding program
updates.
● Square AU Front line teams:
○ Update procedures and/or assign to team members if additional control is needed;
○ Assist the Square AU Regulatory Program Manager in the change analysis in
respect of Square AU Compliance Operations;
○ Provide training where appropriate regarding procedural updates.
3.2. Assurance and Oversight
5

Monitoring
Program Managers and AML/CTF Compliance officer will monitor regulatory change activities on
an ongoing basis to ensure effective implementation and compliance with requirements. Updates,
progress, and supporting evidence will be recorded in the regulatory change register to maintain
visibility and accountability.
Testing
Square AU Compliance will periodically (at least annually) perform a review over the regulatory
change register. The detail review procedure is documented in the operating procedure
documented within the regulatory change register. Each review will be documented with an audit
trail, including the supporting assessment materials and review outcomes, recorded within the
audit component of the register.
Auditing
Internal audit will periodically perform an effectiveness review and/or targeted testing to verify that
implemented regulatory changes are operating as intended and remain effective. Each review will
be documented with an audit trail, including the supporting assessment materials and review
outcomes.
Reporting
Regulatory change updates and outcomes will be reported to relevant global and local governance
committees on a regular basis, with the frequency and level of detail proportional to the nature and
risk of the change. High-impact or material regulatory developments will be escalated promptly to
senior management and the appropriate governance bodies to ensure timely visibility, discussion
and decision-making.
3.3. Escalation and Breach Management
A missed, delayed, or incomplete implementation of a regulatory change may constitute a breach
of this Policy. Where such issues are identified, they must be promptly escalated to the Regulatory
Program Manager and the Chief Compliance Officer for assessment. The incident will be reviewed
to determine whether a reportable breach has occurred, in line with Square AUʼs Breach Reporting
Policy and ASIC Regulatory Guide 78.
All confirmed breaches will be documented, investigated, and remediated appropriately. Root
cause analysis should be undertaken to prevent recurrence, and corrective actions tracked to
completion. Where the breach is determined to be reportable, Square AU will notify ASIC within the
required statutory timeframe.

3.5. Exceptions
The Standard Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership
and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
