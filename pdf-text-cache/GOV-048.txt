1. Introduction and Context
This Incident Response Plan outlines the structure and processes to ensure that Square Canada can
effectively respond to disruptions in a timely manner and in accordance with the requirements of
the Retail Payments Activities Act (“RPAA”). This structure supports the escalation, management,
and remediation of incidents to maintain operational resilience.
Square Canada will both rely upon and will feed into Block Inc.’s existing Incident and Crisis
Management processes in the event of a disruption impacting our retail payment activities. The
purpose of developing and implementing a localised Square Canada Incident Management
structure is to ensure that the impact of the disruption to Square Canada is fully understood,
informing decision making regarding the actions required to mitigate any potential or further
impacts to our retail payment activities.
This playbook establishes the procedures for incident management at Square Canada in
alignment with Block’s global incident response protocols. The objective is to ensure a structured
and efficient response to incidents impacting Square Canada while maintaining coordination
with Block’s central incident response teams.
1.1 Prerequisite Reading
This document does not replace Block’s existing incident plans and runbooks, rather it is to be used
as an extension of existing processes to ensure Square Canada’s alignment with RPAA incident
response and reporting requirements. It is recommended that Directly Responsible Individuals
(“DRI”s) directly or indirectly impacted by this runbook familiarize themselves with Block's Business
Continuity Plan.

2. Definition of an Incident
An incident, for the purposes of this Incident Management Playbook and in accordance with the
RPAA, refers to an event or series of related events that is unplanned by a payment service
provider and that results in, or could reasonably be expected to result in, the reduction,
deterioration or breakdown of any retail payment activity that is performed by the payment
service provider.
Incidents may arise from a variety of sources including, but not limited to, cybersecurity breaches,
system outages or failures, data corruption, third-party service disruptions, operational errors,
fraud, or external threats.

For clarity, an incident is not limited to events that have already resulted in harm, but also
includes events with a reasonable likelihood of causing such harm if left unaddressed. The
classification of an event as an "incident" under this playbook triggers the application of our
formal incident response procedures, including incident assessment, notification (internal and
regulatory), remediation, recovery, and post-incident review.
3. Incident Response Lifecycle
3.1 Detect
Block’s Incident Management Team shall notify Square Canada of any incidents impacting its
operations within 4 hours, but no later than 24 hours of becoming aware of the incident. The
designated Square Canada Incident Lead must acknowledge receipt of the notification and
initiate the assessment process.

The Square Canada Incident Lead shall determine whether the incident is material or not and
present the initial assessment to the Incident Response Team for challenge and/or approval. If
the Incident Response Team disagrees with the Incident Lead’s assessment, they may challenge
the determination. It is recognized that it can be challenging in the early stages of an incident to
be exact on impacts and therefore classification as ‘material’, best practice suggests using
estimates or data from comparable periods (e.g. based on previous incidents and existing
knowledge on services).
If the incident is determined by the Incident Response Team not to meet the material/reportable
threshold under the RPAA, the Incident Lead will continue to follow the Incident Management
policy to manage the incident (including recording details of remediation).
For all incidents assessed (Material or non-Material), the Incident Response Lead must record the
assessment, the Materiality decision, the rationale for the Materiality decision and the
Compliance, Risk and Legal Counsel approves of the Materiality decision and the remediation
action for the incident.
3.3 Engage Relevant Stakeholders
The Incident Lead shall convene the Incident Response Team for a briefing on the initial
assessment in order to define a response and communication strategy. Upon agreement on the
classification by the Incident Response Team, the Incident Lead shall notify any other relevant
Square Canada teams that are not part of the Incident Response Team.
At this stage of the lifecycle, initial notification to the Bank of Canada must occur.
Initial Notification:
● The Incident Response Team will coordinate with External Communications to craft a
written “initial notification” to materially affected end users users, PSPs or clearing
houses (using their most recent contact information provided to them by Square), and
the Bank of Canada without delay but no longer than 48 hours after Square Canada
determines that the incident is material.
● Square Canada must post a notice of the incident on its website to ensure that all
materially affected individuals or entities are notified of the incident. The notice will
include, at a minimum:
○ Square’s name;

○ a description of the incident, including when it began, and the nature of its
material impacts on the individuals or entities; and
○ any corrective measures that could be taken by the individuals or entities.
● Canada Counsel and the Incident Response Team are responsible for reviewing and
approving the written notification. Once approved, External Communications will

● The Chief Risk Officer and Regulatory Manager are responsible for disseminating

All async engagement and updates on the incident shall take place in the Square CA Incident
Response channel. This slack channel should be updated frequently with any information
gathered or communication held related to the incident outside the formal channel.
Continuous communication with Block’s Incident Management Team and, as needed, senior
leadership, shall be maintained throughout the response process.
Note that an initial notification and final notification are required for all material incidents.
However, if the incident is resolved and all required information is available at the time of initial
notification (i.e., within 48 hours of determining the incident to be material), the PSP may submit
a single notification that provides all relevant information (which also serves as the final
notification.
3.4 Remediate
The Incident Lead shall ensure that appropriate response actions are executed based on the
severity and impact of the incident. The situation shall be continuously monitored to determine
whether internal escalation is necessary. Remediation shall include promptly identifying the
status of all transactions that were in progress at the time of the incident. Dedicated monitoring
and reconciliation processes will be activated to determine whether transactions were
successfully completed, delayed, or failed. Where data loss or corruption is detected, Square will
initiate recovery protocols to restore affected records from validated backups and system logs,
ensuring continuity and accuracy of transaction data. Any identified data integrity issues will be
corrected through reconciliation with authoritative records and partner systems, with oversight
from designated incident leads to confirm completeness and accuracy. These actions will be

At this stage of the incident lifecycle, the Incident Lead should monitor the incident to determine
if an interim notification is required.
Interim Notification:
● An interim notification must be communicated by the Regulatory Manager to the Bank of
Canada within 72 hours from the initial notification if there are significant
developments in the incident and when relevant updates become available. Should the
submission time limit fall on a weekend or statutory holiday in Canada, Square Canada
may submit the intermediate report by noon the next working day.
● The Incident Response Team is responsible for providing new information regarding the
incident including changes in prioritization, containment measures, ongoing remediation
actions, and overall progress.
● The Incident Response Team will coordinate with External Communications to craft the
written intermediate report. Canada Counsel and the Incident Response Team are
responsible for reviewing and approving the intermediate notification.
● Once approved, External Communications will share the communication with affected
end users and other PSPs, while the Chief Risk Officer and Regulatory Manager will share
the communication with the Bank of Canada.
● The Chief Risk Officer and Regulatory Manager are responsible for disseminating
questions and feedback from the Bank of Canada back to the Incident Response Team.
The Incident Response Team shall oversee the implementation of response actions and provide
periodic updates to any higher levels of leadership, if required.
3.5 Resolve
An incident shall be deemed resolved when containment measures are validated, all mitigations
are confirmed as effective, and impacted systems are functioning normally. Recovery efforts,
including system restoration and data reconciliation, shall be completed. The Incident Lead shall
secure confirmation from stakeholders before declaring the incident as “resolved” and closing
the incident. The Incident Response Team shall conduct a final review prior to resolution
confirmation to ensure that all risk factors have been addressed.

3.6 Post-Incident Review
Following resolution, a post-incident review shall be conducted by the Incident Lead to assess
root causes, mitigation effectiveness, and areas for improvement. Findings and lessons learned
shall be documented, and internal knowledge bases and playbooks shall be updated as
necessary. The Incident Response Team shall review post-incident findings and make
recommendations for future improvements.
3.7 Report
3.7.1 Report to Regulators
A final notification shall be sent to the Bank of Canada following the resolution of the incident.
Final notification:
● The Bank of Canada requires a comprehensive final report once all required details of
the incident have been determined, including the root cause analysis, and the
incident has been resolved. Square Canada will ensure that this is done without delay
but no later than one month after the latest interim notification.
● The Incident Response Team will coordinate with External Communications to craft the
written intermediate report.
● Canada Counsel and the Incident Response Team are responsible for reviewing and
approving the intermediate notification. Once approved, External Communications will
share the communication with affected end users and other PSPs, while the Chief Risk
Officer and Regulatory Manager will share the communication with the Bank of Canada.
● The Chief Risk Officer and Regulatory Manager are responsible for disseminating
questions and feedback from the Bank of Canada back to the Incident Response Team.
Other regulatory bodies requiring notifications shall be determined by Legal and Compliance.
Notification Guidelines
In accordance with Regulation s. 11(2), Square Canada will ensure that all notifications to the Bank
of Canada regarding incidents are made through the Bank’s designated electronic notification
system. Each notification must include the following minimum content requirements:
1. Identification – The legal name of Square Canada and the contact information of a
designated individual, including name, telephone number, and email address.

2. Incident Description – A clear description of the incident, including its type, timing,
and scope.
3. Material Impact – An assessment of the incident’s material impact on affected end
users, counterparties, or other parties relying on Square Canada’s retail payment
services.
4. Response Measures – A summary of the actions taken or planned to respond to the
incident, including any mitigation, remediation, and communication measures.
Square Canada will retain a record of all notifications submitted to the Bank of Canada, including
confirmation of submission through the Bank’s electronic system. These records will be aligned
with Square Canada’s record retention requirements and made available for regulatory
inspection upon request.
The Incident Response Team shall validate compliance efforts and recommend updates to
reporting protocols as needed.
3.7.2 Report to Senior Officers and the Board
Senior Officers and the Board of Directors will be kept informed of incident activity through
updates provided as part of the regular cadence of Board and Committee meetings.
In the event that an incident is reported to the Bank of Canada under applicable regulatory
requirements, the Board will be notified via email. This notification will include:
● A summary of the incident
● The rationale for regulatory reporting
● The time and method of the report to the Bank of Canada
● Any immediate or planned next steps
The email will be sent promptly following submission to the Bank of Canada and will ensure the
Board is kept informed of regulatory interactions relevant to the incident.
3.8. Archive
Incident reports shall be archived for compliance and audit purposes. in accordance with Square
Canada’s Document Retention Policy. All reports and related documentation will be securely
stored in the incidents reports folder, with access restricted to authorized personnel.

4. Continuous Improvement
This document shall be reviewed and updated periodically to enhance response and reporting
capabilities.
4.1. Testing
Effective testing and training are vital components of Square Canada’s Incident Response Plan,
ensuring preparedness for swift and efficient response to disruptions.
Recovery capabilities identified within the BCP will be tested annually using the methods
outlined in the Business Continuity Management Standard.
4.2. Training
Comprehensive training exercises shall be conducted to ensure team readiness with policies and
procedures. The Incident Response Team shall periodically evaluate incident trends and
recommend strategic improvements to the incident response plan and associated training.
5. Notification and Reporting Requirements
Timelines
● Initial Notification – No later than 48 hours of becoming aware of the incident. Should the
submission time limit fall on a weekend or statutory holiday in Canada, Square Canada
may submit the notification by noon the next working day.
● Intermediate Report(s) – within 72 hours from the initial notification. Should the
submission time limit fall on a weekend or statutory holiday in Canada, Square Canada
may submit the intermediate report by noon the next working day.
● Final Report – no later than one month after the latest intermediate report.
Templates
For an export of the notification and reporting templates, please refer to this folder:
Notification & Reporting Templates . Refer to the latest Bank of Canada guidance for detailed
information and updates on reporting material incidents.

7. Recordkeeping and Retention
All documentation generated as part of the incident response process—including incident logs,
investigation reports, communications, corrective actions, and post-incident reviews—shall be
maintained in accordance with Square Canada’s Record Retention Policy. This includes, but is not
limited to:
● Incident response documentation including:
○ possible or verified impact on retail payment activities;
○ possible or verified impact on end users;
○ possible or verified impact on other payment service providers or on clearing
houses of clearing and settlement systems under the RPAA; and
○ possible or verified impact on systems, data, or information involved in the
performance of retail payment activities.
○ Measures taken to mitigate the impact of an incident, measures to prevent or
mitigate any further damage, measures to identify the root causes of the
incident;
○ The manner in which the incident was reported;
○ The status of all transactions identified, the manner in which the status of
those transactions was identified and the manner in which Square Canada
recovered any lost or corrupted data and corrected any data integrity issues.
● Internal and external communications related to the incident
● Root cause analyses and lessons learned
● Evidence and data collected during investigations
● Regulatory reporting submissions
For detailed retention periods and storage protocols, please refer to the Record Retention Policy.
