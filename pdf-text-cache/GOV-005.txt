individuals and teams who own and manage RPAA relevant records and/or the systems that house
them. It is the responsibility of all staff to ensure these obligations, including with partners and
vendors are adhered to in accordance with relevant policies and standards.

To ensure compliance with Section 40 of the RPAA and its supporting regulations, Afterpay and
Square will implement and maintain robust recordkeeping practices that align with regulatory
expectations and internal standards. The following commitments apply:
2.1. Retention of Records
Afterpay and Square will retain all records required under the RPAA for at least five оВБ5оВВ years
following the date on which they cease to demonstrate compliance with a current RPAA obligation,
unless a longer retention period is required by applicable law or policy.
2.2 Clear and intelligible records
Afterpay and Square shall take reasonable measures to ensure that all relevant records are in a
format that is clear and intelligible, and maintained in a sufficient manner such that it is readily
available and easily retrievable from secured systems of record within a short period of time, and
capable of being reviewed by the Bank of Canada or other regulatory authorities upon request;
This includes all relevant records that are managed by Afterpay, Square, external vendors,
third-party service providers and/or intra-group entities.
2.3. Record Protection and Integrity
Afterpay and Square take reasonable measures to retain the records required under the RPAA to
prevent loss and destruction, prevent their falsification, detect and correct any inaccuracies
contained in them, and prevent unauthorized persons from accessing or using the information
contained in them.
2.4. Third Party Service Provider Record
Where RPAA-related records are created or stored by an agent, mandatary, Third-Party Service
Provider as defined under the RPAA оВБTPSPоВВ, or intra-group entity:
вЧП Afterpay and Square will take reasonable measures to ensure that such records remain
accessible to Afterpay and Square at all times, and can be made available to the Bank of
Canada upon request.
вЧП Afterpay and Square will take reasonable measures to ensure that these records are
retained in accordance with the RPAA requirements, including format, retention period, and
protective measures.
вЧП Contracts with TPSPs will include recordkeeping clauses, in line with Afterpay and Square Љs
Third Party Risk Management Policy, to ensure compliance with these obligations.
2.5. Governance and Oversight
To ensure appropriate governance, accountability, and compliance with the RPAA, Internal Audit
and Compliance will periodically review recordkeeping practices as part of the broader RPAA
compliance monitoring program. These reviews are intended to ensure:

вЧП Documented procedures are in place to classify, store, and retrieve RPAA-related records
effectively;
вЧП Controls for record accessibility, accuracy, and protection are tested periodically to confirm
operational effectiveness;
вЧП Third-party oversight is maintained, ensuring external vendors, TPSPs, and intra-group
entities meet their contractual recordkeeping obligations in accordance with Section 40 of
the RPAA;
вЧП Records are readily available to internal stakeholders, auditors, and regulatory authorities
upon request.
These reviews support Afterpay and Square Љs commitment to data integrity, regulatory readiness,
continuous improvement of its compliance framework and ongoing compliance with RPAA
obligations.
2.6. Retention Schedule
All relevant RPAA records must be retained for a period of 5 years following the date such records
cease to demonstrate a PSP Љs current compliance with the Act and Regulations. The five-year
retention period begins when a record is superseded or no longer used for current compliance, not
when it is created. For example, if a policy is updated in 2026, the previous version must be kept
until 2031.

3. Record Disposal
Records retained under this policy will be disposed of securely and in accordance with Block Љs
Data Retention Standard, the RPAA, and all other applicable regulatory requirements, including but
not limited to the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act оВБPIPEDAоВВ.
Disposal methods will ensure that records are destroyed in a manner that protects confidentiality
and prevents unauthorized access or recovery.
4. Compliance & Enforcement
4.1. Roles and Responsibilities:
The following list is in alignment with the RPAAs approach to the 3 lines of defence (вАЬ3LoDЋЃ) model
that delineates roles and responsibilities across 3 separate lines, providing clarity on ownership,
oversight and escalation paths.
вЧП Record / System Owners оВБ1st and 2nd line roles):
вЧЛ Responsible for ensuring RPAA records are created, maintained, and retained in
accordance with this Policy.
вЧП Legal оВБ2nd line):

вЧЛ Responsible for providing legal advice, guidance and interpretation to the various
lines of defence to ensure compliance with RPAA requirements in accordance with
this Policy.
вЧП Compliance оВБ2nd line):
вЧЛ Responsible for ensuring the ongoing oversight of the RPAA compliance program
including the requirements set out in this Policy.
вЧП Enterprise Risk оВБ2nd line):
вЧЛ Responsible for ensuring that this Policy is aligned with risk appetite and overall
risk management strategy.
вЧП Board of Directors оВБSquare and Afterpay CA Boards):
вЧЛ Holds ultimate compliance accountability and provides oversight to ensure that
RPAA requirements are met including requirements in this Policy.
вЧП Internal Audit оВБ3rd line):
вЧЛ Responsible for conducting periodic audits to assess the effectiveness of risk
controls defined under the RPAA related to record retention requirements set out in
this Policy.
4.2. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Policy Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance
and objective achievement. As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance
will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Afterpay and Square Canada Board and
executive management. Reporting may cover specific information related to:
вЧП Policies and standards
вЧП Procedures

4.3. Training
All relevant staff will receive the RPAA training at least annually covering applicable requirements
and guidance to implement this Policy effectively. All training related activities are monitored by the
Compliance Team and are outlined in the Compliance Training Program.
4.4. Record Retention
All Documents are retained according to the longer of Block's Data Classification & Handling Policy;
Applicable Regulatory Requirements; or this policy.

4.5. Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate the RPAA or other
applicable laws and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a
reason for a request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to
mitigate risks associated with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in
writing and retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions
should be submitted to the Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will
inform Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
