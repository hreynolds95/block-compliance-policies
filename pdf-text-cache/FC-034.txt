1. Introduction
This UUP Standard (“Standard”), established by Cash App, U.S., a Line of Business under Block, Inc.
(“Block”), outlines Cash App’s approach to detecting and monitoring Accounts and Customers for
prohibited activity, and Denylisting relationships that have been determined to be engaging in activitythat
violates Cash App’s Terms of Service, and other policies, including the Acceptable UsePolicy.Arisk-based
approach is used to ensure Customers whomeetalertingcriteriaaresubjectto Accountreviewsandthose
posing a heightened risk are subject to an enhanced due diligence review. This allows the business to
further understand the risks the Customer poses and establish processes to mitigate thoserisks.TheUUP
Standard is a critical element within Cash App's compliance framework, designed to align with regulatory
standards and enhance the integrity of the Cash App business ecosystem. This document delineates the
comprehensiveUUPStandardtailoredforPersonal(P2P)andCashforBusiness(C4B)Customers.
This Standard is established by Cash App Compliance. References to “staff members” throughout this
document shall refer to all Cash App employees or external workers (e.g. contingent labor, contractors)
hiredbyBlockwhosupportstheoperationscoveredbythisStandard.
2. Purpose
Cash App conducts business in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. The purpose of this
Standard is to further Cash App’s UnacceptableUsePolicyprocessbyimplementingarisk-basedapproach
towards preventing and reducing Unacceptable Use, a fundamental aspect of Cash App’s control
framework. One way of executing this risk-based approach is through the assessment of prohibited and
restricted activity associated with Customers, and applying a compliance reviewcommensuratewiththese
identifiedrisks,andwhereappropriateDenylisting AccountsandCustomersengaginginprohibitedactivity.
The UUP Standard captures due diligence to ensure Cash App has a clear understanding of its Customer
base and continuously evaluates risks associated with violations of the Terms of Service Agreement,
Acceptable Use Policy, andotherfinancialcrimes.TheUUPStandardalsoestablishesconsistentrisk-based
monitoringcontrolsforprohibitedactivityontheplatform.
3. Scope
This Standard is established by Cash App and is applicable to the Cash App business or Cash App
subsidiaries. It addresses requirements to assess risk, the UUP Team reviews Accountsthatalertfora
compliance diligence (inclusive of transaction reviews) to activity that is prohibited because it is

4. Standard Requirements
4.1 Unacceptable Use Controls
The Cash App Terms of Service (the “Cash App Terms”) is a legal agreement between the Customer
(business or personal Accounts), as a current or prospective user of the Services, and Block , formerly
known as "Square, Inc." (hereafter, “the Company,” “we,” “our” or us”), and governs the use of Cash App, a
financial platform, which includes mobile applications,websites, software, cloud-based solutions,andother
products and services (the “Service”), offered by the Company. The Unacceptable Use Program uses a
risk-based approach to identify Accounts engagedinviolationsoftheTermsofService(“TOS”)Agreement
or engaging in Unacceptable Use as stated in Cash App’s Acceptable Use Policy (e.g., Gambling, adult
services,anddrugsamongotherslisted).
Compliance conducts annual risk assessments to determine the quantity and quality of risk related to
Unacceptable Use and to detect trends over time. A number of metrics are considered in order to
determine high-risk, Unacceptable Use. Compliance documents the analysis and rationale of selected
metrics. Based on these assessments, Compliance may adjust the approach to monitorandcontrolthe
identifiedrisks.
4.2 Program Structure
All Customers are subject to Cash App’s Know Your Customer (“KYC”) processes, including the Customer
Identification Program (“CIP”), Customer due diligence (“CDD”), and enhanced due diligence(“EDD”).While
those programs and controls are designed to ensure Cash App’s compliance with anti-money laundering
and counter-terrorist financing (“AML/CTF”) laws and regulations, Cash App also monitors activity on the
platform for terms of services violations activities that are not allowed under its TOS and takes action to
ensurethoseactivitiesdonotrepresentsignificantrisktoCashApp.
To do so, Cash App monitors activity on its platforms through this Program, focusing on an activity
monitoring component to identifypotentialUnacceptableUse,areviewcomponenttoconfirmtheactivityis
indeed a breach of Cash App’s TOS, and a referral component to fulfill Cash App’s obligations under
suspicious activity reporting laws and regulationswheretheunderlyingactivityissuspectedtobeillegalor
potentiallysuspicious.

4.2.1CustomerActivityMonitoring
Cash App utilizes various proprietary algorithms to create cases for the UPP team to review. This includes
automated case queues for review. Case queues are generated using machine-learning, heuristics, and
manualmethodologies,suchasescalationsbyotherteamstoUUPforfurtherreview.
Business and personal Accounts associated with Unacceptable Use may generate an automated and
systemic alertwhenadefinedtriggeringeventoccurs,orbeescalatedmanually.Unacceptableuseincludes
behavior that is against CashApp’sTOSorotherinternalpolicies,aswellassuspiciousorillegalactivity.An
alerted Account is manually reviewed, or investigated, to confirm the activity is unacceptable and
determine the appropriate action. Cash App’s UUP team queues areestablishedandmaintainedbyinternal
Engineering team(s). Any new queues or recommended changes to the existing queue models fortheUUP
teamarereviewedandapprovedbyProgramManagersandUnacceptableUseLeads.
When an alert is generated due to a specified triggering event, or a manual escalation occurs, Customers
associated with Unacceptable Use are investigated by a UUP Specialist. During the investigation,
Specialists follow documented review procedures and user guides to ensure a consistent approach and
final action is applied. During the investigation, analysts assess Customer-specific attributes, inclusive of
Customer information collected at onboarding, device and other electronic data, transactional behavior
patterns and activity, and publicly available information such as web presence linkedtotheCustomer.This
helps analysts better understand the nature and purpose of the Account and confirm if the Customer is
engaging in Unacceptable Use of the platform that warrants action being taken onthe Account.CashApp
will occasionally determine that Unacceptable Use is not egregious and does not warrant Denylisting a
Customer.
An enhanced review process is conducted when high-risk Terms of Service and Acceptable Use Policy
violations are detected during the alert reviewprocess.Tocombattheriskidentified,UUPconductsanEDD

4.2.3ReferralofSuspiciousActivity
During the course of the activity investigation, analysts may suspect that transactions areillegal,intended
to hide illegal activity, or an attempt to evade anti-money laundering or counter-terrorist financing laws.
When these transactions meet the requiredthresholds,theanalystisresponsibleforreferringthe Account
to the Suspicious Activity Reporting (“SAR”) Team for additional review. When an Account or transaction
has been referred to the SAR Team for review, an additional investigation is completed by a SAR analyst.
SAR analysts file reports on suspicious activity in compliance with legal, regulatory, and business partner
requirements.
