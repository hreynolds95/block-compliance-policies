1. Overview
1.1 Introduction and Purpose
This Clearpay UK Payment Service Providers PSP Due Diligence Standard (the "Standard")
establishes mandatory requirements for Payment Service Provider due diligence and risk
management within Clearpay Finance Limited's Merchant Services PSPs product offering.
Clearpay Finance Limited ("Clearpay UK") operates as a subsidiary of Block, Inc. ("Block"),
providing Buy Now Pay Later BNPL services in the UK market. Clearpay serves three distinct
customer categories: consumers receiving BNPL loans, merchants utilising Clearpay payment
options, and Payment Service Providers partnering with Clearpay to offer services to their
merchant customers.
This Standard defines specific, measurable requirements that must be achieved to ensure effective
PSP oversight. The requirements establish comprehensive control frameworks, compliance
metrics, technology standards, reporting obligations, exception management protocols, and quality
assurance measures.
1.2 Scope
This Standard applies to Clearpay UK and governs all PSP relationships supporting the Merchant
Services PSPs product offering, including:
● Traditional merchant acquirers
● Payment facilitators
● Embedded finance providers
● MLevel partners
The Standard establishes requirements for due diligence, ongoing monitoring, risk management,
and compliance oversight activities for all PSPs that partner with Clearpay to offer Clearpayʼs BNPL
service to their merchant customers.

2. Regulatory Framework
2.1 CDD Treatment of PSPs
PSPs are not customers under the Money Laundering Regulations because the services they
receive from Clearpay are not listed activities under the MLRs. Nevertheless, Clearpay applies
standards equivalent to customer due diligence under the Money Laundering Regulations to PSPs
to mitigate the risk of regulatory challenge, meet broader financial crime obligations, and ensure
comprehensive risk management.
Clearpay does not apply customer due diligence to PSP Sub-merchants because the firm has no
direct business relationship with these entities. However, Clearpay remains exposed to the
ML/TF/PF and Sanctions risk that processing their transactions poses. Therefore, Clearpay applies
enhanced due diligence measures to all PSPs given their role in facilitating relationships with PSP
Sub-merchants and Clearpayʼs reliance on the controls of PSPS to mitigate the risks posed by their
sub-merchants.

2.2 Policy Integration
This Standard complies with Clearpayʼs AML/CTF/CPF and Sanctions Policy and maintains
alignment with Block's global compliance framework while establishing Clearpay-specific
requirements. Where conflicts exist between this Standard and Block's global standards, UK policy
and regulatory requirements take precedence.
The Standard builds on the Block Standards for Payment Service Provider Oversight and Indirect
Merchant Relationships, incorporating the global framework while addressing Clearpay's business
model, Clearpayʼs AML/CTF/CPF and Sanctions policy requirements, and local regulatory
expectations.
2.3 Supporting Framework and Core Principles
This Standard operates within a comprehensive compliance framework supported by established
Block processes and standards. The Block Standards for Compliance Outreach and Escalations to
PSPs provides the operational foundation for managing PSP compliance communications,
establishing structured timelines, escalation pathways, and accountability measures that ensure
effective oversight execution.
Core Principles of this Standard:
Risk-Based Approach: All PSP oversight activities are calibrated to assessed risk levels,
ensuring proportionate resource allocation and appropriate control measures.
Regulatory Primacy: UK regulatory requirements take precedence over global standards
where conflicts exist, ensuring full compliance with applicable laws.
Comprehensive Due Diligence: PSPs must demonstrate robust compliance capabilities
through comprehensive verification and ongoing assessment processes.
Continuous Monitoring: Ongoing oversight through transaction monitoring, due diligence
refresh and event-driven assessments ensures sustained compliance effectiveness.
Operational Integration: Seamless coordination between Clearpay compliance functions
and Block's enterprise infrastructure ensures consistent and effective oversight.
Technology-Enabled Oversight: Automated systems and real-time capabilities enhance
monitoring effectiveness and compliance efficiency.

3. PSP Risk Assessment Framework
3.1 Risk-Based Approach
All PSP oversight activities operate under a comprehensive risk assessment framework informed
by relevant risk indicators. This approach ensures that all PSPs meet Clearpayʼs enterprise risk
appetite, regulatory and network expectations, and operational oversight standards from
onboarding through the full lifecycle of the relationship.
The risk assessment framework evaluates multiple risk indicators identified through the due
diligence process to determine appropriate oversight measures, monitoring frequencies, and
control requirements while maintaining consistently elevated standards across all PSP relationships
as part of Clearpay's PSP due diligence program.
3.2 Due Diligence and Risk Indicator Identification
PSPs undergo comprehensive due diligence processes that identify and assess risk indicators
through structured information requests and documentation review. The due diligence process
encompasses:
Information Request Process: PSPs complete comprehensive Due Diligence
Questionnaires DDQs and provide supporting documentation that enables assessment of
their business model, operational structure, and internal controls. This process identifies
risk indicators related to business complexity, jurisdictional exposure, ownership
structures, and compliance capabilities.
Risk Indicator Evaluation: Through the due diligence process, various risk indicators are
identified and evaluated, including but not limited to their operations, jurisdictional risk
exposure, beneficial ownership, regulatory compliance history, proliferation financing risk
exposure and control effectiveness. These indicators inform the intensity and scope of
ongoing oversight requirements.
Enhanced Assessment: All PSPs are subject to enhanced due diligence measures
appropriate to their identified risk profile, with additional assessment requirements applied
based on the nature and extent of risk indicators identified during the initial and ongoing
due diligence processes.
3.3 Ongoing Risk Assessment Requirements
The Block MNC must submit active PSPs and their risk assessments for review and approval by the
MLRO at least annually, and following material changes in PSP circumstances, regulatory
developments, or identified concerns. Risk assessments must incorporate current information,
reflect evolving risk profiles, and ensure that oversight measures remain appropriate to identified
risk indicators.
The MLRO must review PSP risk assessments annually as submitted to them.

All PSPs remain subject to due diligence standards regardless of risk indicator assessment, with

4. PSP Onboarding Standards
4.1 Mandatory Eligibility Criteria
All PSPs must satisfy the following eligibility requirements before operational commencement:
UK Entity Establishment: PSPs must be established as UK legal entities or maintain UK
establishment through authorised representatives, representing a mandatory requirement
for regulatory compliance.
FCA Authorisation: PSPs must hold current and appropriate FCA authorisation or
registration for payment services relevant to their intended business activities. Verification
must occur through direct consultation of the FCA firm register at
https://register.fca.org.uk/s/.
Due Diligence Completion: PSPs must complete Clearpay's PSP due diligence process
and receive final MLRO approval before operational commencement.
4.2 Due Diligence Standards
PSPs must demonstrate compliance with comprehensive AML/CTF/CTF and Sanctions due
diligence standards that meet regulatory rules and expectations and the requirements of this
Standard:
Corporate Information Standards: PSPs must provide complete corporate documentation
demonstrating legal establishment, organisational structure, and regulatory status sufficient
to verify entity legitimacy and operational authority.
Ownership and Control Standards: PSPs must demonstrate transparent ownership
structures with complete beneficial ownership disclosure for all individuals or entities with
25% or greater ownership or control, including verification of any identified beneficial
owners.
Compliance Program Standards: PSPs must maintain comprehensive AML/CFT/CPF and
Sanctions compliance programs that demonstrate effective customer due diligence
capabilities, risk assessment methodologies, sanctions screening programs, transaction
monitoring systems, and ongoing monitoring frameworks appropriate to their risk profile
and business model.
Screening and Verification Standards: PSPs must implement screening programs that
encompass PEP identification, sanctions screening, and adverse media monitoring for all

Geographic Risk Assessment Standards: PSPs must demonstrate comprehensive

4.4 Execution and Approval Standards
Due diligence execution must operate through structured collaboration between Block Merchant
and Network Compliance and Clearpay's compliance function, with the MLRO maintaining ultimate
decision-making authority for all PSP onboarding determinations and periodic review outcomes.
All PSPs seeking to onboard with Clearpay must receive MLRO approval. Additionally, all periodic
reviews of existing PSP relationships require MLRO approval to ensure continued compliance with
Clearpay's standards and regulatory requirements and expectations.
5. Ongoing Monitoring Standards
5.1 Review Scheduling
Ongoing monitoring incorporates continuous transaction monitoring and annual due diligence
refreshes, which includes reviews of PSP due diligence, updated risk assessment and verification
that oversight measures remain risk appropriate
5.2 Event-Driven Review Standards
Reviews must be triggered outside regular schedules for:
● Adverse media coverage or regulatory enforcement actions
● Material business model changes or ownership changes
● Unusual transaction patterns identified through monitoring
● Regulatory developments affecting PSP operations
● Material risk escalations from internal monitoring activities

5.3 Transaction Monitoring Standards
Clearpay's Transaction Monitoring System must provide comprehensive oversight of all PSP
sub-merchant transactional activity, monitoring transaction volumes, velocity patterns, geographic
distributions, and risk indicators.
Suspicious activity identified through transaction monitoring must be reviewed by Clearpay's
Transaction Monitoring and SAR team, with material concerns escalated for review and potential
suspicious activity reporting.
5.4 Watchlist Screening Standards
PSPs must implement comprehensive sanctions screening programs encompassing all
sub-merchants and their beneficial owners incorporating sanctioned persons designated under:
● UK HM Treasury Office of Financial Sanctions Implementation OFSI lists
● European Union Consolidated List
● United Nations Security Council Consolidated List
● US Office of Foreign Assets Control OFAC lists
PSPs must establish procedures for investigating potential matches, resolving false positives, and
timely reporting of confirmed sanctions hits.
6. Operational Integration Requirements
Block MNC Collaboration: Block Merchant and Network Compliance team must execute
due diligence reviews utilising established procedures adapted for Clearpay-specific
requirements, ongoing monitoring and periodic review activities, and systematic
documentation ensuring consistency and regulatory compliance.
System Integration Requirements: Clearpay will utilise Block's enterprise systems while
ensuring UK regulatory compliance through:
● ThinkSmart System for DDQ workflow management
● Google Drive for the storage of due diligence documentation
● Blockʼs LexisNexis screening tools
● Clearpay Transaction Monitoring for PSP oversight
Request for Information RFI Standards: When additional clarification is required, RFIs
must be drafted by Block MNC, reviewed by Clearpay Compliance team, and sent via the
PSPʼs relationship manager with complete response review for adequacy.
On-site Visit Assessment Standards: Determination of necessity for on-site visits to gain
comprehensive understanding of a PSP AML/CFT/CPF and Sanctions program must be
documented and justified based on risk assessment outcomes.

7. Compliance & Enforcement
7.1 Roles and Responsibilities
Clearpay Senior Management: Accountable food ensuring that Clearpay UK develops,
establishes and maintains effective PSP oversight policies, procedures, systems and
controls in accordance with regulatory requirements and maintains appropriate compliance
culture.
Money Laundering Reporting Officer MLRO Accountable for PSP compliance program
oversight, approval of all PSP onboarding and periodic review decisions, escalation of
material compliance concerns to senior management, and ensuring alignment between
PSP compliance activities and broader regulatory obligations.
Clearpay Compliance Team: Responsible for supporting the MLRO with day-to-day
oversight of PSP compliance activities, coordination with Block MNC, policy interpretation
and guidance, exception management and approval processes, and strategic oversight
ensuring consistent application of compliance standards.
Block Merchant and Network Compliance Team Block MNC Responsible for execution
of PSP due diligence reviews, ongoing monitoring, refreshes and periodic review activities,
risk assessment, and systematic documentation of all due diligence activities in compliance
with the requirements of the Standard and relevant Block standards.
Transaction Monitoring and SAR Team: Must conduct continuous monitoring of PSP
sub-merchant transaction activity, investigation of suspicious activity alerts, and escalation
for potential suspicious activity reporting.
7.2 Monitoring and Reporting Requirements
Block MNC must provide regular reporting to the MLRO encompassing PSP portfolio risk
assessment updates, due diligence completion statuses and metrics, and material changes in PSP
risk profiles.
The MLRO must monitor compliance with this Standard on an ongoing basis.
7.3 Training Requirements
All relevant Clearpay personnel and Block staff supporting PSP due diligence execution must
receive training covering UK AML/CFT/CPF regulatory requirements applicable to PSP
relationships, due diligence requirements, suspicious activity recognition and reporting obligations,
and sanctions screening procedures.

7.4 Record Retention Requirements
All PSP due diligence documentation and monitoring records must be retained for minimum
periods of five years from relationship termination, with longer retention periods applied where
required by other regulatory obligations.
8. Documentation Standards
8.1 Documentation Quality Requirements
All PSP due diligence and monitoring activities must meet comprehensive documentation
standards:
Written Rationale Requirement: All compliance decisions must include written rationale
documenting the basis for risk assessments, approval decisions, and ongoing monitoring
determinations.
Version Control and Approval: All documentation must follow established version control
and approval workflows ensuring proper authorisation and audit trails.
8.2 Documentation Retention and Management
Documentation management must ensure regulatory compliance and audit readiness:
Structured Filing Systems: All PSP documentation must be maintained in structured filing
systems enabling efficient retrieval and regulatory examination.
Access Controls: Documentation access must be restricted to authorised personnel with
appropriate business need and security clearances.
8.3 Audit Trail Requirements
Comprehensive audit trails must be maintained for all PSP compliance activities:
Decision Documentation: Complete documentation of all compliance decisions including
supporting analysis, risk assessments, and approval processes.
Change Management: Documentation of all changes to monitoring frequencies, or
compliance status with rationale and authorisation.
Review History: Maintenance of complete review history including initial due diligence,
periodic reviews, event-driven assessments, and outcome determinations.
System Activity Logs: Automated logging of all system activities related to PSP
management including screening results, alert generation, and investigation outcomes.

9. Technology and System Standards
9.1 System Requirements Framework
The following technology and system standards must be implemented to support effective PSP
oversight:
Automated Transaction Monitoring Systems: Implementation of automated transaction
monitoring systems with configurable rules capable of detecting suspicious patterns and
generating investigative alerts.
Sanctions Screening Capabilities: Deployment of sanctions screening capabilities
enabling identification of prohibited parties during PSP initial and ongoing reviews.
Automated Monthly Rescreening Functionality: Automated monthly rescreening
functionality for PSP legal entity and UBO screening ensuring ongoing compliance with
sanctions requirements and PEP identification.
10. Exceptions
The MLRO maintains authority to approve exceptions to this Standard provided that proposed
exceptions do not violate UK regulatory requirements and alternative controls are implemented to
mitigate associated risks.
All exception requests must include comprehensive written justification, detailed risk assessment
and mitigation strategy, and proposed alternative controls. All approved exceptions require written
documentation and scheduled review dates for ongoing appropriateness assessment.
