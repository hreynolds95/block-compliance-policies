3. Introduction
This Consumer Protection Policy (“Policy”) outlines Afterpay Canada Ltd.’s (“Afterpay Canada”,
“Afterpay CA”) approach to providing a seamless and reliable "buy now, pay later" service while
prioritizing consumer protection. Afterpay Canada recognizes the significance of adhering to
Canadian consumer protection laws and regulations to ensure the rights and interests of customers
are safeguarded. This Policy serves as a comprehensive guide outlining Afterpay CA's commitments,
practices, and areas of focus to maintain compliance and protect consumer rights.
This Policy shall apply to Afterpay Canada Limited. This Policy has been established in accordance
with federal, provincial and territorial consumer protection legislation in Canada and related rules.
The Head of Compliance, Afterpay Canada is responsible for management of this Policy.

4. Purpose
The purpose of Afterpay CA's Consumer Protection Policy is to ensure a fair, transparent, and secure
environment for consumers using Afterpay services in Canada. This Policy aims to protect
consumers from unfair practices, provide clear information about their rights and obligations, and
foster trust in Afterpay CA's payment platform. It reflects Afterpay CA's commitment to ethical
business practices, compliance with Canadian laws and regulations, and dedication to delivering a
positive user experience.
5. Scope
This Policy is established by Afterpay CA and is applicable across all Afterpay CA business lines within
Canada. It outlines the standards and practices that all Afterpay employees, partners, and affiliates
within Canada are required to adhere to in order to protect consumer rights and interests.
This Policy does not apply to:
● Products that are distributed by third parties
● Bank partners and other financial institutions not directly operated by Afterpay CA.
● Subsidiaries or affiliates operating outside of Canada who are not governed by this Policy
and required to adhere to their respective local regulations and policies.
6. Policy Requirements
5.1 Deceptive Acts and Practices
An act or practice is deceptive where there is a representation, omission or practice that is likely to
mislead consumers who are acting reasonably in the circumstances presented and the
representation, omission, or practice is material. Deceptive acts include false statements, misleading
claims, bait and switch situations or omitting material information that impacts product choice.

5.2 Unfair Acts or Practices
An act or practice is unfair where it causes or is likely to cause substantial injury to consumers which
cannot be reasonably avoided and is not outweighed by countervailing benefits to consumers or to
competition. The injury may involve a small amount of monetary harm to a large number of people
or a significant monetary injury for a select few. Unfair acts may occur when material information is
withheld regarding a product or service such that the consumer cannot make an informed choice or
when the consumer is coerced into an unwanted product or service.
5.3 Abusive Acts or Practices
An act or practice is abusive if it materially interferes with the ability of a consumer to understand a
term or condition of a product or service or takes unreasonable advantage of:
● A lack of understanding on the part of the consumer of the material risks, costs or
conditions of the product or service;
● The inability of the consumer to protect the interests of the consumer in selecting or
using the financial product or service; or
● The reasonable reliance by the consumer on a covered person to act in the interests of the
consumer. The term “covered person” means any person that engages in offering or
providing a consumer financial product or service.
5.4 Marketing
Afterpay Canada’s Legal and Compliance Departments will review and approve all marketing and
other consumer-facing materials prior to their usage to ensure:
● The message is complete and accurate;
● The material could not mislead the average customer of the target market; and
● The overall impression of the ad/webpage is accurate, including:
○ The general message is big enough to attract attention
○ Wording and formatting is understandable
○ All information is in a location where consumers can be expected to look.

5.5 New Product/ Feature Review
Afterpay Canada’s Legal and Compliance Department will review and approve all new products and
features to ensure fairness and transparency to the customer including:
● Considering whether the product and/or feature is (or could be) unfair, deceptive or
abusive
● Ensuring that fees charged are not exorbitant.
5.6 Ensuring Transparent Disclosure
Afterpay Canada’s Legal and Compliance Department will also review and approve all prospective
terms and conditions as well as fee disclosures, including revisions or updates to any previously
approved wording, to ensure fairness and transparency to the customer including:
Clear Terms and Conditions
Afterpay Canada ensures that its terms and conditions are easily accessible, written in plain
language, and prominently displayed during transactions. This includes comprehensive
details regarding payment schedules, fees, penalties, and cancellation policies to maintain
transparency.
Full Disclosure of Fees
Prior to any transaction, Afterpay Canada explicitly outlines all associated fees, including but
not limited to late payment charges or penalties, ensuring customers are well-informed.
5.7 Privacy and Data Security
Afterpay Canada complies with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act
(PIPEDA) regulations, implementing robust measures to protect customer data against
unauthorized access, breaches, or misuse. Afterpay Canada strictly adheres to regulations governing
the collection, use, and protection of personal information.

Afterpay Canada obtains explicit consent before collecting or using any personal information,
providing customers with control over their data and ensuring compliance with privacy legislation.
5.8 Comprehensive Customer Communication
Afterpay Canada shall ensure regular updates to customers regarding payment schedules,
outstanding balances, and any changes to terms and conditions. Additionally, Afterpay CA must
ensure that customer support is readily available to address any queries or concerns regarding
payments or account details.
5.9 Internal Compliance Checks
Afterpay Canada shall conduct regular audits and reviews of policies and procedures to ensure
alignment with consumer protection laws. Furthermore, Afterpay CA and Block conduct employee
training to educate staff on compliance requirements and ethical conduct.
5.10 Transparent Fee Structures
Afterpay Canada maintains a detailed breakdown of fees, penalties, and payment schedules, which
are displayed throughout the transaction process and within customer accounts, ensuring clarity
and transparency.

8. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Policy Owner and/or their designee will monitor Policy related activities to
evaluate adherence to the Policy and determine whether outputs adhere to established objectives.
As outlined in the Compliance Management System (“CMS Policy”), Compliance will provide regular
updates to the Block, Inc. Board (“Board”) and executive management. This reporting may include
specific information related to policies, programs, procedures, audit/exam findings, issues
management, training, and complaints.
9. Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy. Policy exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that Afterpay CA will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team at go/policyhelp. The Policy Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
