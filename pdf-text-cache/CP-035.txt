1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Square AU Pty Ltd (“Square AUˮ) is an Australian Financial Services
License (“AFSLˮ) holder License Number: 513929. This AFSL Representatives Standard
(“Standardˮ) outlines Square AUʼs approach to oversight and training of Representatives. The CCO
Square AU is responsible for management of this Standard.
This Standard outlines the requirements and procedures that Square AU has established to ensure
the effective oversight and training of its representatives under its AFSL in the provision of its
licensed financial services product). Square AU is committed to upholding regulatory standards
and maintaining compliance and competency in line with our regulatory obligations. .
Square AU acknowledges that its representatives play a critical role in delivering compliant and
responsible financial services. This Standard aims to ensure that all representatives are adequately
trained, monitored and supervised, enabling them to meet their obligations under the Corporations
Act 2001, the conditions of the AFSL, and ASICʼs regulatory standards.
Scope: This Standard is established by Square AU and is applicable to Employees of Square AU
and sets out the approach to the monitoring, supervision and training of its representatives.

2. Standard Requirements
2.1 Square AUʼs Responsibilities towards Representatives
Square AU is responsible for ensuring that all Representatives are competent, adequately trained,
and properly supervised to perform their roles. This includes ensuring Representatives:
● Act efficiently, honestly and fairly in accordance with s912A1a of the Corporations Act;
● Comply with all relevant financial services laws, ASIC Regulatory Guides, and internal
policies and procedures; and

● Maintain the skills, knowledge, and competence necessary to discharge their
responsibilities effectively.
Square AU must establish and maintain a structured training and competency program that
ensures Representatives receive initial and ongoing training appropriate to their role and the
complexity of the financial services provided. This includes, where relevant, training on topics
such as advice and disclosure obligations, financial crimes, data and information security,
anti-bribery and corruption, conflicts of interest, complaints handling, and breach reporting.
Ongoing oversight and monitoring will be conducted through supervision, quality assurance
reviews, and periodic performance reviews to ensure that Representatives continue to meet the
required standards. Any performance issues, breaches, or concerns relating to a Representativeʼs
conduct or competence will be investigated and addressed promptly in accordance with Block Inc.
global Code of Conduct and, if a regulatory breach is identified, Square AUʼs Breach Reporting
Policy.
2.2 Representativesʼ Roles in Providing a Licensed Financial Product
Representatives play a critical role in upholding our AFSL obligations when interacting with
customers, providing information or advice about financial products, and facilitating product
access. Every representative must understand the scope of their authority, the limits of the advice
they can provide, and the disclosure requirements that apply to each interaction.
In particular, representatives must:
● Act efficiently, honestly and fairly when dealing with clients.
● Ensure that any financial service provided is within the scope of the AFSL and their
individual authorisation.
● Comply with requirements relating to advice delivery (including general and personal
advice distinctions).
● Provide customers with timely and accurate disclosure documents, such as Product
Disclosure Statements PDS, when required.
● Escalate or refer any matter that exceeds their authorisation or training to an appropriately
qualified person.
2.2.1 General vs Personal Product Advice
Under the Corporations Act 2001 Cth, financial product advice may be either general advice or
personal advice.
● General advice is financial product advice that does not take into account a customerʼs
personal objectives, financial situation, or needs. It may include factual information,
opinions, or recommendations about a financial product, but it must be accompanied by a
General Advice Warning whenever it is provided to a retail client. This warning makes it
clear that the advice is general in nature and that the customer should consider whether it
is appropriate for them before acting on it.

● Personal advice is tailored advice that does take into account one or more of the
customerʼs personal circumstances (objectives, financial situation, or needs) or would
reasonably be expected to do so. Personal advice cannot be provided under Square AUʼs
AFSL.
Square AU ensures that all Representatives dealing with the provision of licensed financial services
understand the distinction between factual information, general advice, and personal advice to
prevent inadvertent provision of personal advice without proper authorisation. Where general
advice is given, the required general advice warning must be communicated at the time. When
giving general advice, the Representative must inform the customer that:
● The advice has been prepared without taking into account the customerʼs objectives,
financial situation or needs; and
● The customer should consider whether the advice is appropriate for them and read the
relevant disclosure documents (such as a PDS.
2.2.2 Provision of Product Disclosure Statement PDS
A Product Disclosure Statement PDS for our licensed financial products is made publicly available
on the Square AU website, is agreed to by Squareʼs customers, and can be accessed by customers
at any time. Representatives may, where appropriate, direct customers to the online PDS or provide
a copy upon request.
Square AU ensures that the PDS is readily accessible so customers can review key information
about our financial products before making a decision.
2.3 Training of Representatives
Square AU is committed to ensuring that all Representatives are competent and adequately trained
to perform their roles in compliance with the AFSL and applicable financial services laws. During
onboarding, all new Representatives must complete mandatory induction and training covering
Square AUʼs AFSL authorisations and obligations under the Corporations Act 2001. The onboarding
program also includes training on key compliance policies and procedures such as conduct,
conflicts of interest, complaints handling, breach reporting, and customer advice.
Representatives are instructed on how to provide general advice and factual information
appropriately, with a clear understanding of the distinction between general and personal advice
when discussing any licensed financial products with customers or prospective customers.
Completion of onboarding training is mandatory prior to engaging in any regulated activity.
Representatives also receive training on regulated products or services at onboarding and prior to
the launch of any new products.
2.3.1 Continuous Professional Development CPD
Square AU maintains a formal annual training and professional development program to ensure
that Representativesʼ knowledge and competence remain current. Ongoing training includes

annual refresher courses on AFSL obligations and conduct, in addition to other annual compliance
training modules. Targeted training is also delivered following any changes to internal policies or
procedures, or where risks, incidents, or thematic reviews identify the need for additional
capability uplift. Completion of required training is monitored by the Block Inc Compliance Policy,
Mandatory Training and Support Team, with progress and results tracked and non-completion
escalated to management and Human Resources where necessary.
2.4 Oversight of Representatives
Square AU ensures effective and proportionate supervision of all Representatives to maintain
compliance with AFSL obligations and to ensure fair outcomes for customers when dealing with
our licensed financial product. Oversight activities include ongoing quality assurance reviews of
customer interactions and advice, and other activities relating to licensed products, ensuring that
they meet both internal policy standards and external regulatory requirements.
The Responsible Managers and Compliance function monitors Representativesʼ interactions and
performance to confirm they are acting in accordance with their authorisation and obligations
under the AFSL. Where risk indicators, complaints, or breaches are identified, additional targeted
monitoring is conducted. The results of all monitoring activities are documented, and remediation
or retraining is implemented promptly where deficiencies are found.
2.5 Conduct and Breach Management
All Representatives are required to uphold the highest standards of integrity, professionalism and
compliance when acting on behalf of Square AU.
All Square AU employees are provided training on the Block go/ethics process for reporting
potential breaches for review. The Square AU Compliance Notion page has a link to the Breach
Management page, which includes a link to the form to lodge an incident for review. All Square AU
Employees have direct access to contact the Compliance Officer via email and Slack (a third-party
secure intercompany communications software), and have been trained to report any breaches or
incidents.
Any instance of actual or suspected misconduct, non-compliance, or breach of AFSL obligations
by a Representative will be promptly reviewed by the Compliance team and escalated to Legal
Counsel where appropriate. Where a breach is determined to be a reportable situation, it will be
escalated to the Responsible Managers and senior management and reported to ASIC within the
prescribed timeframes under section 912D of the Corporations Act 2001.
Corrective and preventive actions, including additional training, procedural changes or disciplinary
measures, will be implemented as appropriate to address the root cause and mitigate the risk of
recurrence.

2.6 Complaints relating to Representatives
Complaints relating to the conduct, advice or service provided by Representatives are managed in
accordance with ASIC Regulatory Guide 271 Internal Dispute Resolution and the Square AU
Complaints Policy. All complaints are acknowledged and assessed within prescribed timeframes,
with a fair, thorough, and objective investigation undertaken to determine an appropriate
resolution. Where a complaint is substantiated, Square AU will provide suitable redress or
remediation to the customer and implement corrective actions to address any underlying process,
control, or training deficiencies.
Complaints data and outcomes are regularly analysed by the Complaints Management Team in
collaboration with Compliance to identify emerging conduct risks, recurring issues, or systemic
trends. Insights gained through complaints handling are incorporated into continuous improvement
initiatives, including Representative training programs and quality assurance reviews. Complaints
metrics and thematic analysis are reported to the Square AU Board on a quarterly basis, with
additional monthly reporting to senior management to support proactive oversight and
accountability.
2.7 Governance
Square AU maintains a robust governance framework to ensure effective monitoring, supervision,
and training of its Representatives in accordance with the obligations of our AFSL. The Board and
senior management are responsible for ensuring that Representatives act efficiently, honestly and
fairly in the provision of financial services, and that appropriate systems and controls are in place
to support compliance with legislative and licence conditions.
Responsibility for day-to-day oversight of Representatives rests with the Compliance and Risk
functions, in collaboration with business leaders. These teams ensure that Representatives are
appropriately authorised, competent, and adequately supervised when providing financial services
on behalf of Square AU. Ongoing monitoring activities, including quality assurance reviews, call
monitoring, transaction testing, and periodic compliance assessments, are undertaken to evaluate
adherence to regulatory and internal policy requirements.
2.8 Regulatory Program Monitoring, Testing and Reporting
Square AU maintains a structured and risk-based approach to monitoring and reporting on its
regulatory programs and in accordance with the compliance Policy. Oversight is coordinated by
the Compliance and Risk functions, with input from stakeholder teams.
We focus on three key activities:
1. Monitoring: Monitoring activities are conducted on a risk-based schedule to confirm that
key compliance controls are operating effectively. These activities may include but are not
limited to quality assurance reviews, dashboards, data reviews, reporting reviews,
exception reporting and analysis of complaints or incidents. Higher-risk areas are subject

to more frequent and detailed monitoring to ensure timely detection and remediation of
issues.
2. Testing: Independent and Internal testing and thematic reviews are undertaken through
Compliance, Risk, or Internal Audit to verify that controls are working as intended. Results
are documented, issues are tracked and remediation actions are followed through until
closed.
3. Reporting: Square AU provides risk based reporting on the performance and effectiveness
of its compliance programs to ensure transparency, accountability, and informed
decision-making. Reports highlight program health, key risks, control effectiveness and
remediation progress, giving senior leaders and committees clear visibility of compliance
outcomes and areas requiring attention. This ensures consistent oversight and supports a
strong culture of governance across the business.
3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1 Roles and Responsibilities:
● Board of Directors of Square AU Pty Ltd (“Squate AUˮ):
○ Oversight and Accountability:
■ The Board is responsible for ensuring the overall compliance of Square
AU's operations with the financial services laws, including the adequacy of
monitoring, supervision, and training programs.
○ Reporting Structure:
■ Establish and maintain a reporting structure that requires all supervisors and
the Compliance Officer to report relevant matters directly to the Board when
significant or regulatory issues arise.
● Responsible Managers:
○ Implementation of Compliance Standards:
■ Oversee the day-to-day compliance with ASIC RG 104 Licensing: Meeting
the General obligations, ensuring that representatives comply with financial
services laws.
○ Training and Supervision:
■ Ensure that representatives providing financial services are trained
according to requirements and continually monitored for adherence to
competency standards.
○ Incident Management:
■ Address non-compliance events promptly, including reporting and
remediation as per the compliance manual.
● Chief Compliance Officer Square AU (“CCO Square AUˮ):
○ Monitoring and Review:

■ Design and execute supervision mechanisms, including periodic reviews,
spot checks, and self-checking processes to ensure representatives meet
RG 104 standards.
○ Training Coordination:
■ Develop training plans in accordance with RG 146 for representatives who
provide financial services, recording all relevant training details in the
Training Register.
○ Non-Compliance and Complaints:
■ Handle and document non-compliance incidents and complaints, ensuring
they are resolved in accordance with the compliance manual and escalate
issues to the Board as needed.
● Representatives Employees, Directors, and Other Authorised Individuals):
○ Adherence to Compliance Procedures:
■ Follow established procedures for providing financial services, ensuring
compliance with ASIC guidelines, particularly in providing general financial
product advice.
○ Training Participation:
■ Complete all required training sessions and maintain updated knowledge of
compliance standards. Retain records of all training completed and provide
access to the Compliance Officer upon request.
○ Reporting and Documentation:
■ Document and report any compliance concerns or breaches to the
Compliance Officer promptly.
● Supervisors:
○ Direct Oversight:
■ Conduct regular checks on the work of representatives under their
management, ensuring compliance with financial services law and internal
policies.
○ Progress Reporting:
■ Provide periodic reports to the Compliance Officer and escalate issues
where further training or disciplinary action may be necessary.

3.4 Exceptions
The Standard Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership
and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
