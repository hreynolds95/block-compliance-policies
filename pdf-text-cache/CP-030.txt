1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The purpose of this Vulnerable Customer Standard (“Standardˮ) is for
Clearpay Finance Limited (“Clearpayˮ) to establish a comprehensive framework for engaging with
vulnerable customers when providing its regulated products and services. Clearpayʼs aim is to
ensure that every customer who has a characteristic or characteristics of vulnerability is treated
fairly, and, where possible, in accordance with their needs. This requirement is designed to ensure
that vulnerable consumers experience outcomes equal to those of other consumers, and receive
consistently good outcomes.
This Standard is Clearpay's enterprise vulnerability standard and establishes the primary
framework for identifying, categorising, and supporting vulnerable customers across all areas of
the business.
Whilst this Standard sets out Clearpay's comprehensive approach to vulnerability, Clearpay
recognises that vulnerability considerations are relevant throughout the organisation. Accordingly,
other Clearpay policies, standards and procedures will reference and incorporate considerations
relating to customer vulnerability where appropriate to their scope and purpose.
Examples of areas where vulnerability considerations are embedded include, but are not limited to:
● Product Design and Development – ensuring products are designed with vulnerable
customers' needs in mind
● Marketing and Financial Promotions – ensuring communications are fair, clear, and not
exploitative
● Credit Decisioning and Affordability Assessments – considering vulnerability factors in
lending decisions
● Customer Communications – ensuring all communications are accessible and easy to
understand
● Collections and Arrears Management – tailoring approaches to vulnerable customers'
circumstances

● Complaints Handling – providing enhanced support to vulnerable customers during the
complaints process
● Data Protection and Privacy – ensuring sensitive personal information is handled
appropriately
● Third Party Management – ensuring outsourced service providers understand and comply
with vulnerability requirements
● Staff Training and Competence – ensuring all relevant staff are equipped to identify and
support vulnerable customers
All Clearpay Employees are responsible for considering vulnerability in their day-to-day work and
ensuring that customers are treated fairly, regardless of whether they have been formally identified
as vulnerable. This enterprise-wide approach ensures that vulnerability is not treated as an
isolated concern but is embedded within Clearpay's culture and business practices.
Scope: This Standard applies to Clearpay, all its employees, outsourced service providers,
consultants, contractors, suppliers and agents (“Clearpay Employeesˮ). It is the duty of all
Clearpay Employees to be familiar with this Standard and any operational routines concerning
vulnerable or potential vulnerable customers which are relevant to their work.

2. Clearpay Requirements
2.1 Regulatory Framework
Clearpay is authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (“FCAˮ) to enter into
regulated consumer credit agreements with customers (“consumersˮ or “customersˮ). In its
Finalised Guidance on the Fair Treatment of Vulnerable Customers (“FG21/1ˮ), the FCA provided
guidance on how authorised firms should treat vulnerable customers, the latest version of which is
referred to throughout this Standard. Authorised firms are required to pay attention to the fair
treatment of vulnerable customers across all products and services. In this Standard, Clearpay
demonstrates how its business model, the actions it takes, and its culture ensures the fair
treatment of customers.
The FCAʼs approach to regulation is outcomes-focused and based on the Principles for Business
(“Principlesˮ), with Principle 12Consumer Duty (the delivery of good outcomes for retail
customers) underpinning the need for Clearpay to take particular care in the treatment of
vulnerable customers. In the context of Clearpayʼs regulated consumer credit agreements,
borrowers will be retail customers.
Clearpay will also comply with its obligations under the Equality Act 2010, particularly in its
approach to vulnerable customers.
2.2 Identifying Vulnerable Customers
The FCAʼs definition of a Vulnerable Customer is:
“...someone who, due to their personal circumstances, is especially susceptible to detriment,
particularly when a firm is not acting with appropriate levels of care.ˮ
In order for the needs of vulnerable customers to be correctly addressed, it is important for
Clearpay to be able to identify them.
The FCA acknowledges that vulnerability is a broad concept and is “such a subjective term that it
is hard to define.ˮ Furthermore, when looking at consumerʼs use of credit products, vulnerability
can manifest itself in a number of ways. Clearpay will need to consider the increased risk of
consumer harm that may be present for vulnerable customers, and should consider the four
objectives of the FCAʼs Consumer Duty when considering areas where foreseeable harm may be
present for vulnerable customers. The below is not an exhaustive list:
● Products and Services:
○ Customers may choose a product that is not suitable for their needs;
○ Clearpay could provide a product to a customer that is not suitable for their needs, if they
have not adequately considered its target market or built vulnerability concerns into its
design;
● Price and Fair Value:
○ Customers may choose a high cost product as a result of poorly informed decisions;
3

○ Customers may incur charges due to being poorly informed of how a product works;
● Consumer understanding:
○ Customers may choose a product that is not suitable for their needs due to lack of
understanding;
○ Customers may not fulfil their repayment obligations due to lack of understanding;
● Consumer support:
○ Customers may not be able to access the services offered by Clearpay designed to
support customers with specific needs if they are not designed with accessibility
considerations;
2.3 Examples of Characteristics of Vulnerability
The FCAʼs FG21/1 guidance requires Clearpay to think about vulnerability on a spectrum of risk.
Clearpay acknowledges that all customers are at risk of becoming vulnerable and this risk is
increased by characteristics of vulnerability related to four key drivers, as set out below.
Health – health conditions or illnesses that affect ability to carry out day-to-day tasks, such as:
● Physical disability
● Severe or long-term illness
● Hearing or visual impairment
● Mental health condition or disability
● Addiction
● Low mental capacity or cognitive disability
Life events, such as:
● Retirement
● Bereavement
● Income shock
● Relationship breakdown
● Domestic abuse (including economic control)
● Caring responsibilities
● Other circumstances that affect peopleʼs experience of financial services eg. leaving care,
migration or seeking asylum, human trafficking or modern slavery, convictions
Resilience – low ability to withstand financial or emotional shocks, such as:
● Inadequate (outgoings exceed income) or erratic income
● Over-indebtedness
● Low savings
● Low emotional resilience
Capability – low knowledge of financial matters or low confidence in managing money (financial
capability), such as:
● Low knowledge or confidence managing finances
4

● Poor Literacy or numeracy skills
● Poor English language skills
● Poor digital or technology skills
● Learning difficulties
● No or low access to help or support
Clearpay recognises that it is a personʼs situation and specific barrier(s) to accessing services that
may make them vulnerable, regardless of whether the customer lives with a disability, illness or
diagnosis. Vulnerability can be a fluid state and customers may transition in and out of vulnerability
during the term of their relationship with Clearpay.
Impact of a change in circumstances: A change in personal circumstances can cause a customer
to fall into a vulnerable situation - all the more so if the customer already has one or more risk
characteristics.
People are particularly at risk in their interaction with financial services when they experience a
change in circumstances that often leads to financial shock.
Examples of a change of circumstances can include:
● A change of job leading to different working patterns, making communication more difficult;
● A change in relationship status;
● An unwell child, relative or dependent;
● A change in physical health leading to reduced working hours;
● Bereavement of a loved one
● Moving house; or
● A change in mental health, making managing finances more difficult
Impact of a change of income: A financial shock could be an unexpected large expense or loss of
income. A reduction in income, or income volatility, can cause particular problems due to the
ongoing commitment presented by many financial products. A reduction in the ability to meet
these financial commitments can cause both immediate and longer-term problems.
Examples of a change of income vulnerabilities include:
● Zero hours contracts (leading to fluctuating income);
● Redundancy;
● Retirement (leading to a drop in income);
● Statutory sick pay or maternity leave (where not paid in full).
With all of this in mind, Clearpay has identified four core risks to customers who have
characteristics of vulnerability that could cause harm if it does not act with the appropriate levels
of care. These are:
● Customers who are unable to use Clearpay in the intended way (including where the design of
our products mean they are not accessible for customers with certain vulnerability
characteristics)
5

● Customers who have issues in communicating with us (including our communications with
them and their understanding)
● Customers who are at risk of not repaying or difficulty managing wider finances
● Customers who are at greater risk of becoming victims of financial crime
Clearpay will consider these core risks when assessing aspects of customer vulnerability across
the business, in order to assess impacts to its target market.
2.4 Consumer Duty
Under the Consumer Duty, Clearpay is required to comply with cross-cutting rules and the four
outcomes when identifying potential characteristics of vulnerability within its target market and in
dealing with vulnerable customers. In order to do this, Clearpay will do the following:
Tailored Products and Services: Accessibility: Clearpay will include accessibility considerations
when designing products and services. It will assess and ensure customers with characteristics of
vulnerability can easily access and use its product, considering factors such as physical
disabilities, cognitive impairments, and language barriers. This may involve providing alternative
formats, simplified language, or assistive technologies to accommodate different needs.
Clear and Transparent Communication Plain Language): To enhance customer comprehension
and reduce confusion, Clearpay will utilise clear and simple language in all customer facing
communications. Clearpay will avoid complex jargon or acronyms that may pose greater
challenges for vulnerable customers. By adopting plain language, Clearpay aims to ensure that
information is easily understandable, enabling informed decision-making.
Communication Channels: Clearpay will establish appropriate communication channels to
accommodate the preferences and needs of vulnerable customers. This may include telephone
helplines, online chat support, and email correspondence. By offering diverse channels, Clearpay
aims to make it easier for vulnerable customers to reach out for assistance and engage with
Clearpay.
Assessment of characteristics of vulnerability within Clearpayʼs target market: Clearpay has
carried out an assessment of characteristics of vulnerability against its target market. This
assessment will be reviewed periodically and will be considered during product development.
2.5 Managing Vulnerability
This section sets out a process-based summary of Clearpayʼs approach towards Vulnerable
Customers and includes some examples of the practices that Clearpay will undertake to ensure
good outcomes.
2.5.1 Advertising, Marketing, and Customer Communications
Clearpay will take precautions in its customer communications to ensure that consumers, including
vulnerable customers, are provided with clear and fair information about the Clearpay product and
6

are not misled or improperly incentivised to enter into credit agreements with Clearpay. Clearpay
will always comply with financial promotion rules in CONC 3, wider consumer protection legislation
and the Advertising Standards Authority Committee of Advertising Practice Codes (the Cap and
BCAP Codes).
For example, Clearpayʼs marketing will never be targeted nor suggest that the Clearpay product is
appropriate for those on the very lowest incomes, and/or those who may be vulnerable due to their
age or experience of credit products.
For more information on Clearpayʼs marketing approach, please refer to the UK Marketing &
Financial Promotions Policy and Customer Communications Standard .
2.5.2 Product Design
The design of Clearpayʼs products and services aims to take into account the needs of vulnerable
customers in its target market and customer base.
2.5.3 During the Life of the Account
We may engage with customers pre-contractually (before they make a purchase), however most of
Clearpayʼs interactions with vulnerable customers are most likely to be during the life cycle of their
account and often when there is a problem, for example if they are unable to make a payment.
As acknowledged above, vulnerability may be temporary or may be more permanent. Clearpay will
encourage customers to communicate with Clearpay whenever their circumstances change.
2.5.4 Collections & Hardship
Clearpayʼs Collections & Hardship processes are sensitive to the fact that consumers that are in
arrears may be vulnerable, however it acknowledges that these two factors are not mutually
exclusive.
Clearpay will treat vulnerable customers fairly and make adjustments to their circumstances.
Clearpay will consider the following options to deliver good outcomes for vulnerable customers in
collections & hardship:
● Providing the customer with sufficient time and space to consider their position and seek
free and impartial debt advice (and provide directions as to how they can seek such
advice)
● Undertaking a review of whether the credit was/is still suitable for the customerʼs needs
● Providing appropriate forbearance to ensure the customer is treated fairly, including:
○ Freezing any charges (e.g. late fees)
○ Writing off charges
○ Agreeing a repayment plan with the customer or their nominated third party
representative
○ Extended space or payment holiday periods
○ Writing off a proportion or total balance of their account
7

○ In the case of significant failing by Clearpay, consider whether redress is required
2.5.5 Medical Information
In certain circumstances, Clearpay may need to obtain medical information (e.g. GP letters, Debt
and Mental Health Evidence Form etc.). It is important to note that medical information should not
be requested to ‘proveʼ the customer has a medical condition/vulnerability. Medical information
should be requested to enable Clearpay to have a better understanding of the customer's situation
to enable it to make an informed decision with regards to next steps for the customer.
Medical information received by Clearpay will be treated as sensitive personal data in accordance
with Blockʼs Data Classification & Handling Policy.
2.5.6 Dealing with Third Parties
For a variety of reasons, a customer may request that a third-party act on their behalf, or a
third-party may be appointed on their behalf due to medical reasons. This may be due to a mental
health capacity issue or illness, or where the customer uses independent debt advice services for
a matter of convenience for the customer.
Where a customer requires Clearpay to liaise directly with a third party acting on their behalf, it
must act in line with its Third Party Authorisation processes. Clearpay will be able to accept a
request from the customer for a third party to act on their behalf via the following means:
● A signed letter of authority
● Written confirmation from the customerʼs registered email address or internal account
● Verbal confirmation from the customer over the telephone once ID&V is confirmed
In certain circumstances, where the customer lacks capacity, they may not be able to provide the
necessary authority. In these instances, Clearpay will require evidence of Power of Attorney that
gives named individuals authority to act on the customerʼs behalf. Care will be taken in these
circumstances that Clearpay does not allow this party to act outside of any restrictions placed on
them by the Power of Attorney.
Clearpay records Vulnerability on the customerʼs account if shared by an authorised third party
and ensures suitable support is provided in light of this information (see Identifying and Recording
Vulnerability section).
2.6 Identifying and Recording Vulnerability
2.6.1 Target Market
Clearpayʼs target market for its regulated lending product is broad, which can include customers
who are vulnerable.
8

2.6.2 Identifying Vulnerability
Clearpay staff will use a range of methods in order to help identify customer vulnerability. Staff are
encouraged to understand the customerʼs circumstances and use open ended questions in their
approach. Clearpay must never pass judgement on a customerʼs circumstances. The outcome
must not be pre-determined and only considered after the representative feels they have gained
enough information to discuss potential suitable outcomes.
Signs of vulnerability relevant to Clearpayʼs customer base
Customer behaviours which may indicate vulnerability are listed below, this is not an exhaustive
list, and presence of one or more of these indicators may not, in itself, demonstrate vulnerability.
Staff are reminded to be on the lookout for:
● Multiple applications for credit across a number of accounts;
● Repeat requests for the same or similar information from the customer by Clearpay;
● Requests for contact or dialogue via non-digital communication methods;
● Requests for a third party representative (e.g. relative or carer) to have access to a
customerʼs account; or
● Complaints or negative comments about aspects of Clearpayʼs products, services, or
delivery channel.
Customer-facing teams should be watchful for indicators, like those listed below, when interacting
with customers. These, or similar, phrases could indicate a customer vulnerability:
● “I canʼt read my agreementˮ
● “I canʼt understand the email/notice/letter you sent meˮ
● “My son/daughter/partner/carer deals with these things for meˮ
Staff are also reminded to be on the lookout for:
● Signs of agitation;
● Signs that the customer has not understood or signs of customer confusion;
● Mention of medication or physical difficulty; or
● The customer keeps repeating themself or repeats the same questions.
Identification of any particular key vulnerability driver does not mean that Clearpayʼs customers are
not at risk of other drivers. Clearpay must remain diligent, open-minded and flexible in light of
customersʼ circumstances.
Dealing with vulnerability
When dealing with vulnerable customers, Clearpay will use Clearpayʼs PATHways model, which
has been developed with consideration of the TEXAS and the IDEA protocols advised for use by
the FCA to help categorise vulnerability.
9

The PATHways model helps staff to manage in depth conversations with customers about
vulnerabilities and to ask key questions as well as to appropriately acknowledge and record details
of a customerʼs vulnerability, help them in identifying the best possible solution to support them
that Clearpay are able to offer, as well as to signpost them to where they may receive additional
support.
Clearpay will record details of disclosed vulnerability on a customerʼs account in line with Blockʼs
Data Classification & Handling Policy. Itʼs important to note that, as a customerʼs circumstances
may change, and so the customer may move from a state of vulnerability to no longer being
vulnerable, Clearpay will need to take steps to remove this sensitive information from its systems
where it is no longer relevant.
2.6.3 Categorising Vulnerability
Clearpay has implemented a Vulnerability Categories framework based on the Financial Conduct
Authority's drivers of vulnerability as outlined in FG21/1 guidance. These categories enable it to
identify and appropriately support customers who may be vulnerable across key areas of their
customer journey.
Application of Vulnerability Categories
Vulnerability category flags are applied to customer accounts by our customer facing teams based
on direct customer interactions and the information customers provide to us. Flags are not
assigned through automated processes; instead, they are determined through meaningful
engagement with customers.
When assigning vulnerability category flags, the Clearpay team applies the PATHways method to
ensure consistent, fair, and appropriate categorisation. This approach allows Clearpay to tailor
treatment in other areas of the business, such as:
● Customer Service Support – including communication preferences and accessibility
requirements
● Marketing and Promotional Content – ensuring messaging is appropriate and not
exploitative
● Collections Processes and Outsourcing – adapting debt management approaches to
individual circumstances
● Credit Decisioning – considering vulnerability factors in lending assessments
● Incident Management – providing enhanced support during service failures or complaints
2.6.4 Dynamic Nature of Vulnerability Categories
Vulnerability is not static. Customers may move in and out of vulnerability categories as their
circumstances change, and a customer may have multiple vulnerability category flags applied to
their account at any one time. Clearpay recognises this fluidity and continues to monitor vulnerable
customers through ongoing engagement to ensure it maintains adequate and up-to-date
10

information. This enables Clearpay to support customers in managing their account with Clearpay
effectively and in line with their evolving needs.
By categorising vulnerability in this way, Clearpay ensures consistent, fair, and appropriate
treatment of customers throughout their relationship with it.
2.6.5 Management Information (“MIˮ)
In line with Clearpayʼs commitment to providing good outcomes for vulnerable customers, it has
established processes to collect MI to monitor and track key indicators related to customer
vulnerability. Clearpayʼs MI framework encompasses a comprehensive set of metrics and data
points designed to provide insights into the experiences and behaviours of vulnerable customers
across various touch points and interactions with its product. This MI will be used to help tailor
support for Clearpayʼs customers and also be reviewed to assist in future product development.
2.6.6 Vulnerable Customer Monitoring and Evaluation
Clearpay has a Customer Forum dedicated to reviewing the MI collected in relation to all
customers, which includes addressing the needs and concerns of vulnerable individuals within its
customer base. Comprised of key members representing various departments and expertise within
Clearpayʼs organisation, the forum will convene on a quarterly basis to review and discuss aspects
related to vulnerable customers. It will be responsible for the following:
● Reviewing MI relating to Vulnerable Customers
● Identify emerging trends
● Evaluate the effectiveness of support measures and existing controls
● Discuss escalated cases to calibrate a business approach
● Escalate and report issues to the Board, as required.
2.6.7 Testing
First line testing
Call Monitoring: The team will regularly listen to recorded calls. They will assess whether
representatives comply with the requirements of this Standard and demonstrate empathy,
patience, and understanding towards customers' needs and concerns.
Review of Written Correspondence: This includes emails, live chat transcripts, and other written
communications. The team will examine the language, tone, and clarity of responses to ensure
they are appropriate and accessible to vulnerable customers.
Documentation Review: The quality assurance team will examine case notes and documentation
related to interactions with vulnerable customers. They will ensure that relevant information is
accurately recorded, actions taken are appropriate, and follow-up steps are documented for future
reference.
11

Feedback and Coaching: Based on their evaluations, the team will provide feedback for
representatives to support with coaching. This may involve highlighting areas for improvement,
reinforcing best practices, and offering additional training or resources to enhance their ability to
support vulnerable customers effectively.
Reporting and Analysis: The team will compile data and generate reports to track trends, identify
recurring issues, and measure performance indicators related to interactions with vulnerable
customers.
Second line testing
Clearpayʼs Compliance team will carry out second line monitoring & testing relating to vulnerable
customers to ensure adherence to this Standard and regulation. Please refer to the Compliance
Monitoring Plan for further details.

On an ongoing basis, the Standard Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance

Finance Limited Board and executive management. Reporting may cover specific information
related to:
● Policies and standards
● Procedures
12

● Audit/exam findings
● Issues management
● Training

3.3 Training
All staff involved in the manufacture and distribution of the Clearpay product will receive training
on vulnerable customers, alongside Consumer Duty. Front line staff who deal directly with
customers will receive more comprehensive training in the identification, and handling of
interactions with customers who have characteristics of vulnerability.
3.4 Record Retention
All Documents are retained according to the longer of:
● Blockʼs Data Classification & Handling Policy
● Applicable regulatory requirements
As standard practice, Clearpay will retain all documents and records associated with this Standard,
including those relating to individual customers, for a minimum of 5 years from the date that its
relationship with the customer ends. Where another Standard requires that Clearpay retain the
same or similar data for a different period of time (e.g. a regulatory requirement), this will be
documented within that relevant Policy and the Block Data Classification & Handling Policy.
3.5 Exceptions
The Standard Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to
this Standard, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws
and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request,
and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks
associated with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and
retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be
submitted to the Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform
Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
Exceptions to this document must be pre-approved by the Chief Compliance Officer or their
designee.
