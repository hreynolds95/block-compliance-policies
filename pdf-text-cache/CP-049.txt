1. Introduction
Afterpay Canada Limited (“Afterpay Canada”) is committed to providing an inclusive and compliant
experience for all its customers, including those in the Province of Quebec. The French Language
Standard (the “Standard”) outlines the importance of operating with knowledge of the French
language and the necessary steps to ensure compliance with Quebec's French Language laws.
2. Purpose
Afterpay Canada's French Language Standard is crucial for ensuring legal compliance and
enhancing customer experience in Quebec. By aligning its operations with Quebec's language laws,
Afterpay Canada can strengthen its market position and demonstrate its commitment to inclusive
and respectful business practices.
Importance of French Language Compliance
Legal Requirements in Quebec
In adherence to the Charter of the French Language (Bill 101), businesses in Quebec must recognize
French as the primary language for commercial activities. This extends to contractual agreements,
advertisements, and communications. Failure to comply with these legal requirements could lead to
severe consequences, including fines and legal actions. Ensuring consistent compliance is
paramount for Afterpay Canada to operate seamlessly in Quebec and build a positive reputation.
Consumer Expectations
Understanding and respecting the linguistic preferences of consumers in Quebec is crucial for
building trust and maintaining positive customer relationships.
3. Scope
This Standard is established by Afterpay Canada and is applicable to all Staff Members.

4.1 French Language Laws in Quebec
Charter of the French Language (Bill 101)
This legislation establishes French as the official language of Quebec and outlines specific
requirements for businesses regarding the use of French in various aspects of their operations.
Consumer Protection Act
The Consumer Protection Act enforces regulations related to consumer transactions and includes
provisions regarding language rights.
Regulations Respecting Language of Commerce and Business
These regulations provide additional guidance on the use of French in commercial activities,
including requirements for contracts and advertising.
4.2 Ensuring Compliance with French Language Laws
Language of Contracts and Agreements
Afterpay Canada understands the importance of language in legal documents. As mandated by
Quebec's legal framework, contracts must be available in French. In case of any differences between
English and French versions, the French version takes precedence. Failure to comply with this
requirement can lead to legal disputes, potentially harming Afterpay Canada's reputation and
finances.
Customer Communication
Afterpay Canada prioritizes effective communication with its customers. This includes ensuring that
emails and notifications are available in French. By default, customers have the option to receive
communications in French. For instance, Afterpay Canada enables language preferences in
customer profiles, allowing users to set their preferred language for communications.
Website and Mobile App
Afterpay Canada’s digital platforms are vital for customer interaction. It is not just a legal obligation
but also crucial for enhancing customer experience that Afterpay Canada’s website and mobile app

4.3 Providing French Language Services
French Language Customer Support
Customer support is often the frontline for consumer interaction. In Quebec, offering dedicated
French language customer support services is not just a legal obligation; it's an essential aspect of
customer satisfaction. Inadequate French language support may lead to misunderstandings,
dissatisfaction, and potential regulatory scrutiny. Therefore, the availability of proficient
French-speaking support agents and clear communication channels in French is crucial.
Marketing and Promotional Materials
All marketing and promotional materials targeting customers in Quebec must be available in
French.
Example: Create French-language versions of promotional materials, advertisements, and
campaigns tailored to the Quebec market.
4.4 Training and Awareness
Employee Training
Train employees on the importance of French language compliance and the specific requirements
outlined in Quebec's language laws.
Example: Conduct regular training sessions for customer support representatives on the use of
French in customer interactions.

4.6 Consequences of Non-Compliance
Non-compliance with Quebec's language laws can have severe consequences. Fines and penalties
are not only financial burdens but also tarnish the reputation of the business. Additionally, legal
actions resulting from non-compliance may lead to protracted disputes that can be
resource-intensive and time-consuming. It is imperative that all employees are aware of the
potential repercussions of not adhering to French language laws and that compliance is treated with
the utmost seriousness. Regular training and updates should reinforce this awareness throughout
the organization.
