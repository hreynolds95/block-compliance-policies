2. Purpose
Block developed this Standard to create a unified Complaint management framework. For the
applicable Block, Inc. entities that develop, manage products and services, and oversee
Customer-facing Complaint Intake Channels, this Standard provides guiding principles for the
developmentandmanagementofbusinesslineComplaintprograms.
The Global Complaints Policy, a separate document, establishes the high-level principles and
expectations for managing complaints across Block, ensuring alignment with company values
andprinciples.Itdefineswhatmustbeachieved,whilethisStandardoutlineshowtoachieveit.
As detailed in the “Complaint Program Framework” Section below, individual businesses and
entities to which this Standard applies are expected to create contextualized guidance
documentsbasedonthisStandard’srequirementsandprinciples.
Further, Block maintains this Standard to proactively comply with legal, regulatory, and
contractualrequirementsgoverningthehandlingofCustomerComplaints.

4.4 Complaint Classification
4.4.1 Complaint, Inquiry, and Feedback Definitions
The initial phase of the Complaint intake process consists of determining whether a
communication is a Complaint, Inquiry, or Feedback; this Standard provides enterprise-wide
Complaint, Inquiry, and Feedback definitions. Global definitions must be adopted by all
businesses subject to thisStandard,absentanapprovedExceptiontoStandard(“ETS”)requestin
accordance with the Exception to Standard process outlined herein Complaint Program
Framework.
TheStandarddefinesthetermsComplaint,Inquiry,andFeedbackasfollows:
● Complaint: An expression of dissatisfaction or grievance (oral or written) by an
individual, business entity, or someone authorized to act on their behalf, with aBlock
product, process, service, decision, vendor, or interaction regardless of the validity.
Complaints are more than just the ‘words’ but also are identified by the tone of and
intention behind the Customer outreach, as well as sentiments of a breach in the
business’s responsibilities. Complaints may also include allegations that Block
engaged in unfair or wrongful conduct. Complaints do not include Disputes of
authorized or erroneous electronic funds transfers, which are handled through a
distinctprocessgovernedbyseparatepoliciesandprocedures.
● Inquiry: A communication (oral or written) that includes general questions, service
requests, or technical issues that do not have sentiments of dissatisfaction or
grievances. Inquiries do not include Disputes of authorized or erroneous electronic
funds transfers, which are handled through a distinct process according to relevant
policiesandprocedures.
● Feedback: Compliments (oral or written) about Block orCustomerrecommendations
for improving Block product, process, or service. Overall, Customers providing
FeedbackaresatisfiedwithBlock,andsimplywanttooffercommendationorideasfor
improvement.

corresponds with the Customer’s Terms of Service agreement. However, if it is feasibleanddoes

4.14.3 Root Cause Analysis
Root Cause Analysis (“RCA”) andtrendreportingisoneofthepillarsofComplaintsmanagement,
which ultimately serves as a Complaint reduction mechanism. RCA helps to inform why
Complaints have been raised,iftheComplaintallegationsweresubstantiated,whethererrorsare
systemicorisolated,andthelevelofseverityorrisktheymaypresenttotheorganization.
Businesses must conduct a RCA with respect to Complaints where appropriate and justified by
the Complaint severity and/or volume. The RCA will be used to determine whether a justifiable
change to product, technology, policies or procedures could be reasonablyexpectedtomitigate
the occurrence of the circumstances leading to such Complaints. Where an underlying issue is
identified that causes Material harm to additional Customers, businesses must take appropriate
remedial action regardless of whether those additional Customers have made a Complaint or
not, including escalating to Issue Management for assistance in identifying all Customers
affected, documenting and resolving systemic issues. Root Cause analyses provide information
onindividualandaggregatelevels,thusbusinessesmustmonitordevelopmentsatbothlevels.
Quarterly,theGlobalComplaintProgramLeadwillreviewbusinessRCAandprovideobservations
and recommendations for improving products or services as needed. The Global Complaint
Program LeadwillalsomonitorsystemicissuesraisedwithIssueManagementuntiltheissuehas
beenremediatedandClosed.
