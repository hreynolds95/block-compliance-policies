1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act Privacy Policy (the "Policy") outlines
Block, Inc.'s enterprise-wide approach to protecting consumer privacy in accordance with the
Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act ("GLBA") and its implementing regulations. This Policy establishes
comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries' collection, use, and
sharing of consumer information while ensuring the security and confidentiality of customer data.
Scope: This Policy applies to Block, Inc. and its subsidiaries (collectively, "Block") in the United
States, governing all activities involving the collection, use, and sharing of nonpublic personal
information from consumers and customers. It establishes requirements for protecting customer
privacy across all products and services subject to GLBA requirements. The only exception is
Square Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated
by the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 Policy Statement
The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act has four key requirements for electronic signatures to be recognized
as valid under U.S. law: 1 the consumer's intent to sign and be bound to the transaction
electronically, 2 the consumer's receipt of required disclosures and affirmative and ongoing
consent to conduct business electronically in a manner that reasonably demonstrates the
consumer can access information in the electronic form used by the parties, 3 association of the
signature with the signer's identity, and 4 record retention. By conforming with these
requirements Block is permitted to provide consumers with information and certain notices
electronically.
2.2 Consumer Notification
Prior to obtaining the consumer's consent to electronic records and disclosures, Block must clearly
and conspicuously inform the consumer of several key elements. This includes an explanation of
whether the consent applies only to a particular transaction that triggered the disclosure or to
specific categories of records that may be provided electronically or made available as part of the
consumer relationship, such as disclosures, consents, policies, change-in-terms, and adverse
action notices.
The notification must address any right or option to receive disclosures in paper form, including
the process for requesting a paper copy and whether any fee will be charged. Additionally, the
notification must include a statement of the right to withdraw consent, the process to withdraw
such consent, and the consequences of doing so. The consumer must be informed of the
2

procedures to update their electronic contact information and the minimum hardware/software
requirements needed to access and retain electronic records.
2.3 Hardware and Software Requirements
Prior to transmitting records electronically, Block must provide consumers with a disclosure
statement of the hardware and software requirements needed for access to and retention of
electronic records. The disclosure must ensure that consumers have the technical means to
receive records and disclosures electronically.
2.4 Changes to Hardware or Software Requirements
When changes occur to hardware or software requirements needed to access or retain electronic
records, and these changes create a material risk that the consumer may not be able to access or
retain subsequent electronic records, Block must notify consumers of such changes. Additionally,
consent must be reaffirmed when there is a change to the hardware and/or software requirements.
The notice must include the revised hardware and/or software requirements for access to and
retention of electronic records, and a statement that the consumer may withdraw consent without
the imposition of any fees or new conditions.
2.5 Consumer Consent Process
Consumer consent must be obtained in a manner that demonstrates the ability to access
information in the electronic form that will be used. The consumer must affirmatively consent to
receive electronic disclosures, and this consent must reasonably demonstrate their ability to
access information in the form that will be used to provide the disclosures.
2.6 Withdrawal of Consumer
Consumers may withdraw their consent to receive electronic records and communications at any
time. Withdrawal can be made using methods established in the disclosures or other supported
methods. Upon withdrawal of consent, appropriate alternative methods of communication must be
established.
The withdrawal of consent to receive electronic records and communications may affect service
access or features. No fee may be imposed to process the withdrawal of consent to receive
electronic records and communications. Any withdrawal of consent becomes effective after a
reasonable period for processing the request, and confirmation of the withdrawal and its effective
date must be provided in writing.
2.7 Invalid or Inoperable Contact Information
When electronic contact information becomes invalid or inoperable, appropriate procedures must
be implemented to handle such situations. This may include treating the provision of invalid
contact information or subsequent malfunction of previously valid contact information as a
withdrawal of consent for electronic communications to that specific contact method.

4.8 State Law Considerations
While ESign provides the federal framework for electronic signatures and records, we recognize
that state laws may impose additional or more stringent requirements. In jurisdictions where state
regulations overlap with ESign requirements, we apply the more conservative approach to ensure
comprehensive compliance. This includes adherence to state-specific versions of the Uniform
Electronic Transactions Act UETA and any state-specific consumer protection provisions.
When conflicts arise between applicable laws and these requirements, we consult with Legal
Counsel to resolve such conflicts and adjust our practices accordingly. This ensures we maintain
compliance while providing consistent service across our operating jurisdictions.
3. Brand-Specific Requirements
Block will comply with GLBA requirements, which may be detailed further in related Standards or
Procedures. This section outlines only the distinct implementations and unique requirements
specific to each subsidiary's lending products.
3.1 Cash App:
Cash App's implementation of GLBA requirements reflects its mobile-first approach to financial
services. During the consumer onboarding process, Cash App requires electronic consent to
various disclosures, including the Cash App ESign Consent, except for circumstances that allow
disclosures to be provided without GLBA compliance.
The consumer's electronic consent at onboarding must demonstrate the ability to access
information provided by Cash App electronically. A consumer cannot proceed in the application
process without consenting to receive information and documents electronically. Reading the
electronic consent form on the Cash App website or application and acknowledging the ability to
receive and download documents electronically constitutes sufficient confirmation of consent.
For withdrawal of consent, Cash App may treat a consumer's provision of an invalid e-mail address
or phone number, or the subsequent malfunction of a previously valid e-mail address or phone
number, as a withdrawal of consent to receive electronic records and communications via the
inoperative contact method.
3.3 Afterpay:
Afterpay maintains specific requirements for buy-now-pay-later transactions that address the
unique aspects of installment payment relationships. Prior to obtaining consumer consent to
electronic disclosures, Afterpay provides clear and conspicuous information about whether the
consent applies to a particular transaction or to specific categories of records available as part of
the customer relationship.
4

For merchant integration, Afterpay requires specific technical standards for capturing consumer
consent during purchase. This includes implementation requirements for checkout flow integration
and enhanced record-keeping requirements for installment payment agreements.
When hardware and/or software requirements to receive electronic disclosures change, Afterpay
provides notice to customers describing the new requirements. This notice contains a statement
that the consumer has the right to revoke their consent without incurring any fees or being subject
to any new conditions not previously disclosed.
The above brand-specific requirements supplement the general compliance GLBA requirements
outlined in this Policy but are only applicable to their respective brands (i.e., Cash App
requirements apply only to Cash App operations and Afterpay requirements apply only to Afterpay
operations).

4.5 Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
