1. Introduction
The Anti-Money Laundering & Countering the Financing ofTerrorismPolicy(“Policy”)outlinesBlock,
Inc.’s and its subsidiaries (“Block” or the “Company”) commitment to compliance with anti-money
laundering (“AML”) and countering the financing of terrorism (“CFT”) laws and regulations (“Local
Laws”) in all jurisdictions in which they operate, and to preventthemisuseofservicesandanyother
related involvement in criminal or unlawful activity. Money laundering and terrorist financing are
serious financial crimes with potentially serious social andfinancialeffects.TheU.S.governmenthas
passed laws that impose affirmative obligations upon covered entities in order to prevent, detect,
andreporttheseactivities.
2.Purpose
This Policy is designed in compliance with applicable Local Laws and establishes Block’s
commitment to a culture of AML/CFT compliance. Because the business activity of each Block
subsidiary and jurisdiction varies, the application of individual AML/CFT compliance requirements
may also vary. This Policy establishes a framework for satisfying relevant AML/CFT compliance
requirements and is intended to be supplemented byadditionalpolicies,programs,andprocedures
where necessary, including but not limited to the Global Sanctions Compliance Policy. Compliance
with Local Laws is to be achieved through the implementation of arisk-basedcomplianceprogram
thatisapprovedbytherelevantgoverningboardofdirectors,whererequired.
3. Scope
This Policy is established by Block and is applicable to Covered Products globally across all Block
businesslines,subsidiaries,andjurisdictions.
In the U.S., in-scope Local Laws include the Bank Secrecy Act, as amended by Title III of the USA
PATRIOTAct(collectively,the“BSA”).AdditionalLocalLawsinclude,butarenotlimitedto:

● BermudaProceedsofCrimeAct1990
● CanadaProceedsofCrime(MoneyLaundering)andTerroristFinancingAct

4.Policy Requirements
4.1 Anti-Money Laundering Program
In accordance with the BSA, U.S. financial institutions are relied upon to assist U.S. government
agenciesindetectingandpreventingMoneyLaunderingandTerroristFinancing.TheBSAmandates
the implementation of written risk-based AML programs, recordkeeping, and reporting
requirements that are reasonably designed to ensure compliance with all applicable BSA
requirements.
The BSA defines financial institutions as inclusive of a list of covered businessactivities;additionally,
the U.S. DepartmentoftheTreasury,throughtheFinancialCrimesEnforcementNetwork(“FinCEN”),
imposesAMLprogramrequirementsspecifictoparticularfinancialinstitutions.
4.1.1 Designation of BSA/AML Compliance Officer
The Board designates the BSA/AML Compliance Officer, who is responsible for oversight of
day-to-day AML compliance with this Policy and Block’s AML/CFT program. The Board has
designatedtheGlobalCCOinthisrole.

In order to manage program operations and mitigate organizational risk, routine reporting is to be

4.1.3 Internal Policies, Procedures & Controls
Block maintains internal policies, procedures, and controls to support ongoingcompliancewiththe
AML/CFT program. These include, amongst other things, requirements to conduct AML/CFT risk
assessments, customer and employee due diligence, and maintaining relevant systems and
procedures to monitor transactions for unusual behavior and report suspicious activity to law
enforcement agencies in the countries where Block is required to under Local Laws. The AML/CFT
program includes additional minimum requirements in respect to key controls such as quality
assurance, line two independent monitoring and testing, record keeping requirements as well as
controlstopreventTippingOff.
4.1.4 AML/CFT Risk Assessments
Block is responsible for conducting AML/CFT risk assessments in order to identify potential money
laundering and terrorist financing risks and to inform the design and priorities of the AML/CFT
complianceprogram.

4.1.5 Training & Awareness
In accordance with Local Laws, Block is responsible for the implementation of an AML/CFT training
program. The Compliance Training Program Standard (“Training Standard”) & Annual Calendar is
risk-based and designed to provide relevant and targeted training. Block uses a number of
mechanisms to deliver training, including online, virtual,andface-to-facemechanisms. AllAML/CFT
training is documented with relevant training records maintainedinaccordancewithBlock’srecord
keepingrequirements.
Upon hire, Staff Members must complete AML/CFT training within areasonableperiodoftheirstart
date and each calendar year thereafter. Training is to be provided via an online Learning
Management System (LMS). The Training Standard also outlines a communication and escalation
processfornon-completionofAML/CFTtrainingwhichmayvarybyjurisdiction.

4.1.6 Independent Testing & Review
In accordance with Local Laws, Block’s Internal Audit team is responsible for conducting
independent, periodic risk-based assessments on the overall adequacy and effectiveness of the
AML/CFT compliance program as well ascompliancewithapplicableLocalLaws.Writtenreportsare
to summarize audit findings and recommend corrective actions as needed. The Board places
accountability on Senior Management to address any results from the audit of the AML/CFT
program in an appropriate manner and to report on the status of any remediation efforts on a
regularbasis.
4.1.7 Know Your Customer Program
The level of KYC performed will be proportionate to the risksidentifiedandminimumrequirements
under Local Laws. KYC may consist of CIP, CDD, and EDD, such as additional collection and
verification requirements based on the legal entity structure of the customer, including beneficial
owners, directors, and other related parties. Complex entities such as multi-layered entities and
trusts may require special due diligence measures. Where a customer isidentifiedashigherrisk,an
EDDprocessgenerallywillbeapplied.
It is Block’s policy to conduct customer risk ratings at the timeofonboardingaswellasperiodically.
In accordance with Local Laws, using a risk-based approach, Block also maintains systems and
processestokeepcustomerinformationuptodate(“KYCRefresh”).

7. Exceptions
The Document Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to
this Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate Local Laws. Policy
exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and provide a
description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with the
exception. Any approved exceptions must be retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any
approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the Policy Governance Team at
go/policyhelp. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership and
applicablecommittees,asneeded,ofexceptions.
