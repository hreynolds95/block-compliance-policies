1. Introduction
This Complaints Standard (“Standard”) outlines the overall strategy and approach that Cash App, a line of
business under Block, Inc.(“Block”),takestotheintake,investigationandresolutionofComplaints.TheCash
AppConsumerProtectionLeadisresponsibleformanagementofthisStandard.
2.Purpose
Cash App conducts business in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. The purpose of this
Standard is to further Cash App’s framework for the intake, investigation and resolution of Complaints in a
fair and consistent manner. Following the expectations of Block’s Global Complaint Program Standard, the
maintenance of this Standard proactively complies with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
governingComplaintmanagement.
3.Scope
This Standard is established for Cash App, a line of business under Block,andisapplicabletomanagement
of Complaints alleged against Cash App received both internally and externally. ExclusionstothisStandard
include Complaints received for Cash App’s investment services; Cash App’s lending products andservices;
Cash App Taxes product or services; initial service Disputes, including chargebacks or allegations of
unauthorized transactions; and Complaints received concerning Block, Inc. and its other subsidiaries. Cash
App has protocols and procedures designed to ensure the detection and appropriate resolution of
Complaintsfortheseexclusions.
4.Standard Requirements
This Standard provides the strategy and approach for Complaint management at Cash App which aligns to
the Block GlobalComplaintProgramStandardandBlockGlobalComplaintPolicy.ThisStandardoutlinesthe
governance,intake,andComplaintclassificationandcategorizationforCashApprelatedComplaints.

Cash App receives Consumer Complaints from various sources and in various forms with respect to its
services and ensures that Consumersaretreatedfairly,respectfully,andconsistently.ConsumerComplaints

4.4 Complaint Trend Reporting
Complaint trend reporting is an important process to understand the health of Cash App’s business, the
strength of its Compliance program, and to identify opportunities to better serve Consumers and maintain
compliance with applicable law. Cash App has documentation that outlines the reporting processes and
analysis conducted on Complaint data, which is provided tostakeholderswithinCashAppinadherencewith
theGlobalComplaintProgramStandardkeymetricsandrequirements.
● The Complaint Insights function supports themonthlyreportingfortheCashAppU.S.Complaint
Program. This reporting covers key metrics such as received and closed volumes, SLA
adherence,andnotabletrendsinConsumerComplaints.
● The monthly reports allow for analysis of emerging and existing trends; outline incoming and
closed Complaint volumes; detail key risk metrics and performance indicators; and serve as a
foundationforrootcauseanalysis.
● Standardized reporting processes provide for consistent analysis and transparent data
presentationtoenhancestrategydevelopmentandhighlightpotentialprocessimprovements.
● The scope of this reporting includes all Complaint intake channels, product types, and locales
within the jurisdiction of this Standard. The Cash App business may separate this monthly
reporting into multiple reports for a given business, product type, or market, based onaudience
and risk appetite. These reports are intended to provide information to the Global Complaint
Program and stakeholders within Cash App, but may be used for a broader audience as
necessary.
4.5 Root Cause Analysis
The Complaint Insights Team conducts analysis on trends, identifying pain points that drive Consumer
Complaints. By sampling Complaints based on severity and risk, they uncover potential gaps in Cash App’s
products, services, or support processes. These gaps are escalated to a cross-functional group for
discussion and prioritization. Material failures aredocumentedandremediatedpertheCustomerOperations
IssueManagementProgram.

8.Training
All relevant Workforce will receive training at leastannuallycoveringapplicablerequirementsandguidance
toimplementthisStandardeffectively.
Comprehensive training ensures compliant and consistentComplaintmanagementinordertobestservethe

All Complaint facing and Complaint-adjacent workforce under Customer Operations are required to take

identifying and escalating Complaints. New members oftheworkforcearerequiredtocompletethetraining
within 30 days of their hire date, or annually for existing workforce. Customer Operations workforce must
meet minimum scoring requirements for all training. Training records are maintained in CashApp’slearning
management tool Customer Operations Grow and monitored by Customer Operations and applicable
businesses.
