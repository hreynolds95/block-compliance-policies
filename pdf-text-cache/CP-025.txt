1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Cash App is committed to the establishment of a comprehensive
Anti-Fraud program for its peer-to-peer product. An integral part of that program relates to Cash
App ľs efforts to remove a form of fraudulently-induced transactions, also known as Scams, from
Cash App's platform.
The purpose of this ÓāÉFiatÓāĄ P2P Scams Policy ("Policy") is to establish and communicate to all
employees, Cash App's holistic approach to preventing, deterring, detecting, and reporting Scams,
and the circumstances under which Cash App will reimburse its customers for money lost as a
result of a Scam. This document defines what a "Scam" is, details how Cash App categorizes
Scams, and outlines the responsibilities of employees to protect Cash App customers from being
victimized by Scams.
Scope: This Policy is established by Cash App US. References to employees throughout this
document shall refer to all full-time employees and fixed-term and contingent workers including
interns, temps, consultants, vendors, third-party providers, and contractors.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1 Awareness and Education
2.1.1 Employee Training
Cash App maintains a schedule of Annual BSA/AML and OFAC training, which sets the expectation
that all employees are responsible for understanding and doing their part to prevent financial
crimes. It is an annual requirement to complete this training. The specific training courses subject
to this requirement are documented in the Block Inc. Compliance Training Program & Annual Plan.
Additional training may be delivered to employees as emerging Scams are identified.
2.1.2 Customer Education
Cash App acknowledges the risk of Scams associated with the fiat P2P product. For that reason, it
uses several channels to proactively educate its customers to prevent them from becoming victims
of scams. Cash App educates customers on the most common types of Scams on Cash App, how
to avoid becoming the victim of a scam, and how to keep their funds safe. Cash App uses the
customer support section of its website, a customer scams education page, and social media to
educate consumers about scams and how to prevent them. Cash App will regularly update these
materials to ensure they are current and meaningful.
2.2 Scam Prevention
2.2.1 Warnings
Cash App provides customer warnings in an effort to prevent senders of payments from falling
victims to Scams. Cash App utilizes machine learning models to trigger clear and concise scam
warnings when attributes of the payment that the customer is attempting to send reflect a higher
probability the payment could be related to a scam. The warning reminds the customer to only
send payments to individuals they know and trust. The warning also reminds customers that
payments are sent immediately and may not be able to be reversed. This warning informs the
customer that they have the option to cancel the payment prior to sending if they do feel like they
5

may be a victim of a scam. The warning inserts an intentional pause in the payment flow in case
the customer has doubts about the transaction they are able to cancel. Also, these warnings are
triggered by machine learning models and not displayed on every transaction to prevent
unconscious click bias by the customer. This allows for the warnings to remain effective at
preventing scams.
2.2.2 Real-Time Transaction Blocking
In addition to the customer warnings described in this Policy, fiat P2P transactions are reviewed by
machine learning models using data sources such as previous transactions, sender and recipient
identities, device characteristics, and linked instruments, to identify transactions that appear likely
to be associated with a scam. In certain instances, these machine learning models will take an
automated action to block or prevent the completion of the transaction, returning the funds to the
sender. A message will be provided to the sending customer indicating the payment could not be
completed. These machine learning models may also suspend the recipient ľs account in certain
instances where it appears the recipient is likely a scam perpetrator.
2.3 Reporting Scams to Cash App
Cash App encourages its customers to inform Cash App when they believe they have fallen victim
to a scam. When customers report scams ("Scam Reports"), it allows Cash App to help stop
additional victims from falling victim to the same scammer or the latest scams. Scam Reports are
used to update the machine learning models designed to warn customers and prevent the
completion of transactions that are potentially scam related. In addition, this reporting helps Cash
App educate customers on emerging scam trends and take action on confirmed scam
perpetrators.
2.3.1 In-App Reporting
A customer can report a potential scam by using Cash App's in-app flow. To access the in-app
scam reporting flow, a customer may go to the Activity feed, select a transaction, select the three
dots at the upper left hand corner, and tap "Report an Issue." Cash Support can be reached the
same way or by tapping the profile icon and scrolling down to Support.
2.3.2 Phone
Customers can also report a potential scam using Cash App's customer service support which can
be connected at 1 ÓāĀ800Óāā 969Óāą1940.
2.4 Investigations
Cash App will perform a systematic review of all reports of potential scams received by Cash App
through an automated machine learning model. This machine learning model will be designed to
triage the potential scam reports and queue those determined to be the highest probability of
involving actual scams for manual review by one of Cash App's Scam Specialists. In cases where
the machine learning model produces a determination with high confidence, as demonstrated by
validated precision and recall metrics, appropriate mitigating actions may be taken based solely on
the model's output without manual review. The manual review process followed is designed to
6

identify signals within the transaction history and profile information which confirms the customer
is a victim of a scam and appropriate mitigating actions will be completed.
2.4.1 Investigative Process
The manual review is a comprehensive investigation that analyzes the transaction history of the
customer who reported the scam, and the accounts that have received payments from the
customer reporting the potential scam for any unusual activity. The investigation will take into
consideration the current identity verification ("IDV") status of the sender and recipient, the
approximate length of time the account has been active, and if there are signals of the account
being inauthentic. Additional account criteria used to consider if usual or activity in common with
scams may be but not limited to is an evaluation of the linked payment sources and if any
mismatches to names, or geographical regions exist, review of recent transaction history for
mismatches in geographical regions, transactions experiencing friction like blocked payments,
revoked payments, refund requests, scam warnings, and credit card chargebacks. Other activities
which could be indicative of scam behavior may also include:

Each scam report which is queued for manual review will be investigated thoroughly and in a timely
manner using the above data elements but not limited to only those to determine if the payment
recipients are confirmed perpetrators of scams. Scam reports actioned by the machine learning
model without manual review will be subject to ongoing monitoring and periodic validation to
ensure the model continues to meet established confidence thresholds. Those recipients which are
confirmed scam perpetrators will be denylisted.
2.5 Reimbursement
In an effort to increase customer trust, Cash App is committed to educating, warning, and taking
actions to prevent Cash App consumers from losing money from scams. In alignment with this
commitment, Cash App will reimburse a customer up to a maximum of $250 USD who has fallen
victim to Imposter or Deception Scams excluding Goods and Services scams for fiat peer-to-peer
payments when:
‚óŹ Cash App's internal investigation determines that the payment was likely associated with
an Imposter or Deception scam; and
7

‚óŹ Cash App is unable to successfully refund the customer's funds directly from the scammer
account; and
‚óŹ The customer reports the scam payment to Cash App within 60 days of the payment
appearing on their statement; and
‚óŹ Cash App failed to warn the customer at or before the time of the payment; and
‚óŹ Cash App did not block the payment; and
‚óŹ The Cash App investigation did not identify terms of service violations by the customer;
and
‚óŹ The Customer has not already received reimbursement for the transaction in question
through other means like chargebacks and/or disputes processing; and
‚óŹ This is the customer's first confirmed scam payment report
As part of the reimbursement process to the sender, Cash App may attempt to clawback the funds
associated with the transaction which was reported as a scam from the recipient. In the
communication to the customer indicating Cash App's decision to reimburse the customer will also
receive scam educational materials to prevent future revictimization within the Cash App platform
as well as other financial services the customer may utilize.
In the event of an Imposter Scam, Cash App should evaluate the display name used by the
recipient and based on that evaluation, determine if any word(s) should be added to the existing
display name controls.
2.6 Denylisting
Cash App may Denylist the SSN or close accounts that appear likely to be involved in the
perpetration of scam related activities. Cash App will provide notice to the customer that their
account is linked to a Terms of Service violation and has been closed. The customer will generally
still be able to withdraw funds in their account but will not be able to perform any other
transactions (e.g., P2P payments or depositing funds).
Cash App may also Denylist, as appropriate, other unique account assets such as social security
numbers, payment instruments, bank accounts, phone numbers, and/or email addresses that
appear likely to be involved in the perpetration of scam related activities, or identified abuse of the
reimbursement policy by falsely reporting scams to seek unwarranted reimbursements.

2.7 Suspicious Activity Report ("SAR") Referrals
All Cash App accounts identified as perpetrating scam activity during the course of a Scam
Investigation will be referred to Compliance for review to determine whether a suspicious activity
report should be filed. Scam investigators will refer to Compliance any case of confirmed scam
activity that involves $2000 USD or more of transactional activity by a single perpetrator (whether
acting through one or multiple accounts) calculated by Total Received GPV.
