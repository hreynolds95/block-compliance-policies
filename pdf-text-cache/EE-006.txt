1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Block, Inc. and its subsidiaries (collectively, “Blockˮ) are committed to
conducting business with honesty and integrity. Block expects all of its Employees, officers,
directors, non-employees, representatives, and any other person working on Blockʼs behalf
(collectively, “Staffˮ) to act ethically and in accordance with the Blockʼs Global Code of Business
Conduct and Ethics (“the Codeˮ) or Supplier Code of Business Conduct and Ethics (“Supplier
Codeˮ) depending on the nature of their engagement with Block, as well as applicable policies and
procedures. If you suspect or observe any conduct by Block and/or its representatives that is
inconsistent with the Code, Block policies, or the law you may report this by using the reporting
channels described below. By doing so, you give us the opportunity to learn about the issue and
take appropriate action.
To Speak Up, Visit www.ethics.block.xyz or Call 18004619330.
If outside the United States, local contact numbers are available in the appendix of this policy.
This Global Speak Up Policy (“Policyˮ) sets out how Staff can speak up and express their concerns
without fear of retaliation.
Scope: This Policy is established by Block and is applicable globally across all Block businesses,
subsidiaries, and jurisdictions. This Policy applies to Staff and to other individuals who raise
concerns to Block relating to the above, including but not limited to Block Employees. This Policy
provides globally-applicable general guidance on reporting concerns and related processes. This
Policy may be supplemented by region- and/or entity-specific policies and procedures. No part of
this Policy should be interpreted as contravening or superseding any other legal or regulatory
requirements imposed upon Block. If there is any conflict between the Policy and applicable laws
or regulations, the more restrictive requirements should apply.

2. Policy Requirements
Block is committed to high standards of business conduct. In line with this commitment, Block
encourages Staff (including, but not limited to, employees, non-employees, vendors, and others that
do business with Block) to raise good faith concerns about Blockʼs and/or its representativesʼ
conduct that may be inconsistent with the Code, Block policies, or the law without fear of retaliation
in any form. Individuals are not expected to substantiate a concern prior to reporting.
Nothing in this Policy, the Code, or other Block policies restricts your ability to engage in
communications or actions protected by applicable law, as described in the Companyʼs Protected
Communications policy, available upon request and contained in the Companyʼs Employee
Handbook.
2.1. Non Retaliation
It is important that Staff feel comfortable raising their concerns. Block prohibits retaliation against
individuals who raise concerns in good faith.
2

Individuals who have any concerns after making a Report can engage their Lead or manager, HRBP,
the Compliance or Legal Teams, or the investigator looking into the concern.
2.2. Reporting Concerns
2.2.1 What Should be Reported
You should report conduct that you believe violates Blockʼs Code or Blockʼs policies, or that you
believe is unethical or unlawful. This includes, but is not limited to, the below conduct:
● Fraud;
● Irregularities regarding accounting, internal accounting controls, or auditing matters;
● Insider trading;
● Corruption and bribery;
● Money laundering or terrorist financing;
● Collusion with competitors or other violation of competition laws;
● Intellectual property violations;
● Theft or misuse of Block assets;
● Harassment;
● Discrimination;
● Unsafe workplace;
● Retaliation;
● Deliberately concealing violations or withholding relevant information concerning a
violation of law; and
● Any other potential violations of Blockʼs Code or policies, or applicable laws or regulations.
2.2.2 Where to Report a Concern
Block provides a variety of channels for individuals to report concerns. You should feel comfortable
reporting a concern using whatever channel or resource you prefer. If you desire to report your
concern anonymously, you may use Blockʼs dedicated Ethics Line. External or contingent workers
should report concerns through the Ethics Line, their external engagement partner, or their Block

2.3. Confidentiality & Anonymity
You are encouraged to provide your identity when making a Report, as this information can be helpful
to the investigation; however, you may choose to remain anonymous by reporting concerns through
the Ethics Line. Regardless of whether you provide your information or remain anonymous, Block will
examine the Report based on its own merits and take appropriate actions, which may include an
investigation.
While Block cannot guarantee complete confidentiality of a reporting party, we treat all such
information with a high degree of discretion, consistent with Block's need to investigate the matter,
and our compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
2.4 Investigations
Concerns that are reported to Block will be timely reviewed and addressed as appropriate. Due to
considerations such as confidentiality and ensuring the integrity of investigations, we may not be able
to share with you the details of such investigations.
Subject to applicable laws, all Employees are required to cooperate with investigations and provide
complete, accurate and truthful information. Retaliating against someone who has made a Report or
participated in an investigation in Good Faith is not tolerated.
