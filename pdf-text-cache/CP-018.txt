1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Policy communicates Block, Inc.'s commitment to compliance with
both consumer and commercial fairness requirements through:
● The Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (“UDAAPˮ) Standards contained in
sections 1031 and 1036 of the Dodd-Frank Act
● The Unfair or Deceptive Acts or Practices (“UDAPˮ) provisions in Section 5 of the Federal
Trade Commission Act
● State laws prohibiting similar acts or practices in both consumer and commercial contexts
This Policy sets forth comprehensive guidelines governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries,
ensuring consistent operations while maintaining appropriate flexibility to address unique business
needs. Block's prevention of UDAAP and UDAP violations requires transparency, truthfulness,
suitability, and fairness across all transactions and dealings with both consumers and merchants.
The distinction between UDAAP and UDAP requirements reflects the different contexts in which
Block operates:
● Consumer financial products and services (subject to UDAAP under Dodd-Frank)
● Commercial and merchant services (subject to UDAP under the FTC Act)
● Products with potential dual consumer and commercial applications
This comprehensive approach ensures appropriate protections while recognizing the different
needs and sophistication levels of consumers and commercial customers.

"Block") and covers:
● Consumer Financial Products and Services UDAAP Block's consumer-focused

or Practices requirements under sections 1031 and 1036 of the Dodd-Frank Act.

1.1 Policy Statement
Block is committed to offering both consumer and commercial products in a fair and responsible
manner. For consumer financial products Cash App and Afterpay), Block complies with UDAAP
requirements under the Dodd-Frank Act. For commercial products , Block complies with UDAP
requirements under the FTC Act. Block ensures that terms and conditions are communicated
clearly and transparently to both consumers and commercial customers.
Wherever state or local regulations overlap with, and are stricter than the requirements set out in
this Policy, the more conservative or restrictive approach will be applied in those jurisdictions
provided such approach is lawful. If any applicable laws conflict with this Policy, Block will consult
with appropriate Legal Counsel to resolve the conflict and adjust Blockʼs policies and procedures
to ensure compliance.
2. Block-Wide Requirements
2.1 General Standards
2.1.1 UDAAP Standards
Block's commitment to preventing unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices is demonstrated
through comprehensive standards that apply across all entities and operations. These standards
establish the foundation for fair treatment of consumers and merchants throughout their
relationship with Block and its subsidiaries.
Unfair Acts or Practices: An act or practice is unfair where it causes or is likely to cause
substantial injury to Consumers which cannot be reasonably avoided and the injury is not
outweighed by countervailing benefits to Consumers or to competition. An act or practice that
causes a small amount of harm to a large number of people may be deemed to cause substantial
3

injury. However, an act or practice is not considered to be unfair if Consumers may reasonably
avoid injury. Consumers cannot reasonably avoid injury if the act or practice interferes with their
ability to make decisions or take action to avoid injury.
Public policy, as established by statute, regulation, or judicial decisions may be considered, with
other evidence to determine whether an act or practice is unfair and the particular facts in a case
are crucial to the determination of unfairness.
Block complies with the guidance that discrimination may be considered an unfair act or practice.
As such, Block will treat all applicants and Consumers equally, and will not discriminate during the
stages of the product lifecycle, including marketing, account opening, servicing, or collections.
Deceptive Acts or Practices:
An act or practice is deceptive where there is representation omission, or practice that misleads or
is likely to mislead Consumers; the Consumer's interpretation of the representation, omission, act
or practice is reasonable under the circumstances; and the misleading representation, omission,
act or practice is material.
A deceptive representation may be an express or implied claim or promise and it may be written or
oral. The omission of material information may be deceptive if disclosure of the omitted
information is necessary to prevent a Consumer being misled. When representations or marketing
practices are targeted to a specific audience, such as the elderly, young people, or financially
unsophisticated, the standard is based upon the effects of the act or practice on a reasonable
member of that group.
Abusive Acts or Practices:
An act or practice is abusive if it materially interferes with the ability of a Consumer to understand
a term or condition of a product or service or takes unreasonable advantage of: (i) the lack of
understanding on the part of the Consumer of the material risks, costs, or conditions in the product
or service; (ii) the inability of the Consumer to protect their interests in selecting or using the
financial product or service; or (iii) the reasonable reliance by the Consumer on a covered person
to act in the interests of the Consumer.
2.1.2 Consumer and Commercial Harm
Any unfair, deceptive, or abusive act or practice can result in harm to consumers or merchants.
Block seeks to prevent harm through clear, transparent, and accurate delivery of products and
services in accordance with applicable federal and state laws and regulations.
For consumers, harm may include:
● Actual or potential financial loss
● Non-financial impacts
● Violations of consumer protection laws
● Impacts from compliance management weaknesses

For commercial customers, harm may include:
● Business losses or operational impacts
● Disruption to merchant services
● Financial impacts on business operations
● Reputational harm
2.3 Product Design and Delivery
2.3.1 Advertising, Marketing, and Disclosure Guidelines
Block maintains comprehensive standards for marketing, advertising, and sales materials across
both consumer and commercial products. All communications must present information fairly,
clearly, and accurately, with appropriate disclosures of terms, benefits, costs, risks, and material
limitations of products or services being offered. This includes any related or optional products
and services that may be available to customers.
In developing marketing materials, Block prohibits any intentional misrepresentation of terms,
benefits, costs, risks, or material limitations, whether through direct statements or by omission.
Headlines and promotional language must be truthful and supported by clear, conspicuous
disclosures rather than relying on fine print or inconspicuous disclaimers to clarify potentially
misleading statements. All representations made in communications must have a reasonable
factual basis and be appropriately documented.
Block ensures that key terms and conditions are prominently displayed and easily understood,
enabling customers to make informed decisions about products and services. This includes clear
and timely disclosure of fees, penalties, or other charges, along with explanations of when and
why such charges may be imposed. When contract terms permit changes to the agreement, these
provisions are clearly communicated. In cases where approved account terms differ from
advertised terms or previously disclosed information, these differences are prominently
highlighted.
When using terms such as "pre-approved" or "guaranteed," Block clearly discloses any limitations,
conditions, or restrictions that may apply. Marketing materials avoid claims, representations, or
statements that could mislead the target audience about cost, value, availability, cost savings,
benefits, or terms of the product or service. Additionally, Block does not advertise services in
connection with accounts unless there is both the intent and ability to provide and monitor such
services for account holders.
For commercial products and services, Block ensures:
● Fair representation of merchant terms and conditions
● Clear disclosure of business obligations
● Transparent presentation of processing requirements
● Accurate representation of merchant benefits
● Fair presentation of qualification criteria

2.3.2 Marketing Review Process
Block implements a comprehensive marketing review process across all entities to ensure
compliance with UDAAP and UDAP requirements while supporting business objectives. All
marketing materials and customer communications undergo review by Compliance and Legal
teams prior to distribution to ensure fair representation of product features, terms, and conditions.
Documentation of marketing review and approval processes is maintained for at least 25 months
from public launch. Marketing teams engage with Product Compliance and Legal counsel during
strategy development to prevent potential UDAAP, UDAP, or fair lending concerns.
2.3.3 Credit-Related Requirements
This section applies specifically to Block's consumer lending subsidiaries Cash App and Afterpay)
and their extension of consumer credit. Credit Practices Rule
In connection with extensions of consumer credit, Cash App and Afterpay will not include any
confessions of judgment, waivers of exemptions, wage assignments, or security interests in
household goods provisions in their contracts. Additionally, these entities will provide required
notices to consumers who cosign obligations about their potential liability. Furthermore, they do
not collect late fees on payments that would otherwise be considered a full payment for the
applicable period and is paid on its due date, or within an applicable grace period, when the only
delinquency is attributable to late fees or delinquency charges assessed on earlier installments.
Preservation of Claims and Defenses
For consumer credit contracts related to the sale or lease of goods or services affecting
commerce, Cash App and Afterpay will include required FTC Holder Rule notices in their consumer
credit contracts, as detailed in Appendix A.
2.3.4 Collections and Communications Standards
Block's collection and communication practices comply with applicable federal and state
requirements, including UDAAP/UDAP standards. Detailed requirements for collections and
communications activities, including specific standards for consumer and commercial collections,
are outlined in Block's Fair Debt Collection Practices Act FDCPA Policy.
For matters not specifically addressed in the FDCPA Policy, Block ensures all collections and
communications practices align with this Policy's principles of fairness and transparency, whether
dealing with consumers or merchants.
2.4 Operations and Controls
2.4.1 Compensation and Performance Evaluations
6

When Block or any third-party vendors provide support center servicing for consumers or
merchants, which includes any servicing communications delivered via existing communication
methods, Block ensures that compensation and performance evaluations of internal employees
and performance oversight of vendor advocates do not contribute to UDAAP or UDAP risk. This
applies across both consumer financial services and merchant support operations, recognizing the
different requirements for each customer type.
Block maintains relevant metrics and controls, which may include quality assurance and case
monitoring, to ensure support operations promote fair treatment regardless of whether they serve
consumers or commercial customers. Performance evaluations and incentive structures are
designed to prevent practices that could harm either consumers or merchants while supporting
compliant operations across all business lines.
2.4.2 Servicing Guidelines
Block maintains comprehensive servicing standards that address both consumer financial services
and merchant/commercial relationships. For third parties or service providers engaged in any form
of servicing, whether consumer loan servicing or merchant support, relevant policies, procedures,
programs, and communications are reviewed by Compliance prior to and during their use to ensure
compliance with both UDAAP and UDAP requirements as applicable.
For consumer financial services, Block ensures servicing practices meet consumer protection
requirements and prevent unfair, deceptive, or abusive practices. For merchant and commercial
relationships, servicing standards focus on fair commercial practices and prevention of unfair or
deceptive acts. Block prohibits practices that could harm either consumers or merchants, or
damage Block's reputation, whether conducted by Block personnel or third-party service
providers.
2.4.3 Third-Party Risk Management Requirements
Block maintains oversight of Third-Party Service Providers who perform activities on Block's
behalf, whether serving consumers or merchants. All Third-Party Service Providers must comply
with applicable UDAAP and UDAP requirements as outlined in their service agreements and the
Block Third-Party Risk Management Policy.
Third-Party Service Providers that interact with consumers or merchants must adhere to the same
standards of fairness and transparency that apply to Block's direct operations. This includes
customer service providers, collection agencies, marketing partners, and other vendors who may
impact the customer experience. Block ensures this compliance through service agreements that
clearly establish UDAAP and UDAP requirements, ongoing monitoring of Third-Party Service
Provider activities, and regular assessment of customer complaints regarding Third-Party Service
Providers.
Block maintains ultimate responsibility for compliance with UDAAP and UDAP requirements,
regardless of whether activities are performed internally or by Third-Party Service Providers. For
detailed requirements regarding Third-Party Service Provider management, refer to the Block
Third-Party Risk Management Policy.

3. Brand-Specific Requirements
Block implements UDAAP and UDAP requirements across its subsidiaries while recognizing the
unique characteristics of each business line. Detailed procedures for implementation are
maintained in subsidiary-specific standards and procedures.
3.1 Cash App:
8

Cash App's consumer-focused financial services require comprehensive UDAAP compliance
across its mobile-first platform. Cash App's implementation emphasizes clear disclosures and
consumer protections within its digital environment, recognizing the unique challenges of
providing financial services through mobile applications.
As a provider of consumer lending products including Borrow, Pre-Purchase Financing, Retroactive
Financing, and Cash App Afterpay Online Checkout, Cash App maintains robust consumer
protection measures appropriate for digital financial services. This includes consideration of
mobile-specific disclosure requirements, digital authentication standards, and consumer protection
measures tailored to app-based financial services.
3.2. Afterpay:
Afterpay's Buy Now, Pay Later service model requires specific attention to UDAAP compliance in
the context of point-of-sale financing. Afterpay's implementation recognizes the unique
characteristics of BNPL products, including integration with merchant platforms, real-time credit
decisions, and standardized payment schedules.
Afterpay maintains appropriate standards for consumer disclosures, payment terms, and merchant
integration requirements. This includes clear communication of payment schedules, fee policies,
and consumer rights, while ensuring merchant partners understand and comply with relevant
requirements for offering BNPL services.
