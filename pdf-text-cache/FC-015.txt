1. Overview
This Policy establishes Clearpay Finance Limited's framework for Anti-Money Laundering,
Counter-Terrorist Financing, Counter-Proliferation Financing and financial sanctions compliance.
The Policy applies to all Clearpay products, Personnel, and operations, and reflects Clearpay's
commitment to compliance with UK legal and regulatory requirements for anti-money laundering,
counter-terrorist financing, and counter-proliferation financing.
Clearpay adopts a risk-based approach to managing financial crime risks, applying measures that
are proportionate to the risks identified through comprehensive risk assessments. The Board of
Directors and senior management provide governance oversight of the framework and are
responsible for ensuring its effectiveness.

4. Regulatory Framework & Legal Obligation
4.1 Applicable Legislation
The MLRs establish requirements for customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring, record-keeping,
risk assessment, and governance. These regulations set out the framework for preventing and
detecting money laundering, terrorist financing, and proliferation financing in the UK.
POCA creates criminal offences for money laundering and establishes obligations for reporting
suspicious activity to the NCA. POCA also includes tipping-off prohibitions that protect the integrity
of investigations by preventing disclosure of suspicious activity reports or related investigations.
TACT creates criminal offences for terrorist financing and establishes obligations for reporting
suspicious activity related to terrorism. The reporting and tipping-off provisions under TACT
operate in parallel to those under POCA.
The Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018 and related regulations establish financial
sanctions obligations, including prohibitions on dealing with designated persons and entities and
requirements to freeze assets and report breaches to OFSI.
Other relevant legislation includes the Criminal Finances Act 2017, which creates corporate criminal
offences for failure to prevent the facilitation of tax evasion.
4.2 Regulatory Guidance
Clearpay has regard to guidance issued by the FCA on financial crime systems and controls,
including expectations for firms' AML/CTF/CPF frameworks. The FCA provides guidance on risk
assessment, customer due diligence, ongoing monitoring, and the treatment of politically exposed
persons. Clearpay also has regard to guidance issued by the Joint Money Laundering Steering
Group JMLSG, which represents industry best practice for implementing the MLRs. JMLSG
guidance is applied where relevant and appropriate to Clearpay's business model and risk profile.
OFSI provides guidance on compliance with UK financial sanctions regimes, including guidance on
asset freezing, licensing, and reporting obligations. Clearpay monitors and applies OFSI guidance
to ensure effective sanctions compliance.
Clearpay monitors regulatory guidance and updates its AML/CTF/CPF framework to reflect
changes in regulatory expectations, emerging risks, and industry best practices.
4.3 Regulatory Status
Clearpay is an Annex 1 financial institution under the MLRs by virtue of carrying out the consumer
credit listed activity, and therefore subject to the requirements of the MLRs applicable to relevant
persons.
Clearpay's Firm Reference Number is 826341. Clearpay is supervised by the FCA for compliance
with the MLRs and is subject to the FCA's supervisory oversight and enforcement powers.

6. Governance & Accountability
6.1 Governance Framework
Clearpay operates a three lines of defence model to ensure effective governance and oversight of
the AML/CTF/CPF framework. The first line comprises business units that are responsible for
identifying and managing risks in their day-to-day operations. First line teams implement controls,
execute processes, and escalate issues in accordance with this Policy and supporting procedures.
The second line comprises the Compliance function, which provides oversight, challenge, and
guidance to the first line. The Compliance function monitors the effectiveness of controls,
identifies emerging risks, and advises on regulatory requirements and best practices. The third line
comprises Internal Audit, which provides independent assurance on the effectiveness of risk
governance, risk management, and internal controls. Internal Audit conducts independent testing
of the AML/CTF/CPF framework and reports findings to senior management and the Board.
Clear allocation of responsibilities exists across the organisation, with defined escalation pathways
and reporting lines to ensure that issues are identified, escalated, and resolved appropriately.
6.2 Board Of Directors
The Board of Directors has ultimate responsibility for AML/CTF/CPF compliance and oversight of
the effectiveness of the AML/CTF/CPF framework. The Board ensures that Clearpay maintains
adequate systems, controls, and resources to prevent and detect money laundering, terrorist
financing, and proliferation financing.
The Board approves this Policy and the Business-Wide Risk Assessment. The Board reviews and
approves material changes to the Policy and the risk assessment to ensure they remain
appropriate and effective.
The Board receives regular reports on compliance with this Policy, the effectiveness of the
AML/CTF/CPF framework, emerging risks, and issues requiring Board attention. These reports
enable the Board to exercise informed oversight and challenge management on the adequacy of
controls and resources.
The Board is responsible for ensuring that adequate resources are allocated to the AML/CTF/CPF
framework, including appropriate staffing, systems, and budget to enable effective implementation
and ongoing operation of controls.

7. Risk Assessment Framework
7.1 Business-Wide Risk Assessment
Clearpay conducts and maintains a comprehensive Business-Wide Risk Assessment that identifies
and assesses the money laundering, terrorist financing, proliferation financing, and sanctions risks
arising from Clearpay's business. The BWRA considers products, services, customers, geographic
exposure, and delivery channels to provide a holistic view of the risks Clearpay faces.
The BWRA assesses the inherent risks associated with each of Clearpay's products, including the
BNPL product and the three Merchant Services products. The assessment considers how each
product could be used or exploited for financial crime purposes and the vulnerabilities that may
exist.
The BWRA assesses geographic risks based on the locations of Customers and Merchants, the
jurisdictions in which transactions occur, and cross-border elements of Clearpay's business. The
assessment considers country risk ratings, financial sanctions regimes, and the prevalence of
financial crime in relevant jurisdictions. The BWRA assesses transaction risks based on the size,
frequency, and patterns of transactions processed through Clearpay's products. The assessment
considers typical transaction profiles and circumstances that may indicate elevated risk. The
BWRA assesses delivery channel risks associated with Clearpay's online and in-store channels.
The assessment considers the controls inherent in each channel and vulnerabilities that may exist
in how customers access and use Clearpay's products. The BWRA informs the design of policies,
controls, and procedures to ensure that Clearpay's AML/CTF/CPF framework is calibrated to the
risks identified. The BWRA is documented and kept up to date to reflect changes in Clearpay's
business, risk profile, and the external environment.
The BWRA is made available to the FCA upon request. The BWRA is reviewed at least annually and
when material changes occur to Clearpay's business, products, customers, or operating
environment. The Board approves the BWRA and material updates to ensure informed oversight of
Clearpay's risk profile.
Clearpay is supported with the design and execution of Clearpayʼs BWRA by the Block Global
Compliance Risk Standards & Assessment Team, in line with the Block Anti-Money Laundering,
Countering the Financing of Terrorism & Sanctions Annual Risk Assessment Standard.

transaction patterns, business activities, and the results of watchlist screening.
Risk ratings for Direct Merchants and PSPs are reviewed periodically to ensure they remain
accurate and appropriate. Risk ratings for all Customers are also reviewed when trigger events
occur that may affect the Customer's risk profile.
The rationale for risk ratings is documented to provide a clear record of the assessment and to
support oversight and quality assurance activities.
8. Customer Due Diligence CDD
8.1 CDD Framework
Clearpay conducts CDD when establishing a business relationship with a Customer, carrying out
occasional transactions above regulatory thresholds (not generally supported by Clearpay's
products), or when there is suspicion of ML or TF. CDD is required to identify and verify the
Customer's identity, understand the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship, and
assess the ML, TF, and PF risks presented by the Customer.
CDD comprises identifying the Customer, verifying the Customer's identity using reliable and
independent sources, understanding the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship,
and conducting ongoing monitoring of the relationship. These elements work together to provide
Clearpay with sufficient knowledge of its Customers to identify and manage financial crime risks.

CDD measures are applied on a risk-sensitive basis. Standard CDD is applied to Customers
presenting standard risk, while EDD is applied to higher-risk Customers. The level of CDD applied
is informed by the Customer risk assessment.
CDD is completed before establishing the business relationship or conducting transactions, subject
to limited exceptions. Where exceptions apply, CDD is completed as soon as practicable and the
relationship is terminated if CDD cannot be completed.
Clearpay does not rely on third parties to conduct CDD on its behalf in accordance with Regulation
39 of the MLRs.
8.2 CDD Measures for Customers
Clearpay applies CDD to all Customers, comprising Consumers, Direct Merchants, and PSPs. CDD
includes identifying and verifying Customer identity using reliable and independent sources,
understanding the purpose and intended nature of the business relationship, conducting watchlist
screening, assessing ML, TF, and PF risk, and applying appropriate due diligence measures.
For Consumers, Clearpay collects name, date of birth, address, and phone number. Verification is
conducted using electronic verification methods or document verification where electronic
verification is not successful. The sole purpose of the business relationship with Consumers is a
BNPL loan for the purchase of retail goods and services at Clearpay Merchants.
For Direct Merchants and PSPs, Clearpay identifies and verifies the entity, whether a company,
partnership, or sole trader. Clearpay collects business registration details, ownership structure,
and information about business activities. Verification is conducted using official registries,
databases, and documentation.
For Direct Merchants, the purpose of the business relationship is to enable Clearpay as a payment
option at checkout or online. Clearpay understands the nature of the business relationship by
understanding the merchant's business model, the goods and services they sell, and their
transaction profile.
For PSPs, the purpose of the business relationship is to enable Clearpay as a payment option at
checkout or online for sub-merchants. Clearpay understands the nature of the business
relationship by understanding the PSP's business model, risk management framework, and PSP
Sub-merchant onboarding processes.
Watchlist screening is conducted as part of CDD. Consumers are screened against PEP databases
and sanctions lists. Direct Merchants, PSPs, and their beneficial owners are screened against PEP
databases, sanctions lists, and adverse media sources.
Clearpay assesses ML, TF, and PF risk based on Customer risk factors, incorporating the results of
watchlist screening. Standard CDD measures are applied to standard-risk Customers. Customers
identified as higher risk, including all PEPs and PSPs, are escalated to EDD as described in Section
9.

8.3 Beneficial Ownership
Clearpay identifies and verifies the beneficial owners of corporate, partnership and trust
Customers. Beneficial ownership requirements apply to Direct Merchants and PSPs, but not to
Consumers who are natural persons. Beneficial ownership is defined as ownership or control of
25% or more of the Customer entity. Clearpay takes reasonable measures to identify beneficial
owners, including obtaining information from the Customer and consulting official registries such
as Companies House.
Clearpay verifies the identity of beneficial owners using reliable and independent sources.
Verification methods include electronic verification, document verification, and registry checks.
Clearpay documents the beneficial ownership structure of Customer entities to provide a clear
record of ownership and control. This documentation is maintained as part of the Customer's CDD
file and is kept up to date through ongoing monitoring and periodic reviews. Material changes in
beneficial ownership trigger a reassessment of the Customer's risk rating and may require updated
verification of new beneficial owners.
Where Clearpay identifies discrepancies between beneficial ownership information obtained
during CDD and information held on the Companies House register, Clearpay reports these
discrepancies to Companies House in accordance with Regulation 30A of the MLRs.
8.4 Timing Of CDD
CDD is completed before establishing the business relationship or conducting the transaction. This
ensures that Clearpay has sufficient knowledge of the Customer to assess and manage ML, TF,
and PF risks before entering into the relationship.
Limited exceptions exist where CDD may be completed during the establishment of the business
relationship if this is necessary not to interrupt the normal conduct of business and the ML and TF
risk is assessed as low. These exceptions are not applied in high-risk scenarios. Clearpay utilises
timing exceptions only with respect to Direct Merchants, and not with respect to Consumers or
PSPs. Where timing exceptions are applied, CDD is completed as soon as practicable after initial
contact.
Where CDD cannot be completed, the business relationship is not established or is terminated.
Clearpay does not conduct transactions where CDD has not been completed, subject to limited
exceptions permitted by the MLRs.
8.5 Simplified Due Diligence SDD
SDD may be applied where Clearpay has established that the Customer or transaction presents a
low risk of ML or TF. SDD involves reduced or simplified CDD measures that are proportionate to
the lower risk identified.

9.2 Politically Exposed Persons PEPs
Clearpay has appropriate risk-based procedures to determine whether a Customer or beneficial
owner is a PEP. These procedures are achieved through watchlist screening at onboarding and
periodically thereafter to identify PEPs, their family members, and their known close associates.
Enhanced measures are applied to PEPs, including senior management approval to establish or
continue the business relationship, establishing the source of wealth and source of funds at
onboarding and on an ongoing basis, and enhanced ongoing monitoring of the relationship and
transactions.
Enhanced measures are applied to PEPs based on risk. The level of enhanced measures reflects
the specific risks presented by the PEP's position, jurisdiction, and connection to Clearpay.
Family members and known close associates of PEPs are considered in the application of
enhanced measures. Clearpay assesses whether these individuals present similar risks to the PEP
and applies appropriate measures.
Enhanced measures continue for at least 12 months after the individual ceases to hold a prominent
public function. This period recognises that risks associated with PEP status may persist after the
individual leaves office.
Foreign PEPs are not permitted as Customers for the BNPL product. This restriction reflects
Clearpay's risk appetite for Consumer relationships and the challenges of effectively managing
foreign PEP risk in the BNPL context.
9.3 High-Risk Third Countries

presenting elevated financial crime risks.
Enhanced measures are proportionate to the nature and level of risk presented by the connection
to the high-risk third country. Clearpay considers the extent and nature of the Customer's
connection to the high-risk country and the rationale for that connection.
The specific EDD measures applied are tailored to the risks identified and may include additional
verification of identity, enhanced scrutiny of the business relationship and transactions, and
obtaining additional information about the source of funds or wealth.

12. Suspicious Activity Reporting
12.1 Legal Framework
POCA and TACT create legal obligations to report knowledge, suspicion, or reasonable grounds for
suspicion of ML or TF to the NCA. These reporting obligations apply to all Personnel.
Failure to report knowledge or suspicion of ML or TF when required is a criminal offence under
POCA and TACT. The offences carry penalties including imprisonment and fines.
Personnel who make reports in good faith are protected from liability for breach of confidentiality
or other restrictions on disclosure. This protection encourages reporting and ensures that
Personnel are not deterred from fulfilling their legal obligations.
12.2 Suspicious Activity Identification
Clearpay trains Personnel to recognise indicators of suspicious activity, commonly referred to as
red flags, related to ML, TF, and PF. These indicators include unusual transaction patterns,
inconsistencies in Customer information, attempts to avoid reporting thresholds, and other
circumstances that may suggest financial crime.
Personnel are required to be alert to suspicious activity in the course of their duties. Vigilance for
suspicious activity is an ongoing responsibility that applies to all Personnel in their interactions with
Customers and in their review of transactions and Customer information.
Training on recognising and reporting suspicious activity is provided to all Personnel at induction
and on an ongoing basis. Training ensures that Personnel understand what constitutes suspicious
activity and how to report it.
Clearpay encourages Personnel to report anything unusual as well as anything suspicious.
Personnel are not required to determine whether activity is definitively criminal before reporting;
the threshold for reporting is knowledge, suspicion, or reasonable grounds for suspicion.

12.5 Defence Against Money Laundering DAML
Clearpay seeks DAML consent from the NCA before proceeding with a transaction or activity that
would otherwise constitute a ML or TF offence. DAML consent provides a defence against
prosecution for ML or TF offences where the transaction is conducted with the NCA's consent.
A DAML request is required where funds in question exceed £3,000 and there is knowledge or
suspicion that proceeding with the transaction would constitute a ML or TF offence. DAML
requests are submitted as part of a SAR to the NCA.
The NCA has 7 working days to provide an initial response to a DAML request. If the NCA refuses
consent, a moratorium period of 31 calendar days applies during which Clearpay must not proceed
with the transaction. If the NCA grants consent or the moratorium expires without further action,
Clearpay may proceed.
Clearpay does not proceed with transactions requiring DAML consent until consent is granted or
the moratorium period expires. Proceeding without consent or before the moratorium expires
would constitute a criminal offence.
DAML requests and responses are documented. This documentation provides a clear record of
requests made, the NCA's responses, and the actions taken.
12.6 Tipping Off Prohibitions
Clearpay and its Personnel are prohibited from disclosing to the Customer or persons outside the
firm that a SAR has been made or that an investigation is underway. Disclosure that could prejudice
an investigation constitutes the criminal offence of tipping off under POCA and TACT.
Tipping off is a criminal offence carrying penalties including imprisonment and fines. The offence
applies to any disclosure that is likely to prejudice an investigation, whether or not the person
making the disclosure intended to tip off the subject.
Limited exceptions to tipping-off prohibitions exist for disclosures within Clearpay on a
need-to-know basis and for disclosures to legal advisers for the purpose of obtaining legal advice.
These exceptions enable Clearpay to manage SARs and investigations appropriately while
protecting the integrity of law enforcement investigations.
Personnel receive training on tipping-off prohibitions and practical scenarios to ensure they
understand what constitutes tipping off and how to avoid it. Training emphasises the importance of
maintaining confidentiality and the consequences of tipping off.

14.4 Training Effectiveness
Training effectiveness is assessed through monitoring of compliance with policies and procedures.

15. Employee Screening
Clearpay screens employees to ensure they have the skills, knowledge, and expertise to carry out
their functions effectively. Employee screening supports the integrity of Clearpay's AML/CTF/CPF
framework by ensuring that employees are fit and proper for their roles.
All employment offers are contingent upon satisfactory employee due diligence prior to starting
employment and during the course of employment. This ensures that Clearpay identifies any
issues that may affect an individual's suitability before they join the firm and throughout their
employment.
Screening includes at minimum financial sanctions, PEP, and adverse media screening,
background checks, employment verification, ID verification, and verification of their right to work
in the UK. These checks provide assurance that employees do not present financial crime or
reputational risks.
Enhanced screening is conducted for roles with access to sensitive information or systems.
Enhanced screening reflects the greater risk presented by these roles and may include additional
checks such as credit checks or enhanced background verification.
Screening results and decisions are documented. This documentation provides a clear record of
the screening conducted and the basis for employment decisions.

16.2 Independent Testing
Independent testing of the AML/CTF/CPF framework is required under the MLRs. Independent
testing provides assurance that the framework is designed and operating effectively and identifies
opportunities for improvement.
Independent testing is conducted by Block Internal Audit or external third parties. The
independence of the testing function ensures objectivity and credibility of the findings.
Testing assesses the design and operating effectiveness of policies, controls, and procedures.
Design effectiveness considers whether controls are appropriately designed to mitigate risks.
Operating effectiveness considers whether controls are functioning as intended in practice.
Testing is risk-based and covers all elements of the framework over a defined cycle. Risk-based
testing ensures that higher-risk areas receive more frequent and detailed testing, while all
elements are tested over time.
Testing results are reported to the MLRO, Executive Committee, and the Board. These reports
provide visibility of the effectiveness of the framework and enable informed oversight.
Management provides responses to testing findings and develops action plans for remediation.
Management responses demonstrate accountability for addressing findings and improving the
framework.

17. Compliance & Enforcement
17.1 Compliance Accountability & Consequences
All Personnel are accountable for compliance with this Policy. Compliance is a fundamental
responsibility of all Personnel and is essential to Clearpay's ability to prevent and detect financial
crime.
The MLRO is responsible for monitoring compliance with the Policy. On an ongoing basis, the
MLRO monitors Policy-related activities to evaluate adherence to the Policy and determine whether
outputs adhere to established objectives.
Personnel who fail, without reasonable justification, to comply with any system, control, or
procedure established under this Policy will be required to undertake refresher training and may be
subject to disciplinary action in accordance with Block's Code of Business Conduct & Ethics. All
breaches are recorded and material breaches are reported to senior management.
Personnel who report concerns in good faith are protected under whistleblowing protections.
Clearpay does not tolerate retaliation against Personnel who raise concerns about potential
compliance issues or violations of this Policy.
17.2 Investigation & Regulatory Reporting
Clearpay investigates policy breaches or compliance failures. Investigations establish the facts,
determine whether a breach has occurred, and assess the cause and severity of the breach.
Investigations are escalated to appropriate levels of management based on the nature and severity
of the potential breach. Escalation ensures that senior management is informed of material issues
and can provide oversight of the investigation and response.
