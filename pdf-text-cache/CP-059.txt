Hardship Support Standard
Square | Square AU Pty Ltd
Last Approved: Feb 9, 2026

1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: At Square AU Pty Ltd, we understand that customers may experience
financial hardship due to unforeseen events or changing circumstances. As a responsible financial
services provider, we are committed to supporting customers during difficult times with empathy,
fairness, and transparency.
Our approach is guided by legal and regulatory obligations, including the Corporations Act 2001,
Australian Consumer Law ACL and relevant ASIC guidance on hardship support, complaints
handling, and fair treatment.
We offer timely, flexible, and practical assistance tailored to individual needs, with clear and
accessible communication channels. We also recognise that financial hardship can intersect with
broader forms of vulnerability, and we are committed to providing support that is inclusive,
respectful, and responsive.
This policy outlines our commitment to helping customers in financial difficulty and ensuring they
are treated with care and dignity.
Scope: This Standard applies to all Square AU employees, contractors, and representatives who
interact with customers, including but not limited to:
● Square sellers (merchants using Square products and services)
● Individuals receiving customer service, onboarding assistance, or technical support
● Individuals lodging complaints, disputes, or requests for help
● Business owners or operators disclosing business or personal hardships
The Standard applies across all products and services offered by Square AU, including Square
Loans, payments, point-of-sale, online commerce, business software, and associated support
functions.

2. Square AUSpecific Requirements
2.1. Understanding Financial Hardship
Financial hardship refers to a situation where a customer is willing but temporarily unable to meet
their financial obligations due to unforeseen circumstances. These circumstances may include, but
are not limited to, loss of employment or reduced income, serious illness or injury, domestic or family
violence, the impact of natural disasters, a relationship breakdown, increased cost of living
pressures, or the death of a family member. Square AU acknowledges that financial hardship can be
short-term or long-term in nature and may affect individuals differently depending on their personal
and financial situation. We are committed to assessing each case with sensitivity and fairness.
2.2. Regulatory and Legal Context
As an Australian Financial Services Licence AFSL holder and payment services provider, Square
AU is subject to several key obligations:
● Corporations Act 2001 (s912A): We must act efficiently, honestly, and fairly, maintain
adequate resources, and implement compliant dispute resolution procedures. ASIC expects
licensees to proactively support customers in hardship, including tracking hardship
complaints and ensuring systems and staff are equipped to respond.
● ASIC Expectations RG 271 and Report 782 ASIC expects accessible hardship channels,
tailored responses based on individual circumstances, appropriate staff training, additional
support for vulnerable customers, and internal tracking of hardship data.

● Australian Consumer Law ACL We must avoid unconscionable or misleading conduct.
This includes treating hardship customers fairly and ensuring any representations made
about support options are accurate.
● Australian Financial Complaints Authority AFCA As a member of AFCA, we are required
to handle hardship disputes fairly, avoid delays, and document all hardship-related
communications. AFCA may award compensation if we fail to meet these obligations.
2.3. Requesting Hardship Support
Customers who are experiencing financial hardship are encouraged to contact Square AU as early
as possible to discuss their situation. Requests for hardship support can be made through our
dedicated support channels, including email, phone, or our online customer portal. At the initial point
of contact, customers will not be required to provide supporting documentation. However, in order
to assess the request accurately and to provide tailored assistance, we may reserve our right to
request reasonable evidence of the customerʼs financial situation—such as a medical certificate, a
recent payslip, or a bank statement. Our goal is to make the hardship application process as
accessible and respectful as possible, while ensuring we have adequate information to make
informed decisions.
We accept hardship support requests from customers or from their authorised representatives,
including those acting under a Power of Attorney or other recognised authority. Each request is
assessed on a case by case basis and handled appropriately internally.
2.4. Assessment and Communication
Square AU aims to acknowledge hardship requests as soon as possible. We will assess all hardship
applications promptly, fairly, and on a case-by-case basis, with the aim of providing an outcome in a
timely manner. Throughout the process, we will maintain open and transparent communication with
the customer, clearly explaining the assistance options available and any decisions made. Where a
hardship request also involves a complaint, the complaint will be resolved within the mandated
timeframe of 21 days. If a request for hardship assistance is declined, we will provide the customer
with a clear explanation of the reasons for the decision and inform them of their right to lodge a
complaint through our Internal Dispute Resolution IDR process or escalate the matter to the
Australian Financial Complaints Authority AFCA.

2.5. Available Assistance Options
Square AU offers a range of assistance options to support customers experiencing financial
difficulty. The type of assistance provided is tailored to the customerʼs individual circumstances and
may include payment deferrals, the restructuring of payment arrangements into more manageable
installments, the temporary suspension of fees and charges, fee waivers or refunds where
appropriate, or other bespoke arrangements. In some cases, we may also refer customers to
independent financial counselling or community support services. We are committed to working
collaboratively with each customer to determine the most appropriate form of support and will
ensure that all solutions are fair, reasonable, and sustainable.
2.6. Supporting Vulnerable Customers
Square AU is committed to supporting customers who may be experiencing vulnerability in addition
to financial hardship. Vulnerability may arise from a range of factors, and may be temporary or
permanent. In line with our Vulnerable Customer Standard and broader customer care principles and
procedures, we take extra care to identify indicators of vulnerability and apply appropriate protocols
to ensure these customers receive the additional support they may need. We recognise the

importance of treating vulnerable customers with heightened sensitivity, confidentiality and respect
at all times.
2.7. Monitoring and Continuous Improvement
Square AU is committed to continuously improving the way we support customers in financial
hardship. We maintain records of all hardship cases and monitor the effectiveness of the support
provided through regular internal reviews, reporting, and feedback mechanisms. This policy is
subject to annual review or more frequently in response to significant regulatory changes, internal
audit findings, or shifts in customer needs. Any updates will be approved by the appropriate
governance body to ensure ongoing alignment with legal, regulatory, and industry best practice
expectations.
2.8. Complaints and Escalation
Complaints must be handled in accordance with the Square AU Complaints Policy, which is aligned
to RG 271.
If a customer is dissatisfied with the handling or outcome of a hardship request, they are encouraged
to raise their concerns through Square AUʼs Internal Dispute Resolution process. We are committed
to resolving all Hardship complaints promptly and within the specified timeframe of 21 days for
Hardship complaints, fairly, and in accordance with our obligations. If the matter remains unresolved
or the customer is dissatisfied with the outcome, they have the right to escalate the complaint to the
Australian Financial Complaints Authority AFCA.
3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1. Roles and Responsibilities:
● Block, Inc. Roles:
○ International Customer Support Teams: Ensure customer support practices
involving Australian customers align with Australian legal and regulatory
obligations.
○ Product and Marketing Teams: Ensure product features and communications are
accessible, fair, and consider the potential impact on customers experiencing
financial hardship.
● Square AU Roles:

○ Chief Compliance Officer: Oversees implementation of the Standard and ensures
compliance with legal and regulatory obligations relating to the fair treatment of
customers in hardship.
○ Risk and Compliance Teams: Monitor adherence to this Standard, support training
and guidance initiatives, and escalate systemic risks affecting customers in
hardship.
○ Complaints Management Teams: Manage hardship-related complaints with
sensitivity and ensure appropriate resolution and escalation pathways are followed.
○ Customer Support Teams: Identify signs of financial hardship or vulnerability
during interactions and provide empathetic, tailored assistance in line with this
Standard.
○ Sales and Account Management Teams: Identify potential signs of hardship, avoid
pressure-based sales tactics, and refer customers to hardship support services as
appropriate.
○ Square Advanced Support Teams: Provide Tier 2 support and manage internal
escalations relating to complex hardship cases.
3.2. Monitoring and Reporting:
On an ongoing basis, the Standard Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance
and objective achievement. As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance
will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management.
Reporting may cover specific information related to:
● Policies and standards
● Procedures
● Audit/exam findings
● Issues management
● Training
● Complaints
3.3. Training
To ensure the consistent and effective application of this policy, all relevant Square AU staff receive
regular training on identifying signs of financial hardship and vulnerability, engaging empathetically
with affected customers, and applying the appropriate support measures. Training also covers our
legal obligations under the Australian Consumer Law and ASIC guidance, including Regulatory Guide
271 Internal Dispute Resolution). The Compliance and Customer Success teams share responsibility
for ensuring adherence to this policy, monitoring outcomes, and promoting a culture of fairness and
accountability in hardship management.

3.5. Exceptions
The Standard Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this
Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and
regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and
provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated
with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained with
recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the
Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership
and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
