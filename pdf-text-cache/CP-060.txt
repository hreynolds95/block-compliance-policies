1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Square AU is committed to treating all customers fairly, with empathy,
and in line with its legal and ethical obligations. We recognise that vulnerability or hardship can affect
a personʼs ability to access services, understand information or manage their affairs.
Our approach is guided by key regulatory frameworks, including:
● Australian Consumer Law, which prohibits unconscionable conduct and promotes fair
treatment, especially for vulnerable consumers.
● ASIC RG 271, which sets expectations for handling complaints from vulnerable and
disadvantaged individuals.
● ASIC Report 782, which highlights the importance of proactive support for small
businesses in hardship.
We also uphold federal anti-discrimination laws, including the Age, Disability, Sex, and Racial
Discrimination Acts, and the Australian Human Rights Commission Act, to ensure inclusive,
respectful service.
Vulnerability can arise beyond traditional lending contexts. Many Square sellers are small businesses
facing external pressures, and while Square AU may not cause that vulnerability, we recognise our
role in supporting these customers with flexible and compassionate solutions.
This Standard sets out how we identify and assist vulnerable customers to meet regulatory
expectations and reflect our values of fairness, accessibility, and economic empowerment.
Scope: This Standard applies to all Square AU employees, contractors, and representatives who
interact with customers, including but not limited to:
● Square sellers (merchants using Square products and services)
● Individuals receiving customer service, onboarding assistance, or technical support

● Individuals lodging complaints, disputes, or requests for help
● Business owners or operators disclosing business or personal hardship
The Standard applies across all products and services offered by Square AU, including payments,
point-of-sale, online commerce, business software, and associated support functions.

2. Square AU Specific Requirements
Square AU recognises that customer vulnerability can affect a person's ability to effectively engage
with our products, services, or support channels. In line with our obligations under Australian
Consumer Law, we are committed to avoiding conduct that is misleading, deceptive, or
unconscionable, particularly where a customer is at a disadvantage.
Additionally, under ASIC Regulatory Guide 271 RG 271, we are expected to identify and
appropriately support vulnerable customers—especially throughout the complaints process. This
Standard outlines the minimum expectations for recognising and responding to customer
vulnerability across all interactions, including in hardship, complaint handling, and everyday service
delivery, to ensure fair and inclusive outcomes.
2.1. Definition of a Vulnerable Customer
A vulnerable customer is an individual who, due to personal circumstances or characteristics, is at
increased risk of detriment, harm, or disadvantage, particularly when accessing or using financial
products or services.

Vulnerability may be temporary, recurring, or permanent, and may not always be disclosed by the
customer. Square AU recognises that vulnerability is dynamic and may arise at any point in a
personʼs life.
In Australia, while no legal definition of a vulnerable customer exists, the Australian Securities and
Investments Commission ASIC and Australian Consumer Law require financial service providers
to exercise due care in their treatment of individuals deemed vulnerable.
Vulnerability can be broken down into four key drivers: health, life events, capability and resilience.
The four drivers are intended to:
● Help firms identify common root causes of vulnerability
● Encourage dynamic, judgment-based responses rather than rigid checklists
● Guide training, policies, and frontline service deliver

2.2. Identifying Vulnerability
Square AU staff are not expected to diagnose or confirm a customerʼs vulnerability. Instead, they must
exercise sound judgment, empathy and caution when potential indicators of vulnerability are present. Staff
should remain alert to verbal, behavioural, or circumstantial cues that suggest a customer may be
experiencing hardship, distress, confusion or difficulty engaging with our services.
All frontline employees are provided with practical guidance on how to identify vulnerability and manage
sensitive situations respectfully. This includes recognising when a customer may require additional support,
alternative communication methods or referral to specialised assistance.
Where a customerʼs experience of vulnerability results in dissatisfaction, hardship, or service issues, staff
should escalate the matter internally and, where appropriate, initiate a complaint in accordance with Square
AUʼs Internal Dispute Resolution IDR process. All complaints involving vulnerable customers must be
handled with heightened care and resolved within the prescribed timeframes under ASIC RG 271 of 21
calendar days where Hardship exists.
2.3. Responding to Vulnerable Customers
When a customer is identified or reasonably suspected to be experiencing vulnerability, Square AU staff must
take proactive, respectful steps to ensure their experience is safe, supportive and fair. A resolution- and
customer-focused approach is paramount in these situations and staff should prioritise practical support. The
focus should be on understanding the customerʼs needs and removing barriers to access or service
wherever possible.
Key principles when responding to vulnerable customers:
● Clear, Compassionate Communication: Staff must communicate using plain, respectful language,
listen empathetically without assumptions and offer follow-up or clarification to ensure the
customer fully understands.
● Reasonable Accommodations: Staff must offer practical adjustments—such as interpreters,
extended time, alternative formats or support persons—tailored to the individual needs of each
customer.
● Respect for Privacy and Confidentiality: All personal information relating to a customer's
vulnerability must be handled with informed consent, strict confidentiality and in line with the
Privacy Act and APPs.
● Cultural Awareness and Sensitivity: Staff must engage customers with cultural respect, avoid
assumptions and provide flexible, culturally safe support where needed.
● Commitment to Positive Outcomes: All interactions must reflect Square AUʼs commitment to
treating customers with respect, dignity, and fairness, supporting informed decision-making,
removing access barriers, resolving issues with care and meeting all legal and regulatory
obligations.

2.4. Escalation and Internal Support
All escalations must be documented, handled sensitively, and managed in line with relevant procedures for
each team. Escalations may be necessary in situations where:
● The customer requires accommodations outside of standard processes or product settings
● The customer is at risk of harm, financial loss or emotional distress
● The matter involves family or domestic violence, financial abuse or other high-sensitivity issues
● A complaint or hardship request is raised that requires specialised handling
Staff must not delay action where there is an immediate risk of harm or distress to the customer. In such
cases, support teams should prioritise a timely and compassionate response, keeping the customer
informed throughout the process where appropriate.
Escalated cases involving vulnerable customers may also be subject to quality assurance QA reviews,
trend monitoring or root cause analysis to ensure continuous improvement in service delivery and regulatory
compliance.
2.5. Support Measures available to Vulnerable Customers
Communication Adaptations
We will adjust our communication methods to support customers with diverse needs, including:
● Using plain language and avoiding technical jargon
● Providing information in alternative formats (e.g. large print, translated materials)
● Allowing additional time to explain options or answer questions
● Engaging interpreters, support workers or culturally appropriate representatives where required
Product Adjustments
We will consider reasonable adjustments to improve accessibility and suitability for vulnerable customers.
This may include:
● Simplifying product features or disclosures
● Adjusting verification or identity processes (while maintaining security)
● Offering enhanced protections for customers at risk of vulnerability
Financial Hardship Provisions
Where vulnerability is linked to financial difficulty, we will apply our financial hardship policies in a
compassionate and flexible manner. Support is assessed on a case by case basis and may include:
● Deferring payments or restructuring obligations
● Reassessment of fee structure

2.6. Supporting Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Customers
Square AU demonstrates its commitment to Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples through inclusive,
respectful, and equitable service delivery, underpinned by comprehensive legal and regulatory compliance.
Operating within the framework of key legislation including the Racial Discrimination Act 1975, Australian
Consumer Law, Privacy Act 1988, ASIC Act 2001 and AML/CTF Act 2006, Square AU implements specific
measures to ensure fair treatment and accessibility.
These include flexible identification procedures aligned with AUSTRAC guidance, culturally appropriate
communication methods, and enhanced privacy protections. Square AU is committed to supporting
Indigenous customers in receiving culturally safe, flexible and empathetic service across all interactions,
including respecting cultural differences, use of inclusive language and provision of appropriate support
including interpreter services and community referrals where needed.

3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1. Roles and Responsibilities:
● Block, Inc. Roles:
○ International Customer Support Teams: Ensure global practices involving
Australian customers align with local regulatory standards for the treatment of
vulnerable individuals.
○ Digital Engagement Teams: Designs and delivers accessible, inclusive digital
experiences that consider the needs of vulnerable customers across all online
channels.
○ Product and Marketing Teams: Design and communicate products and offers with
accessibility and inclusion in mind, minimising complexity and potential harm to
vulnerable individuals.
● Square AU Roles:
○ Chief Compliance Officer: Oversees standard implementation and ensures
compliance with legal and regulatory obligations relating to the fair treatment of
vulnerable customers.
○ Risk and Compliance Teams: Monitor adherence to the standard, support training
and guidance, and escalate risks relating to systemic issues affecting vulnerable
customers.
○ Customer Support Teams: Identify signs of vulnerability during interactions and
provide empathetic, tailored assistance in line with this standard.
○ Complaints Management Teams: Manage complaints involving vulnerable
customers with heightened sensitivity and ensure appropriate escalation and
resolution pathways are followed.
○ Sales and Account Management Teams: Avoid high-pressure tactics, recognise
potential vulnerability, and make appropriate disclosures and referrals to support
services as required.
○ Square Advanced Support Team: Provision of Tier 2 support and handling of
internal escalations where appropriate.
3.2. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Standard Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance and
objective achievement. As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance will provide
regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management. Reporting may cover
specific information related to:
● Policies and standards
● Procedures
● Audit/exam findings
● Issues management
● Training
● Complaints

3.3. Training
All relevant staff will receive training to ensure they can effectively identify, support and escalate cases
involving vulnerable customers. This training will equip staff with the knowledge and practical tools to:
● Recognise indicators of vulnerability
● Understand the underlying causes of vulnerability
● Apply this Standard in practice, including adjusting service delivery, offering appropriate
accommodations and recording interactions sensitively and accurately
● Escalate concerns appropriately
● Engage with empathy and respect
Training is provided for all relevant employees and contractors and targeted refreshers will be issued as
regulatory guidance evolves, in response to emerging risks, systemic issues or updates to internal policy.
