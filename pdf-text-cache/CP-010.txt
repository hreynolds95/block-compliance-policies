Block, Inc.

1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Fair Credit Reporting Act Policy (the "Policy") outlines Block, Inc.'s
enterprise-wide approach to consumer credit reporting, information handling practices, and
identity theft prevention measures. The purpose of this Policy is to establish comprehensive
guidelines governing Block, Inc. and its U.S. subsidiaries' compliance with the Fair Credit Reporting
Act (“FCRAˮ) and its amendment, the Fair and Accurate Credit Transactions Act (“FACTAˮ),
ensuring consistent operations while maintaining brand-specific flexibility, where applicable.
Block's Business Product Compliance Lead, Lending & Credit, and/or their designee, is responsible
for the management of this Policy.
Scope: This Policy applies to all Credit Products offered by Block, Inc., its subsidiaries and
affiliates (collectively, "Block"), The only exception is Square Financial Services (“SFSˮ), Blockʼs
Utah state-chartered Industrial Bank federally regulated by the Federal Deposit Insurance
Corporation, which has its own Compliance Program.

2. Block, Inc. Requirements
2.1 Consumer Report Usage and Permissible Purpose
Block obtains and uses consumer reports solely for legally permissible purposes as defined under
the Fair Credit Reporting Act (“FCRAˮ). Blockʼs permissible purposes for obtaining a consumer
report are 1 to use the information in connection with a credit transaction involving the consumer
on whom the information is to be furnished and involving the extension of credit to, or review or
collection of an account of, the consumer; or 2 to review an account to determine whether the
consumer continues to meet the terms of the account. These permissible purposes primarily arise
in credit transactions where Block has received explicit authorization from the consumer, either in
writing or electronically. This includes situations where the report will support credit-related
activities such as extending new credit, reviewing existing accounts, or supporting collection
activities. Additionally, Block may obtain consumer reports when working with potential investors
or servicers who need to assess credit risks, or in connection with prescreened credit solicitations
where the consumer has provided authorization or the transaction constitutes a firm offer of credit
and the consumer has not opted out.
Block maintains appropriate controls to ensure consumer reports are obtained, used, and
maintained in accordance with FCRA requirements. This includes verification of permissible
purpose, documentation of authorization, and secure handling of consumer report information,
where applicable.
2.2 Credit Decisions and Consumer Notifications
Block follows a structured approach to using consumer reports in credit decisions that emphasizes
fairness, consistency, and regulatory compliance. Our credit decision process includes:
2

● Systematic Evaluation
○ Use of established creditworthiness criteria;
○ Documentation of decision factors;
○ Regular review of criteria;
○ Monitoring for potential disparate impact; and
○ Quality control reviews.
● Adverse Action Notifications
○ When taking adverse action based on consumer report information, Block provides
written notification within 30 days including:
■ Contact information for the consumer reporting agency;
■ Statement that the agency did not make the decision;
■ Consumer rights regarding report access and disputes;
■ Credit score information when applicable; and
■ Key factors affecting the decision.
● Risk-Based Pricing Notice
○ Block provides appropriate notices explaining:
■ How credit information affected terms offered
■ Consumer rights regarding report access
■ Steps for verifying and correcting information
2.3 Information Furnishing and Accuracy
Block maintains comprehensive requirements governing the furnishing of information to Consumer
Reporting Agencies. While furnishing practices vary by subsidiary, Block ensures that any
furnishing of information adheres to FCRA requirements and maintains appropriate controls
regardless of current furnishing status.
For subsidiaries that furnish information to Consumer Reporting Agencies, Block requires:
● Accuracy and integrity in furnished information
● Appropriate notice for negative information reporting
● Timely investigation and response to disputes
● Prevention of reporting identity theft-related information
● Proper documentation and record retention
For subsidiaries that do not currently furnish information, Block maintains baseline requirements to
ensure readiness should furnishing begin. All furnishing activities must comply with applicable
regulatory requirements and Block's information accuracy standards.
2.4 Credit Prescreening
Block conducts prescreened credit solicitations in compliance with FCRA requirements. When
engaging in prescreening activities, Block ensures:
● Firm offers of credit are honored when selection criteria are met
● Required consumer notices are provided in appropriate format
● Opt-out rights are clearly communicated and honored
3

● Prescreening criteria and selection methods are documented
● Consumer privacy and information security are maintained
For subsidiaries that conduct prescreening, Block maintains appropriate controls to ensure
compliance with FCRA requirements, including documentation, notice, and opt-out procedures.
Subsidiaries that do not currently conduct prescreening must implement required controls before
initiating such activities.
2.5 Identity Theft and Prevention and Response
Block maintains a comprehensive Identity Theft Prevention Program as required by the Red Flags
Rule under FACTA. This program forms an integral part of our commitment to protecting
consumers and preventing fraud across our suite of financial products and services. Through this
program, Block identifies, detects, and responds to patterns, practices, or specific activities that
indicate possible identity theft.
Block's approach to identifying and responding to identity theft indicators is guided by the Identity
Theft Red Flags Guidelines in Appendix A, which provide a framework for developing specific
procedures across brands.
The program's oversight rests with the Board of Directors, an appropriate committee, or a
designated senior management member which ensures adequate resources, proper
implementation, and regular assessment of the program's effectiveness. Day-to-day administration
is delegated to designated compliance personnel who develop procedures, coordinate training,
and oversee service provider arrangements.
Block's approach to identity theft prevention begins with regular risk assessments that consider
the nature of our accounts, methods of access, and evolving identity theft risks. These
assessments inform our identification of Red Flags across key categories including alerts from
consumer reporting agencies, suspicious documents or personal information, unusual account
activity, and notices from customers or law enforcement.
Blockʼs detection methods are integrated into account opening procedures, existing account
monitoring, and transaction processing across all platforms. These methods emphasize early
detection while maintaining a seamless customer experience. When potential identity theft is
detected, Block responds with measures appropriate to the level of risk, ranging from enhanced
monitoring to account closure and law enforcement notification.
The Identity Theft Prevention Program undergoes regular updates to address new threats, changes
in business operations, and technological advances. These updates incorporate lessons learned
from actual identity theft incidents, emerging industry threats, and regulatory guidance. Specific
procedures implementing this program are maintained at the brand level to address unique product
features and risk profiles.
Block requires all service providers performing activities in connection with covered accounts to
have reasonable policies and procedures to detect, prevent, and mitigate identity theft. Their
4

performance is regularly monitored to ensure alignment with Block's standards and regulatory
requirements.
2.6 Medical Information Protection
Block strictly limits the use of medical information in credit eligibility determinations as required by
FCRA. We may only use medical information that is:
● Generic in nature and descriptive of the type of medical provider, nature of services
provided, or medical status
● Used in a manner and to an extent no less favorable than similar non-medical information
● Necessary to determine a legitimate credit purpose
Block maintains specific procedures to:
● Limit collection to necessary information
● Protect confidentiality
● Restrict access to authorized personnel
● Ensure proper disposal
● Prevent inappropriate sharing
2.7 Information Sharing and Affiliate Marketing
Block maintains specific controls over sharing and use of consumer information among affiliates.
Before using eligibility information from an affiliate for marketing purposes, Block ensures:
● Clear consumer notice of potential sharing
● Reasonable opportunity to opt out
● Respect for consumer opt-out choices

A. Categories of Identity Theft Risk Indicators
The following categories represent areas where patterns, practices, or activities may indicate
potential identity theft. Each brand develops specific detection and response procedures based on
these categories while considering their unique operational environment.
● Alerts and External Notifications Identity theft risk may be indicated through various alerts
and notifications, including:
○ Fraud alerts on consumer reports
○ Notices of credit freezes
○ Warnings from identity verification services
○ Alerts from fraud detection networks
○ Notifications from other financial institutions
● Documentation and Verification Identity theft risk may be evident in documentation and
verification processes through:
○ Inconsistencies in identification information
○ Discrepancies in authentication results
○ Suspicious patterns in documentation
○ Irregularities in verification responses
○ Unusual application characteristics
● Account Patterns and Behaviors Account activities that may signal identity theft risk
include:
○ Unusual transaction patterns
○ Unexpected account access methods
○ Suspicious changes to account information
○ Irregular payment patterns
○ Unexpected account relationships
9

● External Information Sources Identity theft risk may be identified through:
○ Law enforcement information
○ Customer reports of identity theft\
○ Merchant feedback on suspicious activity
○ Industry fraud alerts
○ Public records information
C. Response Guidelines
Response measures should be appropriate to the level of risk indicated and may include:
● Monitoring Responses
○ Enhanced account monitoring
○ Increased authentication requirements
○ Transaction pattern analysis
○ Device monitoring
○ Location analysis
● Preventive Responses
○ Additional identity verification
○ Transaction limitations
○ Account access restrictions
○ Enhanced authentication requirements
○ Increased documentation requirements
● Investigative Responses
○ Account review procedures
○ Transaction analysis
○ Customer contact protocols
○ Documentation verification
○ Relationship analysis
● Corrective Responses
○ Account closure procedures
○ Credit line adjustments
○ Access restriction protocols
○ Notification requirements
○ Recovery procedures
