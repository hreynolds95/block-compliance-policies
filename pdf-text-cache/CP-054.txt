1. Introduction
This Consumer Protection Program Standard (“Standard”) outlines Cash App’s (“Cash App”), U.S. a Line of
Business under Block, Inc., approach and strategytocomplywithConsumerProtectionlawsandregulations
that are applicable to the products and services offered by Cash App. The Cash App Consumer Protection
ComplianceProgramLeadand/ortheirdesigneeisresponsibleforthisStandard.
2.Purpose
Cash App conducts business in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. This Standard is a
critical component ofCashApp’sConsumerProtectionstrategytoupholdfairandtransparentprinciplesand
practices across all aspects of the Cash App business and to proactively safeguard the interestsandrights
ofitsconsumers.
ThisStandarddefinesthestrategytoensureCashAppis:
● Incompliancewithapplicableconsumerprotectionlawsandregulations;
● Adheringtoconsumerprotectionindustrystandards;
● Implementingcontrolstoensurethatproductsandservicesareadheringtoconsumerprotection
requirements;
● Promptlyaddressingconsumerconcerns;
● Identifyingcommonconsumerprotection-relatedrisks,trends,andemergingareas,and
● Trackingandremediatingsituationsofnon-complianceorprogramdeficiencies.
3.Scope
This Standard is established by Cash App and is applicable to the Cash App U.S. business linesandrelated
productsandservices.

4.1.2RegulationE/DisputesProgram
TheRegulationE/DisputesProgramareasupportsandprovidesgovernancefortheregulatoryrequirements
of the Electronic Fund Transfer Act (“EFTA”) and Regulation E. The EFTA establishes a basic framework of
the rights, liabilities, and responsibilities of Consumers using electronic fund and remittance transfer
services and of the financial institutions or other persons, like Cash App, that offer these services.
Regulation E implements the EFTA and contains two subparts: A and B.SubpartAcontainsregulationsthat
apply to electronic fund transfers (“EFTs”), prepaid accounts, and gift cards, amongotherthings.SubpartA
covers the requirements for statements, disclosures, receipts and the requirements and timelines for error
resolution. Subpart B to Regulation E contains rules regarding remittance transfers, which provide for
prepayment disclosures, post-payment receipts, error resolution, and cancellation rights. The Electronic
Fund Transfer Act (Regulation E) Policy, and associated Procedures outline Cash App’s approach to
adherencefortherequirementsofEFTAandRegulationE,SubpartsAandB.
4.1.3ComplaintsProgram

provide valuable insight into the operations of an organization and often help identify and raise issues
involving consumer harm from UDAAP violations, discrimination, or other regulatory violations. The general

4.1.4UDAAPProgram
Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (“UDAAP”) is the standard of fairness pursuant to the
Dodd-Frank Act that protects Consumers from unfair, deceptive, or abusive actsorpracticesthatcancause
significant financial injury to Consumers, erode Consumer confidence, and undermine the financial
marketplace. Cash App is committed to offering its products through allstagesoftheproductlifecycleina
fair and responsible manner and in accordance with applicable state and federal consumer protection laws
and regulations, which includes those promulgated under state and federal UDAAP laws and regulations.
TheexpectationsandrequirementsforUDAAPcomplianceareoutlinedintheCashAppUDAAPPolicy.

CashApp’sFraudRiskManagementstrategyistoensureitupholdsadequateriskandfraudmitigationinline

administered cross-functionally and are designed to prevent and mitigate fraudulent activity, inclusive of
scams, on Cash App. Cash App’s approach to detecting, preventing, detering, and reporting individuals who
attempt to gain unauthorized access toconsumerfundsoraccountbalances,ormovemoneyusingtheCash
AppwithoutauthorityorthroughdeceptionisoutlinedintheFraudRiskManagementStandard.
4.1.6AppealsProgram
Cash App defines the framework for the Appeals process where a Consumer’s denylisted account or

to an active status.AppealsallowforthemitigationoftheriskofConsumercomplaintsandpotentialUDAAP

4.2.5Training
Training is an integral part of the Program, ensuring that applicable Staff Members are trained at least
annually on the legal and regulatory requirements related to consumer protection and how these
requirements apply to Cash App products and services. Training materials are updated as necessary to
ensure that the materials remain relevant and applicable to the Cash App business. The Program also
ensures that members of the Block Board receive annualtrainingonrequiredconsumerprotectionlawsand
regulations.
4.2.6ThirdPartyRiskManagementandExternalPartnerships
The Program is committed to excellence in supporting third-party risk management and external
relationships through collaboration with Cash App teams who manage these relationships and if required,
working directly with external parties. Cash App engages with bankpartnersandotherthirdpartiestooffer
products and servicesandmayhavecontractualobligationssetbythosepartnerswithwhichCashAppmust
comply. The Program will implement any relevant consumer protection items specified in bank partner and
thirdpartycontracts.
4.2.7ProductComplianceReviews
As part of Cash App’s process to launch new products and features, Cash App Product Managers partner
with Product Compliance for consultation and review of new or enhanced products and services. Product
Compliance will solicit the Program to review and/or document any relevant Consumer Protection
requirements. Cash App maintains a library of approved disclosuresthatrequireLegalreviewwhenupdates
are made or newdisclosuresarecreatedandCompliancereviewsincludetheassurancethattheappropriate
disclosure is used. When the Program is engaged for a review, the Program will provide these reviews in a
timelymannerandinsupportofConsumerProtectionlawsandregulations.
