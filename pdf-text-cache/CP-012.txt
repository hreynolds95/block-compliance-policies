Block, Inc.
Last Approved: Feb 19, 2026
Effective Date: Feb 19, 2026
1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: The Global Complaint Policy ("Policy") establishes a standardized
complaint management framework across Block. This framework is applicable to all Block, Inc.
(“Blockˮ) Businesses that develop and manage financial products and services across all complaint
channels and jurisdictions in which Block operates and oversees, excluding Square Financial
Services (“SFSˮ), which is governed by a separate complaint management framework due to its
chartered banking obligations. The Global Complaint Oversight Lead is responsible for
management of this Policy.
Block is committed to identifying and resolving Complaints in an effective manner. This Policy has
been established to provide guiding principles to be utilized in the development and management
of Business-level complaint programs.
A full-cycle complaint management program creates a uniform framework for the intake, handling,
and resolution of all Complaints. The Global Complaint Oversight function, within
Compliance,establishes standard practices by which all Businesses subject to this Policy must
comply.
Further, Block maintains this Policy to support compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual
requirements governing the handling of customer Complaints for in-scope Block businesses.
Scope: This Policy is established by Block and is applicable globally across all Block Business
lines, subsidiaries, and jurisdictions, excluding Square Financial Services, Inc. and its subsidiaries,
which maintain a separate complaint management framework pursuant to applicable banking
regulatory requirements. This includes all directors, officers, and team members of these Block
legal entities and any subsidiaries thereof (which for these purposes includes temporary or
contract staff and consultants performing work on behalf of these entities).

Definitions:
Terminology Definition
Complaint An expression of dissatisfaction or grievance (oral or written) by an
individual, Business entity, or someone authorized to act on their behalf,
with a Block product, process, service, decision, vendor, or interaction
regardless of validity. Complaints are more than just the 'words' but also are
identified by the tone of and intention behind the customer outreach, as
well as sentiments of a breach in the Business' responsibilities Complaints
may also include allegations that Block engaged in wrongful or unfair
conduct. Complaints do not include Disputes of unauthorized or erroneous
electronic funds transfers, which are handled through a distinct process
governed by separate policies and procedures.
Controls The mechanisms, processes, and procedures designed to minimize risk and
provide reasonable assurance regarding the effectiveness and efficiency of
operations and compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
Dispute A Customer's claim that an electronic funds transfer from their account was
unauthorized, incorrect, or not executed as intended. A Dispute is outside
the scope of the definition of Complaint.
Inquiry A communication (oral or written) that includes general questions, service
requests or technical issues that do not have sentiments of a Complaint.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1 Terms and Definitions
The Global Complaint and Inquiry definition must be adopted by all In-Scope Businesses subject to
this Policy. Businesses with additional legal, regulatory or contractual requirements not captured
within the global definitions may adopt a Business-level complaint definition inclusive of additional
requirements to which they are subject.
2.2 Severity Tier Rating
A tiered severity model is used to assess and prioritize complaints based on the inherent risk
posed by the issue and, in some cases, the source of the Complaint. This model supports
consistent Complaint handling, enables efficient resource allocation, and ensures that higher-risk
matters receive appropriate attention and escalation.
A severity tiering framework must be adopted by all In-Scope Businesses subject to this Policy. If a
Business is subject to additional legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations that go beyond what is
outlined in this Policy, it must adopt a severity model that meets those heightened requirements
while remaining aligned with the core expectations set forth by Block.
Detailed tier definitions and criteria are provided in the applicable Complaint Standards and may
consider factors such as regulatory impact, reputational harm, systemic issues, or potential
consumer harm.
2.3 Complaint Intake Channels
Block is committed to providing customers of In-Scope Businesses with multiple accessible
avenues for submitting Complaints. Complaints may be received through various intake channels,
including but not limited to direct customer communications, company-operated interfaces,
regulatory agencies, and authorized third-party service providers acting on behalf of the customer.
Intake channels are defined and operationalized in greater detail within the applicable Complaint
Standards for each brand or product line. Complaints received by an In-Scope Business are
subject to standardized identification, handling, categorization, and resolution, as outlined in this
Policy and associated Complaint Standards.
3

2.4 Regulatory Authorities, Rules, and Regulations
For In-Scope Businesses operating in jurisdictions with region-or country-specific complaint
obligations, such Businesses must comply with applicable local regulatory complaint requirements
in addition to the requirements set forth in this Policy and associated Complaint Standards.
In-Scope Businesses with contractual obligations established by bank partners must also comply
with such obligations in addition to this Policy and associated Complaint Standards. Bank partner
requirements are documented in contractual agreements and supporting governance
documentation. Block Legal and Compliance functions provide guidance on the integration of bank
partner complaint-related requirements into the Business-level complaint governance frameworks
and processes.
2.5 Complaint Trend Analysis and Root Cause Analysis
Complaint trend analysis provides insight into the types of Complaints raised, the customer
journey and experience, and whether information obtained through Complaint handling
substantiates customer allegations or identifies emerging patterns of concern. In-Scope
Businesses must conduct Complaint trend analysis, where appropriate and commensurate with
Complaint severity, volume, and risk.
In-Scope Businesses must establish feedback loops to relevant stakeholders, including Product,
Operations, Legal, Risk, and Compliance, to assess whether observed trends or volume variations
may reasonably be attributed to products, technology, policies, procedures, or customer
communications, and whether changes could mitigate the circumstances leading to such
Complaints.
Trend analysis outputs must be presented through established governance forums to enable
further evaluation, prioritization, and treatment decisions. These forums will assess potential root
causes, determine whether additional investigation is required, and define appropriate treatment
actions, which may include process enhancements, product changes, risk acceptance, or
escalation to Issue Management.
Root cause analysis may require cross-functional investigation and intervention beyond initial
trend analysis. Where an underlying issue is identified that causes material harm to additional
customers, Businesses must take appropriate remedial action regardless of whether those
customers submitted a Complaint. This includes escalation to Issue Management or incident
response processes, where appropriate, to support identification of affected customers,
documentation of systemic issues, and remediation tracking.
Global Complaint Oversight, as part of the Compliance function, establishes governance
expectations for Complaint trend reporting and systemic issue identification. Each month, Global
Complaint Oversight reviews RCA outputs and Complaint trends to determine whether issues meet
criteria for systemic issue designation, risk acceptance, or other governance actions, and monitors
the remediation lifecycle in coordination with Issue Management.

3. Compliance & Enforcement
3.1 Roles and Responsibilities
Block, Inc. Roles:
● Businesses (“1LODˮ):
○ In-Scope Business teams are subject to this Policy based on the products or
services they develop, manage, and oversee. Businesses are responsible for
implementing, maintaining, and complying with Policy requirements, including
establishing and operating complaint handling processes, controls, and procedures.
○ As the first line of defense, the Business operations team, agent team leaders,
complaint handling units, and quality assurance are responsible for designing,
implementing, and maintaining internal controls necessary to comply with Global
Complaint Policy requirements and applicable contractual, regional, or regulatory
obligations.
● Global Complaint Oversight (“2LODˮ):
○ The enterprise compliance function responsible for establishing the Global
Complaint Policy framework, defining oversight expectations, and monitoring
adherence to Policy requirements across In-Scope Businesses subject to this
Policy.
○ As the second line of defense, Global Complaint Oversight provides independent
oversight, challenge, guidance, and monitoring of Business complaint management
programs, including the execution of risk-based, independent compliance and
validation testing. Global Complaint Oversight escalates material risks and systemic
issues as appropriate but does not execute complaint handling activities.
● Global Complaint Program Lead:
○ Responsible for developing and maintaining the Global Complaint Policy and
associated oversight requirements, ensuring In-Scope Businesses are informed of
policy obligations, and conducting independent monitoring and assessments of
Business-level complaint management.
● Chief Compliance Officer ("CCO"):
○ Senior executive responsible for oversight of the Global Complaint Policy and
Global Complaint Standard. They receive recurring reporting on Policy and
Business level compliance and metrics.
● 3rd Line of Defense ("3rd LoD"):
○ The third-line function consists of the Internal Audit ("Audit") team. The 3LoD is
responsible for conducting period reviews of the Business and global level
programs, as well as independent testing and evaluation of the control
environment.
3.2 Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Policy Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance
and objective achievement. As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance
will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management.
Reporting may cover specific information related to:

All relevant Staff members will receive training at least annually covering applicable requirements
and guidance to implement this Policy effectively. All training activities are monitored by the
Compliance Team and are outlined in the Compliance Training Standard.

3.5 Exceptions
The Document Owner or designee has the authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to
this Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws,
regulations, or contractual obligations.
Policy exception requests must:
● Be submitted in writing through Blockʼs designated exception management process and
system;
● Include a clear business justification for the request;
● Describe the risks associated with non-compliance; and
● Provide a description of the controls Block will implement to mitigate risks associated with
the exception.
Businesses are expected to submit exception requests within forty-five 45 days of becoming
aware of actual or potential non-compliance with this Policy, including but not limited to
non-compliance resulting from new product development or identified procedural, people, or
technological gaps.
All approved exceptions must be documented in writing and retained in accordance with Company
recordkeeping requirements. Approved exceptions or exemptions must be submitted to the Global
Complaint Oversight Lead. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance
Leadership and applicable governance committees, as needed, of approved exceptions.
