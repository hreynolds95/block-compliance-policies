1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: Square AU Pty Ltd (“Square AUˮ) is an Australian Financial Services
License AFSL holder License Number: 513929 and is a reporting entity enrolled on the Reporting
Entity Roll managed by the Australian Transaction Reports and Analysis Centre (“AUSTRACˮ) for the
purposes of AML/CTF Law . This Third Party Risk Management TPRM Policy (“Policyˮ) outlines
Square AUʼs approach to outsourcing. The Chief Compliance Officer (“CCOˮ) for Square AU is
responsible for the management of this Policy.
The Policy describes the outsourcing arrangements that Square AU has in place in relation to the
conduct of various aspects of its activities. Square AU remains fully accountable for compliance
with legislation, regardless of any outsourcing. In accordance with AUSTRACʼs guidance on
Outsourcing, all outsourced AML activities must align with Square AUʼs AML/CTF program,
policy, standard and related procedures.
This Policy is modelled on the processes set out in the Block. Inc, Third Party Risk Management
TPRM Policy.
Scope: This Policy applies to all employees, officers, and contingent workers of Square AU
involved in any aspect of outsourcing or vendor management, including intra-group
outsourcing across the Block group.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1. Guiding Principles
The guiding principles upon which this Policy is implemented by Square AU are as follows:
● Accountability: Square AU remains fully responsible for compliance with all AFSL and
AML/CTF obligations, regardless of outsourcing.
● Proportionality: Risk management controls are commensurate with the materiality,
complexity, and regulatory importance of the outsourced service.
● Regulatory Compliance: Outsourcing must not diminish Squareʼs ability to meet obligations
under AFSL, AML/CTF, Privacy Act, or other applicable laws.
● Due Diligence & Ongoing Monitoring: All service providers are subject to appropriate due
diligence, risk assessment, and periodic performance reviews.
● Contractual Safeguards: Outsourcing agreements must include rights of access, audit, data
security, and termination provisions aligned with ASIC and AUSTRAC guidance.
● Continuity & Contingency: Plans must exist to ensure continuity of critical functions in the
event of vendor failure or termination.
2.2. Planning
Process for engaging vendors
Square AUʼs board of directors (“Boardˮ), Employees and the TPROʼs are ultimately responsible for
identifying the services to be outsourced to third parties and for ensuring compliance with its
obligations as an AFSL holder and an AUSTRAC-regulated entity. As part of this process, Square AUʼs
management team will conduct the following activities (and report to the Board for review and
approval of material Outsourcing arrangements):
● identify all material risks associated with the Outsourcing proposal (the Block Vendor
Management Policy outlines the risks that may be created by an Outsourcing relationship);
● ensure that appropriate Due Diligence has been conducted in relation to prospective
outsource providers; and
● consider relevant documentation, including the necessity for service level agreements.

Documented justification for all outsourcing of critical or customer-impacting functions must be
maintained in accordance with record keeping requirements.
2.3. Due Diligence and Third Party Selection
Initial Due Diligence
Square AU will conduct appropriate initial Due Diligence in relation to prospective third party / Vendor
in order to:
● review the experience and capabilities of those providers; and
● ensure that they are suitable to perform the outsourced functions.
Initial Due Diligence is driven by the inherent risk and measures the residual risk by assessing a third
partyʼs ability to satisfy Square AUʼs requirements for the proposed products and services, while
maintaining appropriate controls to ensure compliance with our policies and regulatory requirements.
When due diligence is required, Square AU requests the third party to complete topic-based
questionnaires (such as information security and privacy) and provide relevant supporting
documentation. Risk SMEs review the responses to identify any gaps or control weaknesses and
determine appropriate mitigations. TPRM, Procurement, and relevant teams — including Legal and
Information Security — then collaborate with the business and the third party to assess risks, agree
on remediation actions, and ensure controls are in place before engagement proceeds. The
frequency of Due Diligence on vendors is outlined in the Block Inc global Vendor Management Policy.
2.4. Process for the selection of Vendors
Planning and assessing risk
● Planning begins when the Square TPRO or Business Unit (“BUˮ) defines business
requirements that involve a third party.
Square AU selects and manages third parties using a value- and risk-based approach, initiated
through a VER to capture engagement details and determine inherent risk, in accordance with the
Block Inc global Vendor Management Policy.
Vendor selection considers the reputation, financial stability, performance history, and operational
capability of the provider. Square AU must be satisfied that each vendor is:
● competent, experienced, and qualified to provide the outsourced service;
● familiar with Square AUʼs business and regulatory obligations; and
● of good fame, character, and integrity.
All use of cloud computing, data hosting/storage, and international vendors must undergo
additional due diligence to assess cyber and data risks.
2.5. Conflicts of Interest

Square AU is committed to managing conflicts of interest to ensure all decisions and interactions are
fair, transparent, and in the best interests of customers and the business. A conflict may occur when
personal interests, relationships, or external obligations could influence, or appear to influence,
professional judgement.
Employees must promptly disclose any potential or actual conflicts so they can be assessed and
managed appropriately — through disclosure, recusal, segregation of duties, or other controls. All
conflicts must be handled in line with the Square AU and Block Inc. Conflicts of Interest Policies.
Conduct that prioritises personal or commercial benefit over integrity or customer fairness is not
tolerated.
2.6. Contracts Management and Negotiation
Square AU will ensure that all third-party engagements are governed by written contracts that align with
the requirements of the Block global TPRM policy. During negotiation and throughout the contract
lifecycle, agreements must address key risk areas including data privacy, security controls, regulatory
compliance, and the right to audit. Contracts will be reviewed in consultation with relevant stakeholders to
confirm appropriate risk mitigation measures are included and that third parties remain accountable for
their obligations. Regular reviews and updates of contractual terms will be conducted to maintain
alignment with evolving business, legal, and regulatory requirements.
2.7. Ongoing Monitoring
Vendor performance, compliance, and risk must be monitored throughout the lifecycle of the
arrangement.The performance of the outsourced service provider will be monitored against the
service level and performance requirements specified in the relevant agreement with the outsourced
service provider.
The ERM has monitoring functions in relation to matters such as the performance of the outsourced
service provider against benchmarks, the quality and timeliness of the delivery of the services as well
as the continued financial and organisational capacity of the Vendor to provide the services.
2.8. Breaches
All actual or suspected breaches by third parties that could result in customer detriment or
non-compliance with legal, regulatory, or contractual obligations must be reported and managed in
accordance with the Square AU Breach Reporting Policy and Third Party Incident Management
Procedures. This includes breaches of legal, regulatory, contractual, or internal compliance
obligations — such as privacy or data protection requirements, service-level commitments, or
consumer protection standards. Examples may include misuse or unauthorised disclosure of data,
failure to deliver agreed services, misleading communications, or conduct resulting in harm or unfair
outcomes for customers. All employees and third parties must report breaches.
Square AU expects all third parties to maintain a transparent and accountable culture where issues
are raised promptly and managed effectively. Employees and partners are encouraged to report
concerns in good faith, with all reports handled confidentially and without retaliation, in line with Block
Incʼs Speak Up Policy.

2.9. Terminations
Where a termination of contract is sought, either resulting from a breach or review, or from a business
decision to end the contract, Square AU must ensure that the termination process is managed in a
controlled and compliant manner. This includes:
● Assessment and Approval: Confirming that termination is consistent with contractual
terms, internal policies, and regulatory obligations, and obtaining appropriate internal
approvals.
● Notification: Providing formal written notice to the Vendor in accordance with the
termination provisions outlined in the agreement.
● Transition Planning: Implementing a transition or exit plan to ensure the orderly transfer or
cessation of services, minimise disruption to business operations or customers, and
safeguard data and confidential information.
● Risk Mitigation: Identifying and mitigating any operational, regulatory, or customer risks
arising from the termination.
● Record Keeping: Retaining records of the decision-making process, approvals,
communications, and closure activities in accordance with Square AUʼs record retention
requirements.
Square AU may terminate Third Party relationships for various reasons, including, but not limited
to:
● Expiration or satisfaction of the contract;
● Desire to seek an alternate third party;
● Desire to bring the activity in-house or discontinue the activity; or
● Breach of contract, bankruptcy, force majeure, or similar.
Termination of contracts will be completed in line with the global Block, Inc. Third Party Risk
Management TPRM Policy.
3.
Compliance & Enforcement
3.1. Roles and Responsibilities:
Square AU operates a Three Lines of Defence (“3LoDˮ) Model. All Staff Members are expected to
comply with this Policy at all times. Square AU is required to demonstrate that it has taken all
reasonable steps and exercised all due diligence in order to identify, assess, manage, and monitor
risks associated with third parties. Therefore, it is essential that all Staff Members and each line of
defence understand their roles and responsibilities in respect to this Policy.
First Line of Defence

● All Staff Members or Staff: All Staff Members are responsible for identifying and managing
Third Party Risks on an ongoing basis and must:
○ Conduct appropriate due diligence before engaging with any third party, including
suppliers, vendors, and partners, to ensure they meet both Square AUʼs and Block,
Inc.ʼs global standards for risk management, compliance, and ethical conduct.
○ Monitor third party relationships to ensure that performance, compliance, and risk
profiles remain within acceptable parameters.
○ Promptly escalate any concerns or incidents relating to third parties through the
designated reporting channels outlined in the TPRM procedures.
● Leads: While the Code applies to all Staff Members, "Leads" Square AU's term for
managers) have a deeper level of responsibility and must lead with integrity and reinforce
Block's ethical culture. Leads are often the first resource for Staff Members who have
questions on ethical issues or potential violations of Block, Inc and Square AU Policy. If
unsure of the best course of action, Leads are responsible for:
○ Ensure the TPRM Policy is embedded and consistently adhered to within their area
of accountability.
○ Support their teams in conducting appropriate third party due diligence and risk
assessments.
○ Promptly report any suspected violations of this Policy, the Code, or law. Leads may
be held accountable if they fail to report misconduct.
○ Foster a culture of transparency and ethical behaviour where Staff Members feel
comfortable escalating concerns about third party risks.
○ Ensuring the Global Code of Conduct training is completed by all Staff Members
within their business unit on time.
Second line of Defence
● Compliance: Compliance, as the owner of this Policy will:
○ Carry out risk-based reviews of the implementation of this Policy, to include the
identification and investigation of breaches and the appropriate action taken to
address such breaches, as required;
○ Have an effective mechanism for identifying and reporting on non-adherence of this
Policy;
○ Report on related management information to the Regulatory Governance
Committee, and the Square AU Board as appropriate.
● Global Third Party Risk Management (“TPRMˮ) Team: The Global TPRM team, within
Corporate Compliance is responsible for:
○ Monitoring and providing oversight at a global level for Third Party providers to
Square AU;
○ Supporting the design, implementation, and ongoing enhancement of the TPRM
framework.

Third line of Defence
● Third Line of Defence (“Internal Auditˮ): Internal Audit forms Square AU's third line of
defence. The independent internal audit function, employing a risk-based approach,
provides assurance to the Square AU Board and senior management.
● Square AU Board Members (“Boardˮ): The Board is responsible for overseeing Square AUʼs
Third Party Risk Management framework, ensuring that management has appropriate
systems and controls in place to identify and manage third party risks. They will also be
responsible for:
○ Supporting the design, implementation, and ongoing enhancement of the TPRM
framework;
○ Provide oversight and decisioning on escalated third party risk issues.
3.2. Monitoring, Testing and Reporting
Square AU maintains a structured and risk-based approach to monitoring, testing and reporting on
its regulatory programs. Oversight is coordinated by the Compliance and Risk functions, with input
from stakeholder teams.
We focus on three key activities:
● Monitoring: Monitoring activities are conducted on a risk-based schedule to confirm that
key compliance controls are operating effectively. These activities may include but are not
limited to vendor reviews, dashboards, risks assessments, exception reporting and analysis
of complaints or incidents. Higher-risk areas are subject to more frequent and detailed
monitoring to ensure timely detection and remediation of issues.
● Testing: Independent and Internal testing and thematic reviews are undertaken through
Compliance, Risk, or Internal Audit to verify that controls are working as intended. Results
are documented, issues are tracked and remediation actions are followed through until
closed.
● Reporting: Square AU provides risk based reporting on the performance and effectiveness
of its compliance programs to ensure transparency, accountability, and informed
decision-making. Reports highlight program health, key risks, control effectiveness and
remediation progress, giving senior leaders and committees clear visibility of compliance
outcomes and areas requiring attention. This ensures consistent oversight and supports a
strong culture of governance across the business.
3.3. Training
All relevant personnel will receive training on the Third Party Risk Management processes at
Square AU. This training will cover aspects of Square AU and Block Inc global policies on TPRM.

3.5. Exceptions
The Policy Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to
this Policy, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws
and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a
request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate
risks associated with the exception. Any approved exceptions must be documented in writing
and retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any approved exceptions or exemptions should
be submitted to the Policy Governance Team. The Document Owner and/or designee will inform
Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of exceptions.
