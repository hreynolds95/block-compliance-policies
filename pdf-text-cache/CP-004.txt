1. Overview
Introduction and Purpose: This Canada Anti-Spam Legislation (“CASLˮ) Policy ("Policy") outlines
Afterpay Canada Limited ("Afterpay Canada") approach to combat spam and regulate electronic
communications in Canada. The Head of Compliance, Afterpay Canada, is responsible for
overseeing this Policy. This Policy should be read in connection with Block's CANSPAM Policy and
if there is any conflict between Block and the applicable local law, this Policy must be followed.
Afterpay Canada conducts business in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations in
accordance with CASL. The purpose of this Policy is to further Block's protection of Canadians
from unsolicited electronic messages while fostering trust and confidence in digital
communications. It applies to a wide range of electronic messages sent for commercial purposes,
including emails, text messages, and other electronic messaging.
CASL not only addresses spam but also governs the installation of computer programs and the
alteration of transmission data. This legislation is crucial for Afterpay Canada as it operates within
the digital payment services sector, where electronic communications play a vital role in customer
engagement and business operations.
In short, CASL aims to:
● Protect individuals and businesses from unsolicited electronic messages, spyware, and
malware.
● Regulate CEMs to ensure transparency, accountability, and trust in digital communications.
● Facilitate a secure and competitive digital marketplace in Canada.
Scope: This Policy is established by Afterpay Canada and is applicable to all Afterpay business
lines and jurisdictions within Canada. It applies to Staff Members involved in the marketing,
distribution, and communication activities of Afterpay Canada.
This Policy does not apply to:
● Products and services that are distributed by third-party partners outside the jurisdiction of
Canada.

● Bank partners who are independently responsible for their compliance with CASL.
● Any business activities or communications managed by external marketing agencies that
operate under their own CASL compliance policies.

2. Policy Requirements
2.1 Who CASL Protects
CASL provides protection to all individuals, businesses, and organizations in Canada from
unwanted electronic communications. This includes consumers, businesses, and government
entities. CASL's reach extends beyond Canadian borders to anyone sending electronic messages
to or from Canada, emphasizing its global impact on digital communications.
2.2 Information requirements in Commercial Electronic Messages CEMs
When sending CEMs, Afterpay Canada must include certain information to comply with CASL. This
includes:
● Identifying information about the Sender, including name and contact information.

● A clear and functional unsubscribe mechanism that allows recipients to opt out of receiving
future messages.
Any additional information required by CASL to ensure transparency and accountability in
electronic communications.
2.3 Commercial Electronic message CEM Content Requirements
Every CEM sent by Afterpay Canada must include:
● Name or business name of the Sender.
● If sent on behalf of another person, the name or business name of that person.
● Contact information (mailing address, phone number, email, or web address) valid for at
least 60 days after sending the CEM.
● An unsubscribe mechanism allowing recipients to opt-out of future messages.
The unsubscribe mechanism in CEMs must:
● Enable recipients to indicate their wish to stop receiving CEMs at no cost.
● Specify an electronic address or webpage valid for at least 60 days after the CEM is sent.
● Be easily accessible, simple, and quick to use.
● Process unsubscribe requests within 10 business days of receipt.
2.4 Consent
CASL mandates that businesses obtain consent before sending CEMs to individuals. Afterpay
Canada must ensure that it has the appropriate consent—either express or implied—before
sending any CEMs. Express consent requires a clear and affirmative indication of consent, while
implied consent may arise from existing business relationships or specific circumstances outlined
in CASL.
2.5 General Requirements for Consent
When seeking consent for any act regulated under CASL, Afterpay Canada must clearly outline the
purpose, identify the requesting party and the party on whose behalf consent is sought, and
provide contact information. Consent can be oral or written and must be obtained separately for
each type of act specified under CASL.
2.6 Express Consent
When seeking express consent to send CEMs, Afterpay Canada must adhere to CASL's prescribed
manner for requesting consent. The intended recipient — that is, the consumer or individual to
whom Afterpay Canada wishes to send CEMs — must actively indicate their consent. Consent
cannot be bundled with general terms and conditions but must be sought separately. The request

for consent must clearly state the purpose, name or business name of the Sender, contact
information, and the recipient's right to withdraw consent.
2.7 Additional Consent Requirements
For acts involving altering transmission data or installing computer programs, Afterpay Canada
must comply with specific requirements to allow for withdrawal of consent and assist in removing
or disabling the program if requested by the recipient.
2.8 Afterpay Responsibilities
Afterpay Canada is committed to upholding the principles of CASL in all its electronic
communications activities. Compliance with CASL is essential to maintaining customer trust,
safeguarding privacy, and avoiding potential legal and financial consequences.
2.9 Marketing and Compliance Controls
Afterpay Canada will implement appropriate compliance and marketing controls to ensure
adherence with CASL, including:
● Obtain required explicit consent from individuals before sending CEMs for marketing
purposes.
● Maintain and update consent records to ensure compliance with consent requirements
under CASL.
● Review all marketing communications to ensure they include required identification
information and disclose the purpose of the message clearly.
● Include a clear and functioning unsubscribe mechanism in all marketing communications.
● Process unsubscribe requests promptly and honor opt-out preferences.
● Conduct regular training sessions for marketing team members on CASL requirements and
best practices.
● Maintain detailed records of consent obtained from individuals and communication
practices.
● Periodically review marketing practices to ensure ongoing compliance with CASL
regulations.
● Ensure that any third-party service providers engaged in marketing activities comply with
CASL requirements.
2.10 Compliance Measures
Afterpay Canada will implement the following compliance measures:
● Staff Training: Where applicable, provide targeted training and awareness programs for
relevant Staff Members involved in electronic communications to ensure understanding of
CASL requirements and compliance obligations.

● Consent Management: Implement robust controls and oversight mechanisms to manage
and track consent from individuals, ensuring that only those who have provided consent
receive CEMs.
● Message Content Review: Regularly review the content of CEMs to ensure compliance with
CASL's information and unsubscribe requirements.
● Record-Keeping: Maintain detailed records of consent obtained and communication
practices to demonstrate compliance with CASL in case of audits or investigations.
2.11 Penalties
Non-compliance with CASL can result in severe penalties, including but not limited to substantial
fines of up to CAD$ 10 million for businesses and CAD$ 1 million for individuals. Additionally,
non-compliance may result in reputational damage and loss of customer trust. Therefore,
adherence to CASL requirements is crucial for Afterpay Canada to operate ethically and
responsibly in the Canadian digital marketplace.
2.12 Conclusion
By adopting and adhering to this CASL compliance Policy, Afterpay Canada demonstrates its
commitment to maintaining high standards of ethical conduct in electronic communications. This
Policy educates all stakeholders, including Staff Members, about the importance of CASL
compliance and its impact on privacy, security, and trust in digital communications within the
Canadian context.
