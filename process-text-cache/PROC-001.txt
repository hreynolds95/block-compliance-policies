Block, Inc.
Last Approved: Oct 30, 2025
Effective Date: Oct 30, 2025

1. Overview 
Introduction and Purpose: The annual review process ensures Block’s policies and standards are reviewed at appropriate intervals to maintain accuracy and effectiveness. Through LogicGate, the Compliance Policy Team manages review dates and notifications, supporting document owners [a]in completing timely review of their documents. 
This procedure establishes:
* How review dates are set in LogicGat;
* When notifications are sent to document owners;
* Steps for completing the annual review; and 
* Process for requesting timeline adjustments.
The Compliance Policy Team coordinates review timing to align related documents where possible, particularly ensuring standards are reviewed in conjunction with their parent policies for efficiency and consistency.  
Scope: This procedure applies to all Block Compliance Policies and Standards, including related subsidiary documentation. The process is managed by the Compliance Policy Team and must be followed by all document owners or delegates responsible for these materials. 
Definitions: 
Terminology [b]
	Definition 
	Policy
	Policies are high level statements of management intent that formally establish requirements to drive compliance and outline the goals, principles, or directives of the Chief Compliance Officer ("CCO"). Policies focus on communicating binding compliance requirements and do not dictate detailed steps. Policies are the overall guidelines for the organization.
	Standard
	Standards assist in decision-making and outline further business, technology, risk or other processes, and controls outlined in overarching policies. Standards may lay out the operational strategy and functions that are performed to implement a Policy's requirements. Standards may also establish lifecycle stages, responsibilities, and any additional product, business or market-specific requirements that go beyond what's required in an overarching Policy.
	Document Owner
	The Owner is an individual responsible for ensuring that a Document makes its way through the entire Document lifecycle. Responsibilities include:
* Identifying the need for potential new Documents and ideating
* Creating initial version of Documents
* Identifying reviewers and approvers
* Planning and conducting awareness campaigns
* Conducting scheduled reviews
* Understanding the Company's current state of Compliance with a Document
* Ensuring forward-looking requirements are accounted for in roadmap planning
* Developing and tracking the execution of the implementation plan
* Completing attestations to the Document's content prior to publication
	Annual Review
	The required process of examining and validating a document[c]'s content at least once every 12 months[d][e] to ensure continued accuracy, relevance, and effectiveness.
	Review Date
	The date by which a document must complete its annual review cycle, typically set as the anniversary of the last review unless adjusted to align with related documents or committee schedules.
	Extension Request
	A formal request to extend a document's review date beyond its scheduled timeline.  Extensions are limited to a maximum of 6 months, require C-Core + 1 approval, and must include business justification. Only one extension is permitted per review cycle.[f]
	Material Changes
	Substantive changes to a Document's requirements or guidance impacting the goals, principles, processes, or directives. Includes new requirements, more restrictive requirements, expansion of scope, or changes that materially impact business operations.
	Immaterial Changes
	Non-substantive changes to a Document's content that do not impact requirements or guidance. May include updating grammar, formatting, correcting naming conventions, or updating links.
	LogicGate
	Block's system of record for managing compliance policies, standards, and procedures, including the annual review process and related workflows.

2. Annual Review Procedure Requirements
   1. Review Date Management
The Compliance Policy Team manages all review dates within LogicGate and has primary responsibility for coordinating the annual review process. When setting review dates, the Policy Team will align related documents to ensure efficient review cycles, particularly striving to align standards alongside their parent policies whenever possible. 
The Compliance Policy Team will: 
* Set initial review dates based on the last approval date ;
* Adjust dates to align with committee schedules; [g]
* Coordinate related document reviews;
* Monitor completion status; and 
* Maintain accurate tracking. 
2.2 Notification Process 
LogicGate automatically generates notifications to Document Owners at three key points in the review cycle: 
At 90 calendar days before the review due date, Document Owners receive their initial notification via email. Upon receipt, Document Owners or delegates should begin planning their review process, including identifying necessary stakeholders and scheduling review meetings. 
At 60 calendar days before the due date, a second notification via email prompts Document Owners to ensure their review is actively in progress. By this point, Document Owners should be gathering stakeholder input and documenting any changes needed. 
The final notification arrives via email 30 calendar days before the due date. This notification serves as both a completion reminder and the deadline for submitting any extension requests. [h]
2.3 Document Review Requirements 
Document Owners must complete their reviews in accordance with the lifecycle requirements established in the Block, Inc. Compliance Policy on Policies (“PoP”).  The full review and approval process, including stakeholder engagement, quality control and approval requirements are detailed in the PoP.  For the complete document review process, including creation, revision, approval, and publication requirements, Document Owners should refer to the PoP.   [i][j]
2.4  Extension Process 
Document Owners requiring additional time beyond the scheduled review date must submit a formal extension request through LogicGate. All extension requests must be submitted before the current review date and include a detailed business justification. [k]
Extension approval authority is structured as follows: 
* Extensions require C-Core+1 member approval[l]
* No extension may extend past six months from the original scheduled due date 
* Only one extension is permitted per annual review cycle
Upon receiving an extension request, the Compliance Policy Team will review for completeness and route to the appropriate approver. Once approved, the Compliance Policy Team will update LogicGate with the new review dates and adjust the notification schedule accordingly.[m] The Compliance Policy Team will maintain documentation of all extension requests and approvals.  [n][o]Documents will be reported as extended on reporting status reports.
2.5  Board and Committee Review Alignment  
The Compliance Policy Team regularly reviews committee schedules and may adjust document review dates to align with the next available Board or Committee meeting. This ensures efficient review cycles and appropriate governance oversight. 
When adjusting review dates for Committee or Board alignment, the Compliance Policy Team will: 
* Identify the next available Committee or Board date, as applicable;
* Update the document review date in LogicGate;
* Notify Document Owners of the revised timeline;and
* Adjust automated notifications accordingly. 
Documents whose review dates are adjusted to align with the next available Committee or Board meeting will not be reported as overdue in compliance reporting. This exception applies only to documents actively scheduled for Committee or Board review and does not apply to other delays or extensions. 
Document Owners should note that the Compliance Policy Team may adjust their document’s review date to either align with related documents (such as Standards with their parent policies) or to accommodate Committee or Board scheduling. These adjustments will be communicated to Document Owners and reflected in LogicGate notifications. [p]
2.6  Documentation and Evidence
All review activities must be documented within LogicGate to maintain a clear audit trail. The Compliance Policy Team maintains responsibility for ensuring proper documentation is retained, while Document Owners are responsible for providing evidence. [q]
2.6.1 Current Documentation (2025 Forward)
LogicGate serves as the system of record for all review activities starting in November 2025. Required documentation includes:
* Review completion records;
* Extension requests and approvals;
* Date adjustment justification; and 
* Committee alignment documentation. 
2.6.2 Historical Documentation (Pre-November 2025) 
For documentation of reviews conducted prior to November 2025, Document Owners should contact the Compliance Policy Team directly. The Compliance Policy Team maintains historical records and can provide evidence of past reviews, approvals, and related documentation upon request. 
The Compliance Policy Team will maintain all available records according to retention requirements specified in the PoP. All documentation should remain accessible for audit purposes and regulatory examinations. [r]
2.7  Reporting and Escalation 
2.7.1 Status Reporting
The Compliance Policy Team monitors review completion status and provides regulator [s]reporting through multiple channels: 
* Monthly Reporting:
   * Review status dashboard with Compliance Policy Governance leadership
   * Overdue document reporting 
   * Upcoming review schedule for next 90 calendar calendar days
   * Extension tracking and status [t]
* Quarterly Report to CPC: 
   * Comprehensive review status[u]
   * Overdue documents requiring attention 
   * Extension trends and patterns
   * Significant timeline adjustments [v]
   * Committee schedule impacts 
2.7.2 Overdue Documents: 
Documents are considered overdue when: 
* Review is not completed by the due date
* No extension requests has been submitted 
* Extension request was not approved 
* Extension period has expired
Note: Documents pending next available Committee or Board review are not reported as overdue, provided they are actively scheduled for Committee or Board review. 
2.7.3 Escalation Process:
For overdue documents, the Compliance Policy Team will: 
* Send immediate notification to the owner via LogicGate 
* Include in monthly reporting to Compliance Leadership
* Report to CPC in quarterly updates [w]
* Track remediation efforts 
Document owners of overdue documents must: 
* Provide a timely status update
* Submit extension request if needed
* Develop remediation timeline
* Provide progress updates until resolved
The Compliance Policy Team maintains oversight of overdue documents until review completion is confirmed in LogicGate.

3. Compliance & Enforcement  
   1. Roles and Responsibilities: 
Block, Inc. Roles: 
* Compliance Policy Team 
   * Manages LogicGate review dates and notifications
   * Maintains documentation and reporting 
   * Coordinates Committee and Board scheduling 
   * Escalates overdue reviews
   * Reports to Compliance Policy Committee and Compliance Leadership
   * Provides historical documentation upon request 
* Document Owners[x]
   * Completes reviews by due dates
   * Follows PoP document lifecycle process
   * Submits extension requests when needed
   * Prepares committee materials
   * Maintains document accuracy 
   * Confirms review completion in LogicGate
* C-Core + 1 Members:
   * Reviews/Approves extensions
   * Supports overdue document escalation 
* Compliance Leadership:
   * Reviews Monthly status reporting
   * Monitors overdue documents
   * Ensures program effectiveness[y]
* Compliance Policy Committee (“CPC”)
   * Reviews quarterly status reports during meetings, as needed
   * Provides governance oversight
   * Addresses systemic issues 
   2. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Procedure Owner [z]and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance and objective achievement.  As outlined in the Block, Inc. Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management. Reporting may cover specific information related to: 
* Policies and standards 
* Procedures
* Audit/exam findings
* Issues management 
* Training 
* Complaints[aa]
   1. Record Retention
All Documents are retained according to the longer of: 
* Block’s Data Classification & Handling Policy
* Applicable regulatory requirements 
   1. Exceptions
The Procedure Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this Procedure, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing,[ab] include a reason for a request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with the exception. The Procedure Owner is responsible for retaining evidence of exception requests and related approvals.
