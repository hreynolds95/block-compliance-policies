Last Approved: Dec 9, 2025
Effective Date: Jan 5, 2026

1. Overview 
Introduction and Purpose: This Compliance Document Management Procedure (the "Procedure") outlines Block's approach to managing compliance Documents through the full Document lifecycle in the Document management system. This Procedure supports the framework in the Block, Inc. Compliance Policy on Policies (“PoP”) to ensure documentation is accurate and supports current laws, regulations, and operational practices.
Scope: This Procedure applies to all Block Compliance Policies, Standards, and Procedures including related subsidiary Documents. The process is managed by the Compliance Policy Team and must be followed by all Document Owners or delegates responsible for these materials.
Definitions: 
Terminology 
	Definition 
	Document
	A Policy, Standard, or Procedure.
	Policy
	Policies are high level statements of management intent that formally
establish requirements to drive compliance and outline the goals, principles,
or directives of the Chief Compliance Officer (“CCO”). Policies focus on
communicating binding compliance requirements and do not dictate
detailed steps. Policies are the overall guidelines for the organization.
	Standard
	Standards assist in decision-making and outline further business,
technology, risk or other processes, and controls outlined in overarching
policies. Standards may lay out the operational strategy and functions that
are performed to implement a Policy’s requirements. Standards may also
establish lifecycle stages, responsibilities, and any additional product,
business or market-specific requirements that go beyond what’s required in
an overarching Policy.
	Procedure
	Procedures change frequently and describe steps that implement a Policy or
process to comply with a regulation or Standard. Procedures focus on a
process and desired outcome between multiple teams. Procedures exist to
standardize a process and provide guidance on timing and output.
	Request ID
	Number assigned through the Document management system for each new request. 
	Compliance Document Approval Matrix
	Framework that specifies which roles or governance bodies must sign off on Document changes based on the hierarchy of the Document and the scale of the change. 
	Material Changes
	Substantive changes to a Document's requirements or guidance impacting the goals, principles, processes, or directives. Includes new requirements, more restrictive requirements, expansion of scope, or changes that materially impact business operations.
	Immaterial Changes
	Non-substantive changes to a Document's content that do not impact requirements or guidance. May include updating grammar, formatting, correcting naming conventions, or updating links.
	LogicGate
	Block's Document management system for managing compliance policies, standards, and procedures, including the annual review process and related workflows.
	Tier 1 Document
	* Limited to Policies only (Standards / Procedures are excluded)
* Policies with a regulatory requirement to be approved by the applicable Block or US Board
* Policies that outline enterprise-wide framework and/or overarching requirements that apply across multiple significant brands, entities or businesses 
* Policies that will have material business impact across multiple brands, entities or businesses 
* Policies that present new or expanded responsibilities for the applicable Block Board or a US Subsidiary Board
* Policies that are more like high level frameworks that are unlikely to change significantly over the year
	Tier 2 Document
	* All other Policies that do not meet Tier 1 criteria
* Policies and Standards that are core to key Compliance laws, rules, and regulations
* Policies and Standards that outlines more technical and brand specific guidance
	Tier 3 Document
	* All Standards that do not meet Tier 1 or Tier 2 criteria
* Standards that support key Compliance laws, rules, and regulations
* Standards that outline day-to-day management and operational activities to execute against existing Policies or Standards (approved at Tier 1 or 2 levels)

2. Procedure Requirements
   1. Document Creation & Update Management
2.1.1 Request Submission: 


The Submitter will access the Document management system to submit a new Document request or request change to an existing Document.  The request will contain the Document type, business need, scope,  urgency, and supplemental documentation as applicable (e.g. regulatory guidance, audit report, etc.). The Document management system will automatically assign a Request ID and notify the Compliance Policy Team. If the Submitter is not the Document Owner, the Document Owner will also review the request and have the authority to add additional stakeholders to review the request. 


2.1.2 Conflict Checks with Existing Documents: 


The Compliance Policy Team will review the existing Document library to identify potential overlaps or conflicts with current Policies, Standards or Procedures. The Compliance Policy Team will Document findings in the Document management system and consult with Document owners of related Documents as needed. 


2.1.3 Document Request Form Handling: 


The Compliance Policy Team will conduct an initial assessment of the request to ensure completeness and request additional information from the Submitter as needed. The Compliance Policy Team will also assess the Document hierarchy to determine if the intended purpose and level of detail for the Policy, Standard or Procedure aligns with the hierarchy in Compliance Policy on Policies | Block. 


Priority assignment may be based on business needs and regulatory requirements. 


2.1.4 Template Provision and Guidance: 


Documents must be drafted on appropriate templates provided to the Submitter by the Compliance Policy Team based on Document type, and must follow current brand guidelines regarding elements including but not limited to font and formatting with approvals Documented in an approval history table. 


The Compliance Policy Team will consult with the Submitter as needed to clarify requirements and communicate timeline expectations. 
2.2 Quality Control Review
The Compliance Policy Team will review the information provided by the Submitter and assess the feasibility of the request, considering factors such as the urgency of the request, requested completion date, resource allocation, competing priorities and determination of approval routing. 


After the feasibility has been assessed, the Compliance Policy Team will communicate the estimated completion timeline and approve the request or send the request back to the Submitter for additional information. 


2.2.1 Initial Quality Control


The Compliance Policy Team will conduct a Quality Control (“QC”) review of the Document in accordance with the Compliance Policy on Policies prior to routing for stakeholder review or document approval. The QC review includes, but is not limited to: 
* Verifying all sections are complete and formatted in accordance with the template
* Checking for consistency in terminology and definitions 
* Validating accuracy of cross-references to other documents 
* Confirming clarity, grammar and readability


2.2.2 Post Approval Quality Control 
[a][b][c]


Prior to publication, the Compliance Policy Team will conduct a final QC review to ensure accuracy with the approver name(s), approval date(s) and formatting. 
2.3 Retirement 
2.3.1 Retirement Request Initiation: 
Documents may warrant retirement for reasons including but not limited to:
* Existing Document superseded by new Document
* Guidance no longer applicable
* Document consolidated with another Document
* Regulatory change
To initiate Document retirement, the Document Owner or the Compliance Policy Team must first identify the Document for retirement. Next, the Document Owner or the Compliance Policy Team will submit the retirement request through the Document management system workflow and Document the reason for retirement, effective date of retirement and replacement Document when applicable. 
The Document Owner is responsible for conducting an impact assessment to identify affected stakeholders and processes and updating the Document management system accordingly.  
2.3.2 Stakeholder Review: 
The Document management system will notify Stakeholder Reviewers of the Document proposed for retirement. If a replacement Document exists, Stakeholder Reviewers will also review the replacement Document for accuracy. 
Stakeholder Reviewers have a review period of 15 business days to provide feedback or object to retirement with justification. The Compliance Policy Team evaluates feedback and objections to determine the appropriate course of action. 
2.3.3 Approval Workflow: 
Approvals for retiring Documents follows the same approval process as the original Document as outlined in the Compliance Policy on Policies | Block. Approvers will confirm the appropriateness of the retirement and ensure the impact is fully understood. Approvers will review other Documents to identify references to the retired Document that must be updated. 
2.3.4 Retirement Execution: 
The Compliance Policy Team is responsible for updating the status of the Document to “Retired” in the Document management system and updating the effective date of retirement. The date of retirement and the final approval will be recorded in the Document management system with supplemental documentation (e.g. meeting minutes). 
The Compliance Policy Team will also remove the retired Document from the published library, and update the title and / or watermark of the retired Document to indicate its retirement status. The retirement notice will also be added to the Document, indicating the retirement date and reason. If a replacement Document exists, the Compliance Policy Team will add a referral to the new Document. 
2.3.5 Communication and Record Keeping: 
The Compliance Policy Team will send a retirement notice to all previous Document recipients to inform of the retirement reason, effective date of retirement and replacement Document (if applicable). The Compliance Policy Team will update all related Documents to remove references to the retired Document in active Documents, and document the change in the revision history. 
Retired Documents must be retained in accordance with the Compliance Policy on Policies| Block and the Data Classification and Handling Policy | Block. 
2.4 Publication and Storage
Drafting and editing of compliance documents take place in a Google Doc that is accessed through the Document management system. This Google Doc serves as the authoritative working version throughout the drafting and revision process. Document Owners, Reviewers and other contributors use suggestion mode to ensure revisions are clearly redlined as the document progresses through review and approval.
Once all required approvals are completed and documented in the SOR, the Compliance Policy Team finalizes the master Google Doc by incorporating approved changes. A PDF version is then generated from the finalized Google Doc and the link to this PDF is published in the SOR within the appropriate policy, standard, or procedure library on the employee dashboard. 
All full time employees have access to the published PDF unless specific access restrictions are requested. In such cases, the Document Owner is responsible for notifying the Compliance Policy Team so that access limitations can be applied.
2.4.1 Version Management
Document versions are maintained in the SOR, which identifies and displays the active version for all employees. Documents are versioned using approval dates, and the most recent approval date determines the current official version. This approval date is also included in the Document title to ensure clarity and consistency across all published Documents.
The SOR preserves a complete version history and clearly marks prior versions as retired once a new version is approved and published. Retired versions are removed from general employee access but remain available to Document Owners and authorized users. 
2.4.2 Storage
It contains the master Google Doc for each Document, as well as the finalized PDF versions that are published to the SOR. The repository also retains PDF copies of the redline Google Docs that are generated before changes are resolved in the master copy and finalized for publication. These redline PDFs support audit requests and provide a complete historical record of revisions. Retired and archived versions are stored in accordance with established retention rules.
Access to this repository is restricted to Compliance Policy Team members and designated leadership. Only the Compliance Policy Team is authorized to modify folder structures, manage permissions, or reorganize records. Special access may be granted when required for authorized purposes, including but not limited to audits and examination teams.
2.4.3. Archival
When a Document version is replaced or retired, the previous version is moved to the designated Archive folder within the Compliance Google Drive. Archived Documents must be clearly labeled as archived in their titles to ensure accurate identification and must be retained in accordance with all applicable regulatory and internal retention requirements.
Archived materials are maintained in a read only state and cannot be modified. They may be shared with authorized users upon request.
Document Owners retain ongoing access through the SOR to the archived versions of the Documents they own. Audit and Examination teams are granted controlled permissions to view archived Documents in both the centralized repository and the SOR.
Once a Document is retired, it is archived in the Compliance Google Drive and removed from general employee access within the SOR to ensure that only the current, approved version is available for operational use.
2.5 Communication & Awareness
2.5.1 General Staff Updates
General Staff updates are intended to provide the entire company, compliance organization, or functional teams with timely updates about the publication of new and updated Documents. 
   * The Compliance Policy and Mandatory Training Team (“Compliance Policy Team”) will send a monthly general notification to announce the publication of new and updated Documents via Slack to the compliance organization. 
   * The Compliance Policy Team will send more targeted Slack notifications to affected individuals or teams for functional policies. 
   * Both the monthly general notification and the targeted notification will include: Document name, effective date, summary of changes, and a link to the published Document in the Document management system.
2.5.2 Compliance Leadership Reporting
* Reporting to Compliance Leadership will include:
      * New documents, 
      * Documents undergoing revision, 
      * Documents that were retired during the reporting period, 
      * Document review status for documents in a current draft workflow, and 
      * exception metrics based on exceptions submitted through LogicGate.
   * The Compliance Policy Team will compile a monthly dashboard on document status which is sent to the Chief Compliance Officer. 
   * The Compliance Policy Team will provide an update on policies and metrics to the CPC at its scheduled meetings, but at least quarterly.
   * The Compliance Policy Team will prepare an annual report to the Block Board of Directors highlighting compliance Document program health with supporting metrics.
   * For policy updates that are urgent or are a result of significant changes to laws or regulations, the Compliance Policy Team will report on an ad-hoc basis and to the appropriate audience.
   * On an as-needed basis, the Compliance Policy Team will prepare executive summaries for leadership.


2.5.3 Stakeholder Notifications


Document Owners are responsible for driving awareness campaigns for newly published or materially changed Documents to key stakeholders. Awareness campaigns generally include announcements, email notification, or trainings.


If the Document Owner deems a policy update as critical[d], the Owner or Delegate should
immediately notify identified stakeholders. The notification should generally include what
changed, how to comply, and any details on an impact analysis.

3. Compliance & Enforcement  
   1. Roles and Responsibilities: 
Block, Inc. Roles: 
* Compliance Policy Team 
   * Manages LogicGate review dates and notifications
   * Maintains documentation and reporting 
   * Coordinates Committee and Board scheduling 
   * Escalates overdue reviews
   * Reports to Compliance Policy Committee and Compliance Leadership
   * Provides historical documentation upon request 
* Submitter:
   * The individual who submits a Document in the Document management system. 
* Document Owners
   * Completes reviews by due dates
   * Follows PoP document lifecycle process
   * Submits extension requests when needed
   * Prepares committee materials
   * Maintains document accuracy 
   * Confirms review completion in LogicGate
* C-Core + 1 Members:
   * Reviews/Approves extensions
   * Supports overdue document escalation 
* Compliance Leadership:
   * Reviews Monthly status reporting
   * Monitors overdue documents
   * Ensures program effectiveness
* Compliance Policy Committee (“CPC”)
   * Reviews quarterly status reports during meetings, as needed
   * Provides governance oversight
   * Addresses systemic issues 


   2. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Procedure Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance and objective achievement. As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management. Reporting may cover specific information related to: 
* Policies and Standards 
* Procedures
* Audit/exam findings
* Issues management 
* Training 
* Complaints
   1. Record Retention
All Documents are retained according to the longer of: 
* Block’s Data Classification & Handling Policy
* Applicable regulatory requirements 
   1. Exceptions
The Procedure Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this Procedure, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with the exception. The Procedure Owner is responsible for retaining evidence of exception requests and related approvals.

This Document is confidential and should only be accessed by employees.
