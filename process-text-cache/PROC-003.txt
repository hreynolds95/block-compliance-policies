1. Overview 
Introduction and Purpose: This Policy Governance Approval Processes Procedure ("Procedure") outlines Block's approach to managing approval workflows for compliance documents through appropriate governance bodies including committees and the Board of Directors. 
Scope: This Procedure applies to Block, Inc., its subsidiaries, and affiliates (collectively, “Block”), including all products.  
Definitions: 
Terminology 
	Definition 
	Document
	A Policy, Standard, or Procedure.
	Policy
	Policies are high level statements of management intent that formally
establish requirements to drive compliance and outline the goals, principles,
or directives of the Chief Compliance Officer (“CCO”). Policies focus on
communicating binding compliance requirements and do not dictate
detailed steps. Policies are the overall guidelines for the organization.
	Standard
	Standards assist in decision-making and outline further business,
technology, risk or other processes, and controls outlined in overarching
policies. Standards may lay out the operational strategy and functions that
are performed to implement a Policy’s requirements. Standards may also
establish lifecycle stages, responsibilities, and any additional product,
business or market-specific requirements that go beyond what’s required in
an overarching Policy.
	Procedure
	Procedures change frequently and describe steps that implement a Policy or
process to comply with a regulation or Standard. Procedures focus on a
process and desired outcome between multiple teams. Procedures exist to
standardize a process and provide guidance on timing and output.
	Tier 1 Document
	* Limited to Policies only (Standards / Procedures are excluded)
* Policies with a regulatory requirement to be approved by the applicable Block or US Board
* Policies that outline enterprise-wide framework and/or overarching requirements that apply across multiple significant brands, entities or businesses 
* Policies that will have material business impact across multiple brands, entities or businesses 
* Policies that present new or expanded responsibilities for the applicable Block Board or a US Subsidiary Board
* Policies that are more like high level frameworks that are unlikely to change significantly over the year
	Tier 2 Document
	* All other Policies that do not meet Tier 1 criteria
* Policies and Standards that are core to key Compliance laws, rules, and regulations
* Policies and Standards that outlines more technical and brand specific guidance
	Tier 3 Document
	* All Standards that do not meet Tier 1 or Tier 2 criteria
* Standards that support key Compliance laws, rules, and regulations
* Standards that outline day-to-day management and operational activities to execute against existing Policies or Standards (approved at Tier 1 or 2 levels)

2. Procedure Requirements
2.1 Governance Approval Process
2.1.1 CPC Approved Documents
The Compliance Policy and Mandatory Training Team (“Policy Team”) is responsible for identifying documents requiring Compliance Policy Committee (“CPC”) approval at the next available meeting. The Governance Approval Matrix in the Compliance Policy on Policies is used to ensure the proper documents are presented to CPC and applicable Boards. The Policy Team is responsible for maintaining the Governance Approval Matrix.
The Policy Team prepares the CPC agenda using the CPC agenda template that can be found in the Team’s shared drive. The information includes:
* Documents being presented for approval and the link to the redline version
* Documents being retired
* Other matters that may require a CPC vote or for information purposes such as Tier 2 policies approved by international boards in the past quarter
The agenda should be drafted at least two weeks in advance of the scheduled meeting date and sent to Leadership for review and comment.
The agenda should be finalized a week before the scheduled meeting and sent to CPC members for a pre-read via slack or email communications.
* CPC members should be added to permissions for each document as a commenter and the document owner should be an editor to address any comments during the read ahead period and the meeting.
At the meeting, the Policy Team is responsible for announcing the agenda, introducing each document and topic, asking if there are questions, soliciting a motion and a second, and determining whether a document is approved, approved with conditions, or whether a document does not receive the requisite approval. 
* The Policy Team is responsible for taking notes to later create minutes with details related to each document presented.
After the meeting the Policy Team is responsible for managing and closing any conditions related to any document and publishing the documents to the SOR.
* Following publication the Policy Team is responsible for sending out the CPC notes using the template.
* Evidence of approval should be added to the appropriate LogicGate document record during the publication process.
Tier 1 documents that are up for Board approval go through the same CPC process as Tier 2 documents except the documents are presented to CPC for recommendation to the Board for approval. The agenda is divided between the two tiers of documents if both are being considered during the meeting and the result of the CPC decision on Tier 1 documents is communicated to the Corporate Governance team to manage the documents through the Board process.
2.1.2 Board Approved Documents
For Tier 1 documents the Corporate Governance team creates a form for the Board meeting with the expected documents to be presented. The Policy Team checks to make sure the document count is accurate and the correct documents are represented. The Board form also contains specific material due dates that must be met. The Board documents may already be under review by the Corporate Governance team before the CPC meeting.
The Policy Team works with Document Owners and delegates to review and prepare documents for CPC and board consideration and manage timelines to ensure the documents are ready to submit either to Corporate Governance on their timeline or for the read ahead materials to be sent to CPC members.
* The Policy Team’s review includes proper template and required language, formatting, and provides comments and minor updates for the document Document Owner to consider prior to submission.
2.1.3 Tracking Pending Updates and Approvals
The Document management system automatically sends document owners and submitters notifications 90, 60, and 30 days before the document is due.
* The Policy Team monitors the dashboard in LogicGate for documents within the 90 day update window and assigns documents to Policy Team members to begin working with document owners on updates.
* Once assigned, the respective Policy Team member will see the document through to committee and board submission or to approval and publication for Tier 3 documents.
* Tier 1 and 2 documents must be approved in the month they were last approved if practicable based on meeting schedules. If the meeting schedule does not align then the approval must be at the next available meeting. For Tier 3 documents, approvals should be as close to the date last approved, but no longer than the last approval month unless and approved extension is logged in LogicGate.
   * For documents at risk of missing approval dates, the team will escalate this information to the Document Owner’s leader within a reasonable time to allow for the deadline to be met. For documents that have missed the approval deadline and that do not have an approved extension, the document will be added to the quarterly report as past due and an action plan must be in place for updating the document as soon as practicable.
* The Policy Team will provide quarterly reporting to leadership on governance approval metrics and will provide the CPC information about the volume of documents pending approval at the next meeting.

3. Compliance & Enforcement  
   1. Roles and Responsibilities: 
Block, Inc. Roles: 
* Role 1: Policy Team
   * The Policy Team is responsible for managing the governance approval process which includes:
      * Monitoring and preparing documents for submission to the CPC and any boards for approval
      * Managing the CPC agenda
      * Managing the CPC meeting and taking detailed minutes regarding voting, outcome of votes, and any action items
      * * Role 2: Document Owner
   * Description 
* Role 2: Corporate Governance 
   1. Monitoring and Reporting
On an ongoing basis, the Procedure Owner and/or their designee monitors activities for compliance and objective achievement.  As outlined in the Compliance Management System Policy, Compliance will provide regular updates, as applicable, to the Block, Inc. Board and executive management. Reporting may cover specific information related to: 
* Policies and standards 
* Procedures
* Audit/exam findings
* Issues management 
* Training 
* Complaints
   1. Record Retention
All Documents are retained according to the longer of: 
* Block’s Data Classification & Handling Policy
* Applicable regulatory requirements 
   1. Exceptions
The Procedure Owner or designee will have authority to approve exceptions and/or exemptions to this Procedure, provided that the proposed exception or exemption does not violate applicable laws and regulations. Exception requests must be documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with the exception. The Procedure Owner is responsible for retaining evidence of exception requests and related approvals.
