1. Overview 
Introduction and Purpose: This Exception Management Procedure (the "Procedure") outlines Block's approach to receiving, evaluating, approving, and tracking Exceptions to compliance policies and Procedures through the SOR workflow system. 
Scope:This Procedure applies to Block, Inc., its subsidiaries, and affiliates (collectively, “Block”), including all products.  
Definitions: 
Terminology 
	Definition 
	Document
	A Policy, Standard or Procedure.
	Exception
	Any deviations from requirements or processes dictated in a given Document.
	Policy
	Policies are high level statements of management intent that formally
establish requirements to drive compliance and outline the goals, principles,
or directives of the Chief Compliance Officer (“CCO”). Policies focus on
communicating binding compliance requirements and do not dictate
detailed steps. Policies are the overall guidelines for the organization.
	Procedure
	Procedures change frequently and describe steps that implement a Policy or
process to comply with a regulation or Standard. Procedures focus on a
process and desired outcome between multiple teams. Procedures exist to
Standardize a process and provide guidance on timing and output.
	Standard
	Standards assist in decision-making and outline further business,
technology, risk or other processes, and controls outlined in overarching
policies. Standards may lay out the operational strategy and functions that
are performed to implement a Policy’s requirements. Standards may also
establish lifecycle stages, responsibilities, and any additional product,
business or market-specific requirements that go beyond what’s required in
an overarching Policy.
	System of Record  (SOR)
	System of Record (SOR) is the designated system used to manage the approval workflows, governance controls, lifecycle management, and authorized access for approved compliance Documents.

2. Procedure Requirements
2.1. Request Submission
Exception requests may be initiated by any employee who requires temporary relief from an established Policy, Standard, or Procedure. Exceptions may apply to policies, underlying Standards, and Procedures, and a single request may reference multiple Documents. Requests must be submitted through the System of Record (SOR), where the requestor provides all required information, identifies the Exception Owner[a], specifies the requested validity period, and attaches or links any supporting materials. Exception validity periods may not exceed six months and must be defined at the time of submission. Optional stakeholder reviewers may be included when needed.


The Exception Owner is determined based on the highest tier of the Document included in the Exception request. [placeholder][b]


Once submitted, the request is routed to the Central Team for governance review. Multiple Exception requests may exist for the same Document, and more than one may remain active at the same time.
2.2. Review and Approvals
The Central Team conducts the initial review to verify completeness and determine whether the request meets the criteria for Exception consideration. After validation, the request proceeds sequentially through designated stakeholder reviewers. A rejection at any point terminates the workflow, and a new request must be submitted if reconsideration is desired.


After stakeholder review, the request advances to the Exception Owner. The Exception Owner is responsible for reviewing the business justification for the Exception, and evaluating the impact assessment and mitigation plans provided by the requestor. The Exception Owner must determine whether the proposed mitigation and eventual closure plans are sufficient to manage the associated risks. Approval should only be granted when the Exception Owner believes these measures adequately address the identified impacts. The Exception Owner may also adjust the validity period if warranted and then renders a decision.
2.3. Exception Validity and Renewal
Exceptions remain active for the validity period defined in the approved request. The SOR issues automated reminders 30 days prior to expiration to ensure timely action. The requestor must either renew or close the Exception before it expires.


Renewals require submission of a new request and are subject to the same review and approval process to validate ongoing justification and governance oversight. Exceptions that are not renewed before expiration lapse automatically.
3.4. Recordkeeping, Retention, and Reporting
All Exception records are stored in the SOR, which serves as the authoritative repository for Exception Documentation and maintains a complete audit trail of all actions and decisions. The SOR provides visibility into active and historical Exceptions for monitoring, governance oversight, audits, and examinations. 


Access to Exception information is restricted to the Compliance team and authorized stakeholders. Exception records are retained in accordance with all applicable regulatory and internal retention requirements.






Exception Management Process
6.3 Exception Handling
A Document may allow for Exceptions. Owners and/or Delegates have authority to approve
Exceptions and/or exemptions to Documents, provided that the proposed Exception or exemption
does not violate applicable laws and regulations. Document Exception requests must be
Documented in writing, include a reason for a request, and provide a description of the controls that the Company will implement to mitigate risks associated with the Exception. Any approved
Exceptions must be Documented in writing and retained with recordkeeping requirements. Any
approved Exceptions or exemptions should be submitted to the Policy Team. The Owner and/or
designee will inform Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as needed, of Exceptions.
The Owner and/or designee will inform Compliance Leadership and applicable committees, as
needed of Exceptions. If an Exception requires renewal, approval of an extension is required.[c]




1. Exception Request Submission**
*    • Exception requests submitted through LogicGate Exception workflow
*    • Request form captures: Policy/Procedure being excepted, specific requirement being excepted, business justification, duration of Exception, and compensating controls
*    • Requestor must be business owner or authorized representative
*    • Supporting Documentation required: business case, risk assessment, and proposed mitigation measures[d]
*    • System automatically assigns Exception request ID and notifies Compliance team


2. Exception Evaluation**[e]
*    • Compliance team conducts initial completeness review within 3 business days
*    • Risk assessment performed to evaluate potential compliance and business risks
*    • Evaluation of proposed compensating controls for adequacy
*    • Consultation with Policy owner, Legal, Risk, and other stakeholders as needed
*    • Determination if Exception violates laws or regulations (automatic denial if yes)
*    • Assessment of precedent and impact on overall compliance program


* 3. Approval Workflow**
*    • Approval authority determined by risk level [f]and Policy tier
*    • Low risk Exceptions: Policy Owner approval
*    • Medium risk Exceptions: Chief Compliance Officer approval
*    • High risk Exceptions: CPC or Board approval
*    • All Exceptions require Legal and Risk concurrence
*    • Approval decision Documented with conditions, compensating controls, and expiration date
*    • Denied Exceptions include rationale and alternative solutions if available


4. Documentation Requirements**
*    • All Exception requests and decisions Documented in LogicGate
*    • Approved Exceptions include: approval date, approver, conditions, compensating controls, and expiration date
*    • Exceptions linked to related Policy/StandardProcedure in system
*    • Supporting documentation attached to exception record
*    • Approval evidence retained per record retention Policy
*    • Exception register maintained with all active and historical Exceptions


5. Storage of Exception Records**
*    • All Exception records stored centrally in LogicGate system
*    • Access restricted to Compliance team and authorized stakeholders
*    • Exception records retained for minimum of 7 years or per regulatory requirement
*    • Audit trail maintained for all Exception activities
*    • Regular backups of Exception data performed


6. Tracking and Reporting of Exceptions**
*    • Active Exceptions tracked in real-time dashboard
*    • Automated notifications sent 30 days before Exception expiration
*    • Exception requestor must renew or close Exception before expiration
*    • Monthly Exception metrics reported to Compliance leadership
*    • Quarterly Exception report to CPC including: new Exceptions, expired Exceptions, risk trends, and compensating control effectiveness
*    • Annual Exception analysis to identify Policy improvement opportunities
*    • Patterns of repeated Exceptions escalated for Policy review or process change
